# Method Catalog Surface Stability Ladder Source

Calibration analysts document package defects inside the intake bay using two
inspection methods: edge profiling and residue spectroscopy. Edge profiling
uses a laser rail to measure seam offset and corner lift on cartons. Residue
spectroscopy runs under a blue filter and reports adhesive solvent bands. The
intake bay is only where inspection begins; release approval happens elsewhere.

Harbor planners compare dock vibration in the north alcove using impact logging
and acoustic ranging. Impact logging records peak force and rebound interval
after each hammer tap. Acoustic ranging operates during low tide and estimates
echo distance along the retaining wall. The north alcove is not the public
ticket area.

Clinical technicians screen stored samples under the sterile hood with two
bench methods: droplet imaging and reagent tracing. Droplet imaging captures
surface bubbles and rim separation before incubation. Reagent tracing uses an
amber indicator and marks delayed color change. The sterile hood is the handling
location, not the final archive.

Field coordinators verify generator readiness from the mobile command trailer
using load cycling and thermal review. Load cycling applies a stepped draw and
records voltage sag and recovery time. Thermal review runs after the second
cycle and notes casing hotspots. The mobile command trailer stores the readiness
logs but does not house the generator.
