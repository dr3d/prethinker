# Clinic Device Custody Packet

The North Hall clinic opened a custody packet for portable oxygen pump P-17 after a cracked seal was found during evening handoff.

The official equipment register entry ER-442 was filed by records clerk Imani Bell on 2026-04-03. It says P-17 is active equipment, lists the cracked seal, and marks the respiratory team as the temporary custody holder until the seal is replaced.

On 2026-04-04, staff nurse Omar Reyes wrote note SN-19. The note recommended keeping P-17 in the station cabinet overnight, but the note was marked "staff note only" and did not grant release authority.

On 2026-04-04, supply lead Nia Park drafted recommendation DR-6. It proposed releasing P-17 to the patient guardian after a visual check. The recommendation remained draft because the safety committee had not voted.

The clinic's device-release rule R-8 governs cracked-seal devices. Under R-8, a device with a cracked sterile seal can be moved only for inspection or replacement, and family pickup requires a later controlling source.

On 2026-04-05, the safety committee voted 5-0 to place P-17 under the respiratory team until the seal was replaced. The vote did not authorize family pickup.

County court order CO-14 was issued by Judge Vale on 2026-04-06. It authorized family pickup of P-17 only after the seal replacement was logged and named the patient guardian as the pickup actor.

The compliance office issued controlling finding CF-3 on 2026-04-07. CF-3 says the respiratory team remains the custody holder until replacement is complete, and CO-14 controls family pickup only after that condition.

A copied lobby notice LN-2 still says "guardian may collect P-17 after visual check." LN-2 is noncontrolling because it copied draft recommendation DR-6 and was superseded by the safety committee vote and CF-3.

Access to P-17 is controlled by the duty respiratory therapist during the temporary custody period.
