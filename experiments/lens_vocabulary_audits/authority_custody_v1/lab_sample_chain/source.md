# Lab Sample Chain Packet

The Riverbend lab opened a chain packet for sample vial V-31 after a freezer alarm and a disputed release request.

The official chain-of-custody record CC-31 was filed by lab registrar Hana Ortiz on 2026-02-02. CC-31 says vial V-31 is stored in freezer bay 4 and names freezer custodian Theo Lane as custody holder.

On 2026-02-03, analyst Priya Desai wrote staff note SN-28. The note recommended moving V-31 to bench storage for retesting, but it was only a staff note and did not change custody.

On 2026-02-03, project lead Cal Ward prepared draft recommendation DR-9. DR-9 proposed release of V-31 to the sponsor after a temperature review. The draft had no effect until the ethics board acted.

The lab biospecimen rule L-5 governs alarm-affected samples. L-5 says an alarm-affected vial stays with the freezer custodian until the biosafety officer records a release finding.

On 2026-02-04, the ethics board voted 4-2 to keep V-31 in freezer bay 4 and allow only a sealed duplicate report to leave the lab.

Research court order RO-2 was issued on 2026-02-05. RO-2 permits sponsor inspection of the custody log but does not permit release of the vial.

The biosafety officer issued controlling finding BF-7 on 2026-02-06. BF-7 says Theo Lane remains custody holder, L-5 controls physical custody, and RO-2 controls log inspection only.

A copied sponsor summary SS-3 says "V-31 may leave after temperature review." SS-3 is noncontrolling because it copied draft recommendation DR-9 and ignored the ethics board vote and BF-7.

Access to freezer bay 4 is controlled by freezer custodian Theo Lane and the biosafety officer.
