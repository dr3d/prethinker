# Transit Lost Property Release Packet

The transit office opened release packet LP-50 for a recovered violin case.

The official lost-property register LR-50 was filed by clerk Mina Holt on 2026-07-01. LR-50 names depot custodian Orin Vale as custody holder and marks the violin case as held property.

Staff note SN-50 was written by platform supervisor Gia Ren on 2026-07-02. The note recommended letting the claimant inspect the case at the depot desk, but it did not authorize release.

Draft recommendation DR-50 was prepared by claims aide Pavel Iri on 2026-07-03. It proposed releasing the case after a phone description match, but it stayed draft pending the review board vote.

Transit property rule TP-7 governs instruments. TP-7 says an instrument can be released only after a board vote or a court order names the claimant.

On 2026-07-04, the review board voted to allow inspection but keep the case with the depot custodian.

Court order CO-50 was issued by Judge Mira on 2026-07-05. CO-50 authorized release only after the claimant signed receipt form RF-50.

The claims office issued controlling finding CF-50 on 2026-07-06. CF-50 says depot custodian Orin Vale remains custody holder until RF-50 is signed, and CO-50 controls release after that signature.

A copied kiosk notice KN-50 says "case may be released after phone match." KN-50 is noncontrolling because it copied DR-50 and omitted the later board vote and CF-50.

Access to LP-50 is controlled by depot custodian Orin Vale.
