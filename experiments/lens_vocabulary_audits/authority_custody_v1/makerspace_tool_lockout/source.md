# Makerspace Tool Lockout Packet

The makerspace opened packet TL-9 for CNC tool C-9 after a failed calibration check.

The official tool register TR-9 was filed by equipment clerk Alma Chen on 2026-08-10. TR-9 names tool steward Ben Ives as custody holder and marks C-9 locked out.

Staff note SN-9 was written by mentor Ro Sato on 2026-08-11. The note recommended allowing supervised setup practice, but it was marked staff guidance only.

Draft recommendation DR-9 was prepared by operations aide Lin Moss on 2026-08-12. DR-9 proposed unlocking C-9 after one clean test cut, but it stayed draft until the safety board acted.

Machine rule MR-4 governs lockout tools. MR-4 says a locked-out machine remains under the tool steward until either the safety board votes or an inspector order clears it.

On 2026-08-13, the safety board voted to keep C-9 locked out and allow only diagnostics by the tool steward.

Inspector order IO-9 was issued by city inspector Neel Rao on 2026-08-14. IO-9 allowed diagnostics but did not authorize member operation.

The safety office issued controlling finding CF-9 on 2026-08-15. CF-9 says Ben Ives remains custody holder, MR-4 controls the lockout, and IO-9 controls diagnostics only.

A copied member bulletin MB-9 says "members may operate C-9 after a clean test cut." MB-9 is noncontrolling because it copied DR-9 and ignored the safety board vote and CF-9.

Access to C-9 is controlled by tool steward Ben Ives.
