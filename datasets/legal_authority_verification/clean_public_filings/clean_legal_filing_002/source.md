# Clean Public Legal Filing Excerpt 002
## Criminal Procedure Safeguards

This excerpt collects criminal-procedure authorities. Each citation and each
quoted span is reproduced verbatim from the public-domain United States Reports
opinion identified, at the pinpoint page given.

Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436, 444 (1966), required "procedural safeguards effective to secure the privilege against self-incrimination."

Gideon v. Wainwright, 372 U.S. 335 (1963), guaranteed appointed counsel to indigent defendants in state felony prosecutions.

Mapp v. Ohio, 367 U.S. 643 (1961), applied the exclusionary rule to the States.

Katz v. United States, 389 U.S. 347 (1967), reframed Fourth Amendment protection around reasonable expectations of privacy.
