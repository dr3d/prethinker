## Substantive Due Process and Fundamental Rights

This excerpt collects substantive-due-process authorities. Each citation and each
quoted span is reproduced verbatim from the public-domain United States Reports
opinion identified, at the pinpoint page given. The lead citation uses the spaced
reporter form to exercise citation normalization.

Obergefell v. Hodges, 576 U. S. 644, 675 (2015), recognized: "same-sex couples may exercise the fundamental right to marry."

Griswold v. Connecticut, 381 U.S. 479 (1965), recognized a constitutional right to marital privacy.

Lawrence v. Texas, 539 U.S. 558 (2003), protected consensual intimate conduct from criminal sanction.

That marriage holding is reported at 576 U.S. 644.
