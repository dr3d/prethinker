# Clean Public Legal Filing Excerpt 001
## Equal Protection and Public Education

This excerpt collects controlling equal-protection authorities. Each citation and
each quoted span is reproduced verbatim from the public-domain United States
Reports opinion identified, at the pinpoint page given.

Brown v. Board of Education of Topeka, 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954), held: "Separate educational facilities are inherently unequal."

Bolling v. Sharpe, 347 U.S. 497 (1954), applied equal-protection principles to the District of Columbia under the Fifth Amendment.

Loving v. Virginia, 388 U.S. 1 (1967), struck down race-based restrictions under the Equal Protection Clause.

The pinpoint holding on inherent inequality also appears at 347 U.S. 483, 495.
