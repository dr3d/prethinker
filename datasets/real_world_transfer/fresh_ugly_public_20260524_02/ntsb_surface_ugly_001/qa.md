# ntsb_surface_ugly_001 QA

Questions only. Reference answers are isolated in `oracle.jsonl`.

1. What NTSB report number, NTSB accident case number, document publication date, and accident date are assigned to this investigation?
2. The combination vehicle that crashed consisted of a truck-tractor pulling a cargo tank semitrailer. Give the model year, make, and model designation of each piece (the truck-tractor and the cargo tank semitrailer).
3. Who operated the combination vehicle (carrier name), where was the carrier facility located, when did the carrier obtain its USDOT number and operating authority, and what was the composition of the carrier's fleet at the time of the crash?
4. State the year, make, and model of the minivan involved; the age of the driver; the class and state of issuance of the driver's license; and the dates the license was issued and was set to expire.
5. State the highway designation, the mile markers between which the crash occurred, the town, the county, and the state of the crash location.
6. List, in order, the times and events for the hazmat emergency-response sequence up to the request for MABAS mutual aid, beginning with the crash itself: crash time, first 911 notification time, order closing both approaches, time both directions were closed, and time of the MABAS request.
7. Figure 4 labels five video events with cumulative offsets from event (1) (the combination vehicle entering the no passing zone). Give the elapsed time between each consecutive pair of labeled events and the total cumulative elapsed time from event (1) to event (5).
8. How many minutes elapsed between the crash itself and the Effingham County Sheriff's Office dispatchers receiving the first notification of the crash? Show the clock times and the arithmetic.
9. How many hours and minutes elapsed between MABAS Division 26 first entering the hot zone and the hazmat team's fifth entry the next morning when a member was splashed? Show the clock times and the arithmetic.
10. How many hours and minutes elapsed between the crash itself and the hazmat operation being completed (when US-40 was reopened)? Show the clock times and the arithmetic, and state how the report describes that elapsed time.
11. Reproduce the Casualty Summary fields and values for: Location, Date, Time, Involved vehicles, Involved people, Injuries, Weather, and Roadway information.
12. List the five fatally injured people as described in section 1.3, including the vehicle each was associated with (if any) and where each was found.
13. The Casualty Summary lists "8 serious (occupants of nearby vehicles)" bystanders. Section 1.3 enumerates them. List the eight seriously injured people other than the combination vehicle driver, identifying them by the vehicle each was occupying.
14. The report's References section cites six prior NTSB publications. List them in source order with their report or proceedings numbers and one-line descriptions.
15. According to footnote 24, the FMCSA's BASIC system has seven categories. List the seven BASIC categories in source order, and state which one had a value for Prairieland Transport (and that value) at the time of the crash, and what the alert threshold was for hazmat carriers.
16. What did the combination vehicle driver's postcrash toxicology screening (conducted by the FAA at NTSB request) test positive for, and why did Prairieland Transport itself not conduct a postcrash drug test under USDOT requirements?
17. What did the minivan driver tell Illinois State Police about her actions during the crash, and what was her interview status with the NTSB?
18. What were Prairieland Transport's safety ratings from FMCSA compliance reviews before and after the crash, and how many violations did the postcrash review identify (and how many of those were critical)?
19. The Emergency Response Guidebook recommends Level A PPE for responding to anhydrous ammonia. What level of PPE did the MABAS Division 26 hazmat team members actually wear, what did the Charleston Fire Chief describe versus what the interviewed team members described, and was Level A PPE available?
20. Describe the federal agricultural HOS exemption the carrier was operating under, the geographic radius and reference point the exemption applied to in the crash trip, and how many of the driver's on-duty hours did not count toward the 14-hour regulatory limit.
21. State the NTSB's probable cause for the crash as written in section 3.1, and state separately whether the report includes any contributing factor or contributing factors language.
22. Compare the speeds reported by the report's narrative: (a) the combination vehicle's speed in the first 45 seconds of video; (b) the combination vehicle's speed when it crossed onto the shoulder; (c) the minivan driver's reported speed during the passing maneuver; (d) the witness's estimated speed of his own vehicle pulling a camper behind the combination vehicle; and (e) the posted speed limit on US-40 at the crash location.
23. The report describes federal violations relating to two different parties. State the violation count and any critical-violation detail for: (a) the FMCSA postcrash inspection of Paul Akers, Inc. (the cargo-tank recertification facility), and (b) the FMCSA postcrash compliance review of Prairieland Transport itself.
24. According to section 1.5.1, anhydrous ammonia is regulated differently for domestic versus international transportation. State (a) the UN identification number; (b) the domestic CFR classification division and what that division denotes; (c) the international classification division and what that division denotes; and (d) the special additional requirement imposed on domestic shipments to compensate for the lower division.
25. The driver went on duty at 6:22 a.m. and the crash occurred at 8:41 p.m. that evening. (a) How many hours did the driver work? (b) What is the general regulatory on-duty limit per shift for property-carrying commercial motor vehicle drivers? (c) How many of the driver's on-duty hours did not count toward the 14-hour regulatory limit, and why?
