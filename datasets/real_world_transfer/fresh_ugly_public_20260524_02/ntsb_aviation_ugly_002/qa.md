# ntsb_aviation_ugly_002 QA

Questions only. Reference answers are isolated in `oracle.jsonl`.

1. What is the NTSB accident number, the aircraft registration, and the aircraft make and model for this report?
2. Where (city and state) and when (date and Local time) did the accident occur?
3. Who is the registered owner of the airplane, who is the operator, and what operating certificate does the operator hold?
4. Who is the Investigator In Charge (IIC), and list the six Additional Participating Persons by full name, organization, and city as they appear in the Administrative Information block.
5. What is this report's Investigation Class, and what is the URL for the Investigation Docket?
6. In the History of Flight section, the report uses clock times in the 10:xx hour. List, in chronological order, what occurred at each of these clock anchors (give the time and a brief description of the event): 1037; 1044:00; 1046:33; 1046:37; 1046:47; 1047:44.
7. According to the History of Flight, how many seconds elapsed from when the cockpit area microphone recorded the stick shaker sound to when the flight track data show the airplane began the rapid corkscrew descent?
8. According to the Routine Maintenance subsection, how many days elapsed from the date the airplane arrived at the maintenance facility to the date it was returned to service?
9. How many days elapsed from the date the airplane was returned to service to the date of the accident?
10. Within the History of Flight FDR sequence, how many seconds elapsed between Stall Valve A first opening (consistent with stick shaker active) and both Stall Valve A and Stall Valve B transitioning to OPEN (consistent with stick pusher activation)?
11. The "Findings" table lists ten findings, each with a category and a description. List all ten in source order as `Category — Description`.
12. The CAE Simulator Flight section lists six configuration items for the simulator test runs. Reproduce that six-item configuration list in source order.
13. The Routine Maintenance subsection summarizes maintenance work-order entries on four distinct calendar dates in January 2024. List the four dates in chronological order and, for each, briefly describe what was performed.
14. Section 57-41-00 of the structural repair manual lists the two conditions that require the post-maintenance stall test flight. State both conditions.
15. The POM "required conditions" for the stall test (in the Stall Test Procedure from Pilot's Operating Manual subsection) include an altitude rule, a cloud-clearance rule, a maximum altitude, and several other conditions. List all the POM-required conditions as enumerated in that paragraph.
16. Which of the POM stall-test conditions did the accident crew comply with, and which did they not comply with? Distinguish the two sets based on what the Accident Flight Stall Test subsection says.
17. According to the report, how many prior inflight stall tests had the PIC and SIC each conducted, and what role did the PIC have in his prior stall test?
18. What did FAA toxicology testing detect in the PIC's samples, and what did it identify in the SIC's samples?
19. Could the investigation definitively determine whether wing contamination was introduced by the maintenance team during reassembly? Why or why not?
20. Were both pilots' Last FAA Medical Exam dates the same, and what were their two most-recent Last Flight Review or Equivalent dates?
21. The Probable Cause statement and the report's analysis distinguish causal factors from contributing factors. State, in the report's own structure: (a) what the probable cause findings are, and (b) what the contributing factor is.
22. The Previous Accident (CHI06IA127) section describes a prior event. What aircraft type was involved, what was the key cause finding in that investigation, and which two elements of that finding parallel the Westwater accident?
23. From the Pilot Information tables, compare the two pilots' total flight time, total time in this make and model, and Pilot In Command time. State each pilot's three values.
24. The CAE Simulator Flight section reports test results in icing conditions where the simulated stall response was "subtle" with no roll indications. Compare that simulator behavior to what the FDR shows happened during the accident at and immediately after 1046:37. What does this comparison imply about simulator training adequacy for this maneuver?
25. The Wing Contamination analysis identifies two possible explanations for the degraded lift-to-AOA relationship. Name both, and explain why the report could not conclusively determine which explanation applied.
