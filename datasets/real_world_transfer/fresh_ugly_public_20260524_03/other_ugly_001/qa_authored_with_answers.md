   The U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Indiana. (Source: "Comment Period" section.)

4. **What estimated amount will the defendant spend on the mitigation project?**

## List / table / identifier

11. **List the four pollutants for which EPA gives an estimated annual tonnage reduction, along with their tonnages.**
    From the "Environmental Benefits" section, first sentence: nitrogen oxide — 1,517 tons; particulate matter — 11 tons; nonmethane hydrocarbons — 81 tons; carbon monoxide — 538 tons. These figures are stated as "on an annual basis."

12. **List the three vehicle manufacturers whose diesel trucks are named as among those targeted by the defeat devices sold.**
    Ford, General Motors, and Stellantis. (Source: "Summary of the Violations" section, final sentence. Note the phrase "numerous vehicle models, including diesel trucks manufactured by" — the list is illustrative ("including"), not closed.)

13. **List the items contained in the bulleted "In addition, the consent decree requires Meyer Distributing to:" list in the order they appear.**
    From the "Summary of Consent Decree" section, in source order: (a) Refrain from providing technical support for the aftermarket defeat devices; (b) Ensure all of Meyer Distributing's fleet vehicles' emission controls are present and fully operational; (c) Deny all warranty claims for the aftermarket defeat devices; (d) Refrain from disseminating marketing materials with information relating to replacing, overwriting, deleting, bypassing, defeating, or rendering inoperative any emission control; (e) Notify known customers of the aftermarket defeat devices of the settlement using specified language that explains the Clean Air Act's defeat device prohibition; (f) Notify Meyer Distributing's officers and employees of the Clean Air Act prohibitions using specified language that explains the Clean Air Act's defeat device prohibition; (g) Require Meyer Distributing's officers to forfeit all aftermarket defeat devices in their possession or installed on any motor vehicle they own or operate; (h) Provide Clean Air Act compliance training for all of Meyer Distributing's officers, employees, contractors, and consultants. Total: 8 bullets.

## Obligations / violations / consequences / corrective actions

20. **What corrective actions must Meyer Distributing's officers take with respect to defeat devices they personally possess or have installed on vehicles they own or operate?**
    The consent decree requires Meyer Distributing's officers to forfeit all aftermarket defeat devices in their possession or installed on any motor vehicle they own or operate. (Source: "Summary of Consent Decree" section, seventh bullet of the "In addition" list.)

21. **The page describes both annual pollution reductions and lifetime tugboat-project reductions for nitrogen oxide. How do those two NOx figures compare, and what time bases do they use?**
    The page reports 1,517 tons of NOx reduction "on an annual basis" attributable to the broader settlement, and 1,484 tons of NOx reduction "over 20 years" from the tugboat replacement project. The annualized figures are roughly comparable in magnitude, but the tugboat figure is a cumulative 20-year estimate while the 1,517-ton figure is per year. They are not directly additive without converting to the same time base. (Source: "Environmental Benefits" section, first and final sentences.)

22. **Approximately how many aftermarket defeat devices did the defendant sell during the violation period, and what is the per-device civil penalty if one divides the penalty by that count?**
    The page states "over 90,000 aftermarket defeat devices." Dividing the $7,400,000 civil penalty by the floor of 90,000 yields approximately $82 per device (penalty count is "over 90,000," so the per-device figure is an upper bound). (Source: "Summary of the Violations" first sentence; lead paragraph penalty figure.) The page itself does not perform this division.

## Cross-section

23. **The page reports a "Page last updated" date and an "article:published_time" date. What are those two dates?**
    Page last updated: January 15, 2026. article:published_time (metadata): 2025-01-10T14:40:53-05:00. Both appear in the page metadata at the top of the source. The original announcement and the publication time agree (January 10, 2025); the "last updated" reflects a later edit roughly one year afterward.

24. **The page lists a "DC.date.reviewed" metadata field. What does it claim and does it appear consistent with the rest of the page?**
    DC.date.reviewed: 2027-01-15. This is a year ahead of the "Page last updated" date of January 15, 2026 and a year ahead of the article:modified_time of 2026-01-15. The 2027 review date appears to be a forward-scheduled review target, not a date on which the page has actually been reviewed; it should be treated as a planned/future review timestamp, not a fact about past activity. (Source: page metadata block.)

25. **The settlement requires destruction of defeat devices already in the defendant's possession. Does the page also describe what must happen to devices already sold to and installed by customers? If so, where; if not, note the absence.**
    Partial. The "Summary of Consent Decree" section says the consent decree "requires the destruction of all aftermarket defeat devices in its possession or control" (defendant-side). It also requires the defendant to "Notify known customers of the aftermarket defeat devices of the settlement using specified language that explains the Clean Air Act's defeat device prohibition." However, the page does NOT state that customers themselves are required to surrender, destroy, or uninstall already-installed devices. The customer-side remedy described on this page is limited to notification with prohibition language. (Source: "Summary of Consent Decree" section: forfeit obligation, by contrast, is restricted to "Meyer Distributing's officers".)
