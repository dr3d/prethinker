## Source-coordinate / provenance

**6. Under which named section heading is the inline quote "MACA may helps [sic] to reduce erectile dysfunction" located?**

Under the "Unapproved New Drugs" section, specifically inside the bulleted list following the line "Your firm's website, www.unitechusk.com, states the following under your MACA labeling:". The "[sic]" preserves a grammatical error in the original label.

**7. The letter contains an "Adulterated Dietary Supplements" section listing four numbered CGMP violations. Which violation cites 21 CFR Part 111 Subpart N, and what general topic does that violation cover?**

Violation #4 cites 21 CFR Part 111, Subpart N. It covers written procedures for *returned* dietary supplements — including when returns must be destroyed, when they may be salvaged, requirements for reprocessing, release decisions, and investigation of other batches when a return implicates them.

**8. In which section is the website URL www.unitechusk.com first introduced, and is this URL the same as the recipient's email domain?**

It is first introduced in the opening paragraph of the body ("…review of your website, www.unitechusk.com…") and again in the "Unapproved New Drugs" section. It is *not* the same as the recipient's email domain (amd@nupacknutrition.com → nupacknutrition.com). The firm appears to operate under two distinct domains.

**9. What named section contains the bulleted list of label claims for the Cleanse-CAMP product?**

The "Unapproved New Drugs" section. Specifically, the Cleanse-CAMP claims appear under the prose lead-in "Your firm's Cleanse-CAMP label states the following:". Note that the formatting of the Cleanse-CAMP and Kidney Cleanse bullet blocks runs together in the source — the Kidney Cleanse lead-in sentence appears at the end of the second Cleanse-CAMP bullet line.

## Obligations / violations / consequences / corrective actions

**21. List the possible enforcement consequences identified if the firm fails to adequately address the violations.**

The letter names only two specific consequences: legal action including, "without limitation, seizure and injunction." Unlike some other FDA letters in this family, this letter does not enumerate Export Certificate withholding or new-application approval withholding. The "without limitation" phrasing means the named consequences are not exhaustive.

**22. Why does the letter conclude that Cardiag, Diabetech, and MACA cannot have "adequate directions for use" written for layperson use?**

The letter reasons that these three products are intended for the treatment or prevention of diseases that are not amenable to self-diagnosis, treatment, or prevention without the supervision of a licensed practitioner. Because a layperson cannot safely self-treat the conditions implied by the labeling claims (cardiovascular disease, diabetes, erectile dysfunction, hypertension), no directions written for layperson use can be "adequate." This makes the products misbranded under section 502(f)(1) of the Act.

**23. What corrective-action expectations does the response paragraph place on the firm beyond simply listing steps taken?**

The response must include:

- An explanation of *each step* being taken to *prevent recurrence* of violations (not just to address the current ones).
- Copies of related documentation supporting the corrective actions.
- If corrective actions cannot be completed within 15 working days, the reason for the delay and the timeline for completion.
- If the firm believes its products are not in violation, its reasoning and supporting information.

**25. The letter says "we acknowledge receipt of your response dated January 12, 2026, requesting an additional four weeks to respond. To date, we have not received further correspondence." Cross-reference with the letter date and inspection close date: how much elapsed time is implied between the FDA 483 issuance and the date of the warning letter, and what does the four-week extension request imply about the firm's projected response date?**

Sequence of events recoverable from the letter:
- September 24, 2025 — Form FDA 483 issued at conclusion of inspection.
- January 12, 2026 — firm responded asking for four more weeks. That is approximately 110 days (≈15.5 weeks) after the 483.
- February 9, 2026 (approximately) — projected response date implied by the four-week request from January 12.
- March 20, 2026 — warning letter signed. This is approximately 39 days after the projected extended response date.
- As of the warning-letter date, FDA "has not received further correspondence" from the firm.

So the warning letter is issued roughly six months after the FDA 483, after the firm's extension request expired without substantive follow-up. Note the answer depends on calendar arithmetic; the letter does not state these intervals explicitly.
