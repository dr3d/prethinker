# Questions with Reference Answers: OSHA News Release — Revoli Construction Fatal Yarmouth Cave-in

## Direct source facts

**1. What is the OSHA release number assigned to this news release?**

26-574-ATL. Stated at the bottom of the release under "Release Number:".

**2. What is the date of the news release?**

April 1, 2026.

**3. Who is the U.S. Secretary of Labor quoted in the news release?**

Lori Chavez-DeRemer, U.S. Secretary of Labor.

**4. From which city dateline was this release issued?**

BOSTON. The dateline appears at the start of the lead paragraph and is repeated.

**5. What is the proposed total penalty amount assessed against Revoli Construction Co. Inc.?**

$4,699,362 in proposed penalties (also expressed as "$4.6M" in the release's subhead/tagline).

## Source-coordinate / provenance

**6. The release contains two boilerplate caveats at the top before the main headline. What do they say in general terms?**

- "Notice: Information in some news releases may not reflect the current or final status of a case, including, but not limited to, the penalty amount(s) or classification(s)."

**9. How many distinct citation classifications does OSHA describe in the release, and what are the counts for each?**

Three classifications:
- Willful: 7 citations
- Repeat: 33 citations
- Serious: 17 citations

**10. The release distinguishes "willful," "repeat," and "serious" classifications. Where in the release are the counts of each (in the same sentence, in separate sentences, or in a list)?**

All three counts appear in a single sentence: "The agency cited the employer with seven willful citations, 33 repeat, and 17 serious violations for:" — followed by a single bulleted list of hazard categories. The counts are *not* themselves rendered as a list. The headline rephrases the same information ("7 willful, 33 repeat") but omits the serious count.

## List / table / identifier

**11. List the seven enumerated hazardous conditions for which OSHA cited the employer.**

1. Failing to provide workers with a safe way to exit the trench.
2. Lack of adequate cave-in protection.
3. Having unsupported underground utilities.
4. Maintaining spoil piles within two feet of an excavation.
5. Neglecting to install a shoring system per the design.
6. Using a damaged protective system.
7. Exposing employees to numerous electrical and fall hazards.

**12. List the two distinct dates mentioned in the release that relate to the incident itself and to the release publication.**

- Incident date: Nov. 18, 2025 (also phrased as "November 2025" in the lead).
- Release date: April 1, 2026.

**13. Sum the count of citations across all three classifications (willful + repeat + serious). What is the total?**

7 + 33 + 17 = 57 total citations.

**14. The "$4.6M" tagline and the "$4,699,362" precise penalty figure both appear. Are they consistent? Provide the precise figure rounded to the nearest $0.1 million and confirm whether "$4.6M" is correctly rounded.**

$4,699,362 rounds to $4.7M when rounded to the nearest $0.1 million (since $4,699,362 / $1,000,000 = 4.699362, which rounds up to 4.7). The release's tagline "$4.6M" is therefore an under-rounding — strictly, the figure is closer to $4.7M than to $4.6M. Several secondary news outlets reporting on this same release used "$4.7 million." The internal inconsistency is a feature of the source.

## Dates / deadlines / duration / sequence

**15. What was the incident date, and what was the news release date — and how many days elapsed between them?**

Incident: Nov. 18, 2025. Release: April 1, 2026. Elapsed: 134 days (approximately 4 months, 14 days).

**16. How many business days does the employer have to respond, and what are the three response options listed?**

15 business days from receipt of the citations and penalties. Three options:
1. Comply with the citations and pay penalties.
2. Request an informal conference with OSHA's area director.
3. Contest the findings before the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.

## Obligations / violations / consequences / corrective actions

**17. What two outcomes happened to workers on the date of the incident (one each)?**

- One worker was engulfed and sustained fatal injuries.
- A second worker was seriously injured.

(Both were trapped inside the trench.)

**18. The release is dated April 1, 2026, and the incident occurred Nov. 18, 2025. What is the elapsed time between the incident and the issuance of citations, expressed in months and days (approximately)?**

Approximately 4 months and 14 days. This is below OSHA's six-month statutory deadline for issuing citations following an inspection (29 U.S.C. § 658(c)).

**19. List the three things the employer may do within the 15-business-day response window, per the release.**

Same as Q16:
1. Comply with the citations.
2. Request an informal conference with OSHA's area director.
3. Contest the findings before the Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission.

**20. What activity were the workers performing immediately before the trench collapse, and what soil/material gave way?**

Workers were removing sandy soil and installing steel plates outside of the trench. Backfilled sand collapsed (gave way) into the trench, trapping two workers.

**21. According to the release, what specific design element did the employer "neglect to install" that was part of the project design?**

A shoring system. Bullet #5 in the cited-hazards list reads: "Neglecting to install a shoring system per the design." The phrase "per the design" indicates the system was specified in the project design but not installed.

## Cross-section

**22. The release mentions both an "independent" body for contesting findings and "OSHA's area director" for informal conferences. Identify each by its named role/title in the release.**

- Independent body for contesting: "the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission" (OSHRC).
- For informal conferences: "OSHA's area director" (no individual named).

The release identifies two different procedural paths involving two different bodies — one inside OSHA (the area director) and one independent of OSHA (OSHRC).

**23. The release describes the incident outcome as "one worker engulfed and sustained fatal injuries" and "another seriously injured." How many total workers were trapped, and which suffered which outcome?**

Two workers were trapped inside the trench. One was engulfed by the collapsing sand and sustained fatal injuries; the other was seriously injured but survived. (The release does not name either worker; secondary reporting identifies the deceased worker, but the OSHA release itself does not.)

**24. The release describes both employer-specific findings and broader policy context. Identify (a) the named OSHA emphasis program referenced, and (b) the named direct quote from a federal official — and explain how the latter ties the cited violations to a stated DOL policy commitment.**

(a) "OSHA's National Emphasis Program on Trenching and Excavation."
(b) Secretary Lori Chavez-DeRemer is quoted saying: "This cave-in is a solemn reminder of the dangers construction workers face when basic safety procedures and safe engineering solutions are ignored. Through our trench safety initiatives, the Department of Labor remains committed to ensuring every worker returns home safe at the end of the day…We will continue holding employers accountable and providing resources dedicated to hazard training and required engineering controls to put a stop to these preventable tragedies."

The quote ties the specific cited violations (cave-in protection, shoring system) to DOL's ongoing trench-safety policy. The phrase "required engineering controls" maps directly to bullet #5 ("shoring system per the design").

**25. The release lists seven bulleted hazard categories *and* refers to the citations as "seven willful, 33 repeat, and 17 serious." Does the seven bulleted hazards list correspond one-to-one with the seven willful citations? Justify your answer from the text.**

The text does not say so explicitly. The seven bullets are framed as the *subjects* of the combined willful + repeat + serious citations ("cited the employer with seven willful citations, 33 repeat, and 17 serious violations **for:**" followed by the bullets). With 57 total citations spread across 7 categories, the bullets must be hazard *categories*, not 1:1 with willful citations. The numerical coincidence (7 bullets, 7 willful) is misleading; the answer to the question is **no, the correspondence is not one-to-one**, and the release does not assign specific bullets to specific willful/repeat/serious classifications. A qualified, uncertainty-acknowledging answer is the correct one.
