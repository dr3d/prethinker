## Dates, deadlines, durations, sequence

16. What is the start date and end date of the Transition Period?

    Start: May 20, 2026 (when Pacious steps down as President & CEO and becomes an advisor). End: August 31, 2026. Defined by the parenthetical: "Mr. Pacious will serve as an advisor to the Company through August 31, 2026 (the 'Transition Period')."

17. When is the $500,000 cash bonus to Mr. Dragisich payable, and under what condition?

    Payable December 31, 2026, conditioned on Mr. Dragisich's continued employment through that date. If he leaves before December 31, 2026, the bonus is not earned. The 8-K does not state what happens to the bonus if he is appointed permanent CEO before December 31 — that contingency is unaddressed.

18. The "Stay at Choice" benefit has two phases with different annual amounts. What are the two phases?

    Phase 1: "the remainder of 2026" — up to $40,000.
    Phase 2: "thereafter through 2037" — annual benefit of $25,000 per year.

    Both phases are "in all cases without any tax gross-up." If Phase 2 starts January 1, 2027, the program runs 11 calendar years (2027 through 2037 inclusive); the document does not specify the exact start day of Phase 2. (Qualified.)

19. Through what date does Mr. Pacious's eligibility for health-and-welfare insurance premium payments extend?

    September 30, 2032, "from the Separation Date until September 30, 2032, to the extent that Mr. Pacious is not eligible for coverage under another employer's plans." Eligibility is conditional on non-coverage elsewhere; coverage cuts off if he obtains it. From a presumed Separation Date of August 31, 2026, this benefit could last up to ~6 years and 1 month.

## Obligations, violations, consequences

20. What restrictive covenants does Mr. Pacious agree to as part of the Separation Agreement?

    Per the penultimate paragraph of Item 5.02: confidentiality, non-competition, non-solicitation, non-disparagement, "and other customary terms and conditions." The document also states "Each of the Company and Mr. Pacious is providing a release of claims" — i.e., the release is bilateral (both parties release claims against the other), which is a stronger arrangement than a one-way employee release.

21. What is the difference between the "Transition Period" and the "Separation Date" as the document uses these terms?

    "Transition Period" — May 20, 2026 through August 31, 2026, during which Pacious is an advisor to the Company.
    "Separation Date" — defined as August 31, 2026, OR an earlier date if Pacious's employment with the Company actually terminates earlier.
    If Pacious serves the full Transition Period, the Separation Date and the end of the Transition Period coincide (both August 31, 2026). If Pacious's employment ends earlier — for example, if he resigns or is terminated mid-Transition — the Separation Date moves up to that earlier termination date, but the Transition Period as defined ends August 31, 2026 regardless. So in a normal scenario, the two terms refer to the same end-of-period event; in an abnormal scenario they diverge.

22. What obligations does the Company assume with respect to Mr. Pacious's pre-2011 equity awards?

    None — the continued-vesting commitment in the second severance bullet applies only to "unvested equity awards granted after May 5, 2011." Awards granted before that date (if any remain unvested, which is unlikely 15 years later) are not covered. This is a date carve-out an extractor must preserve.

    Qualified answer: cannot be determined from the 8-K alone.

24. The document defines "Separation Date" in the second severance bullet but uses the term "Transition Period" earlier (in the first paragraph). Cross-section: what is the relationship between these two terms, and could the Separation Date occur during the Transition Period?

    Yes — the Separation Date can occur during the Transition Period if Mr. Pacious's employment terminates before August 31, 2026. The Transition Period is fixed (May 20 through August 31, 2026) and represents Pacious's advisory engagement. The Separation Date is variable: August 31, 2026, or any earlier termination date. If Pacious's employment ends, say, July 15, 2026, then the Separation Date is July 15, 2026 — inside the Transition Period. In that scenario, the equity continued-vesting period (which "commences on August 31, 2026, or such earlier Separation Date") would commence July 15, 2026, and the health-premium eligibility would also start that earlier date. So the two terms interact: the Transition Period brackets the advisory engagement; the Separation Date triggers the start of severance entitlements. A clean read of the document treats them as nominally aligned but operationally separable.
