## Dates / deadlines / duration / sequence

16. **According to the page, what consumer remedy is offered: refund, repair, or replacement?**
    None. The page states only that consumers should "stop using the walking pads and treadmills immediately and dispose of them" and "Do not sell or give away these hazardous products." No refund, no repair, no replacement, no contact information for the firm. This is consistent with the firm having refused to agree to an acceptable recall.

17. **Does the page state when the importer/firm refused the recall? If not, note the absence.**
    No. The page says only "has refused to agree to an acceptable recall" without a date. The page also does not state whether negotiations took place, how many recall proposals were rejected, or whether the refusal is final or ongoing.

18. **The page tags this as a "Product Safety Warning," not a "Recall." What does the page imply distinguishes the two, given the firm's response?**
    The page distinguishes the two by the source's URL path (`/Warnings/2026/...` rather than `/Recalls/2026/...`) and by its labeled fields ("Product Safety Warning Date," "Product Safety Warning Number"). The implied distinction, given the firm's refusal of an acceptable recall, is that this is a unilateral CPSC public warning rather than a voluntary or Commission-ordered recall coordinated with the firm. The page reinforces this with: "Federal law prohibits any person from selling products subject to a Commission ordered recall or a voluntary recall undertaken in consultation with the CPSC." — a passage that describes the two recall types neither of which describes this action.

19. **Does the page state how many units were sold? If not, note the absence.**
    No. The page does not report a unit count, a price, manufacturing date ranges, or sales date ranges. These are typically present on CPSC recall pages but are missing here.

CPSC fulfilled this requirement by including the statement "The company objects to this press release." That single sentence is the entirety of the manufacturer's comment as represented on this page. CPSC did not include any longer comment or paraphrase of the firm's substantive objections.

## Cross-section

23. **The "Incidents/Injuries" field gives one set of numbers. The Description paragraph gives a different (additional) set of numbers. Are both sets of numbers reported in the same field on this page? Explain.**
    No. The "Incidents/Injuries:" labeled field repeats only the thermal-incident numbers (573 reports of overheating/fires/thermal incidents and four reports of minor burns). It does NOT include the stability-failure numbers (201 reports, 66 falls/injuries, at least one concussion). Those stability numbers appear only in the Description paragraph. A consumer or extractor reading only the "Incidents/Injuries" field would miss the stability-failure totals entirely.

24. **The Description lists models as "Pro, Q1, RM-01 and RM-02." The carousel orders them differently. Is either ordering canonical, and what is the union of the model list?**
    Neither ordering is canonical on the face of the page. The Description orders them Pro → Q1 → RM-01 → RM-02; the carousel orders them Q1 → Pro → RM-02 → RM-01. The union is the same four-element set: {Pro, Q1, RM-01, RM-02}. Both orderings are presentational; the canonical extracted set should be the unordered set of four model designations.

25. **Does the page disclose unit counts, prices, manufacturing date ranges, or sales date ranges? Compare the structure of this Warning page to a typical CPSC Recall page.**
    No on all four. A typical CPSC Recall page has labeled "Units," "Sold At" with date ranges and prices, "Recall Date," "Recall Number," and consumer remedy (refund/repair/replacement) with contact information for the recalling firm. This Warning page has "Product Safety Warning Date" and "Product Safety Warning Number" in place of the recall date and recall number; "Manufactured In:" but no manufacturing date range; "Sold At:" with platforms but no date ranges or prices; no Units field; no remedy contact for the firm. The absence is structural and reflects the fact that the firm declined to participate in a coordinated recall.
