## Dates, deadlines, durations, sequence

16. What is the "Revocation Period" and when does it expire?

    The Revocation Period is seven business days from March 27, 2026, excluding such date. The Release Agreement "will only go effective after the Revocation Period ... has expired." Counting seven business days starting March 30 (the next business day after March 27, 2026, which was a Friday), the period would end approximately April 7, 2026, depending on holidays — the document does not give an explicit expiration calendar date. (Qualified answer: the duration is exact but the calendar end is derived, and "excluding such date" introduces a possible off-by-one.)

17. When does Mr. Lisi's stock and option acceleration take effect, and for how long do the accelerated awards remain exercisable?

    Acceleration is effective immediately as of March 27, 2026 (the date of separation). The accelerated awards "shall continue to remain exercisable for twenty-four (24) months from March 27, 2026," i.e., through approximately March 27, 2028.

18. What date does Mr. Lisi's resignation become effective, and what date did he notify the Board?

    Notification date: March 26, 2026. Effective date: March 27, 2026. (Item 5.02, paragraph 1.) There is therefore a one-calendar-day gap between notification and effectiveness — a detail an extractor must preserve, not collapse.

19. What date is Robert Goodman's CEO appointment effective?

    March 27, 2026 — the same date Mr. Lisi's resignation becomes effective. (Item 5.02.) The transitions are simultaneous, not sequential.

## Obligations, violations, consequences

20. What conditions must be satisfied before the Company is obligated to pay Mr. Lisi the separation pay?

    Two: (a) Mr. Lisi must have executed the Release Agreement (which "contains customary protections, including a general release of claims by Mr. Lisi in favor of the Company and certain other related parties"); and (b) the seven-business-day Revocation Period from March 27, 2026 must have expired without revocation. Only "after the Revocation Period" is the Company obligated to pay.

21. What does the filing say about Mr. Goodman's compensation arrangements at the time of filing?

    The Company has not yet entered into an employment agreement or made other compensation arrangements with Mr. Goodman. "As of the date of filing of this Current Report on Form 8-K, no material changes to Mr. Goodman's existing compensation arrangements have been made in connection with his appointment as Chief Executive Officer." The Company intends to begin negotiations and will disclose any agreement in a subsequent SEC report. This is a forward-looking commitment — the current 8-K does not commit Mr. Goodman to any specific compensation.

22. What does the filing disclose about family relationships and related-party transactions involving Mr. Goodman?

    The filing affirmatively negates both: "There are no family relationships between Mr. Goodman and any director or executive officer of the Company, and there are no transactions between Mr. Goodman and the Company that require disclosure pursuant to Item 404 of Regulation S-K." These are negative-fact assertions, not absence-of-disclosure — the difference matters: the filing claims none exist, not that they were not investigated.

## Cross-section questions

23. Item 1.01 references the Separation and Release of Claims Agreement but does not describe it. Where in the filing is that description, and what exhibit contains the full agreement?

    Item 1.01 contains only a pointer: "The description in Item 5.02 below, as it relates to the terms and conditions of the Separation and Release of Claims Agreement with Mr. Lisi, a copy of which is filed herewith as Exhibit 10.1, is incorporated herein by reference." The actual description is in Item 5.02, paragraphs 2 and 3. The full agreement is Exhibit 10.1. The 8-K therefore uses three coordinated locations for one agreement: Item 1.01 (the reference), Item 5.02 (the summary), and Exhibit 10.1 (the full text).

24. Combining the Item 5.02 disclosure with the Item 9.01 exhibit index: by what mechanism does the filing handle the fact that the Separation Agreement is summarized in the text but governed by a longer document?

    By a qualification clause: "The foregoing description of the Release Agreement is not complete and is subject to and qualified in its entirety by reference to the full text of the Release Agreement, which is attached as Exhibit 10.1 to this Current Report on Form 8-K and incorporated herein by reference." This is the standard SEC mechanism for handling a summary-vs-full-text mismatch: the summary in the body is non-binding to the extent it conflicts with the exhibit. An extractor that treats the Item 5.02 summary as authoritative may diverge from the actual agreement terms if Exhibit 10.1 says something different.

25. The Item 7.01 disclosure refers to a press release dated March 26, 2026 — but Item 5.02 also references a separation agreement dated March 27, 2026. Reconcile these two dates against the "Date of report (Date of earliest event reported)" on the cover page, and explain which event triggered the 8-K filing.

    Cover page date of earliest event reported: March 26, 2026. The earliest event on that date is Mr. Lisi's resignation notification to the Board (which Item 5.02 places on March 26, 2026), and the Company's issuance of the press release (also March 26, 2026). The Separation Agreement was not executed until the next day (March 27, 2026), so its execution is a subsequent event covered by the same 8-K. The 8-K is triggered by the notification of resignation under Item 5.02(b) — that is the first reportable event in the chain. The press release (Item 7.01) happens contemporaneously; the Separation Agreement execution (Item 1.01 / Item 5.02) happens the following day. All three events fall within the four-business-day reporting window keyed to the March 26 notification date.
