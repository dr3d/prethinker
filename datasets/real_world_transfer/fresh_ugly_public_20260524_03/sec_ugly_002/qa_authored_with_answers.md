    The document affirmatively states: "Dr. Coben's departure is not the result of any disagreement with the Company on any matter relating to the Company's operations, policies, or practices." This is the standard SEC negative-fact disclosure required when a director or executive departs. The document frames the departure as part of a planned succession, but does not otherwise explain the underlying reasons.

The 8-K does not pre-suppose election success; it states he will be nominated.

## Cross-section questions

23. Combine the Item 5.02 narrative with the cover-page Date of Report: which event triggered the filing, and why is the filing dated January 7 when the earliest event reported is January 6?

    The triggering events under Item 5.02 are the Board's appointment of Gaudette as President (effective immediately, January 6, 2026), the Board's contingent appointment of Gaudette as CEO effective April 30, 2026, Coben's notification of intent to step down, Coben's resignation as President (effective immediately, January 6, 2026), and Carrillo's appointment as Chair effective April 30, 2026 — all dated January 6, 2026, which is therefore the "date of earliest event reported" on the cover page. The 8-K was signed and dated January 7, 2026 (one day later) because companies generally file 8-Ks the next business day after the triggering event. The press release (Exhibit 99.1) was also dated January 7, 2026, consistent with this one-day lag. The filing window for an Item 5.02 8-K is four business days, so January 7 is well within compliance.
