## Source coordinate and provenance

6. What is the full release number printed at the bottom of this document, including any parenthetical?

   25-1508-ATL (123). The "(123)" parenthetical is preserved literally; the document does not explain what it refers to.

7. The plant operator is named in the release. Is the plant operator cited as a violator? Where does this question get answered?

   The plant operator named in the lede is "Hanwa Q Cells Georgia Inc." The release does NOT cite the plant operator. Only Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. (the engineering and construction company working at the site) is named as a cited employer in paragraph 2. The non-citation of Hanwa Q Cells is established by absence — the document never names it as a cited party. The honest answer must flag that the release neither cites nor exonerates the operator; it simply does not cite it.

8. In which sentence does OSHA describe the second of the two serious violations?

   The first sentence of paragraph 2 contains both violations in a single compound sentence: the second clause begins "and another serious violation for not providing effective information and training on hazards related to nitrogen gas and oxygen-deficient atmospheres."

9. Where in the document does the company's deadline to respond appear?

   First sentence of paragraph 3: "The company has 15 business days from receipt of their citations and penalties to comply, request an informal conference with OSHA, or contest the findings."

10. Where in the document is the deceased worker named?

    Nowhere. The release refers only to "a worker fatality" without naming the deceased. This is a deliberate absence in the source; downstream reporting (Fox 5 Atlanta, etc.) names the worker, but the OSHA release itself does not. Any extractor that supplies a name has hallucinated.

## Obligations, violations, consequences

20. What are the four response options Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. has within 15 business days?

    The release lists three, not four:
    - comply;
    - request an informal conference with OSHA;
    - contest the findings before the OSHRC.

    The premise of the question is wrong. (Qualified answer.)

21. Why does the release cite the OSH Act general duty clause rather than a specific standard?

    The release does not explain. It simply states the citation was issued "under the Occupational Safety and Health Act's general duty clause for failing to protect employees against asphyxiation hazards related to nitrogen gas." A reasonable inference is that no specific OSHA standard squarely addresses nitrogen-gas asphyxiation in this work context, so OSHA fell back on the catch-all general duty clause — but this is not stated in the document.

22. What does the release say about the finality of the cited counts and penalty?

    Paragraph 3 explicitly disclaims finality: "Penalties and citations may be adjusted throughout the course of the case process. Please check the OSHA establishment search page periodically for any changes in the inspection or penalty status."

## Cross-section questions

23. The lede paragraph names two distinct corporate entities. Which one was cited, which one was not, and what is the relationship between them as the document presents it?

    Cited: Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. (paragraph 2). Named in the lede but NOT cited: Hanwa Q Cells Georgia Inc. — described as the operator of the plant in White, Georgia where the fatality occurred. The relationship as the document presents it: Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. is "an engineering and construction company" whose work led to the worker fatality at Hanwa Q Cells' plant. The release does not state whether the deceased worker was Hyoungwon's employee or a Hanwa Q Cells employee; the citation against Hyoungwon implies the former, but this is left implicit.

24. The release headline describes the company as "Georgia-based" but the company name is "Hyoungwon E&C America Inc." What does the body of the release say about where the company operates, and is the headline's "Georgia-based" label supported by the body text?

    The body of the release says only that the work occurred at "the Hanwa Q Cells Georgia Inc. plant in White, Georgia." It does not state where Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. is headquartered or incorporated. The headline's "Georgia-based" label is therefore ambiguous: it could describe the site of the cited work (supported) or the corporate domicile of Hyoungwon (not supported by the body). This is a similar shape to the SV Labs case in osha_ugly_002, but here the company-domicile claim is not actively contradicted — it is merely not addressed.

25. Combine the violation descriptions in paragraph 2 with the response options in paragraph 3: if Hyoungwon E&C America Inc. wishes to contest the general duty clause citation specifically, which body decides that contest, and on what timeline does the contest have to be initiated?

    Body: the independent Occupational Safety and Health Review Commission (OSHRC). Timeline: the contest must be initiated within 15 business days from the company's receipt of the citations and penalties. Note that the release describes a single 15-business-day window covering all three response options (comply, request informal conference, or contest), so the contest option must be exercised within that same window. The release does not separately treat the general duty clause citation from the training-and-information citation — both are subject to the same 15-day clock.
