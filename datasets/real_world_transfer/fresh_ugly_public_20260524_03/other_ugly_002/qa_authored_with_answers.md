## List / table / identifier

11. **List the three categories of conduct the proposed settlement permanently prohibits OkCupid and Match from misrepresenting.**
    From the bullet list following "permanently prohibited from misrepresenting or assisting others in misrepresenting:": (a) the extent to which the companies collect, maintain, use, disclose, delete or protect any personal information such as photos and demographic and geolocation data; (b) the purpose for which they collect, maintain, use or disclose such personal data; (c) the function of privacy controls they provide consumers through user interfaces, any consumer choices afforded to consumers under applicable state privacy laws, or any other mechanisms the companies offer consumers to limit or manage the processing of personal data.

12. **List the four legitimate sharing categories OkCupid's then-current privacy policy claimed personal information might be shared with.**
    From the paragraph paraphrasing the privacy policy at the time: (1) service providers; (2) business partners; (3) other entities within its family of businesses; (4) circumstances when it informed consumers about such data sharing and gave consumers the chance to opt out. The fourth item is a notice-and-opt-out condition rather than a recipient category; treat as a hybrid list.

## Dates / deadlines / duration / sequence

16. **According to the press release, since what date did Match and OkCupid allegedly take steps to conceal the data sharing?**
    Since September 2014. (Source: paragraph beginning "The FTC also alleged that, since September 2014...".) The day of the month is not stated.

17. **Roughly how many OkCupid user photos did the third party receive?**
    Nearly three million. (Source: "OkCupid provided the third party with access to nearly three million OkCupid user photos...".) The figure is approximate; an exact count is not given.

18. **The press release describes both (a) the alleged sharing and (b) the alleged concealment. Are start or end dates given for each?**
    Partial. For the concealment, the page gives a start ("since September 2014") but no end date. For the data sharing itself, the page gives neither a start date nor an end date — it does not specify when the sharing of the ~3M photos began or stopped, only that the concealment of it has continued since September 2014.

19. **According to the press release, does the proposed settlement have the force of law as of the release date? Explain.**
    No. The NOTE paragraph states: "Stipulated final orders have the force of law when approved and signed by the District Court judge." The release describes the settlement as "the proposed settlement" and as "the complaint and stipulated final order" filed in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas. Force of law is contingent on subsequent court approval and signing, which is not asserted in the press release.

## Obligations / violations / consequences / corrective actions

20. **What obligations or restrictions does the press release describe being placed on the unnamed third party? If none, note the absence.**
    None. The press release describes prohibitions placed on OkCupid and Match (Humor Rainbow, Inc. and Match Group Americas) but does not describe any FTC obligation, restriction, or remedy applied to the unnamed third-party data recipient. The third party is referenced only as the recipient of the data and is not a respondent to the settlement on the face of the press release.

21. **List the kinds of personal information enumerated in the first prohibition bullet of the proposed settlement.**
    The first bullet enumerates "personal information such as photos and demographic and geolocation data." Note the "such as" phrasing — the list is illustrative, not exhaustive.

22. **The press release names two distinct legal-respondent entities. What are they, and how does the page treat "OkCupid" relative to those entities?**
    The two legal-respondent entities are (1) Humor Rainbow, Inc. (Dallas-based, operator of OkCupid) and (2) Match Group Americas (services provider for Humor Rainbow). The page treats "OkCupid" as a product/operation/brand operated by Humor Rainbow, not as an independent legal entity. The proposed settlement is filed against "OkCupid and Match" in headline shorthand, but the underlying respondents are Humor Rainbow and Match Group Americas (as indicated by the stipulated-order PDF filename "MatchGroupAmericasandHumorRainbowStipulatedOrder.pdf"). Honorific/entity normalization is required.

## Cross-section

23. **The press release describes the motive imputed to the third party for requesting the data. What motive is stated, and is the third party identified by name?**
    Motive: "OkCupid's founders were financial investors in the third party." The third party is not identified by name in the press release. The stated motive is a relationship-based one (investor relationship), not a contractual or business-purpose one.

24. **The "NOTE" paragraph distinguishes the standard for filing a complaint from the legal effect of a stipulated final order. State both standards as they appear in the page.**
    Complaint filing standard: "The Commission files a complaint when it has 'reason to believe' that the named defendants are violating or are about to violate the law and it appears to the Commission that a proceeding is in the public interest." Stipulated final order effect: "Stipulated final orders have the force of law when approved and signed by the District Court judge." Both standards apply in this matter as of the release date.

25. **The Commission vote is reported as 2-0. The FTC normally has five commissioner seats. Does the press release explain why only two votes were cast?**
    No. The press release reports the vote as "2-0" but does not explain whether the remaining seats were vacant, recused, or not participating. The release does not name the commissioners who voted. (External context indicates a depleted Commission at that time, but the press release itself does not provide that context.)
