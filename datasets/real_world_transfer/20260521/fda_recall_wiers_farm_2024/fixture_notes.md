# Fixture Notes — fda_recall_wiers_farm_2024

## Document type
FDA-posted company recall announcement (an expansion of a previously-issued voluntary recall).

## Issuing organization
Wiers Farm, Inc. of Willard, Ohio. The announcement is hosted as a public-service posting by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).

## General subject matter
The notice expands an earlier July 12, 2024 voluntary recall to cover additional fresh produce — whole and bagged cucumbers, peppers, green beans, squash, bagged salads, and herbs sold under the Wiers Farm and Freshire Farms brands — because the products may be contaminated with *Listeria monocytogenes*. Distribution covered retail chains across roughly a dozen states plus Ohio foodbanks. The recall stems from a positive finding initially reported by Michigan's state agriculture agency.

## Characteristics that make this document interesting for evaluation

- Lifecycle / supersession structure: this announcement explicitly expands a prior recall and dates the expansion against the original recall date.
- Multi-jurisdictional state agency attribution: actions are attributed to Michigan (MDARD) and Ohio (ODA), with the recalling company sitting in a third role.
- Dense structured data: many SKUs with UPCs, package sizes, brand names, and store distribution.
- A long state-by-state distribution table with retailer overlaps and per-retailer product carve-outs ("cucumber only", "green bell pepper and pickling cucumber only", etc.).
- Negative facts: explicit statements that no illnesses or consumer complaints have been reported, and that FDA does not endorse the company or product.
- Heterogeneous identifiers: full numeric UPCs, state abbreviations, brand names, retailer names.
- Both retail-packaged and bulk-by-the-pound distribution categories, each with their own listing.
- Boilerplate health information about *Listeria monocytogenes* embedded mid-document, which is substantively distinct from the recall-specific facts.
