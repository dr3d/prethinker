# Fixture Notes — federal_register_flra_fsip_2024

## Document type
Final rule published in the Federal Register, amending Title 5 of the Code of Federal Regulations.

## Issuing organization
Federal Labor Relations Authority (FLRA), specifically its Federal Service Impasses Panel (FSIP).

## General subject matter
A small procedural rulemaking that amends the FSIP's filing and form-submission procedures under 5 CFR Parts 2471 and 2472. The rule encourages eFiling, accommodates an office relocation within the same building, and switches in-person filing to an appointment-only basis. The amendments are technical and were adopted as proposed because no comments were received.

## Characteristics that make this document interesting for evaluation

- Standard Federal Register front-matter (Agency, Action, Summary, Dates, For Further Information Contact) that is structurally separate from the body.
- A clear lifecycle: proposed rule (Feb 16, 2024) → comment period closing (Mar 18, 2024) → final rule published (Mar 26, 2024) → effective date (Apr 25, 2024). Multiple dates that need to be distinguished.
- Several distinct phone numbers and a fax number (the area-code-771 numbers vs. the (202) 482-6674 fax), each used in slightly different contexts.
- Statutory references (5 U.S.C. 7119, 7134, 6131; section 605(b) of the Regulatory Flexibility Act; section 804 of SBREFA) that look similar and are easy to mis-cite.
- A small set of "the FLRA is exempt because it is an independent regulatory agency" rationales repeated across the boilerplate certifications.
- Both substantive policy content (the amended regulations themselves) and procedural boilerplate (Regulatory Flexibility Act, E.O. 12866, E.O. 13132, Unfunded Mandates, SBREFA, Paperwork Reduction Act).
- Signature block with name and role distinct from the agency contact, enabling questions about who signed vs. who is point of contact.
- Negative facts: zero comments received; no additional Paperwork Reduction Act requirements; no significant impact on small entities.
- Two essentially parallel sections (Part 2471 and Part 2472) with very similar paragraph language, posing a real disambiguation challenge between the two parts.
