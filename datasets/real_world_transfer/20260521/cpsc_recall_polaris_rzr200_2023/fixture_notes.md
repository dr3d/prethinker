# Fixture Notes — cpsc_recall_polaris_rzr200_2023

## Document type
CPSC consumer-product recall notice (Fast Track Recall, joint with a Canadian counterpart action).

## Issuing organization
U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), in cooperation with Polaris Industries Inc. of Medina, Minnesota.

## General subject matter
A voluntary recall of Model Year 2021-2024 Polaris RZR 200 Youth Recreational Off-Road Vehicles after Polaris received 32 reports of steering-system lockup. The recall covers approximately 19,000 U.S. units (plus about 950 in Canada) sold at Polaris dealers nationwide from May 2021 through September 2023 for $6,300-$7,500. The remedy is a free repair to replace the steering rack assembly. The notice is structurally organized as a series of labeled fact fields plus narrative description.

## Characteristics that make this document interesting for evaluation

- A highly structured fact sheet with labeled fields: Name of Product, Hazard, Remedy, Recall Date, Units, Sold At, Importer, Manufactured In, Recall number. Good for direct-fact questions.
- Multiple distinct numeric facts (32, 2, 2, 2; 19,000, 950; $6,300-$7,500; 2021-2024) — small differences in cardinality matter and are easy to confuse.
- A product-identification description with specific physical markings ("POLARIS stamped on the grille", "POLARIS and RZR printed on the sides", VIN plate "visible from the right rear wheel well") that supports compositional questions.
- A clear remedy lifecycle: stop use → contact dealer → schedule free repair → steering rack assembly replaced.
- Two phone numbers (Polaris consumer line; CPSC hotline) and a TTY line, easy to swap.
- A cross-border note ("In Conjunction With" Transport Canada) that distinguishes U.S.-only units from total units sold.
- Negative facts: federal law prohibits sale of recalled products, and the notice itself does not contain individual Commissioner statements — those are pointed to externally.
- An "About the U.S. CPSC" boilerplate section that is generic and not specific to this recall, supporting questions about what a system attributes vs. ignores.
- The Recall Date (December 14, 2023) and the manufacture/sale window (through September 2023) interact in compositional questions about timing.
