# fixture_notes — es_public_procurement_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **Three coexisting price fields with non-trivial arithmetic.** Valor estimado 100.724.911,52 € (sin IVA) ≠ Presupuesto base de licitación 121.877.142,94 € (con IVA). The difference 21.152.231,42 € is the IVA at 21 %, but the IVA / VE ratio is not exactly 21,00 % (it's 21,00064... %) because the Valor Estimado includes contemplated modifications and prorrogas, while the Presupuesto Base only the initial scope. A reader treating them as the same quantity gets wrong totals; a reader assuming PBL = VE × 1,21 gets a rounding error every time.
- **Spanish thousand-point + decimal-comma in financial figures.** 100.724.911,52 — eight digits before the comma. Any parser that treats the period as a decimal separator reads 100 with .724.911,52 as a syntax error. The same digits parsed under en-US convention would give 100,724,911.52 → identical numeric value, but the rendering path through quotation must preserve the source form.
- **Three CPV codes with different functional roles.** 34946000-0 (materiales y elementos para vías férreas) is the principal CPV; 45234122-7 (trabajos de construcción de plataformas) and 50220000-3 (reparación/mantenimiento de material rodante) are complementary. The principal-vs-complementary distinction determines which thresholds and notice templates apply under the Public Contracts Directive (2014/24/EU and the special-sectors 2014/25/EU).
- **Two-document tender lifecycle.** The original BOE-B-2024-29300 (13 Aug 2024) and the modifying BOE-B-2024-29851 (22 Aug 2024) are separate publications with separate IDs. A reader who reads only one of them gets either the wrong deadline (original) or no context (modification alone). Real-world tender intelligence requires linking the two.
- **Pages 45265–45269 across the BOE pagination.** The notice spans five pages of the daily issue. Page numbers in the BOE are continuous across the calendar year (not per-issue), so « 45265 » means roughly the 45 thousandth page of BOE 2024. Tools that interpret this as a count of pages mis-scope the document.
- **Three layered deadlines with different semantics.** Plazo total ejecución 5 años (relative — starts from formalization). Hito 60/70 antes del 31/12/2026 (absolute calendar date, independent of formalization date). Mantenimiento 2 años postinstalación (relative — starts from a different anchor). All three must be respected simultaneously; a delayed formalization can cause the 5-year plazo to slip while the 31/12/2026 fixed-date hito remains unchanged.
- **Stair-step scoring split 50+50 = 100.** Half technical, half economic. Common in Spanish procurement but variable: 51/49 or 60/40 are also valid. The precise split is decisive in determining whether qualitative differentiation pays off. The split is in the PCAP, not in the BOE notice — the notice merely points to the PCAP.
- **Eñe and accents in proper names.** « Avda. de Asturias » (no accent), « Plaza de Chamberí » (accent on the i), « Nuria López Gimeno » (accent on López), « Comunidad de Madrid » (no accent), « Cláusulas Administrativas Particulares » (accent on the a in Cláusulas). ASCII folding will mangle most of these to non-standard forms.
- **« Avda. » as abbreviation for « Avenida ».** The trailing period is part of the abbreviation, not a sentence boundary. Tokenizers that split on period drop « Avda » as a fragment and lose the address.
- **NIF prefix-letter encodes entity type.** A28001352 — the leading « A » means « Sociedad Anónima » (joint-stock company); contrast « B » for « Sociedad Limitada », « G » for « Asociación », « W » for « establecimiento permanente de entidad no residente ». Different prefix → different juridical regime → different applicable tax / liability rules.
- **5ª planta uses feminine ordinal indicator.** Spanish ordinal indicators are gendered: 5ª for « quinta » (5th, feminine, agreeing with « planta »), 5º for « quinto » (5th, masculine). The superscript a or o (U+00AA / U+00BA) is the standard form; ASCII fallback strips it.
- **Hora-12:00 vs hora-24:00 ambiguity.** « Las 12:00 horas del 5 de septiembre » is unambiguously noon (12:00 in 24h) under Spanish public-tender convention. The same string in another regulatory context could be midnight; the BOE convention is fixed.
- **Multi-layered legal-framework references.** LCSP (Ley 9/2017 de Contratos del Sector Público) for the substantive procurement rules, plus the special-sectors layer for utilities (Real Decreto-ley 3/2020 transposing 2014/25/EU), plus the autonomous-community layer (Tribunal Administrativo de Contratación Pública de la Comunidad de Madrid is a regional appellate body, not state). Hierarchy: EU directive → state law → regional implementation. Misidentifying which layer governs a given clause produces wrong legal conclusions.
- **Geographic scope: « línea 6 ».** The Madrid Metro line 6 is the « circular » line covering 70 platforms (35 stations × 2 platforms each). The « 70 » in the document is platforms, not stations. Confusing the two halves the apparent scope.
