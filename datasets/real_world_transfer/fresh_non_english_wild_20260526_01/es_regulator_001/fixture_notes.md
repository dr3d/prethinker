# fixture_notes — es_regulator_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **Spanish decimal-period and thousand-period notation.** « 300.000 € » means three hundred thousand euros, not three-hundred-point-zero-zero-zero. The same string parsed under en-US conventions becomes 300.000 = 300. The document carries multiple thousand-period numbers in the same paragraph (« 300.000 », « 150.000 », « 450.000 », « 360.000 », « 270.000 ») — getting any one of them wrong cascades through the rest of the financial reasoning.
- **Currency sign position.** Spanish convention places the « € » sign AFTER the number with a non-breaking space: « 300.000 € ». ASCII-folded output collapses to « 300.000EUR » or « €300.000 », neither of which is the original.
- **Dual case identifier system.** The same procedure carries TWO official identifiers — EXP202304685 (expediente, the AEPD's investigation file) and PS/00238/2024 (procedimiento sancionador, the formal sanctioning track that the expediente turned into). Both must coexist in the metadata; using only one loses traceability of the case lifecycle.
- **NIE / NIF / DNI as overlapping identity codes.** Spanish corporate NIFs for foreign-branch entities begin with a letter (W8266168G — « W » designates foreign-entity branches). DNI is for Spanish nationals; NIE is for foreign residents. The breach involved a mix of DNI (Spanish staff) and NIE (foreign staff) — both appearing in the same data field with a single label.
- **Stacked-reductions arithmetic.** The two 20-percent reductions are NOT a single 40-percent reduction. They stack multiplicatively: 450.000 × 0,80 × 0,80 = 288.000 €, yet the resolution accepts a final paid amount of 270.000 €. The discrepancy reflects a real practice where the AEPD applies the second reduction against the post-first-reduction amount but rounds or recalculates on a slightly different base. A naive 40-percent-off calculation gives 270.000 €, while the strictly compounded math gives 288.000 € — the document carries both and the reader must reconcile.
- **Layered regulatory regime — RGPD over LOPDGDD over LPAC.** The text invokes (a) RGPD (Reglamento UE 2016/679) for substantive infractions, (b) LOPDGDD (Ley Orgánica 3/2018) for the Spanish protection-of-data layer, and (c) LPAC (Ley 39/2015 de Procedimiento Administrativo Común) for procedural rules including the « reconocimiento de responsabilidad » reduction in art. 85.2. Each layer has its own article-numbering convention.
- **Spanish jurisprudential citation style.** « STS 188/2022 » follows the year/number convention; the slash is the separator, the number comes BEFORE the year. SAN (Sala Audiencia Nacional) uses the same convention. Pattern-matchers expecting « 2022/188 » will miss the citation.
- **Two distinct counts of affected persons that must sum.** « 287 empleados activos + 160 antiguos empleados = 447 trabajadores en total ». Each count appears in a different paragraph (one in the comunicación a afectados, the other in the antecedentes generales); reconciling the total requires arithmetic across non-adjacent sections.
- **Ñ in the AEPD Director's name.** « Mar España Martí » contains both ñ (U+00F1) and í (U+00ED). ASCII-folding turns it into « Mar Espana Marti » — a wrong literal that breaks signature-line matching.
- **Address format C/ Jorge Juan, 6 — 28001 Madrid.** Spanish street addresses abbreviate « Calle » as « C/ » with a trailing slash; postal code precedes the city. Geocoders trained on US conventions split « C/ » as a token and lose the street.
- **« Sucursal en España » as a juridical category.** Not a Spanish company; it's a Spanish branch of a foreign company (UNIQLO EUROPE LTD, an English Ltd.). Different from a Spanish S.A. or S.L. — the branch has no separate juridical personality, the parent is the legal subject. This affects how liability and assets are framed.
- **Two parallel deadlines.** Three months to implement remediation measures vs two months to file recurso contencioso-administrativo before the Audiencia Nacional. Both start « al día siguiente a la notificación » — a Spanish convention that excludes the notification day itself. A reader counting inclusively gets off-by-one.
- **STS 188/2022 invoked on a specific doctrinal point.** The resolution uses this Tribunal Supremo decision (and STS 196/2020) on the doctrine of « concurso ideal de infracciones » — whether the same factual conduct can support two infractions when the protected legal interests differ. The doctrine is technical and citing it without context loses the reasoning.
- **« Reconocimiento de responsabilidad » vs « pago voluntario » as procedural figures.** Both grant a 20 percent reduction but they're triggered by different acts: the first is acceptance of the imputation (procedural concession), the second is paying within the voluntary-payment window. They can be combined — but only if the responsible party explicitly invokes both.
- **AEPD vs. ALODE vs. AEPD-Catalan / Vasco / Andaluz.** The Agencia Española de Protección de Datos is the national authority but there are three regional authorities (APDCAT, AVPD, CTPDA). The resolution must be from AEPD specifically; misattribution to a regional authority changes the jurisdictional analysis.
