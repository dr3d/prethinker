# fixture_notes — ja_regulator_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **Reiwa-era dates interleaved with Gregorian dates.** The press release header uses Gregorian (「2024年6月24日」) while the report deadline uses Reiwa (「令和6年7月24日」). Both refer to the same calendar system but require knowing 令和元年 = 2019, so 令和6年 = 2024 (year-of-era − 1 + 2019). A reader who assumes one or the other consistently miscalculates one of the two dates.
- **Two distinct administrative measures in one press release.** 業務改善命令 (Business Improvement Order — operational, requires remediation) and 報告徴求 (Report-Submission Order — informational, requires only reporting). The two are NOT equivalent in legal weight; a reader collapsing them loses the distinction between "MUFG (parent, reporting only)" and "subsidiaries (must act)".
- **Three-tier corporate structure with one ROOT and two SUBS.** 株式会社三菱UFJフィナンシャル・グループ (MUFG, holdco) — 株式会社三菱UFJ銀行 (banking sub) — 三菱UFJモルガン・スタンレー証券株式会社 (securities sub, JV with Morgan Stanley). Each carries a different juridical form prefix; the securities sub uses the full Japanese name PLUS the partner-name katakana 「モルガン・スタンレー」 — embedding a Western brand inside a Japanese juridical name.
- **Interpunct (・) as a name-internal separator.** 「三菱UFJモルガン・スタンレー証券」 uses the Japanese middle dot (U+30FB) between Morgan and Stanley. ASCII tokenizers split on this dot and lose the surname pair.
- **Mixed-script names: kanji + Latin + katakana.** 「三菱UFJ銀行」 mixes kanji (三菱), Latin (UFJ), and kanji (銀行) in a single token. Tokenizers that handle scripts homogeneously break the entity name.
- **Two different governing statutes for the same press release.** 銀行法 (Banking Act) for the banking sub; 金融商品取引法 (FIEA) for the securities sub. The same FSA action invokes both statutes simultaneously — and each statute has its own article-numbering convention. 銀行法 第26条第1項 vs 金融商品取引法 第51条の2 share no structure; coexist in the same document.
- **Article numbering with branch points.** 「第12条の2 第2項」 — Article 12-bis (the second-added article between 12 and 13), paragraph 2. Japanese statutes use 「の」 (no) to mark inserted articles after legislative amendments; tools that ignore 「の」 read 「第12条 第2項」 and find the wrong provision. The same shape appears in 「第52条の31 第1項」.
- **「第52条の31第1項」 — a banking-law provision unique to financial-holding companies.** This article is specifically for 報告徴求 from 銀行持株会社 (bank holding companies). The article number itself signals that the report-demand is structured as a holding-company obligation, not a general bank obligation.
- **Officer titles in three forms.** 「代表取締役 社長」 (representative director, president) for MUFG and UFJ-MS; 「取締役 頭取」 (director, president — banking-specific title using 頭取 / tōdori) for UFJ銀行. Banking-sector top role is 頭取, not 社長; misattributing the title to 社長 is a domain error specific to Japanese banking.
- **Acronym ambiguity: MUFG, UFJ, BTMU, MUFG Bank.** The current name is 三菱UFJ銀行 / MUFG Bank, but legacy material refers to BTMU (Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi UFJ), MUTB (Mitsubishi UFJ Trust and Banking), and other variants. Cross-period reasoning over MUFG releases must disambiguate.
- **「銀証連携」 (gin-shō renkei) as a single regulatory concept.** Compound term meaning "bank-securities collaboration". Decomposes into 銀行 (bank) + 証券 (securities) + 連携 (collaboration), but 「銀」 and 「証」 are abbreviated single kanji. The compressed two-kanji prefix 銀証 is opaque to a tokenizer that requires full kanji words.
- **「他業禁止」 as a foundational doctrine.** Banking-law term meaning "prohibition of non-banking business". The doctrine is implicit in 銀行法 第12条 and underpins much of Japanese financial supervision. Without prior knowledge of the doctrine, the relevance of 第12条 to this case is opaque.
- **Continuing report frequency expressed as 「四半期末経過後15日以内」.** "Within 15 days after the end of each quarter." Both 四半期 (quarter) and 末 (end-of-period) and 経過後 (after passage) compose into a single temporal clause. Stripping any kanji loses the frequency anchor.
- **「SESC勧告」 vs 「FSA命令」.** SESC (証券取引等監視委員会) is the supervisory body for securities markets and recommends to the FSA Commissioner (金融庁長官); FSA (金融庁) issues administrative actions. The recommendation-to-order pipeline is asymmetric — SESC can only recommend, only FSA can order. A reader treating them as parallel authorities mis-attributes the legal weight.
- **MUFG press release archives the original FSA release.** The PDF is the bank's mirror of the official FSA document. Some quoted Article numbers appear with full-width kanji digits in some renderings (「第１２条」) and halfwidth Arabic in others (「第12条」), depending on the PDF generation path. The two forms must be normalized for citation matching.
- **「業務改善計画」 as a formal deliverable.** The required deliverable is a 計画 (plan), not a report — distinct from the periodic 報告. A reader confusing plan with report mis-categorizes the 2024-07-24 deadline.
