# fixture_notes — fr_regulator_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **Mixed legal citation systems.** The text interleaves French national references (« L. 34-5 du CPCE », « article 82 de la loi Informatique et Libertés », « article 22 de la loi du 6 janvier 1978 modifiée ») with EU references (« règlement (UE) 2016/679 », named CJUE judgment from 25 November 2021). Each form has its own punctuation and abbreviation conventions.
- **Two parallel CNIL identifier streams.** The délibération itself is « SAN-2024-019 », but the procedural antecedents refer to internal control decisions « n° 2023-093C du 20 mars 2023 » and « n° 2023-123C du 26 juin 2023 ». A reader expecting a single identifier line will miss that the two « 2023-…C » numbers are control authorizations distinct from the sanction delibération.
- **Case name preserves non-ASCII orthography.** The CJUE case « StWL Städtische Werke Lauf a.d. Pegnitz GmbH v eprimo GmbH » mixes upper/lowercase brand forms (« eprimo »), an umlaut (« Städtische »), and a Latin abbreviation (« a.d. »). Any system that normalizes case or strips diacritics will corrupt this identifier.
- **French numeric conventions.** « 50 millions d'euros », « 7,8 millions », « 100 000 euros » use a comma as decimal separator and a non-breaking space for thousands. An English-trained reader can misread « 7,8 millions » as 78 million.
- **Two-layer sanction structure.** A single sanction document carries (a) one administrative fine for two distinct breaches against two distinct legal bases and (b) an injunction with a per-day astreinte tied only to the second breach. Confusing the perimeter of the astreinte with the perimeter of the fine is an easy mistake.
- **Aggravating factor cited twice with different framing.** « plus de 7,8 millions de personnes ayant vu s'afficher les publicités » appears once as a factual aggregate and is then implicitly reused in the assessment of the amount of the fine. There is no separate table.
- **Multiple named decision-makers with French honorifics.** « M. Philippe-Pierre CABOURDIN, président » and « Mme Anne DEBET » — the rapporteure's title and the president's name carry an internal capitalization convention (LAST NAME in capitals) that systems frequently strip.
- **Defined-term aliases.** « la formation restreinte – organe de la CNIL chargé de prononcer les sanctions » is introduced once parenthetically and then used as a definite term. Same for « la loi Informatique et Libertés », « la CNIL », « la Commission », « le groupe ORANGE », « GIE ORANGE PORTAILS ».
- **Partial public-figure anonymization mixed with named-entity disclosure.** The CNIL communiqué names the company (ORANGE SA) and individual decision-makers but uses « […] » in adjacent CNIL decisions for other parties — a reader pattern-matching on anonymization will misread the document type.
- **Same date appears in two roles.** « 14 novembre 2024 » is both the date of the délibération and the date that anchors the start of the « délai de trois mois » for the injunction. Date-extraction systems that collapse identical dates lose this overload.
- **Cross-reference to external corrective measure with a separate ID.** The communiqué refers to a closure delibération that is dated 11 septembre 2025 (SAN-2025-007) — out of scope here but liable to bleed into answers about the « état de l'injonction » if the question wording is loose.
