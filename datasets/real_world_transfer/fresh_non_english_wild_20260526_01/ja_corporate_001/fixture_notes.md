# fixture_notes — ja_corporate_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **Mixed Japanese counter system for monetary amounts.** The same document uses 百万円 (million yen) for table figures and 億円 (hundred-million yen) for inline narrative; 1億円 = 100百万円, so 2,500億円 = 250,000百万円. Both forms appear in the same sentence (e.g., 経常利益 2,500億円 in the forecast narrative vs the table row showing 250,000 百万円). A pipeline that maps both labels to "million" without converting is off by 100×.
- **Triangle-minus (△) for negative numbers.** Year-on-year percentages and absolute changes use △ (U+25B3) instead of the minus sign. △8.7 % means −8.7 %, not "delta 8.7 %". A normalizer that treats △ as a leftover artifact and strips it converts losses into gains.
- **Fullwidth digits and punctuation interleaved with halfwidth.** Table rows mix fullwidth digits (０１２３) and halfwidth digits (0123) and commas; the same value can appear as 2,387,240 (halfwidth) or ２，３８７，２４０ (fullwidth) depending on which page rendered the table. ASCII-folding fullwidth digits is straightforward, but tools that don't normalize get false mismatches.
- **Japanese calendar year + fiscal year designation.** "2024年3月期" means "the fiscal year ending in March 2024" — which started in April 2023. A reader interpreting it as "the year 2024" gets the start date wrong by one full year. The document also uses 令和-era dates in some places (e.g., 令和6年7月) and Western dates in others; both must be reconciled to the same Gregorian timeline.
- **Six segments, only two with disclosed revenue numbers in the main tanshin body.** Detailed segment-by-segment revenue and operating profit appear in the separate 補足資料 file; the main body discloses only the principal segments by narrative. An LLM asked "what are all six segment revenues" must answer "not in main body" rather than fabricating.
- **Equity-method consolidation with a specific named affiliate (ONE社).** ONE社 (OCEAN NETWORK EXPRESS PTE. LTD., Singapore) is a 31 % equity-method affiliate (with商船三井 31 % and 川崎汽船 38 %). The container-shipping cycle dominates NYK's 経常利益 line via ONE's contribution. A reader trying to consolidate ONE's revenue directly into NYK's revenue is wrong — equity method picks up only the income line, not gross revenue.
- **Two-currency commodity quote.** 平均燃料油価格 US$620.83/MT — bunker fuel is quoted in dollars per metric tonne even in a yen-denominated tanshin. The same paragraph has 平均為替 143.82円/US$ (yen per dollar). Converting one to the other requires holding both units in scope.
- **Treasury-stock arithmetic with a discrepancy.** 49,096,700 acquired vs 49,165,294 cancelled — the 68,594 difference is金庫株 (existing pre-2024 treasury stock) added to the acquisitions and cancelled in the same operation. Tools that assume acquired-equals-cancelled get a different total and trigger false reconciliation errors.
- **Two-pronged share-buyback authorization with three dates.** New frame: ¥100 bn or 35 million shares, period 2024-05-09 ~ 2025-04-30, cancellation 2025-05-30. Three distinct dates anchoring start, end-of-acquisition-window, and cancellation; conflating them produces wrong settlement-date predictions.
- **Post-balance-sheet events (後発事象) with bond details.** Two bond series — 第47回 (¥15 bn, 0.722 %, 5-year, LNG/LPG) and 第48回 (¥10 bn, 1.175 %, 10-year, ammonia) — disclosed inside the tanshin because the issuance occurred after 2024-03-31 but before the May 8 release. A reader treating these as fiscal-year debt rather than post-balance-sheet events gets the period attribution wrong.
- **Stock-swap effective-date revision (NCA / ANA).** Original effective date 2024-04-01, revised to 2024-07-01. Two dates for the same transaction; the document gives only the revised date in its closing-balance treatment but mentions the original in narrative. Conflating drives the wrong fiscal-year attribution.
- **666 vessels split 278 owned + 388 chartered.** Sum checks (278+388=666). 58,180千K/T = 58.18 million dwt. The unit 千K/T = "thousand kilotonnes" is non-standard outside Japanese shipping; the K/T notation packs both "kilo" and "tonne" abbreviations and gets corrupted in OCR.
- **Multi-row dividend table.** 中間 60円, 期末 80円, 年間 140円 — sum check 60+80=140. The forecast row for 2025年3月期 disclosed similarly. Tools that read row-by-row but don't relate "interim + final = annual" miss the cross-row constraint.
- **〔日本基準〕marker on the cover page.** Single notation establishes the accounting basis for the entire document. A reader who skips the cover may default to IFRS assumptions. NYK does NOT use IFRS — Japanese rules-based GAAP with rule differences in lease accounting, goodwill amortization, and revenue recognition.
- **Multi-line representative-officer signature with kanji name + role.** 代表取締役社長 曽我貴也 — the role 代表取締役社長 (representative-director president) is a single compound title in Japanese corporate law. Splitting on whitespace loses the title structure. The romanization of Soga can be ambiguous (Soga vs Sōga vs Sogō).
- **Sail Green, Drive Transformations 2026 — English-titled medium-term plan inside a Japanese document.** The plan name is in English with a year suffix; it's referenced multiple times. A reader treating it as a foreign-language insertion may mis-segment the surrounding Japanese sentence.
