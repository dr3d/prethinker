# fixture_notes — de_corporate_001

## Real features that make this document hard

- **German decimal-comma collides with English thousand-comma.** „2,4 Mrd. EUR" is 2.4 billion, not 2,400. The same string „2,4" parsed under en-US conventions becomes „twenty-four hundred" or worse. The document carries multiple comma-decimal numbers within the same sentence: „31,9 %", „5,6 %", „5,0–5,5 Mrd. EUR", „2,4 bis 4,4 Mrd. EUR".
- **„Mrd." abbreviation for Milliarden.** Distinct from „Mio." (Millionen) and from „Bn" (English billion). A pipeline that maps „Mrd. → million" or strips the abbreviation as noise will report values 1000× wrong.
- **Range expression with „bis" or en-dash.** „2,4 bis 4,4 Mrd. EUR" and „2,4–4,4 Mrd. EUR" both occur — bis is the explicit German word, the en-dash (U+2013) is the typographic form. Parsing must accept both as the same operator.
- **Two date-related dimensions of the same revision.** The Ad-hoc-Mitteilung was issued 27.09.2024 AND applies to the 2024 fiscal year ending 31.12.2024. A pipeline conflating „publication date" with „period being reported on" gets the metadata wrong.
- **Six different financial metrics in the comparison table.** VW Konzernumsatz (bisher / neu), VW operative Umsatzrendite (bisher / neu), VW operatives Ergebnis (bisher / neu derived), Porsche SE Konzernergebnis nach Steuern (bisher / neu), Porsche SE Nettoverschuldung (bestätigt). Each is one row; conflating across rows is a typical extraction error.
- **„Bestätigt" vs „angepasst" as a status marker.** German Ad-hoc-Mitteilungen explicitly mark which numbers are confirmed and which are revised. A reader skipping the status tag may treat the confirmed Nettoverschuldung range as a new figure.
- **Cross-entity attribution.** The Porsche SE Ad-hoc-Mitteilung describes both VW AG and Porsche SE figures inside one document. Misattribution between the two issuers is the dominant error mode for this document type.
- **Porsche SE ≠ Porsche AG.** The Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche Automobil Holding SE is the Stuttgart-based holding company owning the VW AG stake. Porsche AG is the operating carmaker (also a separate listed entity). Same family name, different juridical persons, different ISINs, different fundamentals.
- **Full juridical name carries academic titles.** „Dr. Ing. h.c. F. Porsche Automobil Holding SE" — „Dr. Ing." is an engineering doctorate, „h.c." is honoris causa, „F." is Ferdinand (Porsche). These get treated as garbage by NER tools that expect commercial names.
- **Equity-method linkage requires accounting knowledge.** The 31,9 % stake flows through to Porsche SE results via at-equity consolidation. The text uses the German term „Equity-Methode" / „at-equity" without defining it; the document assumes the reader knows IFRS consolidation rules.
- **„Anstieg bis 5 %" is an upward-bounded relative range.** The original VW forecast was „Anstieg bis 5 % über 322,3 Mrd. EUR (2023)" — meaning growth of up to 5 % over the 2023 figure. „bis" here means „up to" not „from". This caps the upside at 322,3 × 1,05 = 338,4 Mrd. EUR but does not give a lower bound. Easy to misread as „at least 5 %".
- **MAR vs national regimes.** „Art. 17 MAR" is the EU Marktmissbrauchsverordnung (Regulation (EU) 596/2014), which applies directly without German transposition. References to German WpHG (Wertpapierhandelsgesetz) sections appear in older Ad-hoc-Mitteilungen — knowing the boundary matters for citation correctness.
- **„Vorzugsaktie" vs „Stammaktie".** The Porsche SE has both non-voting Vorzugsaktien (preference shares, ISIN DE000PAH0038, exchange-listed) and voting Stammaktien (held by the Porsche-Piëch families, not publicly listed). Confusing the two leads to wrong ISIN reporting.
