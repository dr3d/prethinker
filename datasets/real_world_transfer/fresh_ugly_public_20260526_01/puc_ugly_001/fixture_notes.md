## Why messy

- **U-prefix docket numbering.** Michigan PSC uses 'U-' followed by a 5-digit sequence (U-18014, U-18369, U-21297, U-21585, U-21637, U-21638, U-21860, U-21870, U-21906). Eight distinct prior cases cross-referenced in this order. Sequence numbers track chronology loosely.
- **Statutory citations in three citation styles.** MCL (Michigan Compiled Laws) form: MCL 460.6a, MCL 460.6a(1), MCL 460.6a(5), MCL 460.6a(6), MCL 460.6aa(3), MCL 462.26. Public Act form: 'Public Act 231 of 2023 (Act 231),' 'Public Act 341 of 2016 (Act 341).' Administrative Code form: 'Mich Admin Code, R 460.149,' 'R 460.149(3),' 'R 460.149(5),' 'R 792.10431(1),' 'R 792.10431(5)(a).' Three parallel citation conventions for the same body of law.
- **Multi-entity coalition filings with overlap.** Two named coalitions in the initial-comments paragraph: 'CEOs' (Clean Energy Organizations) = 4 entities; 'NRDC' coalition = 12 entities. Ecology Center appears in BOTH coalitions. The CEOs coalition then grew in reply comments by adding Soulardarity, Urban Core Collective, and We Want Green, Too — all of whom are also in the NRDC coalition. Cross-coalition membership.
- **CEOs abbreviation collision.** 'CEOs' here means 'Clean Energy Organizations,' not 'Chief Executive Officers.' Disambiguation requires reading context.
- **Three commenters per round.** Initial comments (Sep 27, 2024) from 10 filers; reply comments (Oct 25, 2024) from 8 filers; further comments on three specified issues (Sep 10, 2025) from 7 filers. Different commenter sets per round.
- **MEIU is itself a 2-entity coalition.** Michigan Energy Innovation Business Council + Advanced Energy United = MEIU.

- **Forward-dated effectiveness.** Provision B effective for rate cases filed 'on or after March 1, 2026' — 73 days after order issuance (December 18, 2025).
- **Default-approval consequence.** MCL 460.6a(5): if Commission misses the 10-month window, the petition is 'considered approved.' Default approval = adverse-to-Commission consequence built into the statute.
- **12-month to 10-month timeline change.** Act 341 (2016) reduced the previous 12-month window to 10 months. Order notes the 'eight years' of operating under the compressed timeline.

## Extraction caveats

- Treat U-21637 as the present case; U-18014, U-21297, etc. are prior cases referenced in the order but with their own substantive content not extracted here.
- DTE = DTE Electric Company + DTE Gas Company (two entities). Do not collapse to one.
- MEIU = Michigan Energy Innovation Business Council + Advanced Energy United (two entities).
- CEOs = the 4-entity Clean Energy Organizations coalition for initial comments; expanded to a 7-entity coalition in reply comments (added Soulardarity, Urban Core Collective, We Want Green, Too — all also in NRDC coalition).
- Ecology Center is in BOTH the CEOs coalition and the NRDC coalition. Same entity, two filings.
- 'CEOs' here means 'Clean Energy Organizations,' not 'Chief Executive Officers.' Do not conflate.
- Public Act 231 of 2023 (signed Nov 28, 2023; effective Feb 13, 2024) is distinct from Public Act 341 of 2016. Both relate to MCL 460.6a but amend different sections (Act 341 amended § 6a(5); Act 231 added § 6aa(3)).
- The 10-month rate case decision window is statutory (MCL 460.6a(5) via Act 341). The Commission cannot unilaterally extend; only the filing utility can request an extension. Default consequence of missed deadline: 'considered approved.'
- Operative Provisions A and B are independent obligations. A addresses DR cost requests (where they're filed); B addresses customer notifications (what's included and how delivered).
- Provision B is forward-dated to March 1, 2026. Rate cases filed BEFORE that date are not affected by Provision B. Rate cases filed on or after that date are bound.
- Both physical AND digital channels are required for Provision B notifications. Not alternative.
- Provision B notifications must include BOTH dollar AND percentage impact. Not alternative.
- The 60-day post-conclusion window for notifications is in addition to the active-case duration. Coverage = full rate case duration + 60 days.
- Appeals to Michigan Court of Appeals must be filed within 30 days. Appellant must notify BOTH the Executive Secretary AND the AG's Public Service Division — dual notification, not alternative.
- The notary block has a county anomaly ('Shiawassee County... As acting in Eaton County'). Preserve verbatim; Michigan notaries can be commissioned in one county and act in adjacent counties under MCL 55.273.
- The 'three-part process approved in Case No. U-18369' is a precedent referenced but not detailed in this order. Provision A triggers after the IOU completes its reconciliation under that process.
- The Commission noted Provision A implementation 'is likely a two-year process' for full transition.
- The 'eight years' of operating under Act 341's 10-month window (2016 to ~2024 when the investigation opened) is the temporal frame for the case-volume expansion analysis.
- 'Tital Gas, LLC d/b/a CleanSkyEnergy' in the service list is a preserved typo for 'Titan'; do not silently correct.
- The GEMOTION distribution list is a procedural artifact (proof of service); it is not part of the substantive holding. Most names in the list represent regulated utilities, intervenors, and counsel — many also appear as named parties or commenters in the body.
- Some recipients in the GEMOTION list share email domains (wpsci.com appears for ~7 cooperatives; cmsenergy.com for ~7 Consumers Energy addresses; dteenergy.com for ~6 DTE addresses). Domain-to-entity mapping is not 1-to-1.
- The order is a final order on the present matter but explicitly defers cost-of-service and rate-design issues to a future separate order (per footnote 3).
