## Why messy

- **Two-document filing.** Joint Motion (Doc. 3268) and attached Consent Judgment (Doc. 3268-1) are filed together but separately paginated and separately captioned. The Joint Motion is what the court is asked to act on; the Consent Judgment is the substantive instrument that would be entered upon remand.
- **Case-number format drift.** Joint Motion caption: '4:05-CV-00329-GKF-SH.' Exhibit 1 caption: '05-CV-0329-GKF-SH' (dropped '4:' division identifier; lost one leading zero in the case digits). Page footers use the canonical '4:05-cv-00329-GKF-SH.' Three formatting variants of the same case number.
- **Indicative ruling procedural posture.** The Joint Motion uses FRCP 62.1(a)(3) — the indicative-ruling procedure — because Tyson's pending appeal has divested the district court of jurisdiction (per Griggs v. Provident). The court is asked to signal intent, not to grant the motion outright. The Tenth Circuit must then remand under FRAP 12.1(b) before the district court can actually enter the Consent Judgment. Three-step procedural workflow.
- **Dense doc number cross-references.** Joint Motion references Doc. 2979, 3161, 3192, 3199, 3203, 3229, 3241, 3249, 3258, 3263, 3265 in the body and footnotes. Each doc number anchors to a specific prior court event. Twelve total cross-referenced docs.
- **Hearing-date reschedule chain.** Footnote 2 documents a three-stage hearing date chain for the George's defendants' consent judgment: Feb 2 → Feb 27 → Mar 2, with Tyson asking the Court to align hearings on Mar 2 for judicial economy.
- **Six adjacent appellate case numbers.** Paragraph 8 cites Tenth Circuit Nos. 26-5000, 26-5001, 26-5002, 26-5003, 26-5004, 26-5005 — six adjacent numbers for six defendant groups' parallel appeals. The Consent Judgment in paragraph 4 then specifies 26-5002 as Tyson's specific appeal — implicitly assigning the other five to the other defendant groups.
- **Four-corporate-name defendant group with name-order drift.** The "Tyson" collective covers four entities (Tyson Foods, Tyson Poultry, Tyson Chicken, Cobb-Vantress) which appear in three different orderings across the document: opening paragraph (Foods/Poultry/Chicken/Cobb-Vantress), defendants' signature header (same), and counsel-for-defendants attribution (Foods/Chicken/Poultry/Cobb-Vantress — Poultry and Chicken swapped). Punctuation also drifts (commas before 'Inc.' appear/disappear).
- **Cobb-Vantress in the 'Tyson' collective.** The 'Tyson' shorthand includes Cobb-Vantress, Inc. — a corporate entity whose name does not start with 'Tyson.' Implicit corporate-family knowledge required.
- **Two-state, seven-county watershed geography.** The IRW spans 4 Oklahoma counties (Adair, Cherokee, Delaware, Sequoyah) and 3 Arkansas counties (Crawford, Benton, Washington).
- **8-Digit Hydrologic Unit Code.** '11110103' is the federal HUC for the IRW — domain-specific identifier appears only in the Exhibit 1, not in the Joint Motion.
- **Approximate vs. exact qualifier asymmetry.** IRW: 'approximately 1,069,530 acres.' Tenkiller Ferry Lake: '12,900-acre' (no qualifier).
- **Aka river name.** 'Baron (aka Barren) Fork River' — parenthetical alternate spelling preserved verbatim.
- **Recital lettering vs. operative numbered paragraphs.** Exhibit 1 uses lettered recitals A-F (similar to WHEREAS clauses) followed by 'NOW, THEREFORE' and then Roman-numbered sections (I, II, III, IV, V) with Arabic-numbered paragraphs. Mixed numbering schemes.
- **MFN ('most favored nation') election clause.** Paragraph 6 allows Tyson to elect, by written notice, to adopt the least restrictive land application restrictions from any other defendant's binding-and-enforceable consent judgment. Conditional clause with four sub-conditions.
- **Three-tier declining land application restriction table.** Years 1&2 (40%), Years 3&4 (30%), Years 5,6&7 (20%). Note 'Years 5, 6 & 7' covers three years, not two like the prior tiers.
- **Extensive footnote chains.** Joint Motion footnote 2 references three different docs in sequence to document the hearing reschedule chain. Exhibit 1 Recital A references three different OSU Bates numbers (OSU0005147, OSU0005160, OSU0005174) within the same recital.
- **Bates-stamped trial exhibit cross-references.** 'State's Ex. 3351 at OSU0005147' — trial exhibits cited by Bates number within the Consent Judgment.
- **Daily transcript citation.** 'Daily Trans., 10105:7-20 (Grip Testimony)' — line-and-page citation to trial transcript.
- **Hyperlinked footnote URL.** Footnote 2 of Exhibit 1 includes a full URL to an Oklahoma DEQ appendix PDF.
- **Multiple law firms per side.** Plaintiffs: OK AG Office (4 attorneys) + Riggs Abney (4 attorneys at two addresses) + Bullock Law Firm + Motley Rice (4 pro hac vice). Eleven plaintiff attorneys across four firms. Defendants: Sidley Austin (5 attorneys including a typo in name) + McDaniel Acord (1 attorney). Six defendant attorneys across two firms.
- **Preserved typo.** 'Peter A, Bruland' (Sidley Austin signature) has a comma where a period after the middle initial would be expected.
- **OBA bar numbers preserved.** Five OBA numbers in plaintiffs' signature block (Drummond #16645, Ely #35840, Gaskins #20212, Lewis #32819, Riggs #7582, Koepsel #19147, Nance #6581, Edmondson #2628, Bullock #1305). All Oklahoma bar numbers.
- **Pro hac vice annotation.** Four Motley Rice attorneys (Baker, Solomon, Hermiz, Becker) are 'admitted pro hac vice' — temporary admission for this case only.
- **'/s/' electronic signature convention.** Two '/s/' signature lines: one for Plaintiffs (Drummond) and one for Defendants (Todd, on behalf of Sidley Austin).
- **Conditional stay treatment.** January 16, 2026 order (Doc. 3241) stayed monetary portions but declined to stay injunctive portions. Bifurcated stay treatment.
- **Successor-instrument language.** The Consent Judgment 'supersedes and replaces' the December 19, 2025 Final Judgment 'as to Tyson' — i.e., only as to the settling defendant group, not as to other defendants (George's etc.).
- **Three exclusions for the Monetary Relief Fund.** Paragraph 5 explicitly excludes characterizations as penalties, fines, treble or multiple damages, forfeitures, or punitive recoveries. Three-verb negation ('shall not constitute, nor shall it be construed as, or treated as').
- **No-admission language in two places.** Recital B of Exhibit 1; 'NOW, THEREFORE' introductory clause; further restated in Joint Motion paragraph 9 ('the Parties to Settlement agree that, if approved, the attached Consent Judgment supersedes and replaces').
- **Suspension clause with no-lift sub-condition.** Paragraph 4: if appeal/objection pending on the 30-day deadline, obligations are suspended — but 'unless Tyson's obligations under this Paragraph are no longer enforceable as a result of the appeal or objection.' The suspension lifts when the appeal is resolved, OR alternatively, the obligations expire altogether.

## Extraction caveats

- Treat the Joint Motion (Doc. 3268) and the Consent Judgment (Doc. 3268-1) as TWO distinct documents that travel together. Page footers and case numbers differ.
- The case is a long-running 2005 IRW pollution case with multiple defendant groups (Tyson, George's, and others). The Consent Judgment here resolves only the Tyson group.
- 'Tyson' as a collective label includes Cobb-Vantress, Inc. despite the name. Do not exclude Cobb-Vantress from the defendant group.
- Preserve all three corporate-name orderings (opening paragraph, defendants' signature header, counsel attribution) and flag the drift as inconsistent.
- Preserve 'Peter A,' (with comma instead of period after middle initial) verbatim.
- Preserve 'Baron (aka Barren) Fork River' verbatim.
- The 8-Digit Hydrologic Unit Code (11110103) appears ONLY in the Exhibit 1, not in the Joint Motion. An extractor searching the Motion alone will not find it.
- The Tenth Circuit case numbers 26-5000 through 26-5005 are six adjacent numbers; Paragraph 4 of the Consent Judgment assigns 26-5002 specifically to Tyson. The other five belong to other defendant groups (George's etc.).
- The Joint Motion is a procedural motion requesting an indicative ruling under FRCP 62.1(a)(3); it is NOT itself an order or judgment. The Consent Judgment (Exhibit 1) would become an order only after Tenth Circuit remand and district court entry.
- Distinguish the December 19, 2025 Final Judgment (Doc. 3192) from the proposed Consent Judgment (Exhibit 1) which would replace it 'as to Tyson' upon entry.
- The bifurcated January 16, 2026 stay (Doc. 3241) stayed only the monetary portions of the Final Judgment, not the injunctive portions. Preserve this distinction.
- The Monetary Relief Fund ($18,050,000) and the special master escrow ($950,000) are two separate Tyson payments under the Consent Judgment.
- The MFN election clause in Paragraph 6 means Tyson's actual obligations may depend on later-entered settlements with other defendants. Preserve as conditional.
- The 30-day payment deadline in Paragraph 4 has a suspension condition that may itself terminate the obligation. Preserve as conditional + potentially expiring.
- Preserve the recital-lettering (A-F) vs. paragraph-numbering (Roman + Arabic) hierarchy of the Consent Judgment.
- Trial exhibit citations (State's Ex. 3351 at OSU0005147 etc.) and trial transcript citations (Daily Trans., 10105:7-20) should be preserved as provenance markers, not treated as standalone document references.
- All OBA bar numbers should be preserved verbatim.
- 'admitted pro hac vice' annotations should be preserved with the attorney name.
- '/s/' electronic signature convention should be preserved; it is the official signature form for ECF-filed documents.
- Watch for the question-premise check in Q14 ('four counsel' — actually five) and Q9 ('four-tier table' — actually three-tier). Preserve premise-correction behavior.
