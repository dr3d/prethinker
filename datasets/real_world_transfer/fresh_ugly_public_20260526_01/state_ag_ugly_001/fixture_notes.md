## Why messy

- **Multi-plaintiff caption with "by" signatory.** Plaintiffs are two entities ("The People of the State of New York" AND "the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation"), prosecuted "by Letitia James, Attorney General of the State of New York." The "by" convention attaches the natural-person signatory to the institutional plaintiffs.
- **Defendants are a parent/subsidiary pair with an f/k/a annotation.** "Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC and Mercedes-Benz Group AG (f/k/a Daimler Aktiengesellschaft)." The legal-form suffix "Aktiengesellschaft" is preserved in full German rather than abbreviated to "AG" (despite the new corporate name itself ending in "AG").
- **Two-tier alphabetic definitions list.** Section II Paragraph 4 uses lowercase letters (a) through (oo) — a 41-entry alphabetical list. Single-letter (a–z) followed by double-letter (aa, bb, cc... oo). Several definitions defer to external documents (US-CA Consent Decree for AEM, Eligible Vehicle, Subject Vehicle, Emission Control System Modification Warranty, Environmental Mitigation Projects).
- **Table embedded inside a definition entry.** Entry (jj) "Subject Vehicle" includes a 15-row BlueTEC II Diesel Vehicles table directly inside the definition text. The table is part of the definition.
- **Single-year vs. range asymmetry.** Some vehicle entries have a single-year value (2009, 2016, 2015) rather than a range (e.g., 2009-2009). Asymmetric format within the same column.
- **Dual-brand row.** Two Sprinter rows are dual-branded "Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter (4-cylinder)" and "(6-cylinder)" — the same model is sold under two manufacturer brands. Cylinder count is in parentheses inline with the model.
- **Lower-bound counts with multiple qualifiers.** "more than 211,000 Subject Vehicles" / "at least 19,237 of which were registered in New York." Both quantities are lower bounds with different phrasings.
- **49-state Multistate Working Group listed as inline comma-separated names.** Entry (gg) lists all 49 (Attorneys General of 47 states + DC + Puerto Rico) inline. A 9-state Multistate Executive Committee subset is in entry (ff).
- **Five-affiliate enumeration with punctuation drift.** Entry (a) lists five subsidiaries with commas inside the entity names ("Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC"). Entry (n) references one of these ("Daimler Vans USA LLC") without the comma between "USA" and "LLC." Drafting inconsistency.
- **Multiple monetary amounts spelled out in non-standard punctuation.** "Twenty-Nine Million Six Hundred and Seventy-Three Thousand Seven Hundred and Fifty Dollars ($29,673,750)" — atypical comma placement in the spelled-out form. The dollar figure in numerals is the operative value.
- **Defined-term drift in paragraphs.** Paragraph 9 uses "Initial Settlement Amount" while definition (bb) defines "Initial Multistate Working Group Settlement Amount." Same dollar amount, abbreviated form in operative paragraph.
- **Mediation procedure with multi-level nesting.** Paragraph 13 has sub-paragraphs 13.a–13.j; 13.f has 13.f.i and 13.f.ii; 13.f.ii has further nested numbered items 1–3. Three levels of nesting (alphabetic letter → Roman numeral → Arabic numeral).
- **Cross-references to three related cases.** US-CA Consent Decree (No. 1:20-cv-02564 D.D.C.), California Partial Consent Decree (No. 1:20-cv-02565 D.D.C.) — adjacent docket numbers — and Class Action (No. 2:16-cv-881 D.N.J.).
- **Parallel consumer relief streams.** Class-action payments (up to $3,290 owner / $822.50 lessee, from the D.N.J. class settlement) and AEM Installation Incentive Payment ($2,000 per vehicle, under this Consent Judgment) are separate, parallel entitlements. Easy to conflate.
- **Conditional reduction with cap.** Initial Suspended Settlement Amount ($29,673,750) is reduced by $750 per Subject Vehicle for any of three dispositions, capped at $750 per vehicle (so a vehicle qualifying under multiple categories only reduces once).
- **Footnote constraint on disposition.** Footnote 1 says Subject Vehicles purchased by Defendants between Aug 1, 2023 and Aug 31, 2026 "shall not be sold, leased, or reintroduced into commerce in the United States without an AEM" — a side-constraint not captured in the body of Paragraph 11.
- **"On or about" qualifier on dates.** US-CA Consent Decree was "lodged with the United States District Court for the District of Columbia on or about September 14, 2020 and entered on or about March 9, 2021." Both dates are approximate.
- **Lodged vs. entered distinction.** Federal court procedure terminology: a consent decree is first "lodged" (proposed/filed for public comment period) then "entered" (formally adopted by the court). Different dates, different legal status.
- **Statutory authorities span multiple bodies of law.** Environmental Conservation Law (Article 19), Vehicle and Traffic Law (§§ 375.28-a, 375.28-c), General Business Law (Article 22-A, §§ 349, 350), Executive Law § 63(12). Four different NY statutory schemes invoked simultaneously.
- **Without-admission boilerplate appears three times.** Seventh WHEREAS clause; Paragraph 8 ("Without admitting any of the factual or legal allegations"); eighth WHEREAS clause (settlement motivation). Three different formulations of the same defensive principle.
- **Dollar amounts in three places.** $120M appears in: (a) fourth WHEREAS clause; (b) definition (bb); (c) Paragraph 9. Each phrasing differs slightly.

## Extraction caveats

- Treat Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC and Mercedes-Benz Group AG as TWO separate defendants in a parent/subsidiary relationship; do not collapse to one.
- Preserve "Daimler Aktiengesellschaft" as the historical name (f/k/a annotation); do not normalize to "Daimler AG."
- Preserve the 15-row BlueTEC II Diesel Vehicles table as a table, not as inline definition text.
- Preserve single-year entries ("2009", "2016") as single-year, not as range "2009-2009".
- The two Sprinter rows are model-dual-branded ("Mercedes-Benz or Freightliner Sprinter"); preserve the OR semantics — these are the same vehicle sold under two brands.
- Bind ">211,000" and ">19,237" as lower bounds; do not assert exact counts.
- Distinguish the Multistate Executive Committee (9 states) from the Multistate Working Group (49 entities). The Executive Committee is a subset.
- The Multistate Working Group lists 47 states + DC + Puerto Rico = 49 entities. Some sources may include "and territories" — preserve the entry (gg) enumeration verbatim.
- Distinguish the AEM Installation Incentive Payment ($2,000, this Judgment) from class-action per-vehicle payments ($3,290 owner / $822.50 lessee, separate D.N.J. settlement). Both are payable; one does not extinguish the other.
- The $750 per-vehicle reduction is capped: a vehicle qualifying under multiple categories reduces by $750 once, not $2,250.
- Footnote 1's "shall not be sold, leased, or reintroduced into commerce in the United States without an AEM" is a separate operative constraint, not just commentary.
- "Lodged" and "entered" are distinct legal events with different dates for the US-CA Consent Decree; preserve both.
- "On or about" qualifiers preserve approximate-date semantics — do not commit to exact dates.
- Initial Settlement Amount (Para 9) and Initial Multistate Working Group Settlement Amount (entry (bb)) refer to the same $120M; canonicalize to the full defined term while preserving Para 9's abbreviated wording verbatim.
- The Initial New York Settlement Amount payment is contingent on three "Settlement Documents" being delivered; do not assert payment is automatic upon entry of the Judgment.
- If Defendants dispute the Final Suspended Settlement Amount, a binding mediation procedure applies (Paragraph 13); preserve the conditional trigger.
- Index No. blank in caption is a fill-in placeholder, not a redaction.
- Preserve the punctuation drift between "Mercedes-Benz USA, LLC" and "Daimler Vans USA LLC" / "Daimler Vans USA, LLC" — both variants appear in the source.
- The seventh and eighth WHEREAS clauses contain defensive language ("nothing in this Judgment shall constitute an admission of any wrongdoing") that should not be paraphrased as concessive or admissive when extracted.
