## Reasoning / join

**q016.** Which product is cited under all three legal theories (unapproved new drug, misbranded drug, and adulterated dietary supplement)?  
**A.** Cardiag and MACA. (Diabetech is cited as unapproved + misbranded but not under the adulterated dietary supplement / CGMP section. Cleanse-CAMP / Cleanse-camp is cited as unapproved + adulterated dietary supplement but not as misbranded.)

**q017.** Why does FDA conclude that Cardiag, Diabetech, and MACA in particular cannot bear "adequate directions for use" by a layperson?  
**A.** Because they are intended for treatment or prevention of one or more diseases that are not amenable to self-diagnosis, treatment, or prevention without the supervision of a licensed practitioner. It is therefore impossible to write adequate directions for a layperson to use them safely for their intended purposes.

**q018.** Identify the website-based MACA claim and the website-based Diabetech claim that each appear to be intended-use evidence under section 201(g)(1)(B).  
**A.** MACA: "MACA may helps [sic] to reduce erectile dysfunction." (or "MACA may reduce blood pressure"). Diabetech: e.g., "Helping in daily regulation of blood glucose levels" (or any of "Diabetech powder for Diabetics", "supports blood sugar control", "helps enhance insulin sensitivity").

**q019.** The letter cites two specific CFR sections that the firm must also comply with after establishing product specifications. Which two?  
**A.** 21 CFR 111.73 and 21 CFR 111.75.

**q020.** Map each Cleanse-CAMP / Kidney Cleanse / Pure Psyllium Husk product to the quoted claim that places it among the unapproved new drugs.  
**A.** Cleanse-CAMP: "Relieves Constipation" and "[H]ealthy alternative to over-the-counter laxatives…". Kidney Cleanse: "This supplement helps…reduce inflammation". Pure Psyllium Husk: "Support to lower LDL Cholesterol".

## Negative / exception / contrast

**q021.** Did the firm provide a substantive response to the Form FDA 483 prior to the warning letter?  
**A.** No. The firm's January 12, 2026 reply only requested an additional four weeks to respond, and FDA states it received no further correspondence from the firm.

**q022.** Is Kidney Cleanse cited in the CGMP / adulterated-dietary-supplement section of the letter?  
**A.** No. The CGMP section names only Cardiag, Cleanse-camp, and MACA 1000 mg as examples; Kidney Cleanse is named only as an unapproved new drug.

**q023.** Does FDA name Pure Pysyllium Husk as misbranded under section 502(f)(1)?  
**A.** No. Only Cardiag, Diabetech, and MACA are named in the misbranded-drugs section.

**q024.** Did the firm dispute FDA's claims and provide reasoning in its response?  
**A.** No, not as reported in the letter. The letter says only that the firm requested a four-week extension and did not follow up.

**q025.** Are Nupack Inc. and the website www.unitechusk.com the same entity name?  
**A.** No. Nupack Inc. is the firm name and the legal recipient; www.unitechusk.com is the website where Nupack takes orders for these products. They are not the same string and the letter treats them as distinct identifiers, though both refer to Nupack's operations.
