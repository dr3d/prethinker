## Reasoning / join

**q016.** How many people in total were fatally injured in this accident, combining airplane crew and ground fatalities?  
**A.** 14 (3 crew + 11 on the ground).

**q017.** Of the three crewmembers, which had the most total reported flight hours, and how many?  
**A.** The relief officer, about 15,250 hours.

**q018.** Of the three crewmembers, which had the fewest hours in the MD-11 make/model?  
**A.** The first officer, with about 994 hours in type.

**q019.** Was the special detailed inspection (SDI) of the left pylon aft mount lugs overdue at the time of the accident? Explain briefly.  
**A.** No. The SDI would have been due at 29,200 cycles; the accident airplane had accumulated 21,043 cycles, so the inspection was not yet due.

**q020.** Comparing total fatalities in the 1979 American Airlines flight 191 accident described in the "Similar Events" section to the UPS flight 2976 accident, which had more total fatalities and by how many?  
**A.** AA flight 191 had more fatalities: 273 (271 onboard + 2 on the ground) compared with 14 for UPS flight 2976 — a difference of 259.

## Negative / exception / contrast

**q021.** Was an ATC group formed for this investigation?  
**A.** No. An NTSB ATC specialist was on site to collect ATC information, but an ATC group was not formed.

**q022.** Did the forward lug's outboard fracture surface show evidence of fatigue cracking?  
**A.** No. The forward lug's outboard fracture surface consisted entirely of overstress with no indications of fatigue cracking.

**q023.** Did the right engine separate from the right wing?  
**A.** No. The right engine remained attached to the right wing pylon; both were found at the accident site. (The right pylon's forward and aft mounts had separated from the right wing pylon but the right engine itself was still attached to the pylon.)

**q024.** Does this preliminary report state a probable cause for the accident?  
**A.** No. This is a preliminary report and explicitly notes the investigation is ongoing; the report describes findings such as fatigue and overstress at the left pylon aft mount lugs but does not declare a probable cause.

**q025.** Was the accident airplane equipped with GE CF-6 engines or Pratt & Whitney PW4000 engines?  
**A.** GE CF-6 engines. (The MD-11 can be equipped with either GE CF-6 or PW4000; the accident airplane had the GE CF-6.)
