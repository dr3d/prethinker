# QA — ntsb_marine_ugly_001

## Direct lookup

**q001.** What was the name of the towing vessel involved?  
**A.** Baylor J. Tregre.

**q002.** Who owned the barge MARMAC 27?  
**A.** McDonough Marine Service. (It was leased by Manson Construction Co.)

**q003.** What is the NTSB accident case number for this investigation?  
**A.** DCA24FM038.

**q004.** What was the estimated dollar damage to the towing vessel?  
**A.** $2 million.

**q005.** What was the cargo on the barge MARMAC 27?  
**A.** A production platform and a helideck.

## Timeline / date / order

**q006.** When did the Baylor J. Tregre depart Houma, and approximately at what time?  
**A.** May 11, 2024, about 0340 (CDT).

**q007.** At what time was the mate's VHF distress call made?  
**A.** 1657 (CDT).

**q008.** At what time was the last AIS transmission from the Baylor J. Tregre?  
**A.** 1712 (CDT).

**q009.** What was the time-order in which these NWS products were issued on May 13, 2024: the Coastal Waters Forecast, the Severe Thunderstorm Watch, the Special Marine Warning, and its update?  
**A.** Severe thunderstorm watch (1257) → Coastal Waters Forecast (1409) → Special Marine Warning (1501) → Special Marine Warning update (1544).

**q010.** Over what dates did salvage operations take place, and when was the vessel brought to the Galveston salvage dock?  
**A.** Salvage operations were conducted from May 18 to June 15, 2024; the vessel was transported to a salvage dock in Galveston, Texas, on June 12, 2024.

## Reasoning / join

**q016.** What was the probable cause as determined by the NTSB?  
**A.** The mate's inability to maneuver the tow into the wind due to the overwhelming towline force generated by the towed barge during the sudden onset of severe weather, resulting in unrecoverable heeling.

**q017.** What was the elapsed time between the mate's VHF distress call and the Coast Guard response boat recovering all four crewmembers?  
**A.** From 1657 to 1808 — about 71 minutes (1 hour 11 minutes).

**q018.** The body of the report describes a 1,800 ft figure and an 800 ft figure for tow wire. What does each refer to?  
**A.** 1,800 feet was the length of 1.25-inch tow wire carried on the aft double-drum towing winch (i.e., total wire on the drum). 800 feet was the length of the main tow wire (towline) actually paid out, connecting the towing arrangement to the barge MARMAC 27.

**q019.** Which Coast Guard entity received each of the following: (a) the VHF distress call; (b) the EPIRB alert; and which entities launched (c) the response boat and (d) the rescue helicopter?  
**A.** (a) Coast Guard Sector Houston-Galveston received the VHF distress call. (b) Coast Guard District 8 New Orleans received the EPIRB alert. (c) Station Freeport (Texas) launched the response boat. (d) Air Station Houston launched the MH-65 helicopter.

**q020.** Why was the production platform/helideck barge unable to use the Gulf Intracoastal Waterway?  
**A.** Because the platform on the barge was too tall to transit under some bridge and powerline crossings on that waterway.

## Negative / exception / contrast

**q021.** Did the Baylor J. Tregre have an emergency tow-release function in the wheelhouse?  
**A.** No. The report states there was no emergency release function in the wheelhouse, nor was one required (per 46 CFR Subchapter M).

**q022.** Were the fractures and indentations on the vessel's superstructure caused by the capsizing event itself?  
**A.** No. The NTSB investigators found the fractures and indentations on the superstructure all appeared to be from recovery (salvage) activities, not from the casualty event.

**q023.** According to the NTSB analysis, would the crew have been able to avoid the storm if they had received the Special Marine Warnings via VHF?  
**A.** No. The Analysis explicitly states that even if the crew had received the Special Marine Warnings, they would not have been able to avoid the storm given the vessel's speed (~4–5 knots), the tow's position (~17 miles from Galveston, surrounded by storms), and the speed of the storm's advance.

**q024.** Did the suitability survey conducted on April 22, 2024 specify weather criteria limits for the tow?  
**A.** No. The survey recommended "Careful consideration is to be given to weather criteria for the tow of MARMAC 27" but did not specify weather criteria limits.

**q025.** Was the vessel renamed before or after Trinity Tugs LLC acquired it, and what was its prior name?  
**A.** It was renamed by Trinity Tugs LLC after acquisition. The vessel was built in 1997 as the Trent Joseph for Coastal Towing LLC; Trinity Tugs LLC acquired it in 2023 and renamed it Baylor J. Tregre.
