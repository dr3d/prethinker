**q018.** Which CGMP violation explicitly discusses the firm using a process qualification study performed at a different facility?  
**A.** Violation 2 — 21 CFR 211.100(a), the production and process control violation.

**q019.** The firm argued in some cases that its conduct was at the customer's direction (e.g., labeled storage range, discontinued process validation). Does FDA accept that as a defense, and where does the letter say so?  
**A.** No. The "Responsibilities as a Contractor" section states FDA regards contractors as extensions of the manufacturer, and that the firm is responsible for the quality of drugs it produces "regardless of agreements in place with product owners."

**q020.** The letter recommends the firm engage a qualified consultant. What two tasks does FDA say that consultant should perform?  
**A.** (1) Evaluate the firm's operations and assist it in meeting CGMP requirements, and (2) perform a comprehensive six-system audit of the entire operation for CGMP compliance and evaluate the completion and efficacy of corrective actions and preventive actions before the firm pursues resolution of its compliance status with FDA.

## Negative / exception / contrast

**q021.** Does FDA cite specific failing CFU/g numbers for the contaminated lots?  
**A.** No. The specific lot identifiers, products, and CFU numbers are redacted as (b)(4); only one entry is partially visible as "TYMC: (b)(4) colony forming units (CFU) per gram." The actual failing numerics are not in the source.

**q022.** The letter says the firm's response to violation 1 was inadequate. What did the firm's risk assessment fail to extend to?  
**A.** The risk assessment did not extend to all drug products where the (b)(4) treatment was attempted, and the response did not address product quality and patient safety risks for lots on which (b)(4) treatment was used or product released after failing microbiological results. Nor did it address whether additional bacterial species were present, or provide evidence that growth on total yeast and mold plates was solely bacterial.

**q023.** Did the firm provide revised complaint and investigation procedures in its response?  
**A.** No. The letter states the firm committed to revising the procedures but did not provide the revised procedures.

**q024.** Under FDA's analysis, are any of the firm's products approved as new drugs under section 505 of the FD&C Act?  
**A.** No. The letter states no approved applications pursuant to section 505 are in effect for these products, and therefore the products are unapproved new drugs.

**q025.** For the stability violation (211.166(a)), the firm reduced the labeled temperature range in its response. Does FDA find the reduced range acceptable?  
**A.** No. FDA notes the firm reduced the labeled temperature limits but states they were still outside the range supported by the firm's stability data.
