q011. Which specific claim numbers of the appealed application did the examiner reject (the "contested claims")?
Answer: Claims 1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, and 25-39.

q012. Independent claim 1 of the appealed application recites three lettered sub-elements. What does each sub-element describe?
Answer: (a) a matrix polymer selected from the group of a first polymer having a crystalline melting temperature ("Tm") or a glass transition temperature ("Tg") of 180 degrees C or higher, a second polymer other than the first polymer, and combinations thereof; (b) an open and interconnecting pore structure characterized by a plurality of open and interconnecting pores, a Gurley air permeability of 1 sec/100 cc or greater, and a porosity of 90% or less; and (c) an ionic conductivity characterized by a MacMullin number ("NM") of 1 to 15.

q013. State the specific numerical parameters recited in claim 1 for: crystalline melting/glass transition temperature; Gurley air permeability; porosity; and MacMullin number.
Answer: Tm or Tg of 180 degrees C or higher; Gurley air permeability of 1 sec/100 cc or greater; porosity of 90 % or less; MacMullin number (NM) of 1 to 15.

q014. Under what statutory grounds and against which claim ranges did the examiner reject the contested claims using Song '656 and Song '525?
Answer: Under 35 U.S.C. § 102(a)(1) as anticipated by Song '656 for all contested claims (1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, and 25-39). Under 35 U.S.C. § 102(a)(1) as anticipated by Song '525 for claims 1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, 25-27, 38, and 39. Under 35 U.S.C. § 103 as obvious over Song '525 for claims 28-37.

q015. What is the content of Footnote 1?
Answer: Citations to "J.A." refer to the corrected joint appendix filed by the parties. Dkt. No. 24.

q016. Footnote 2 addresses appellant's § 102(b)(2) argument that his own patent publications should not be prior art. What did the court conclude about why § 102(b)(2) does not disqualify the references?
Answer: Section 102(b)(2) can only disqualify prior art cited under § 102(a)(2). Here, the examiner cited the references as printed publications under § 102(a)(1). The examiner correctly determined that the exception under § 102(b)(2) does not apply to disqualify Song '656 or Song '525, which were both published before the critical date of the appealed application.

q017. What argument did appellant raise against the § 103 obviousness rejection of claims 28-37, and how did the court treat that argument?
Answer: Appellant argued only that Song '525 is "unanalogous" to the appealed application. The court held that appellant forfeited this argument by not raising it before the Board (citing In re Google Tech. Holdings LLC, 980 F.3d 858, 863 (Fed. Cir. 2020)), and in any event found the analogousness argument unpersuasive.

q018. Did the court treat the word "separator" in the preamble of claim 1 as a limiting term, and what did it explain about the term?
Answer: No. The court agreed with the Board that the word "separator" indicates only the intended use of the film and that claim 1 recites a structurally complete device without the preamble. The court explained that the specification does not suggest any structural meaning of "separator" for the film itself but explains that a "separator is located between positive and negative electrodes in an electrochemical cell"; the positive and negative electrodes are not part of the claimed structure.

q019. What did the court conclude about the comparison between Figures 2A and 2B of the appealed application and Figures 2A and 2B of Song '656?
Answer: The court noted that Figures 2A and 2B in the appealed application, showing electron micrographs of "interconnecting pores . . . for an unfilled and filled separator film," appear to be identical to Figures 2A and 2B in Song '656, which show a micrograph of "a typical [polyolefin]-based separator film and a filled Example separator film." Additionally appellant conceded that Song '656 discloses an open pore structure with interconnecting pores.

q020. Why did the court say it did not need to address the Board's inherent-anticipation theory for the Song '525 ground?
Answer: Because the court found it unnecessary to determine if the method of Song '525 inherently discloses an open and interconnecting pore structure: Song '525 directly discloses the structure. The examiner observed that Figure 2 of Song '525 is identical to Figure 2B in the appealed application, which appellant describes in his own specification as showing "a plurality of open, interconnecting pores." Song '525 thus discloses an open and interconnecting pore structure by appellant's own description.

q021. How did the court treat appellant's argument that distinctions such as "multiple form factors" and "complete in-plane interconnectivity" between pores establish a difference between the claim and the prior art?
Answer: The court rejected the argument, holding that the distinctions identified by appellant ("multiple form factors" and "complete in-plane interconnectivity") are not recited in claim 1. These unrecited properties do not distinguish the claim from the prior art.

q022. List four Federal Circuit cases the panel cites for anticipation, obviousness, and forfeiture standards (with citation).
Answer: Rapoport v. Dement, 254 F.3d 1053, 1057 (Fed. Cir. 2001) (anticipation requires disclosure of every limitation expressly or inherently); Adasa Inc. v. Avery Dennison Corp., 55 F.4th 900, 913 (Fed. Cir. 2022) (need not use same language to anticipate); Am. Calcar, Inc. v. Am. Honda Motor Co., 651 F.3d 1318, 1341 (Fed. Cir. 2011) (enablement for POSA); Arendi S.A.R.L. v. Apple Inc., 832 F.3d 1355, 1361 (Fed. Cir. 2016) (single reference may render obvious); MEHL/Biophile Int'l Corp. v. Milgraum, 192 F.3d 1362, 1365 (Fed. Cir. 1999); In re Google Tech. Holdings LLC, 980 F.3d 858, 863 (Fed. Cir. 2020) (forfeiture for arguments not raised before Board).

q023. In what proceeding below was the case decided before reaching the Federal Circuit, and what is identified as the underlying Board number?
Answer: Appeal from the United States Patent and Trademark Office, Patent Trial and Appeal Board in No. 18/199,940. The examiner's final rejection was affirmed by the Board, and appellant timely appealed to the Federal Circuit.

q024. What did appellant describe some of the resulting films of his disclosed process as having, and how did the court relate that description to the inherent-anticipation theory?
Answer: Appellant described some of the resulting films as having "clogged or collapsed pores" rather than open pores, suggesting that some version of the process, as identically disclosed in Song '656, does not necessarily result in the claimed open and interconnected pore structure. The court therefore noted that anticipation did not need to rely on an inherency theory because Song '656 explicitly discloses the open and interconnected pore structure ("a fibrous matrix interconnected with a plurality of open pores").

q025. In one word, what is the disposition of the appeal, and which Board decisions did the court specifically affirm?
Answer: AFFIRMED. The court affirmed the Board's decision with respect to Song '656 and with respect to Song '525, and the Board's decision overall.
