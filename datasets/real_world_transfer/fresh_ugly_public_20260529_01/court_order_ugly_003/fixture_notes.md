# Fixture notes — court_order_ugly_003

## Why this document is messy
It is a nonprecedential per curiam Federal Circuit opinion affirming the Patent Trial and Appeal Board's affirmance of an examiner's rejection of a pro se inventor's patent application. The opinion layers many of the structures that make patent-appeal opinions difficult to read:
- A pro se appellant whose own prior publications are cited as the prior art against him, and a four-attorney USPTO Solicitor's Office team representing the named appellee.
- A claim 1 with three lettered sub-elements, each with multiple internal limitations including quantitative parameters with units ("180 degrees C", "1 sec/100 cc", "90 %", "MacMullin number ... of 1 to 15") and abbreviations introduced parenthetically (Tm, Tg, NM).
- Two prior-art references whose short-form names ("Song '656" and "Song '525") are easy to confuse and whose statutory grounds vary by claim range.
- Two footnotes — one a citation-form footnote, the other a doctrinal explanation of § 102(b)(2) — that interact with the main text.
- A companion case (In re Song, No. 2025-1653) decided the same day on a related application.
- Multiple Federal Circuit case citations distributed across legal-standard, anticipation, and forfeiture discussions.
- A "preamble as limiting" ruling resting on the specification's structural meaning of "separator"; an inherent-anticipation theory the court declines to reach; and a forfeiture finding for an argument not raised before the Board.

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: appeal number 2025-1705; application number 18/199,940; companion case number 2025-1653; two prior-art publication numbers (2018/0043656 and 2016/0243525); jurisdictional statute (28 U.S.C. § 1295(a)(4)(A)).
- Date/sequence: Song '525 published August 25, 2016 → Song '656 published February 15, 2018 → application filed May 20, 2023 → Federal Circuit decision February 18, 2026.
- Table/list/citation: the contested claim range (1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, and 25-39); the three sub-elements of claim 1 with their numerical parameters; the three statutory grounds (§ 102(a)(1) anticipation by Song '656, § 102(a)(1) anticipation by Song '525, § 103 obviousness over Song '525) and the corresponding claim ranges; six Federal Circuit case citations.
- Source-state/exception: the doctrinal footnote 2 explanation of why § 102(b)(2) does not disqualify the references; the forfeiture finding under In re Google Tech. Holdings; the court's choice to not reach the inherent-anticipation theory.
- Cross-section: the Song '656 vs. Song '525 cross; the unrecited-properties-do-not-distinguish ruling; the Figure 2A/2B comparison between the application and Song '656 (and Figure 2B vs. Song '525 Figure 2).

## Known ambiguities in the source
- "Song '656" and "Song '525" are similar enough to easily transpose, and the statutory grounds and claim ranges differ between them in non-obvious ways.
- The opinion's main text says "We have considered appellant's other arguments and find them unpersuasive" without specifying which arguments, so readers may conflate the rejected arguments listed in Parts I and II with the unspecified "other arguments" in Part III.
- The companion case (In re Song, No. 2025-1653) is mentioned only briefly; readers may misread the relationship between the two appeals.
