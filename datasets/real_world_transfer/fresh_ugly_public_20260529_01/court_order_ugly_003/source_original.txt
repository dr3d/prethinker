PER CURIAM.

Appeal from PTAB; application No. 18/199,940 filed May 20, 2023, prosecuted pro se.

Prior art:
- U.S. Patent Publication No. 2018/0043656 to Song et al. ("Song '656"), published February 15, 2018
- U.S. Patent Publication No. 2016/0243525 to Song et al. ("Song '525"), published August 25, 2016

Companion case: In re Song, No. 2025-1653 (also decided today).
Jurisdiction: 28 U.S.C. § 1295(a)(4)(A).

Contested claims: 1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, and 25-39.

Claim 1 (porous separator film) recites:
(a) matrix polymer with Tm or Tg >= 180 C;
(b) open and interconnecting pore structure (Gurley air permeability >= 1 sec/100 cc; porosity <= 90 %);
(c) ionic conductivity characterized by MacMullin number (NM) 1-15.

Statutory grounds:
- 35 U.S.C. § 102(a)(1) anticipation by Song '656 (all contested claims)
- 35 U.S.C. § 102(a)(1) anticipation by Song '525 (claims 1, 2, 4, 6-9, 12-21, 23, 25-27, 38, 39)
- 35 U.S.C. § 103 obviousness over Song '525 (claims 28-37)

Footnote 1: "J.A." = corrected joint appendix; Dkt. No. 24.
Footnote 2: Appellant invoked § 102(b)(2). Court: § 102(b)(2) can only disqualify prior art cited under § 102(a)(2); here references cited under § 102(a)(1); exception inapplicable.

Cases cited:
- Rapoport v. Dement, 254 F.3d 1053, 1057 (Fed. Cir. 2001)
- Adasa Inc. v. Avery Dennison Corp., 55 F.4th 900, 913 (Fed. Cir. 2022)
- Am. Calcar, Inc. v. Am. Honda Motor Co., 651 F.3d 1318, 1341 (Fed. Cir. 2011)
- Arendi S.A.R.L. v. Apple Inc., 832 F.3d 1355, 1361 (Fed. Cir. 2016)
- MEHL/Biophile Int'l Corp. v. Milgraum, 192 F.3d 1362, 1365 (Fed. Cir. 1999)
- In re Google Tech. Holdings LLC, 980 F.3d 858, 863 (Fed. Cir. 2020)

Holdings:
- Preamble "separator film" not limiting; claim 1 recites structurally complete device.
- Song '656 explicitly discloses open and interconnected pore structure; inherent anticipation not necessary.
- Song '525 directly discloses open and interconnecting pore structure (Figure 2 identical to Figure 2B in appealed application).
- Appellant forfeited "unanalogous" argument by not raising before Board.

Disposition: AFFIRMED.
