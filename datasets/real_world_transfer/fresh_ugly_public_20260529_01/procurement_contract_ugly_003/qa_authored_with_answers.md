q018. How did the RFP define a "relevant" past performance contract (recency, type of work, and minimum value)?

This protest (B-423552.3) was denied (with one ground dismissed as untimely). [BACKGROUND]+[CONCLUSION]

q022. On what general basis did GAO decline to address every collateral argument raised by the protester?
Answer: GAO noted: "We note that the protester raises several collateral arguments. While our decision does not specifically address every argument, we have reviewed all the arguments and conclude that none provides a basis to sustain the protest." [DISCUSSION - PAST PERFORMANCE - GENERAL]

q023. Why did GAO dismiss as untimely ESI's challenge to the agency's having credited BryMak with CBFS's past performance?
Answer: Because GAO had reviewed that allegation in the prior protest and found it did not provide a basis to sustain; the agency's reevaluation did not change or address that aspect; the arguments raised were substantially similar to those raised earlier and did not rely on any new information gleaned as part of the reevaluation; and ESI did not file a request for reconsideration within the 10-day deadline under 4 C.F.R. § 21.14(b). Accordingly the repetitive allegation amounted to an untimely request for reconsideration that GAO declined to consider. [DISCUSSION - DISMISSAL OF CBFS SUBCONTRACTOR GROUND AS UNTIMELY]

q024. On what two respects did GAO find that the facts of this case were not like those in eAlliant, LLC?
Answer: First, in eAlliant the agency removed strengths without explanation, whereas here the agency's underlying evaluation of the aspect of ESI's proposal at issue did not change (both the pre- and post-corrective action SSEB reports specifically stated that one ESI contract "had [three] marginal ratings on a 6-month interim CPAR evaluation"). Second, unlike in eAlliant, GAO had sustained the original protest in part on the basis that the agency had failed to look behind the adjectival ratings and qualitatively compare the relative merits of the offerors' past performance — so the SSA's actions here were viewed not as changing evaluation conclusions but as performing the required qualitative comparison for the first time. [DISCUSSION - BEST-VALUE TRADEOFF - SSA'S CONSIDERATION OF PAST PERFORMANCE]

q025. In ESI's reading of GAO's prior recommendation, what key phrase did ESI's excerpt of that recommendation omit, and why did GAO find this omission significant?
Answer: ESI's excerpt of the recommendation that the agency "reevaluate proposals in a manner consistent with the terms of the solicitation" omitted the end of the sentence: "and this decision." GAO found the omission significant because that language limited the recommended reevaluation to the errors identified in the prior decision rather than recommending a completely new evaluation, and therefore ESI could have requested reconsideration of the protest grounds GAO denied or dismissed.
