# Fixture notes — labor_board_ugly_003

## Why this document is messy
It is an NLRB Weekly Summary of Decisions covering one full week of Board activity. The document layers in a single compact text many of the structures that make labor-board documents difficult to parse:
- Two Summarized Board Decisions with distinct case numbers (one C case, one R case) and distinct case captions, including a multi-respondent "Alter Egos" caption with two d/b/a designations (La Quinta Inn & Suites Fairfield; Ramada by Wyndham).
- Nine Unpublished Board Decisions in Representation Cases (R Cases) covering multiple Starbucks subsidiaries, an Allied New York Services case with a Mayer concurrence that cites the National Mediation Board's Swissport Cargo Services decision, a Grindr supervisory-status determination, an Armour-Globe election ruling for Clean Harbors, and others.
- Three Unpublished C Cases including two same-day Sutphen Corporation entries with identical case numbers on consecutive days.
- A 9th Circuit appellate enforcement of the underlying Cemex decision (372 NLRB No. 130), with Supreme Court citations (Collins v. Yellen and Gissel Packing Co.) and a dissenting-judge note.
- Eight Administrative Law Judge decisions with mixed JD and JD(SF) numbering formats and multiple ALJs (Eleanor Laws, Sharon Levinson Steckler appearing twice, Arthur J. Amchan appearing twice, Brian D. Gee, Geoffrey Carter, Mara-Louise Anzalone).

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: NLRB case-number formats (-CA-, -RC-, -RD-, -RM-, -UC-, -CA- combined with et al.); NLRB volume citations (374 NLRB No. 99, 374 NLRB No. 100, 372 NLRB No. 130); ALJ decision numbers (JD-21-26, JD-22-26, JD-23-26, JD-24-26, JD(SF)-05-26 through JD(SF)-08-26); the 9th Circuit unpublished-opinion date.
- Date/sequence: each item carries its own date within April 20-24, 2026, plus the February 9, 2026 ALJ Giannopoulos decision date and the underlying Cemex elements (2017 organizing campaign, 2018 majority status, 2019 election with 179 to 166 vote margin).
- Table/list/citation: a compact list of fifteen-plus cases each with case number, location, and date; statutory cites to Section 2(3), Section 2(11), Section 8(a)(3), 8(a)(5), and (1); two Supreme Court citations; the National Mediation Board Swissport Cargo Services LP, 52 NMB 25 (2024) citation.
- Source-state/exception: the "informational purposes only" disclaimer; the denial-without-prejudice rationale for SAAS Hotels; the Mayer concurrence in BeLeaf Medical; the dissenting-judge note in the 9th Circuit's Cemex opinion; the Member Mayer separate statement in Allied New York Services.
- Cross-section: the same ALJ (Sharon Levinson Steckler) issuing two ALJ decisions in the same week (ASARCO LLC and RJ Staab Stone Company); the same ALJ (Arthur J. Amchan) issuing two ALJ decisions in the same week (Wrightsville Firewater and Johns Hopkins Medical Associates); the two Sutphen Corporation entries on consecutive days with identical case numbers; the cross between the 9th Circuit's appellate disposition and the underlying 372 NLRB No. 130 Board decision.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- "374 NLRB No. 99" and "374 NLRB No. 100" are consecutive Board citations issued one day apart for fundamentally different proceedings (a default-judgment ULP case in NJ versus a request-for-review denial in MO).
- Member Mayer's concurrence in BeLeaf Medical contains a grammatical artifact in the quoted phrase ("emerging or rapidly industries"). The exact phrase is reproduced verbatim from the official summary.
- The two Sutphen Corporation entries appear with the same case numbers but on different dates (April 20 and April 21) and concern related but distinct procedural rulings (subpoena-revocation grant versus denial); these are easy to conflate.
- The Cemex appellate narrative in the Summary refers to the Board's "new framework" as having been adopted in 372 NLRB No. 130 — readers may not realize that this is a backward-looking reference to an earlier Board decision (the Cemex precedent), not a new framework adopted during the April 20-24, 2026 week.
