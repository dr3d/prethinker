# QA with reference answers — labor_board_ugly_003

Source: NLRB, Summary of NLRB Decisions for Week of April 20 - 24, 2026 (published May 20, 2026).

q001. What is the title and date range of this weekly summary document, and which agency published it?
Answer: Summary of NLRB Decisions for Week of April 20 - 24, 2026, published by the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB).

q002. To which Office should inquiries about the Summary be directed, and at what phone number?
Answer: The Office of the Executive Secretary at 202-273-1940.

q003. Which three Board members participated in both of the summarized Board Decisions?
Answer: Chairman Murphy and Members Prouty and Mayer.

q004. What are the full names of the two respondents in the SAAS Hotels / Rollo Hospitality decision (including the d/b/a designations), and how does the case caption characterize their relationship?
Answer: SAAS Hotels NJ LLC d/b/a La Quinta Inn & Suites Fairfield, and Rollo Hospitality LLC d/b/a Ramada by Wyndham. The case caption characterizes them as "Alter Egos."

q005. What are the two case numbers consolidated in the SAAS Hotels/Rollo Hospitality matter, and what is the NLRB volume citation?
Answer: 22-CA-315658 and 22-CA-323960; 374 NLRB No. 99.

q006. Where is the SAAS Hotels/Rollo Hospitality decision located (city, state), and on what date was it decided?
Answer: Fairfield, NJ; April 22, 2026.

q007. Who filed the charges in the SAAS Hotels/Rollo Hospitality matter?
Answer: Hotel and Gaming Trades Council, AFL-CIO.

q008. What is the case number and NLRB volume citation for the BeLeaf Medical, LLC decision?
Answer: 14-RC-325871; 374 NLRB No. 100.

q009. Where is the BeLeaf Medical decision located, and on what date was it decided?
Answer: St. Louis, MO; April 23, 2026.

q010. Who is the Petitioner in the BeLeaf Medical case?
Answer: United Food & Commercial Workers Local 655.

q011. In the Starbucks Corporation C case from Portland, OR, who was the Administrative Law Judge, on what date did the ALJ issue the decision, and what were the case numbers?
Answer: Administrative Law Judge John T. Giannopoulos issued the decision on February 9, 2026; case numbers 19-CA-325519 and 19-CA-326902.

q012. In the Centers for Family Medicine / Healthcare Partners Medical Group ALJ matter, who was the ALJ, what is the case number, and on what date did she issue her decision?
Answer: Administrative Law Judge Eleanor Laws; case numbers 21-CA-333729 and 21-CA-370914 (JD(SF)-06-26); decision issued April 20, 2026.

q013. In which two Administrative Law Judge decisions during this week is Arthur J. Amchan listed as the ALJ, and what are their case numbers, locations, and decision dates?
Answer: Wrightsville Firewater LLC d/b/a John Wright Restaurant (05-CA-331875; JD-21-26), Wrightsville, PA, April 21, 2026; and Johns Hopkins Medical Associates (05-CA-319331 and 05-CA-322175; JD-24-26), Lutherville, MD, April 24, 2026.

q014. In which two Administrative Law Judge decisions during this week is Sharon Levinson Steckler listed as the ALJ, and what are their case numbers, locations, and decision dates?
Answer: ASARCO LLC (28-CA-255235, et al.; JD(SF)-05-26), Hayden, Kearny, Sahuarita, Marana, AZ and Amarillo, TX, April 20, 2026; and RJ Staab Stone Company of Florida, LLC (12-CA-334616; JD-22-26), Newberry, FL, April 22, 2026.

q015. In the SAAS Hotels/Rollo Hospitality decision, which two pairs of NLRA Section subsections did the Board find were violated, and what did each cover?
Answer: Section 8(a)(3) and (1) — discriminating in regard to the hire or tenure or terms and conditions of employment, thereby discouraging membership in a labor organization; and Section 8(a)(5) and (1) — failing and refusing to bargain collectively with the exclusive collective-bargaining representative of its employees.

q016. In BeLeaf Medical, what NLRA section governs the exclusion of "agricultural laborers" and what statute's definition does the Board use for that classification?
Answer: Section 2(3) of the National Labor Relations Act governs the exclusion of agricultural laborers, and the Board uses the Fair Labor Standards Act definition in determining that exclusion.

q017. Which appellate court enforced the Board's order in CEMEX Construction Materials Pacific LLC, on what date, and was the opinion published or unpublished?
Answer: The Ninth Circuit (U.S. Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit) enforced the Board's order in an unpublished opinion that issued on Tuesday, April 21, 2026.

q018. What U.S. Supreme Court case did the Ninth Circuit cite in dispensing with Cemex's argument that NLRB Administrative Law Judges enjoyed unconstitutional removal protections, and what year was that case decided?
Answer: Collins v. Yellen, 594 U.S. 220 (2021).

q019. What Supreme Court case did the Board cite as the established precedent for a bargaining order applicable when a fair rerun election has been rendered unlikely?
Answer: NLRB v. Gissel Packing Co., 395 U.S. 575 (1969).

q020. What is the underlying Board citation (NLRB No.) for the CEMEX Construction Materials Pacific LLC case discussed by the Ninth Circuit, and what is the Board case number?
Answer: Board Case No. 28-CA-230115, reported at 372 NLRB No. 130.

q021. In Allied New York Services, Inc., Member Mayer agreed to deny review but said the Board should consider referring a similar future case to which body, and to confirm that its position from which decision remains unchanged (including citation)?
Answer: The National Mediation Board, to confirm that its position from Swissport Cargo Services, LP, 52 NMB 25 (2024) remains unchanged.

q022. What disclaimer about the Summary's purpose appears at the start of the document?
Answer: "The Summary of NLRB Decisions is provided for informational purposes only and is not intended to substitute for the opinions of the NLRB."

q023. Why did the Board deny the General Counsel's Motion for Default Judgment without prejudice as to SAAS Hotels NJ LLC?
Answer: Because the General Counsel failed to prove effective service to Respondent SAAS Hotels NJ LLC, d/b/a La Quinta Inn & Suites Fairfield.

q024. What did Member Mayer state in his concurrence in BeLeaf Medical, including the exact phrase he used to describe certain industries?
Answer: Member Mayer concurred and stated that the Board should continue its case-by-case approach in distinguishing between excluded agricultural laborers and included statutory employees, especially in "emerging or rapidly industries."

q025. In the Sutphen Corporation entries, two separate Board decisions (one on April 20 and one on April 21, 2026) appear with the same two case numbers. What are those case numbers, where is Sutphen located, and what is each decision about?
Answer: Case numbers 09-CA-366492 and 09-CA-367418; Dublin, OH. The April 20, 2026 decision denied the Respondent's request for Special Permission to Appeal the informal rulings purportedly made by the judge during a pre-hearing conference call which granted in part the General Counsel's and the Union's petitions to revoke the Respondent's subpoena duces tecum. The April 21, 2026 decision denied the Respondent's request for Special Permission to Appeal the informal rulings purportedly made by the Administrative Law Judge during a pre-hearing conference call which denied in part the Respondent's petition to revoke the General Counsel's subpoena duces tecum; the April 21 decision also denied the Respondent's request to stay the hearing and its motion to stay its subpoena and privilege log. Charges in both were filed by the International Brotherhood of Teamsters (IBT), Local Union No. 284.
