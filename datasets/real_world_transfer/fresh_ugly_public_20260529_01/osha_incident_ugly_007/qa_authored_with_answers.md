# QA with reference answers — osha_incident_ugly_007

Source: OSHRC ALJ Decision and Order, Acting Secretary of Labor v. DISH Network, L.L.C., OSHRC Docket No. 23-0180 (March 18, 2025).

q001. (metadata) What is the OSHRC docket number for this case, and which administrative law judge issued the Decision and Order?
Answer: OSHRC Docket No. 23-0180; John B. Gatto, United States Administrative Law Judge.

q002. (party) Name the two attorneys for the Complainant and the two attorneys for the Respondent, with the offices/firms each appeared on behalf of.
Answer: For Complainant: Megan J. Harris and Amy Tai, Attorneys, Office of the Solicitor, U.S. Department of Labor, New York, NY. For Respondent: Jeremy K. Fisher and Amy Conley, Jackson Lewis, P.C., Atlanta, GA.

q003. (party) Who was the DISH field technician at the worksite, and who was his supervisor (give title)?
Answer: Travis Heim, a DISH field technician; Brandon Barrett, his supervisor and DISH's field service manager.

q004. (party) Identify the two OSHA Compliance Safety and Health Officers who first observed the worksite from across the street.
Answer: Ashley Irwin and Lydia Calderon.

q005. (metadata) How does the decision explain why the Acting Secretary of Labor is named differently from the Acting Secretary who originally filed the case, and on what date did the substitution occur?
Answer: Footnote 1 explains that on January 20, 2025, Vincent N. Micone III became the Acting Secretary of Labor and was automatically substituted sub nom. for former Acting Secretary of Labor Julie A. Su under Fed. R. Civ. P. 25(d).

q006. (metadata) How many employees did DISH have at its Syracuse office at the time of the OSHA inspection, and approximately how many field technicians did DISH employ nationwide?
Answer: Twenty-three employees at the Syracuse office; about 3,200 field technicians nationwide.

q007. (id) Under what statutory and procedural authority did the Court issue the Decision and Order?
Answer: Section 12(j) of the Act and Commission Rule 90(a)(1) (29 U.S.C. § 661(j); 29 C.F.R. § 2200.90(a)(1)).

q008. (date) On what date did the OSHA inspection at the Sonic worksite take place?
Answer: September 7, 2022.

q009. (date) On what date was the citation issued to DISH, and on what date was the Decision and Order signed?
Answer: Citation issued December 15, 2022; Decision and Order dated March 18, 2025.

q010. (sequence) Place these events in chronological order: (a) OSHA opens an inspection; (b) Irwin enters the worksite; (c) Calderon notices someone on the roof; (d) the abatement photographs are taken.
Answer: (c) Calderon notices someone on the roof (from across the street, before lunch arrived); (a) OSHA opens an inspection (after Irwin drove from the restaurant to the area office); (b) Irwin enters the worksite; (d) the abatement photographs (Ex. P-1, pp. 17-18) are taken after Heim returned to the roof tied-off.

q011. (date) What was the time stamp on Irwin's first photograph, and how many minutes elapsed between that first photograph at the restaurant and her entry to the worksite?
Answer: 12:16 p.m.; 14 minutes elapsed between the first photograph at the restaurant and her entering the worksite.

q012. (sequence) How long was the trial, and where was it held?
Answer: A 2-day trial in Syracuse, New York.

q013. (exact_wording) Per Footnote 8, by what means did the Court state the timing of the first photograph was established (i.e., what was the source of the timing claim)?
Answer: Irwin's field notes (the first photograph was time-stamped at 12:16 p.m.; according to Irwin's field notes only 14 minutes elapsed between that photograph and her entering the worksite).

q014. (citation) Identify the four elements of the Secretary's prima facie case as laid out in the Court's legal standard, with the case cited as the source.
Answer: (a) the applicability of the cited standard; (b) the employer's noncompliance with the standard's terms; (c) employee access to the violative conditions; (d) the employer's actual or constructive knowledge of the violation. Cited from Jake's Fireworks Inc. v. Sec'y of Labor, 893 F.3d 1248, 1256 (10th Cir. 2018) (citing Atlantic Battery Co., 16 BNA OSHC 2131, 2138 (No. 90-1747, 1994)).

q015. (table) Which exhibit and page ranges did the Court identify for: (i) photographs taken from the restaurant; (ii) photographs taken from outside the building during Irwin's walk-up; and (iii) abatement photographs after Heim re-tied?
Answer: (i) Ex. P-1, pp. 1-12 (from inside the restaurant); (ii) Ex. P-1, pp. 13-16 (during Irwin's walk from her car to the Sonic building); (iii) Ex. P-1, pp. 17-18 (abatement photographs after Heim returned to the roof tied-off).

q016. (citation) State the fall-protection standard cited (with full regulatory citation) and the height threshold it specifies.
Answer: 29 C.F.R. § 1926.501(b)(1); it mandates protection for each employee on a walking/working surface with an unprotected side or edge which is 6 feet (1.8 m) or more above a lower level.

q017. (citation) Identify two cases the Court cites for the proposition that actual or constructive knowledge may be imputed to the employer through its supervisory employee.
Answer: Austin Bldg. Co. v. Sec'y of Labor, 647 F.2d 1063, 1067-68 (10th Cir. 1981); and New York State Elec. & Gas Corp. v. Sec'y of Labor, 88 F.3d 98, 105 (2d Cir. 1996).

q018. (list) List the specific physical characteristics of the fall restraint rope and the D-ring as described in the Worksite background section.
Answer: Rope: one-inch diameter, grey, 25 feet long. D-ring: in the center back of the harness, about four to six inches in diameter.

q019. (citation) What proposed penalty was associated with the cited willful violation, and under what specific statutory framework was that citation issued?
Answer: $124,305.00 proposed penalty; willful citation issued under the Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970, 29 U.S.C. §§ 651-678.

q020. (absence) What did Irwin admit she never did concerning Heim and the anchor point on the roof?
Answer: She admitted she never attempted to interview Heim, never asked him where the anchor point was located on the roof, never asked whether he had worked on the roof without fall protection, never asked Barrett if he knew whether Heim was tied off, and never climbed the ladder herself to personally observe whether Heim was tied off.

q021. (source_state) How does the Court characterize Barrett's comment that employees had been fired for less, and what is the rationale for that characterization?
Answer: The Court finds Barrett's comments about the company's policy of firing employees for fall protection violations were not an admission that he knew Heim had been working without fall protection. The evidence shows Barrett had no reason to believe, prior to meeting Irwin, that Heim was working without fall protection.

q022. (source_state) What does the decision say about the visibility of Heim's D-ring in the photographs Irwin took from inside the restaurant?
Answer: Despite Irwin's testimony that she could see the D-ring on Heim's back from her vantage point inside the restaurant, the D-ring is not visible in the photographs she took from that same vantage point. The photographs show a front or side view of Heim, not the middle of his back where the D-ring is located.

q023. (source_state) On the constructive-knowledge element, what does the Court conclude about DISH's safety program and what reasonable diligence required?
Answer: The record reflects DISH's safety program, training, and supervision were robust; DISH had no reason to believe Heim required continuous monitoring for use of fall protection; reasonable diligence did not require DISH to continuously verify Heim was following its safety policy. The Court concludes the Secretary did not prove a lack of reasonable diligence and thus did not prove constructive knowledge. And even if it was, the Court concludes the conduct was not foreseeable.

q024. (cross_section) Cross-reference the worksite description and the analysis: how does the Court use the placement of the D-ring on the harness to discredit Irwin's testimony?
Answer: The worksite description establishes that Heim's D-ring was in the center back of the harness (four to six inches in diameter). The analysis observes that the photographs from inside the restaurant show only a front or side view of Heim, not the middle of his back, and that the D-ring is not visible in those photographs. Because Irwin testified she could see the D-ring on Heim's back from inside the restaurant but the D-ring is not visible in the photographs she took from that vantage point, the Court does not credit her testimony.

q025. (cross_section) Combining the Introduction and the Order: identify both grounds on which the Court concludes the Secretary failed to establish a prima facie case, and the resulting disposition of the citation.
Answer: The Court concludes the Secretary failed to prove (1) that the cited standard was violated and (2) that DISH had knowledge of the violative condition. Accordingly, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED THAT the citation is VACATED.
