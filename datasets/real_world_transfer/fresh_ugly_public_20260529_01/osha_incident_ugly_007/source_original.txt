I. INTRODUCTION
Respondent DISH Network, L.L.C. was installing a DISH antenna at a Sonic restaurant in Syracuse, New York, when investigated by OSHA. OSHA issued a willful citation for an alleged violation of the Act with a proposed penalty of $124,305.00. Citation issued December 15, 2022.
Allegation: willful violation of 29 C.F.R. § 1926.501(b)(1) (fall protection at 14-foot fall height).
2-day trial held in Syracuse, New York.
The Court concludes the Secretary failed to establish all elements of his prima facie case and VACATES the citation.

II. BACKGROUND
Date of inspection: September 7, 2022.
Worksite: Sonic restaurant, Syracuse, NY. Roof was 14 feet above the ground.
DISH employees: Travis Heim (field technician), Brandon Barrett (field service manager/supervisor).
OSHA officers: Ashley Irwin, Lydia Calderon (Compliance Safety and Health Officers).
Initial observation: from restaurant 175-200 feet across the street. First photograph time-stamped 12:16 p.m. About 14 minutes elapsed from first restaurant photo to entering the worksite.
Photographs:
  Ex. P-1 pp. 1-12: from inside restaurant (overcast sky)
  Ex. P-1 pp. 13-16: from outside Sonic, drive-up
  Ex. P-1 pp. 17-18: abatement photos after Heim re-tied
Heim's gear: 1-inch grey 25-foot fall restraint rope; D-ring center-back of harness (4-6 inches diameter); anchored to HVAC unit within 25 feet of antenna mounting location.
Heim took the rope off 20-30 times during the day.

III. ANALYSIS
Legal standard (Jake's Fireworks Inc. v. Sec'y of Labor, 893 F.3d 1248, 1256 (10th Cir. 2018)):
  (a) applicability of the cited standard
  (b) employer's noncompliance with standard's terms
  (c) employee access to the violative conditions
  (d) employer's actual or constructive knowledge

Applicability + exposure: established by the Secretary.
Standard violated: NOT established.
  - Photographs do not show the D-ring
  - Irwin's testimony not credited
  - Barrett and Heim credible witnesses
Knowledge: NOT established (actual or constructive).
  - Barrett observed Heim was tied off at start of day
  - Then Barrett worked inside; did not observe Heim later
  - Barrett's "fired employees for less" comment NOT an admission
  - Reasonable diligence did not require continuous monitoring
Conduct not foreseeable.

IV. ORDER
For all the foregoing reasons, the citation is VACATED. SO ORDERED.
