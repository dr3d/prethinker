BEFORE THE PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION OF UTAH

Application of Rocky Mountain Power for Authority to Increase its Retail Electric Utility Service Rates in Utah and for Approval of its Proposed Electric Service Schedules and Electric Service Regulations.

DOCKET NO. 24-035-04
ORDER MEMORIALIZING DECISION
ISSUED: June 24, 2025

On December 10, 2024, Rocky Mountain Power (RMP) and the Utah Large Customer Group (UTLCG, collectively with RMP, the "Settling Parties") filed a Partial Settlement Stipulation, wherein UTLCG agreed to withdraw certain recommendations, RMP agreed to provide individual and power quality data on a quarterly basis to certain Schedule 9 customers for a period of one year, and the Settling Parties agreed to meet after one year to discuss resolution of any identified chronic, high-impact, or high-risk issues.

The PSC heard testimony on December 16, 2024. RMP and UTLCG provided supporting testimony. No party testified in opposition.

The PSC found the Settlement "just and reasonable in result" and "in the public interest." (Footnote 1: Dec. 16, 2024 Hr'g Tr. at 1364:22-1365:7.) The PSC indicated it would memorialize this decision in its final order. However, in the lengthy order issued April 25, 2025, the PSC neglected to include language memorializing approval. The PSC apologizes for the oversight.

On May 22, 2025, RMP and UTLCG filed a Motion for a Written Order Memorializing the Approval of the Settlement. No party objected.

Utah Code § 54-7-1 statutorily encourages stipulated agreements. The PSC may adopt "any settlement proposal" under § 54-7-1(2)-(3) if evidence supports a just-and-reasonable finding.

Order: Consistent with the PSC's decision at hearing, this order memorializes the PSC's approval of the Settlement.

DATED at Salt Lake City, Utah, June 24, 2025.
