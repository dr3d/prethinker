q012. Within how many days after issuance of this written order must a party seek agency review or rehearing?
Answer: Within 30 days after issuance of the order. [NOTICE OF OPPORTUNITY FOR AGENCY REVIEW OR REHEARING]

q013. Within how many days of the filing of a request for review or rehearing must responses be filed?
Answer: Within 15 days of the filing of the request for review or rehearing. [NOTICE...]

q014. If the PSC fails to grant a request for review or rehearing within a certain period, what is the result?
Answer: If the PSC fails to grant a request for review or rehearing within 30 days after the filing of a request for review or rehearing, it is deemed denied. [NOTICE...]

q015. Within how many days after final agency action may judicial review be sought, and with which court?
Answer: Within 30 days after final agency action, by filing a Petition for Review with the Utah Supreme Court. [NOTICE...]

q016. Place these events in chronological order: the Phase I hearing testimony; the Partial Settlement Stipulation; the Motion for a Written Order Memorializing the Approval; the prior lengthy order; this Order Memorializing Decision.
Answer: (1) Partial Settlement Stipulation filed December 10, 2024; (2) Phase I hearing testimony December 16, 2024; (3) prior lengthy order April 25, 2025; (4) Motion for a Written Order Memorializing the Approval filed May 22, 2025; (5) Order Memorializing Decision issued June 24, 2025. [BACKGROUND]+[ORDER]

q017. Which Utah statute is cited as encouraging stipulated agreements, and which subsections govern the PSC's adoption of a settlement proposal?
Answer: Utah Code § 54-7-1; the adoption authority is set out in subsections § 54-7-1(2)-(3). [DISCUSSION]

q018. Which Utah statutory citations appear in the Notice of Opportunity for Agency Review or Rehearing?
Answer: Utah Code Ann. §§ 63G-4-301 and 54-7-15 (for the right to seek agency review or rehearing), and §§ 63G-4-401 and 63G-4-403 (for the requirements of a Petition for Review), together with the Utah Rules of Appellate Procedure. [NOTICE...]

q019. What is the source citation in Footnote 1 of the order?
Answer: Dec. 16, 2024, Hr'g Tr. at 1364:22-1365:7. [BACKGROUND] footnote

q020. Under which tariff schedule were the customers to receive the quarterly individual and power quality data under the Settlement, and for how long?
Answer: Schedule 9 of Rocky Mountain Power's tariff; for a period of one year. [BACKGROUND]

q021. What are the three terms of the Partial Settlement Stipulation as recited in the order?
Answer: (1) UTLCG agreed to withdraw certain recommendations it raised in this proceeding; (2) RMP agreed to provide individual and power quality data on a quarterly basis to certain customers receiving service under Schedule 9 of its tariff for a period of one year; and (3) the Settling Parties agreed to meet after one year to discuss resolution of any identified chronic, high-impact, or high-risk issues. [BACKGROUND]

q022. What admission does the PSC make about its prior lengthy order, and what exact words does it use?
Answer: The PSC states that "in the lengthy order the PSC issued on April 25, 2025, addressing the myriad issues raised in this docket, the PSC neglected to include language memorializing its approval of the Settlement," and writes: "The PSC apologizes for the oversight." [BACKGROUND]

q023. What was the statutory standard the PSC found the Settlement satisfied, in the exact words used in the order?
Answer: The PSC found the Settlement to be "just and reasonable in result" and "in the public interest." [BACKGROUND]

q024. What does the order state about opposition to the Settlement at hearing, and about objections or responses to the Motion?
Answer: The order states that "[n]o party testified in opposition to the PSC's approval of the Settlement or otherwise opposed it," and that "[n]o party filed a response or otherwise objected to the Motion." [BACKGROUND]

q025. What is the relationship between the PSC's hearing announcement, the April 25, 2025 lengthy order, and the issuance of this Order Memorializing Decision?
Answer: The PSC announced at the December 16, 2024 Phase I hearing that the evidence supported its finding that the Settlement is just and reasonable in result, and indicated it would memorialize this decision in its final order. However, the April 25, 2025 lengthy order neglected to include language memorializing the PSC's approval of the Settlement (an oversight for which the PSC apologizes). RMP and UTLCG then filed an unopposed May 22, 2025 Motion for a Written Order Memorializing the Approval, and the PSC issued this Order Memorializing Decision on June 24, 2025 consistent with the PSC's earlier decision at hearing. [BACKGROUND]+[ORDER]
