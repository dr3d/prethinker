# Fixture notes — puc_order_ugly_003

## Why this document is messy
It is a Utah Public Service Commission order that exists only to fix a documentation oversight in the Commission's own prior order: the PSC had announced on the record that it found a settlement stipulation just and reasonable, and "indicated it would memorialize this decision in its final order," but then issued a long April 25, 2025 order that omitted the memorializing language. After the parties filed an unopposed Motion, the PSC issued this Order Memorializing Decision and expressly "apologizes for the oversight." The document layers a four-character docket number, a brief but procedurally precise background, three commissioners' signatures and a Secretary attestation, a document-control identifier, a Notice of Opportunity for Agency Review or Rehearing with multiple statutory deadlines, and a multi-page Certificate of Service distribution list.

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: docket number (24-035-04), document control number (DW#340357), schedule reference (Schedule 9), and a dense net of statutory citations (Utah Code § 54-7-1; § 54-7-1(2)-(3); Utah Code Ann. §§ 63G-4-301, 54-7-15, 63G-4-401, 63G-4-403).
- Date/sequence/deadline: five filing/decision dates in sequence (December 10, 2024 / December 16, 2024 / April 25, 2025 / May 22, 2025 / June 24, 2025) and four review-procedure deadlines (30 days to request agency review or rehearing; 15 days for responses; 30 days for the deemed-denied rule; 30 days for a Petition for Review to the Utah Supreme Court).
- Table/list/coordinate: the three Settlement terms; the multi-party Certificate of Service distribution list with parties grouped by counsel; the three-commissioner signature block.
- Source-state/exception/limitation: the PSC's express admission of an omission and apology; the "no party testified in opposition" finding; the "no party filed a response or otherwise objected" finding.
- Cross-section: the hearing announcement versus the April 25, 2025 lengthy order's omission versus the corrective Order Memorializing Decision.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- The PSC's "indicated it would memorialize this decision in its final order" implies a memorialization that did not occur in the April 25, 2025 order; readers may conflate the two orders.
- The Notice of Opportunity for Agency Review or Rehearing contains three different "30-day" deadlines: 30 days to request review/rehearing; 30 days for the deemed-denied rule; and 30 days for a Petition for Review with the Utah Supreme Court. These deadlines apply at different procedural stages and are easy to conflate.
