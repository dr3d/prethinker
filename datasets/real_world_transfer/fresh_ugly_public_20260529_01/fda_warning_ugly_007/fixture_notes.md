# Fixture notes — fda_warning_ugly_007

## Why this document is messy
It is a "repeat-offender" warning letter to a facility whose ownership has changed. Several distinctive complications layer onto a standard three-violation CGMP letter:
- Dual addressees: the recipient block names only Venkata R. Gogineni, Ph.D., CEO, but the salutation greets "Dr. Gogineni and Mr. Lingam" (Mr. Lingam is unnamed elsewhere in the letter).
- Change-of-ownership history: a prior 2019 warning letter (CMS #572904) was issued to a different named company (Kingston Pharma, LLC) at the same facility with multiple of the same responsible officials.
- Repeat-violation timeline interleaves three subsequent inspections (Sept 2021, Sept 2023, June 2025) with two intervening regulatory meetings (May 3, 2022, May 13, 2024).
- A focused DEG/EG safety concern for products marketed to infants and children, paired with a verbatim quote of Seaway's enforceability defense and FDA's response that USP is enforceable under section 501(b).
- Verbatim FDA redaction markers "(b)(4)" embedded in the Violation 3 process-validation discussion.

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: warning letter number 320-26-22; MARCS-CMS 717355; FEI 3012275336; predecessor company CMS number 572904; internal SOP designation "SOP No. QA-009, Revision 01".
- Date/sequence: 2019 WL → Sept 2021 inspection → May 2022 meeting → Sept 2023 inspection → May 2024 meeting → May-June 2025 inspection → July 2025 Form 483 response → December 2025 WL → December 9 2025 page posted.
- Table/list/citation: three numbered CGMP violations, each with a primary 21 CFR cite; two sub-citations under Violation 1 (211.165(a), 211.194(a)); statutory adulteration cite (501(a)(2)(B)); USP enforceability cite (501(b)); consultant qualification cite (21 CFR 211.34).
- Source-state/exception/limitation: the "all-inclusive" disclaimer; the change-of-ownership-not-exculpatory finding; Seaway's verbatim defense and FDA's verbatim rejoinder.
- Cross-section: the salutation dual-addressee inconsistency with the recipient block; the cross between the predecessor's 2019 letter and this 2025 letter; the cross between the QU release-without-review finding and the strength-testing + records sub-citations.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- "Mr. Lingam" appears only in the salutation; readers must infer he is an additional named recipient even though the recipient block lists only Dr. Gogineni.
- The "Content current as of: 12/09/2025" date refers to when the FDA web page was last updated, not the issuance date of the letter (which is December 1, 2025); these can be conflated.
- The (b)(4) redaction marker appears twice in the process-validation discussion in the same paragraph and could be misread as referring to the same redacted entity.
