Adulteration: drugs are adulterated within the meaning of section 501(a)(2)(B) FD&C Act, 21 U.S.C. 351(a)(2)(B).

CGMP Violations:
1. Failed to establish/follow QU written responsibilities and procedures (21 CFR 211.22(d)).
   Released numerous batches between April 2024 and May 2025 prior to reviewing all test results (SOP No. QA-009 Rev 01 Batch Record Review and Release for Distribution).
   Sub-citations: 21 CFR 211.165(a) strength testing; 21 CFR 211.194(a) lab records.

2. Failed to conduct at least one identity test of each component (21 CFR 211.84(d)(1)).
   High-risk DEG/EG concern: glycerin, sorbitol 70% used in OTC oral liquids for infants and children prior to identity testing.
   Seaway argued no enforceable DEG/EG provisions; FDA responded USP is enforceable under section 501(b) FD&C Act, 21 U.S.C. 351(b).

3. Failed to establish adequate written procedures for production and process control (21 CFR 211.100(a)).
   Inadequate process validation for (b)(4) drug product; missing pre-defined critical steps, component lot, equipment ID.

Responsibilities as a Contractor: FDA regards contractors as extensions of the manufacturer.

Change of Ownership at Facility:
- Previous WL May 14, 2019 (CMS #572904) to Kingston Pharma LLC -- same facility, multiple same responsible officials.
- Subsequent inspections: September 2021, September 2023, June 2025.
- Regulatory meetings: May 3, 2022 and May 13, 2024.
- Conclusion: executive management oversight and control over the manufacture of drugs are inadequate.

Ineffective Quality System / CGMP Consultant Recommended (21 CFR 211.34) / six-system audit.

Conclusion: not all-inclusive list. Respond within 15 working days. Email CDER-OC-OMQ-Communications@fda.hhs.gov, ATTN: Lynnsey Renn.
