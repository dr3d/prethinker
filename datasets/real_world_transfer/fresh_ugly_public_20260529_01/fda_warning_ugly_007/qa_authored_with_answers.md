q014. List the three numbered CGMP violations cited in the letter together with their 21 CFR citations.
Answer: 1. Failed to establish adequate written responsibilities and procedures applicable to the quality control unit and to follow such written procedures (21 CFR 211.22(d)). 2. Failed to conduct at least one test to verify the identity of each component of a drug product (21 CFR 211.84(d)(1)). 3. Failed to establish adequate written procedures for production and process control designed to assure that the drug products have the identity, strength, quality, and purity they purport or are represented to possess (21 CFR 211.100(a)). q016. Under what statutory provision did FDA say the United States Pharmacopeia (USP) is enforceable for purposes of the DEG and EG limits?

q019. Under Violation 1, the letter identifies two further CFR sub-citations for QU failures beyond the underlying 21 CFR 211.22(d) charge. What are those two sub-citations and what does each cover?
Answer: 21 CFR 211.165(a) — failure to ensure that each batch of drug products was tested for the strength of active ingredients prior to release; and 21 CFR 211.194(a) — failure to ensure that laboratory records included complete data derived from all tests necessary to ensure compliance with established specifications and standards. [VIOLATION 1]

q020. Under what regulation does FDA say a CGMP consultant should be qualified?

q021. What disclaimer does the Conclusion section give about the completeness of the cited violations?
Answer: The violations cited in the letter are not intended to be an all-inclusive list of violations that exist at the facility; the firm is responsible for investigating and determining the causes of any violations and for preventing their recurrence or the occurrence of other violations. [CONCLUSION]

q022. What was Seaway Pharma's argument regarding DEG/EG enforceability, and what was FDA's response to it?
Answer: Seaway Pharma stated: "Currently, there are no enforceable provisions in either the United States Code or the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) regarding: 1) Defined acceptance criteria for DEG and/or EG." FDA responded that identity testing is required under 21 CFR 211.84(d)(1) and that the limits for DEG and EG tests are specified in the United States Pharmacopeia (USP), which is enforceable under section 501(b) of the FD&C Act, 21 U.S.C. 351(b). [VIOLATION 2]

q023. Under the "Change of Ownership at Facility" finding, what does the letter conclude about executive management oversight, and what makes the change of ownership not exculpatory?
Answer: The letter concludes that "executive management oversight and control over the manufacture of drugs are inadequate." Although there was a subsequent transfer of ownership of the company, the manufacturing facility and multiple responsible officials remained the same, and both current and past owners had been notified of the violations through the 2019 warning letter, the September 2021 and September 2023 inspections, and the May 3, 2022 and May 13, 2024 regulatory meetings, but the firm continued to fail to implement quality commitments within a timely manner. [CHANGE OF OWNERSHIP AT FACILITY]

q025. Pulling together the repeat-offender narrative: what specific elements does the letter combine to support the finding that the firm has failed to implement quality commitments in a timely manner?
Answer: The letter combines: (1) a prior May 14, 2019 warning letter to predecessor Kingston Pharma, LLC (CMS #572904) citing similar CGMP violations at the same facility with multiple of the same responsible officials; (2) repeated failures shown by subsequent inspections in September 2021, September 2023, and June 2025; (3) persistent violations communicated to the firm at two regulatory meetings on May 3, 2022 and May 13, 2024; and (4) the most recent May 19 - June 18, 2025 inspection that produced three numbered CGMP violations (21 CFR 211.22(d), 211.84(d)(1), 211.100(a)) plus the inadequacy of the firm's July 30, 2025 Form FDA 483 response — together demonstrating that, while current and past owners have been notified, the firm continues to fail to implement quality commitments within a timely manner. cross-section
