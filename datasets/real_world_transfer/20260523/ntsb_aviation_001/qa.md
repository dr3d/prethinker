# ntsb_aviation_001 QA

Questions only. Reference answers are isolated in `oracle.jsonl`.

1. What is the NTSB accident number for this investigation?
2. In what city and state did this accident occur?
3. What is the aircraft registration (tail number)?
4. Who was the Investigator In Charge?
5. What type of flight operation was this aircraft conducting?
6. How many people were injured, and what was the severity?
7. What is the aircraft serial number?
8. What was the aircraft's maximum gross weight?
9. On what date and at what local time did the accident occur?
10. How much total flight time did the pilot have in this specific aircraft type before the accident?
11. What was the date of the pilot's most recent medical certificate examination?
12. How long had it been since the pilot's last flight review?
13. What was the departure time of the accident flight, and what was its destination?
14. What was the pilot's age and commercial rating level?
15. Who was the registered owner and operator of the aircraft?
16. What was the pilot's seating position, and was a second pilot present?
17. Name the FAA participating person listed in the investigation.
18. According to the NTSB, what was the probable cause of the accident?
19. What specific phenomenon does the aircraft's operator's manual describe regarding the collective control system?
20. What is the threshold (in pounds) for absolute friction that prevents collective bounce according to the operator's manual?
21. What two personnel issues were identified as findings in the accident investigation?
22. The operator reported one finding about preaccident conditions. What did the operator claim, and how did the NTSB's probable cause finding differ?
23. The investigation summary includes a note about the NTSB's presence at the scene. What does this note say, and what is the investigation class?
24. What were the meteorological conditions at the time of the accident (cloud, visibility, wind, temperature)?
25. According to the operator's manual quoted in the report, what action should a pilot take to prevent collective bounce in all flight conditions?
