# QA

Q1. What is the OSHRC docket number and who was the presiding judge?
Expected: Docket No. 03-1583; Administrative Law Judge Nancy J. Spies.

Q2. What standard was RK Hydro-Vac cited for violating, and what does it require?
Expected: 29 C.F.R. § 1926.501(b)(1), which requires each employee on a surface with an unprotected side or edge 6 feet or more above a lower level to be protected by guardrail systems, safety net systems, or personal fall arrest systems.

Q3. On what date did the OSHA inspection occur, and who was the compliance officer?
Expected: July 23, 2003; compliance officer Michelle Sotak.

Q4. When was the citation issued and when was the decision dated?
Expected: The citation was issued August 1, 2003; the Decision and Order is dated December 15, 2003.

Q5. What was the height of the roof and the name of the exposed employee?
Expected: Approximately 30 feet; the employee was Fernando Acateca.

Q6. What affirmative defense did RK Hydro-Vac assert, and what was the result?
Expected: The affirmative defense of unpreventable employee misconduct; it was rejected.

Q7. Which element of the Secretary's case was the only one actually in dispute?
Expected: Whether RKH had actual or constructive knowledge of the violation (applicability, noncompliance, and employee exposure were undisputed).

Q8. Why did the actual-knowledge theory fail?
Expected: The Secretary's theory that Mike Petrey was Acateca's supervisor was undercut by testimony that the two-man crews were equal with no supervisory authority over each other, and the Secretary abandoned the actual-knowledge argument in her post-hearing brief.

Q9. What two findings established RKH's constructive knowledge?
Expected: (1) Inadequate supervision of its remote Alabama worksites — no one from RKH or its insurance carrier had been to Alabama in the past year; and (2) the lack of an enforcement/disciplinary policy.

Q10. What were the four elements of the unpreventable-employee-misconduct defense, and which did RKH fail?
Expected: (1) established work rules, (2) adequate communication of the rules, (3) steps to discover violations, and (4) effective enforcement. RKH satisfied (1) and (2) but failed (3) and (4).

Q11. What did the employee say about why he was not tied off?
Expected: He stated that he had been tied off before the lunch break and forgot to put his safety harness back on when he went up to the roof after lunch; a harness was available on site.

Q12. What penalty did the Secretary propose, and what penalty was assessed?
Expected: The Secretary proposed $2,500.00; the judge assessed $750.00.

Q13. Why was the penalty reduced?
Expected: Due to RKH's history (no prior OSHA citations in its 20-year history) and the good faith demonstrated by its extensive written safety program, safety equipment, and intensive training.

Q14. What type of proceeding was this, and what is RK Hydro-Vac's business?
Expected: An E-Z proceeding under Commission Rule 2200.200 et seq.; RK Hydro-Vac is a commercial-roofing subcontractor that vacuums gravel off roofs for reroofing projects.

Q15. What awareness barrier was in place, and what had happened to it where the employee was working?
Expected: A string of flags used as an awareness barrier had been placed 6 feet from the edge of the roof; it had been taken down where the employee was located.
