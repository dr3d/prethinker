# QA

Q1. Who received the warning letter, and what is the reference number and date?
Expected: Medical Chemical Corporation (Mr. Emmanuel R. Didier, President and CEO); Warning Letter 320-25-91 (MARCS-CMS 706007), dated July 9, 2025.

Q2. When was the facility inspected, and where is it located?
Expected: February 12 to 18, 2025; the facility is at 19430 Van Ness Avenue, Torrance, California (FEI 2013736).

Q3. What products does the firm manufacture?
Expected: Over-the-counter (OTC) topical drug products, including hand sanitizers, Tincture Green Soap USP, and Isopropyl Alcohol 70% v/v.

Q4. What was the first CGMP violation cited and under which regulation?
Expected: Failure to conduct at least one test to verify the identity of each component, and failure to validate and establish the reliability of the component supplier's test analyses (21 CFR 211.84(d)(1) and 211.84(d)(2)).

Q5. Which specific contaminants and components did the FDA highlight?
Expected: Methanol contamination in ethyl alcohol (ethanol), and diethylene glycol (DEG) or ethylene glycol (EG) contamination in high-risk components such as glycerin.

Q6. What did the FDA say about the firm's reliance on suppliers?
Expected: The firm relied on suppliers' certificates of analysis (COAs) without establishing the reliability of the suppliers' test analyses, and the FDA stated the firm is responsible for sampling, testing, and examining components before use.

Q7. What was the second CGMP violation, and what was the central example?
Expected: Failure to establish adequate written procedures for production and process control (21 CFR 211.100(a)); the central example was using a "(b)(4) water" system that lacked validation and TOC testing and showed excessive microbial growth at a point of use.

Q8. What was the third CGMP violation?
Expected: The quality control unit failed to exercise its responsibility (21 CFR 211.22), including failing to ensure adequate batch production/control records (211.188(b)) and laboratory records (211.194(a)).

Q9. Why did the FDA consider the firm's February 28, 2025 response inadequate?
Expected: Because the firm failed to provide supportive documentation or adequate evidence of corrective actions to bring operations into CGMP compliance (e.g., no details that identity testing met compendial requirements; no DEG/EG or methanol testing addressed; no initial water-system validation).

Q10. Why is "MED CHEM Tincture Green Soap" considered an unapproved new drug?
Expected: It is intended as a topical antiseptic drug, is not generally recognized as safe and effective (GRASE), has no approved application under section 505, and does not conform to the 1994 Tentative Final Monograph (as amended) or meet the conditions of section 505G(a)(3); its active ingredient (ethyl alcohol) is not among the three Category III consumer-antiseptic-wash ingredients.

Q11. What drug listing problem did the FDA identify?
Expected: Tincture Green (NDC 12745-041) and Isopropyl Alcohol (NDC 12745-105) were not certified as required under section 510(j)/21 CFR Part 207 and were inactivated by FDA in January 2024, rendering them misbranded under section 502(o).

Q12. What did the FDA recommend regarding a consultant, and what audit scope?
Expected: Engaging a qualified consultant under 21 CFR 211.34 to evaluate operations and perform a comprehensive six-system audit of the entire operation for CGMP compliance; use of a consultant does not relieve the firm's obligations.

Q13. Within what timeframe must the firm respond, and what did the FDA ask regarding retains?
Expected: Within 15 working days; the FDA asked for methanol test results within 30 calendar days from testing retains, and DEG/EG test results within 30 calendar days from testing retains (or testing implicated finished-product retains if component retains are unavailable).

Q14. Who signed the letter?
Expected: Francis Godwin (Director, Office of Manufacturing Quality) and Tina Smith (Captain, U.S. Public Health Service; Director, Office of Unapproved Drugs & Labeling Compliance), both of the Office of Compliance, CDER.

Q15. What overarching statutory consequence flows from the CGMP nonconformity?
Expected: Because the firm's methods, facilities, or controls do not conform to CGMP, its drug products are adulterated within the meaning of section 501(a)(2)(B) of the FD&C Act.
