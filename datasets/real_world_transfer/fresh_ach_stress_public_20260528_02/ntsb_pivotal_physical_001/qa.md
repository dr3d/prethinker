# QA

Q1. What flight was involved?
Expected: American Airlines flight 383.

Q2. Where did the accident occur?
Expected: Chicago O'Hare International Airport, Chicago, Illinois, on runway 28R.

Q3. What was the date of the accident?
Expected: October 28, 2016.

Q4. What airplane model was involved?
Expected: A Boeing 767-323, registration N345AN.

Q5. Which engine component failed?
Expected: The right engine HPT stage 2 disk.

Q6. What internal anomaly did the NTSB identify?
Expected: A discrete dirty white spot, an internal subsurface manufacturing anomaly.

Q7. What did the disk fragment damage?
Expected: It severed the main engine fuel feed line and breached the right-wing fuel tank.

Q8. How many serious injuries were reported?
Expected: One passenger received a serious injury.

Q9. How many minor injuries were reported?
Expected: Twenty passengers and one flight attendant received minor injuries.

Q10. Did the report identify structural, engine, or system failures before the uncontained failure?
Expected: No; it stated no evidence indicated such failures before the uncontained engine failure.

Q11. What did the report say about the captain's rejected takeoff?
Expected: The captain made a timely decision and performed it according to company training and procedures.

Q12. What inspection technique did the report mention as potentially detecting subsurface cracks?
Expected: Ultrasonic inspection or another subsurface inspection technique.

Q13. What was the NTSB report number?
Expected: NTSB/AAR-18/01.
