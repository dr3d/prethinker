[ITEM 4.02]
On May 1, 2025, the Audit Committee of the Board of Directors of Axon Enterprise, Inc. concluded that the Company's previously issued consolidated financial statements should no longer be relied upon and need to be restated because of an error in the balance sheet presentation of the Company's $690.0 million aggregate principal amount of 0.50% Convertible Senior Notes due 2027.

[CAUSE]
The Company identified an error in the balance sheet presentation of the 2027 Notes between current liabilities and long-term liabilities. The 2027 Notes should have been presented as current liabilities rather than long-term liabilities because holders could convert their notes during the subsequent calendar quarters after the Company's common stock price exceeded 130% of the conversion price for the required number of trading days.

[AFFECTED PERIODS]
The restatement affects the Company's annual consolidated financial statements for the year ended December 31, 2024, and the quarterly consolidated financial statements for the quarters ended March 31, 2024, June 30, 2024, and September 30, 2024. The Company intends to file an amendment to its Annual Report on Form 10-K for the year ended December 31, 2024.

[SUMMARY OF IMPACTS]
The correction did not impact Total Assets, Total Liabilities, or Stockholders' Equity. It did not impact the Company's consolidated statements of operations and comprehensive income, consolidated statements of cash flows, or consolidated statements of stockholders' equity. The correction reclassifies amounts between current liabilities and long-term liabilities.

[INTERNAL CONTROL]
Management determined that the Company had an additional material weakness in internal control over financial reporting due to a lack of an effectively designed control activity to periodically monitor the satisfaction of contingent conversion provisions and assess current versus long-term classification of the 2027 Notes.

This material weakness is separate from the previously reported material weakness related to revenue recognition.

[AUDITOR]
The Audit Committee and management discussed the matters disclosed in this Item 4.02 with PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP. PwC's opinion on the effectiveness of the Company's ICFR as of December 31, 2024, should no longer be relied upon.
