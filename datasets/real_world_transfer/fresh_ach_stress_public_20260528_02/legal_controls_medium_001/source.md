[SUMMARY]
These proceedings arise out of misleading statements in a July 2020 press release and failures to disclose material related party transactions. American Electric Power formed, fully funded, and controlled Empowering Ohio, Inc., an Ohio 501(c)(4) entity. The press release was misleading because it omitted key facts about American Electric Power's relationship with Empowering Ohio and about Empowering Ohio's contributions to Generation Now.

An American Electric Power employee directed Empowering Ohio's contributions to Generation Now, a 501(c)(4) entity associated with Ohio House Speaker Larry Householder. The omissions made American Electric Power's statement that it made no contributions to Generation Now materially misleading.

[RESPONDENT]
American Electric Power Company, Inc. is a New York corporation headquartered in Columbus, Ohio. It is one of the largest public utility holding companies in the United States. For the year ended December 31, 2023, American Electric Power reported approximately $19.0 billion in revenues, $3.6 billion in operating income, and $2.2 billion in net income.

[OTHER RELEVANT ENTITIES]
Generation Now pleaded guilty to RICO charges in federal court. Householder was convicted of racketeering conspiracy and sentenced to 20 years in prison. These criminal proceedings involved payments to 501(c)(4) entities associated with Householder.

[EMPOWERING OHIO]
From December 2014 through July 2020, American Electric Power was Empowering Ohio's sole funding source. American Electric Power employees approved and directed Empowering Ohio's contributions. In 2019, Empowering Ohio made multiple payments to Generation Now and to other entities associated with Householder.

[RELATED PARTY DISCLOSURES]
American Electric Power did not disclose the 2019 payments to Empowering Ohio as related party transactions in its 2019 Form 10-K. Under applicable accounting guidance, related party relationships and transactions can require disclosure when material to investors.

[INTERNAL CONTROLS]
American Electric Power lacked sufficient internal accounting controls to provide reasonable assurances that payments to Empowering Ohio were analyzed for related-party disclosure requirements. The company also did not maintain disclosure controls sufficient to ensure that information about Empowering Ohio and its relationship to American Electric Power was elevated to disclosure personnel.

[BOOKS AND RECORDS]
Two payments totaling $700,000 to Empowering Ohio during 2019 were recorded within American Electric Power's accounting system with no indication that the payments were made to a related party. The records did not reflect the relationship between American Electric Power and Empowering Ohio in a way that would allow the accounting and disclosure analysis to be performed reliably.

[ORDER]
The Commission finds that American Electric Power violated Section 17(a)(2) of the Securities Act and Sections 13(a), 13(b)(2)(A), and 13(b)(2)(B) of the Exchange Act. American Electric Power shall cease and desist from committing or causing future violations and shall pay a civil money penalty in the amount of $19,000,000. A Fair Fund is created for the penalty.
