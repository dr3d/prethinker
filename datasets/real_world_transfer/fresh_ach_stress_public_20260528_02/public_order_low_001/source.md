[DECISION]
The Federal Trade Commission has reason to believe that Respondents have violated the Federal Trade Commission Act. Respondents neither admit nor deny any of the allegations, except as specifically stated, and admit the facts necessary to establish jurisdiction.

[RESPONDENTS]
General Motors LLC, General Motors Holdings LLC, and OnStar LLC are Delaware limited liability companies with principal offices at the Renaissance Center, 300 Renaissance Center, Detroit, Michigan.

[AFFIRMATIVE EXPRESS CONSENT]
Affirmative Express Consent means any freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of an individual consumer's wishes demonstrating agreement by the consumer, such as by an affirmative action, following a clear and conspicuous disclosure. The act of hovering over, muting, pausing, or closing a given piece of content does not constitute Affirmative Express Consent.

[COVERED DRIVER DATA]
Covered Driver Data means Location Data or any data concerning a consumer's operation or use of a connected vehicle, including events such as hard braking, hard acceleration, hard cornering, crossing of a designated high-speed threshold, seat belt usage, and late-night driving, linked or reasonably linkable to a consumer or vehicle.

[PROVISION I]
For five years, Respondents must not disclose Covered Driver Data to any person when that person is acting in the capacity of a Consumer Reporting Agency.

[PROVISION II]
Within 180 days, Respondents must obtain the relevant U.S. consumer's Affirmative Express Consent prior to collecting, using, or disclosing Covered Driver Data to any third party. This requirement does not apply in limited circumstances, including: To disclose Covered Driver Data to emergency responders; to respond to a consumer-initiated communication; to ensure safe operation of a vehicle; to comply with lawful requests; for research or safety when data is deidentified or aggregated; for quality, safety, recalls, cybersecurity, warranties, diagnostics, or prognostics.

[PROVISION III]
Respondents must not limit a U.S. consumer's ability to withhold or withdraw Affirmative Express Consent by degrading the functionality of a product or service unless the degradation is technically necessary to provide the product or service without the relevant data.

[PROVISION IV]
Respondents must refrain from collecting more Covered Driver Data than reasonably necessary for the specific purpose disclosed to the consumer.

[PROVISION V]
Respondents must establish, implement, and adhere to a retention schedule for Covered Driver Data and must not retain such data longer than reasonably necessary for the disclosed purpose.

[PROVISION VI]
Respondents must delete prior-retained Covered Driver Data subject to specified exceptions and legal preservation requirements.

[PROVISION VII]
Respondents must provide a clear and conspicuous means for a consumer to request a copy of their Covered Driver Data and to request that Respondents Delete their Covered Driver Data.

[PROVISION VIII]
Respondents must request that third-party data sharing partners delete Covered Driver Data previously received from Respondents, subject to exceptions.

[PROVISION IX]
Respondents must provide a means for consumers to disable the collection of Location Data from their connected vehicles.

[PROVISION X]
Respondents must provide a means for consumers to disable remote collection of Covered Driver Data if the consumer declines or unenrolls from OnStar connected services, subject to specified exceptions.

[PROVISION XI]
Respondents must not misrepresent, in any manner, expressly or by implication, the extent to which they collect, use, disclose, delete, or allow consumers to control Covered Driver Data.
