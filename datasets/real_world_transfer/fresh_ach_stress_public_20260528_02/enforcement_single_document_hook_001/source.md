[INSPECTION BACKGROUND]
The U.S. Food and Drug Administration inspected your drug manufacturing facility, A. Nelson & Co. Ltd., FEI 1000568184, at 5 Endeavour Way, London, SW19 8UH, United Kingdom, from September 15 to 19, 2025. This warning letter summarizes significant violations of Current Good Manufacturing Practice (CGMP) regulations for finished pharmaceuticals.

[VIOLATION 1]
Your firm failed to conduct, for each batch of drug product, appropriate laboratory testing, as necessary, required to be free of objectionable microorganisms (21 CFR 211.165(b)).

You manufacture and release homeopathic drug products, some of which are labeled for use in children as young as two years old, without adequate testing for critical microbial attributes. Rather than testing every batch, you conducted (b)(4) tests on some of your batches. You did not test each batch before release to determine whether each batch was free of objectionable microorganisms.

[RESPONSE ASSESSMENT]
Your response is inadequate. A drug product was detained and refused admission at the U.S. border after FDA testing found microbiological contamination. Without testing each batch prior to release, you did not have scientific evidence that all (b)(4) drug product batches were free of objectionable microbial contamination before distribution. You also did not commit to testing your retain samples to determine whether previously released batches were contaminated.

[VIOLATION 2]
Your firm failed to establish and follow written procedures applicable to the responsibilities of the quality control unit (21 CFR 211.22(d)).

Your quality unit (QU) failed to provide adequate oversight and to ensure the reliability of data generated at your facility. FDA investigators observed duplicate records, discarded analytical forms, and handwritten notes related to analytical review, including a sticky note marked out-of-trend among discarded materials.

[DATA INTEGRITY]
Your response states that you will perform an external data integrity audit beginning in May 2026. However, your response does not provide sufficient interim controls to ensure that all data used to make batch release decisions are complete, consistent, and accurate.

[CONSULTANT]
Based upon the nature of the violations we identified, you should engage a consultant qualified as set forth in 21 CFR 211.34 to assist your firm in meeting CGMP requirements.
