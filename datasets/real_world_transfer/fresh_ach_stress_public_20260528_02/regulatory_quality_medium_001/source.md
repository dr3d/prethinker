[INSPECTION BACKGROUND]
The U.S. Food and Drug Administration inspected your drug manufacturing facility, Wisconsin Pharmacal Company, LLC, FEI 2124143, at N168 W 22223 Main St., Jackson, Wisconsin, from March 3 to March 7, 2025. This warning letter summarizes significant violations of Current Good Manufacturing Practice regulations for finished pharmaceuticals.

[VIOLATION 1]
Your quality control unit failed to exercise its responsibility to ensure drug products manufactured are in compliance with CGMP and meet established specifications for identity, strength, quality, and purity (21 CFR 211.22).

Your firm released drug product filled from multiple vessels after one vessel had failed assay testing. Your quality unit rejected units associated with one vessel but later allowed the distribution of approximately half of the rejected drug product.

[RESPONSE ASSESSMENT]
Your response states that the rejected product was not adequately identified and contained due to improper scanning procedure. Your response is inadequate. You fail to address the fundamental deficiencies in your QU that allowed rejected product to be distributed. You did not provide a comprehensive corrective action and preventive action plan for ensuring that rejected drug product is promptly identified, segregated, and prevented from distribution. Your firm's quality systems are inadequate.

[VIOLATION 2]
Your firm failed to thoroughly investigate any unexplained discrepancy or failure of a batch or any of its components to meet any of its specifications, whether or not the batch has already been distributed (21 CFR 211.192).

Your firm identified Staphylococcus aureus in microbiological testing on November 11, 2024, and August 19, 2024. You released the referenced batch for commercial distribution based on a passing retest result. Microbial contamination may not be uniformly distributed within a batch and retesting alone is not a substitute for a thorough investigation.

[DATA INTEGRITY]
In response to this letter, provide a comprehensive, independent assessment of your overall system for investigating deviations, discrepancies, complaints, out-of-specification results, and failures. Identify and evaluate vulnerabilities in performance and security of all of these computer systems, including but not limited to configurations, administrative privileges, audit trails, and data review procedures.

[REQUESTED REMEDIATION]
Provide a comprehensive assessment and remediation plan to ensure your quality unit is given the authority and resources to effectively function. Include a review of procedures, retrospective testing where appropriate, quality-unit oversight, final batch review, and investigations.
