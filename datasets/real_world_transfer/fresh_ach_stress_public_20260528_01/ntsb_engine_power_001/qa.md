**Q6 [competing_cause]** Which three explanations for the loss of engine power does the report weigh?
**A:** Fuel exhaustion; carburetor icing; and a throttle/mechanical malfunction (the report notes throttle-cable travel could not be documented). The report selects fuel exhaustion as most likely.

**Q7 [competing_cause]** What evidence in the report supports carburetor icing as a cause?
**A:** A review of the FAA Carburetor Icing Probability Chart showed the conditions in which the airplane was operating were conducive to the formation of serious icing at glide power.
**Coords:** [CARBURETOR ICING ASSESSMENT]

**Q8 [competing_cause]** What evidence in the report weighs against carburetor icing?
**A:** The pilot stated that he applied carburetor heat and enrichened the mixture when he began descending.
**Coords:** [ANALYSIS]; [HISTORY OF FLIGHT]

**Q9 [competing_cause]** Why could the report not fully rule out a throttle/mechanical problem?
**A:** Accident damage to the engine section prevented documentation of the throttle cable's full range of travel.

**Q10 [competing_cause]** What did examiners find about the throttle linkage and engine apart from the undocumentable cable travel?

**Q11 [pattern_evidence]** How did the pilot describe the manner in which power was lost?
**A:** A sudden loss of engine power (stop or change in rpm) did not occur; the engine "simply stopped responding to throttle inputs."

**Q12 [sensitivity]** If the finding that the tanks were empty with no leak were removed from the record, would the fuel-exhaustion conclusion still be the strongest? Explain using only the report.
**A:** It would be substantially weaker. Without the empty-tanks/no-leak finding, the only remaining support for fuel exhaustion is the inference that the pilot's fuel calculations "could have been incorrect," while the report independently states conditions were conducive to serious carburetor icing at glide power. The pilot's expectation of 10+ gallons would, on its face, point away from running out of fuel. So removing that one finding plausibly shifts the best-supported explanation toward carburetor icing.
**Coords:** [ANALYSIS]; [WRECKAGE — FUEL]; [CARBURETOR ICING ASSESSMENT]
