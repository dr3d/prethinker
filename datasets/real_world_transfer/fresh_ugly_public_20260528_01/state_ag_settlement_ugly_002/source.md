ASSURANCE OF DISCONTINUANCE.
The Office of the New York State Attorney General ("OAG") commenced an investigation pursuant to Executive Law § 63(12), General Business Law §§ 349, 350, and 527-a, and the Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act, 15 U.S.C. § 8403(3), into the enrollment and cancellation policies and practices of Equinox Group, LLC ("Equinox Group" or "Respondents") at the fitness centers and related services offered under the brand names Equinox, Equinox Media, SoulCycle. This Assurance of Discontinuance ("Assurance") contains the findings of the OAG's investigation and the relief agreed to by the OAG and Respondents whether acting through its respective directors, officers, employees, representatives, agents, affiliates, or subsidiaries (collectively, the "Parties").

[OAG'S FINDINGS — LEGAL FRAMEWORK]
1. Sellers that offer consumers products and services on an automatically renewing basis are subject to several requirements under state and federal law. Such automatic renewals are known as "negative options" because the product or service is provided (and the consumer is charged) continuously unless and until the consumer takes action to cancel.
2. New York General Business Law ("GBL") Article 29-BB requires "[a] business that makes an automatic renewal offer or continuous service offer" to present the offer terms "in a clear and conspicuous manner before the subscription or purchasing agreement is fulfilled and in visual proximity . . . to the request for consent to the offer." GBL § 527-a(1)(a). If the offer also includes a free gift or trial, it must include a clear and conspicuous explanation of the price charged after the trial.
3. The "automatic renewal offer terms" that must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously include: (a) that the agreement will continue until the consumer cancels; (b) the cancellation policy; (c) the recurring charges, whether the amount may change, and the changed amount if known; (d) the length of the automatic renewal term or that the service is continuous; and (e) the minimum purchase obligation, if any. GBL § 527(2).
4. "Clear and conspicuous" means "in larger type than the surrounding text, or in contrasting type, font, or color to the surrounding text of the same size, or set off from the surrounding text of the same size by symbols or other marks, in a manner that clearly calls attention to the language." GBL § 527(3).
5. The business must also obtain the consumer's affirmative consent to the agreement containing the automatic renewal offer terms before charging the consumer. GBL § 527-a(1)(b).
6. Additionally, the business must provide a "cost-effective, timely, and easy-to-use mechanism for cancellation." GBL § 527-a(2). A consumer who accepts an offer online "shall be allowed to terminate . . . exclusively online." GBL § 527-a(3).
7. Finally, the business must provide an acknowledgment that includes the offer terms, cancellation policy, and information regarding how to cancel in a manner capable of being retained by the consumer. GBL § 527-a(1)(c), (2).
8. The federal Restore Online Shoppers' Confidence Act ("ROSCA"), 15 U.S.C. § 8403, contains similar provisions, requiring a seller marketing a negative option feature over the internet to clearly and conspicuously disclose all material terms before obtaining billing information, obtain express informed consent before charging, and provide simple cancellation mechanisms.
9. According to the FTC's 2021 Enforcement Policy Statement Regarding Negative Option Marketing ("FTC Policy Statement"), ROSCA compliance requires clear and conspicuous disclosure of: (a) that the consumer will be charged; (b) that the charge will be recurring; (c) if applicable, that the charge will increase after a trial; (d) each deadline by which the consumer must act to stop charges; (e) the amount and frequency of charges; (f) the billing date; (g) all information necessary to cancel; and (h) any other terms necessary to prevent deception.
10. "Clear and conspicuous," per the FTC Policy Statement, means "difficult to miss (i.e., easily noticeable) or unavoidable and easily understandable by ordinary consumers."
11. To obtain express informed consent, a negative option seller should obtain the consumer's unambiguously affirmative consent to the negative option feature separately, and not include information that undermines that consent.
12. Per the FTC Policy Statement, cancellation mechanisms should be "at least as easy to use as the method the consumer used to initiate the negative option feature."

[OAG'S FINDINGS — BACKGROUND]
13. Equinox Group is a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 31 Hudson Yards, New York, NY 10001.
14. Equinox Group is a fitness company that operates several fitness and gym brands, including Equinox, Equinox Media, and SoulCycle.
15. The Equinox Group subsidiary Blink Fitness filed for bankruptcy on August 12, 2024 and its assets have been sold.
16. Since at least February 9, 2021 and until at least July 28, 2024, Respondents offered automatically renewing memberships through Equinox and Equinox Media that violated the laws described above. SoulCycle began offering similar automatically renewing memberships in September 2021. Among other things, Respondents (i) failed to clearly and conspicuously disclose the material terms before consumers agreed to the purchase; (ii) failed to provide acknowledgements compliant with GBL § 527-a; and/or (iii) failed to provide online cancellation mechanisms that were cost-effective, timely, and at least as easy for the consumer to use as enrollment.

[OAG'S FINDINGS — I. EQUINOX ENROLLMENT]
17. Equinox is a chain of high-end fitness centers owned by Equinox Holdings, Inc, a subsidiary of Equinox Group. Memberships to Equinox start at approximately $200 per month. There are currently 43 Equinox gyms in New York state, more than in any other state.
18. As described below, Equinox's actions violated New York Executive Law § 63(12), GBL §§ 349, 350, 527-a, and ROSCA, 15 U.S.C. § 8403. Among other violations, the company did not: (i) present all material terms of the offer clearly and conspicuously before the subscription was fulfilled; (ii) obtain the consumer's affirmative consent to the agreement containing the automatic renewal offer terms; and (iii) provide a cost-effective, timely, and easy-to-use mechanism for cancellation. GBL § 527-a(1)-(2).
19. Since at least February 9, 2021, Equinox offered three tiers of membership: the "Select" tier (access to only a single gym); the "All-Access" tier (access to all U.S. Equinox locations); and the "Destination" Tier (access to Equinox locations worldwide).
20. All Equinox membership plans included a one-year minimum commitment period (the "Obligation Period"). Members were locked into the one-year commitment and could not cancel early unless they invoked a statutory exception, and so were responsible for the entire year's fees (paid monthly), amounting to thousands of dollars.
21. Equinox Members could also purchase automatically renewing packages of personal training sessions and/or Pilates classes on the Equinox mobile application; these packages renewed upon use of the package's last session.
22. Since at least February 9, 2021, the material terms of an Equinox gym membership included: (a) the benefits/locations of access; (b) the monthly cost, starting at approximately $200; (c) that the monthly fee would be charged on the 23rd day of each month; (d) the initiation fee of $300 or more; (e) that all plans included a one-year Obligation Period and would convert to month-to-month after the first year; (f) that Members were required to provide at least 45 days of notice to cancel; (g) that a Member could cancel without penalty on proof of one of: (i) a physical disability preventing use for more than six months; (ii) moving more than 25 miles away from any Equinox location; (iii) Equinox could no longer provide the services (e.g., location closed); or (iv) death, in which case the estate could cancel; (h) that under New York GBL § 624(2), Members in New York could cancel without penalty within three days of joining; and (i) that Members could cancel by certified mail, in person, via email, and via online chat.
23-29. [Equinox online enrollment flow.] A consumer enrolling online navigated through several pages. The "12-mo commitment" appeared as a fine-print statement in small grey font approximately one third the size of the large, bolded marketing text. The initiation fee (up to $300) was disclosed for the first time on the payment page, only after the consumer had navigated three other pages, and after the enrollment flow obtained the consumer's consent to be contacted by a Membership Advisor. Equinox obtained consent to the linked Terms and Conditions and to billing on the 23rd, but did not request the consumer's express affirmative consent to the negative option feature at any point in the online enrollment flow. (Process updated September 2022 with no material revisions.)
30. The enrollment confirmation email included a summary of fees and a link to the membership agreement, but the body did not include the material terms (other than price), the cancellation policy, or how to cancel.
31. Since 2021, numerous consumers submitted complaints to the FTC, the BBB, and Equinox about confusion related to enrollment, particularly that they were not adequately informed of the one-year commitment period. [Representative complaint, March 2021: a consumer who toured the facility and signed up reported never being told the membership was annual rather than monthly… and that cancellation in the first year was virtually impossible unless they "relocated more than 25 miles of the location."] [Additional representative complaints from October 2021, July 2022, and January 2023 are quoted in the source.]

[OAG'S FINDINGS — EQUINOX CANCELLATION]
32. Since at least February 9, 2021, Equinox Members could cancel by certified mail, at the front desk, by emailing cancellations@equinox.com, and by webchat on the Equinox website or App. Equinox did not provide a pre-formatted email for cancellation.
33-34. The cancellation mechanisms were disclosed in small font in the membership agreement and the FAQ section, but neither document informed Members they could cancel via webchat; the cancellation policy and mechanisms were not clearly and conspicuously disclosed in the enrollment flow or the confirmation email body, and were accessible only through a general FAQ link at the bottom of the website.
35. Members still in their one-year obligation period could not cancel without charge unless they met one of the New York statutory exceptions (physical disability for 6+ months with proof; moving more than 25 miles with proof; death; or Equinox no longer able to provide services).
36-37. Members in the obligation period who wanted to cancel were typically offered a freeze of up to three months (extending the obligation period), and otherwise had their cancellation processed as of the last day of the obligation period, remaining responsible for monthly dues until then.
38. Members no longer in their obligation period were permitted to cancel but were likely to encounter delays. (a) An in-person request would not be processed at the time; the front desk associate was instructed not to process the cancellation even if the Member insisted. (b) A manager was directed to "TRY TO SAVE THE MEMBER" and was instructed that they should not actively inform Members that they might be able to cancel without further charge in certain circumstances; the instruction stated, "If a member is suffering from a medical condition or relocating away from their club(s), this is something that will come up in conversation and is not something we need to offer." (c) Email cancellations were first met with a save attempt. (d) Webchat cancellations were offered a freeze.
39-45. [Equinox cancellation mechanisms not cost-effective, timely, and easy to use.] The "Contact Us" page offered information on how to join but not how to cancel. From at least February 9, 2021 until March 2023, Members could cancel online via webchat with an Equinox Concierge (Monday-Friday, 8 AM-8 PM Eastern), but Equinox did not publicize this option. The webchat required responding to between 1 and 4 automated prompts, waiting to connect to a Concierge, and at least one "save" attempt before cancellation would begin — significantly more burdensome than the enrollment flow, which required navigating through just three pages with prominent visual and textual cues. Equinox stopped using webchat cancellation in March 2023.
46. Since 2021, consumers submitted complaints to the FTC, the BBB, and Equinox about frustration with the cancellation processes. [Representative complaints from January 2021, September 2021, and March 2022 are quoted in the source.]

[OAG'S FINDINGS — EQUINOX APP PT/PILATES]
47-54. Equinox Members could purchase automatically renewing personal training ("PT") and Pilates class memberships on the Equinox App, charged for the selected number of sessions each time their inventory reached zero. Neither the price of the sessions nor the cancellation policy was disclosed when toggling on a PT or Pilates auto-renew membership, and Equinox failed to obtain the consumer's express affirmative consent to the negative option feature. The process was revised in July 2021 without remedying the deficiencies. Beginning July 28, 2024, Equinox established an online cancellation option for all New York members, in addition to improved disclosures and consents. A confirmation email was sent for each new package purchased but did not include instructions on how to cancel.

[OAG'S FINDINGS — II. SOULCYCLE]
55. SoulCycle is a chain of high-end indoor cycling studios owned by Equinox Group. In New York, individual classes cost as much as $40.
56. SoulCycle's actions violated New York Executive Law § 63(12), GBL §§ 349, 350, 527-a, and ROSCA, 15 U.S.C. § 8403, including failing to (i) present the terms clearly and conspicuously before fulfillment; (ii) obtain affirmative consent to the automatic renewal terms; and (iii) provide a cost-effective, timely, and easy-to-use cancellation mechanism.
57. In September 2021, SoulCycle began offering an automatically renewing membership feature, Soul Renew, under which a consumer selected the number of classes to purchase each month and was automatically charged that amount every month until cancellation, at a discounted per-class rate.
58. Since September 2021 the material terms of a Soul Renew membership included: (a) the price (depending on number of classes); (b) that it renewed automatically each month until cancelled; (c) that the classes expired after 30 days and did not roll over; (d) that the classes were non-transferable; (e) that SoulCycle reserved the right to incrementally increase the price upon advance written notice; (f) the monthly billing date; (g) that a Member had to cancel at least 24 hours prior to the next billing cycle to avoid being charged for the following month; (h) that a Member could cancel without penalty on proof of the same four circumstances as Equinox; (i) that under New York GBL § 624(2) Members could cancel without penalty within three days of joining; and (j) that Members could cancel in person, by phone, by letter to their home studio or SoulCycle's headquarters, or by emailing yoursoulmatters@soul-cycle.com.
59-67. [Soul Renew online enrollment.] The SoulCycle home page displayed a prominent bright-yellow "Sign Up and Save" button next to a fine, transparent "Learn More" button. The class packages showed the number of classes and monthly price prominently, with "Expires in 30 days" in small fine grey font roughly 50% smaller than the marketing text above. On July 15, 2023, SoulCycle updated the page to add "This membership is a recurring payment and expires 30 days from purchase date." The billing page identified the package and price but nothing on it indicated the purchase was a renewing monthly subscription. SoulCycle did not request the consumer's express affirmative consent to the negative option feature at any point in the online enrollment flow. [The shopping-cart footer reproduced the SOUL RENEW TERMS AND CONDITIONS and a cancellation notice referencing SoulCycle Inc., 609 Greenwich Street, New York, New York, 10014.]
68. From time to time, SoulCycle offered promotions on Soul Renew packages. For instance, in January 2023, SoulCycle ran a promotion giving consumers 20 percent off a Soul Renew purchase with code SOULRENEW20. The discount applied only to the consumer's initial purchase; subsequent packages would automatically renew at the higher, standard price. However, this fact was not disclosed anywhere: not in the promotional offer, not in the online purchase flow, and not in the terms and conditions that appeared in the purchase flow.
69-76. [Soul Renew App enrollment and cancellation.] The App's default "Memberships" page showed Soul Renew packages with "Expires in 30 days" in small fine grey font approximately 50% smaller than the marketing text; the consumer toggled to "Class Packs" to see non-renewing options. Material terms appeared only in fine print or lengthy terms and conditions, and SoulCycle did not request express affirmative consent to the negative option feature. Soul Renew Members could cancel in person, by phone, by emailing yoursoulmatters@soul-cycle.com, or by certified mail; SoulCycle did not provide a pre-formatted cancellation email, and the FAQs page did not disclose all available cancellation mechanisms (it just directed Members to contact their local studios).

[OAG'S FINDINGS — III. EQUINOX+ APP]
77-80. The Equinox Group operated a digital fitness-class platform called Equinox+ through Equinox Media; it is also accessible through the display on the SoulCycle-branded at-home Bike. If a consumer purchased a Bike, they had to purchase an Equinox+ App membership with a 12-month commitment period that converted to month-to-month; all other Equinox+ memberships were month-to-month. The membership price was around $40 per month, billed monthly on the anniversary date of the initial purchase. Members generally could cancel through the App, on the website, or by emailing hello@equinoxplus.com or hello@equinoxmedia.com, with exceptions for App Store/Play Store and Bike purchasers (Bike members could cancel only via email; App Store/Play Store members had to cancel via those third parties).
81-87. [Equinox+ online enrollment.] Equinox+ ran a continuous promotion of seven days of App access for free. The enrollment pages used much smaller, thinner disclosure font (the disclaimer statements appeared approximately 33% the size of the marketing text), informed the consumer their card "will not be charged until the complimentary 7-day trial period ends," and disclosed the automatic-renewal agreement only in a dense paragraph of small white font on the final account-creation page. Equinox Media did not request the consumer's express affirmative consent to the negative option feature at any point in the online enrollment flow. The Equinox+ Bike enrollment flow was substantially similar except that no seven-day free trial was offered; the membership was described as a "12-month Equinox+ Membership" that would become month-to-month after the first 12 months.
