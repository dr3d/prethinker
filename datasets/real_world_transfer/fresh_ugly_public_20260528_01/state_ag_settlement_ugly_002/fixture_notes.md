# Fixture notes — state_ag_settlement_ugly_002

## Why this document is messy
It is a state Attorney General consumer-protection Assurance of Discontinuance whose Findings run dozens of numbered paragraphs and span four distinct product lines (Equinox gyms, Equinox App PT/Pilates packages, SoulCycle "Soul Renew," and the Equinox+ App), each with its own enrollment flow, material-terms list, and cancellation process. It interleaves statutory text (with nested quoted definitions), corporate-structure facts, dollar figures, dates, embedded consumer-complaint quotes, and references to figures, producing many near-parallel passages that are easy to conflate.

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: an assurance number (24-099), four distinct corporate Respondents, multiple email/contact addresses, statutory citations (GBL § 527-a/§ 527/§ 349/§ 350/§ 624(2); Executive Law § 63(12); ROSCA 15 U.S.C. § 8403), and two physical addresses.
- Date/sequence: a conduct-period start (Feb 9, 2021), a SoulCycle start (Sept 2021), process-revision dates (Sept 2022, July 2021, July 15, 2023), a webchat-cancellation end (March 2023), an online-cancellation establishment date (July 28, 2024), and a subsidiary bankruptcy date (Aug 12, 2024).
- Money disambiguation: Equinox ~$200/month and up to $300 initiation; SoulCycle classes up to $40; Equinox+ ~$40/month; a 20% promotional discount.
- Table/list/coordinate: the enumerated automatic-renewal offer terms, the three Equinox tiers, the four no-penalty cancellation circumstances (repeated for Equinox and SoulCycle), and the Soul Renew material-terms list; paragraph numbers serve as fine-grained coordinates.
- Source-state/limitation: the repeated finding that express affirmative consent to the negative option was never obtained; the "save the member" instructions; the non-publicized webchat option.
- Cross-section: the same three-part violation recited for three brands; the enrollment-vs-cancellation burden comparison; the promotion's undisclosed renewal-price term versus the base terms in fine print.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- The same four no-penalty cancellation circumstances and the same three-part violation language recur across Equinox and SoulCycle, inviting attribution errors.
- Multiple cancellation email addresses appear (cancellations@equinox.com; yoursoulmatters@soul-cycle.com; hello@equinoxplus.com; hello@equinoxmedia.com), tied to different brands.
- The Findings describe several overlapping conduct windows and process-revision dates per product line.
