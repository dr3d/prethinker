q014. What four CGMP regulations were cited, with their CFR citations, in order?
Answer: (1) 21 CFR 211.113(a) — failure to prevent objectionable microorganisms; (2) 21 CFR 211.100(a) — production/process control procedures; (3) 21 CFR 211.192 — failure to thoroughly investigate discrepancies/failures; (4) 21 CFR 211.166(a) — stability testing program. [CGMP VIOLATION 1-4 headings]

q015. Under which statutory sections does FDA say the products are unapproved new drugs?
Answer: Sections 301(d) and 505(a) of the FD&C Act (21 U.S.C. 331(d) and 355(a)). [OPENING / UNAPPROVED NEW DRUG VIOLATIONS]

q016. Which three FDA/ICH quality-system guidances are referenced for CGMP-compliant quality systems?
Answer: Q8(R2) Pharmaceutical Development; Q9(R1) Quality Risk Management; and Q10 Pharmaceutical Quality System. [QUALITY SYSTEMS]

q017. Which CFR section qualifies the recommended consultant, and what kind of audit must the consultant perform?
Answer: 21 CFR 211.34; a comprehensive six-system audit of the entire operation. [CONSULTANT RECOMMENDED]

q018. Per the labeling examples, what is Hydro-Q Gel (hydroquinone 4%) indicated for?
Answer: The gradual bleaching of hyperpigmented skin conditions such as chloasma, melasma, freckles, senile lentigines and areas of melanin hyperpigmentation. [UNAPPROVED NEW DRUG VIOLATIONS]

q019. Which listed product is indicated for the topical treatment and removal of common warts and plantar warts?
Answer: Salicylic Acid 28.5% Solution. [UNAPPROVED NEW DRUG VIOLATIONS]

q020. What redaction marker is used throughout the letter to withhold confidential details (such as the treatment protocol, lot numbers, and temperatures)?
Answer: "(b)(4)". [appears throughout, e.g., CGMP VIOLATION 1 and 4]

q021. How were the TAMC and TYMC microbiological results described for the cited lots in violation 1?
Answer: As "too numerous to count (TNTC)" for both TAMC and TYMC (one lot's TYMC was reported as "(b)(4) colony forming units (CFU) per gram"). [CGMP VIOLATION 1]

q022. Why did FDA find the firm's risk assessment for the microbiological contamination inadequate?
Answer: Because it did not extend to all drug products where (b)(4) treatment was attempted, did not provide plans for product-quality and patient-safety risks for lots treated or released after failing results, did not address whether additional bacterial species were present, and provided no evidence that growth on total yeast and mold plates was solely bacterial. [CGMP VIOLATION 1]

q023. What does the Conclusion say about whether the cited violations are a complete list?
Answer: The violations cited in the letter are "not intended to be an all-inclusive list of violations that exist at your facility." [CONCLUSION]

q024. In violation 2, what did the firm do at its customer's direction, and what did it continue to do anyway?
Answer: At the customer's direction the firm discontinued its efforts to complete process validation for a product, yet it continued manufacturing batches of that product for commercial distribution. (The "Responsibilities as a Contractor" section states the firm is responsible for drug quality regardless of agreements with product owners.) [CGMP VIOLATION 2 + RESPONSIBILITIES AS A CONTRACTOR]

q025. In violation 4 (stability), what storage-condition problem did FDA cite, and what did the firm concede about relying on product owners?
Answer: The firm labeled much wider storage conditions (in degrees Fahrenheit) than its stability data supported, at the instruction of its customer (and even after narrowing the range, it remained outside the supporting data); in response the firm conceded it "can no longer rely on written justifications from product owners to deviate from the general [C]GMP requirements." [CGMP VIOLATION 4]
