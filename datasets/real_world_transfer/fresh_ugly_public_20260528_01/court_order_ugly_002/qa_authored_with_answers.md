# QA with reference answers — court_order_ugly_002

Source: U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit, Hornberger v. MSPB, No. 2025-1156 (Dec. 2, 2025), nonprecedential per curiam.

q001. Which court issued this decision, and what is the appeal number?
Answer: The United States Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit; No. 2025-1156. [CAPTION]

q002. Who is the petitioner, where is he located, and is he represented by counsel?
Answer: Kevin Bailey Hornberger, North East, MD, pro se. [CAPTION]

q003. Who is the respondent, and who represented it?
Answer: The Merit Systems Protection Board; Constance E. Travanty of the Office of the General Counsel, U.S. Merit Systems Protection Board, also represented by Katherine Michelle Smith. [CAPTION]

q004. What is the MSPB docket number of the underlying appeal?
Answer: DC-3443-24-0906-I-1. [CAPTION]

q005. Which judges sat on the panel, and how was the opinion issued?
Answer: Circuit Judges Dyk, Prost, and Reyna; the opinion was issued per curiam. [CAPTION]

q006. What was Hornberger's position, and where did he work?
Answer: He worked as a Management Analyst with the licensing division of the Library of Congress's Copyright Office. [BACKGROUND]

q007. On what date was the decision issued?
Answer: December 2, 2025. [CAPTION]

q008. When was Hornberger terminated from his position?
Answer: February 2023. [BACKGROUND]

q009. What was the date of the Board's initial decision, and when did it become final?
Answer: The initial decision was dated October 4, 2024 (M.S.P.B.) and became final on November 8, 2024. [CAPTION]/[BACKGROUND]

q010. Put these events in chronological order: the Federal Circuit affirms; the Copyright Office termination; the Board dismisses for lack of jurisdiction; the LOC sustains the removal.
Answer: (1) Copyright Office termination (February 2023); (2) LOC sustains the removal; (3) Board dismisses for lack of jurisdiction (October 2024); (4) Federal Circuit affirms (December 2, 2025). [BACKGROUND]+[CONCLUSION]

q011. Under what statute does the Federal Circuit have jurisdiction over this petition?
Answer: 28 U.S.C. § 1295(a)(9). [BACKGROUND]

q012. What citation does the court give for the standard of review of the Board's decision?
Answer: 5 U.S.C. § 7703(c). [DISCUSSION]

q013. State the three grounds on which the court must set aside the Board's decision under § 7703(c).
Answer: "(1) arbitrary, capricious, an abuse of discretion, or otherwise not in accordance with law; (2) obtained without procedures required by law, rule, or regulation having been followed; or (3) unsupported by substantial evidence." [DISCUSSION]

q014. What case is cited for the proposition that the petitioner bears the burden of establishing reversible error?
Answer: Sistek v. Dep't of Veterans Affs., 955 F.3d 948, 953 (Fed. Cir. 2020). [DISCUSSION]

q015. How does the court review the Board's legal decisions versus its factual findings, and what case is cited?
Answer: Legal decisions are reviewed de novo and findings of fact for substantial evidence; McIntosh v. Dep't of Def., 53 F.4th 630, 638 (Fed. Cir. 2022). [DISCUSSION]

q016. Which two precedents about Library of Congress employees does the court rely on (give the full citations)?
Answer: Gardner v. Libr. of Cong., 774 F.2d 1081, 1082 (Fed. Cir. 1985); and Nieuwdorp v. Libr. of Cong., 872 F.2d 1000, 1002 (Fed. Cir. 1989). [DISCUSSION]

q017. Why did the Board dismiss Hornberger's appeal?
Answer: For lack of jurisdiction: to appeal an adverse action an employee must generally be employed in an Executive Branch agency or the competitive service, and LOC employees work in the Legislative Branch and do not hold competitive-service positions. [BACKGROUND]

q018. Quote the Gardner holding the court relies on.
Answer: "[b]ecause the [LOC] is part of the Legislative Branch, its employees are not in the competitive service . . . and may not appeal adverse actions to the [Board]." [DISCUSSION]

q019. Besides affirming, what does the court note the Board lacks authority to do, and under what statute?
Answer: The Board lacks authority to transfer Hornberger's appeal to another forum (5 U.S.C. § 1204). [DISCUSSION]

q020. What does footnote 2 say about the Strausbaugh v. Government Printing Office case that Hornberger cited?
Answer: That whatever the meaning of that decision (cited for the proposition that Government Printing Office employees may appeal an adverse action to the Board), it is not binding on the Federal Circuit. [FOOTNOTES] fn 2

q021. What does the abbreviation "S.A." refer to, per footnote 1?
Answer: The supplemental appendix included with the government's informal brief. [FOOTNOTES] fn 1

q022. What is the court's disposition, in the exact word used?
Answer: "AFFIRMED" — the court affirms the Board's dismissal of the appeal. [CONCLUSION]

q023. What does the court say about costs, and what is the precedential status of the disposition?
Answer: "No costs"; the disposition is nonprecedential ("This disposition is nonprecedential."). [CONCLUSION]+[COSTS]+[CAPTION]

q024. What argument did Hornberger make to the court, and why did the court reject it?
Answer: He argued the Board could have heard the merits of his case instead of dismissing for lack of jurisdiction; the court rejected it because binding precedent (Gardner and Nieuwdorp) holds LOC employees are not in the competitive service and may not appeal to the Board, and Hornberger did not explain why that precedent would not apply. [DISCUSSION]

q025. Trace the procedural path of the adverse action from the Copyright Office to the Federal Circuit.
Answer: The Copyright Office terminated Hornberger (February 2023); the LOC sustained the removal after reviewing an advisory decision by the Federal Mediation and Conciliation Service; Hornberger appealed to the MSPB, which dismissed for lack of jurisdiction (October 2024, final November 8, 2024); he then petitioned the Federal Circuit, which affirmed (December 2, 2025). [BACKGROUND]+[DISCUSSION]+[CONCLUSION]
