# Fixture notes — procurement_contract_ugly_002

## Why this document is messy
It is a GAO bid protest decision that interleaves several structural registers: a counsel/roster caption; a numbered three-point DIGEST; a narrative BACKGROUND with an embedded ratings/price table; three separately reasoned DISCUSSION subsections (OCI, technical evaluation, best-value tradeoff); a CONCLUSION; and a RECOMMENDATION. It carries dense legal citation strings (B-numbers, "CPD ¶" reporter cites, FAR and C.F.R. cites), nine footnotes that carry substantive qualifications (e.g., the RFQ/RFP nomenclature dispute, rating definitions, the abandoned argument), and bracketed editorial substitutions in quotations ("[o]fferor," "[s]imple," "[SOW]").

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity: the solicitation number (9531BP25Q0010), file number (B-423689), and dollar figures ($1,793,354 vs $1,831,111) are easy to transpose.
- Table extraction: the three-row evaluation table (Technical Approach / Past Performance / Price) across two offerors.
- Cross-section reasoning: the OCI mechanism is split between BACKGROUND (the July 2023 CME task order) and the OCI subsection; the "lowest price but excluded from tradeoff" point requires joining the table with the tradeoff subsection and footnote 8.
- Source-state/limitation: the decision repeatedly stresses what the record did NOT contain (no documented OCI analysis; no timeline requirement in the SOW; an abandoned argument; no finding on who should win).
- Exact-wording: citation strings and quoted solicitation language with bracketed substitutions.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- The solicitation is referred to inconsistently as an RFQ and an RFP; footnote 1 records GAO's choice to call it an RFQ. Questions use "RFQ/solicitation" to track the source.
- "June 17" and "June 2025" both appear (selection-notice date vs. predecessor-BPA expiry); they are distinct and separated in the text.
- The term "deficiency" is undefined in the solicitation (footnote 8), and a "marginal" rating is defined (footnote 3) but does not render a quotation ineligible — a subtlety that the tradeoff analysis turns on.
