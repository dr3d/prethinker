q012. In what month and year did the agency issue the acquisition-support order to CME, and what role does that order give CME relative to the contracting officer?
Answer: July 2023; under that delivery order CME provides contractor personnel as contract specialists, and a CME contract specialist provides direct support to the contracting officer who in turn was the source selection authority for the protested procurement. [IMPAIRED OBJECTIVITY OCI]

q013. Within how many days, and to whom, must the protester submit its certified claims for protest costs?
Answer: Within 60 days after receipt of the decision, submitted directly to the agency (4 C.F.R. § 21.8(f)). [RECOMMENDATION]

q014. Place these four events in chronological order: protest filed; acquisition-support order to CME; solicitation issued; selection notice to Castro.
Answer: (1) acquisition-support order to CME — July 2023; (2) solicitation issued — April 25, 2025; (3) selection notice to Castro — June 17, 2025; (4) protest filed (after a requested debriefing). [combines IMPAIRED OBJECTIVITY OCI (July 2023) with BACKGROUND (2025 events)]

q015. In the evaluation summary table, what technical approach ratings did Castro and CME receive?
Answer: Castro: Marginal; CME: Outstanding. [BACKGROUND, evaluation table; AR, Exh. 9, Summary of Award at 3-4]

q016. What total price did each offeror quote in the evaluation table?
Answer: Castro: $1,793,354; CME: $1,831,111. [BACKGROUND, evaluation table]

q017. What past performance confidence ratings did Castro and CME receive?
Answer: Castro: Moderate Confidence; CME: High Confidence. [BACKGROUND, evaluation table]

q018. What were the three evaluation factors, and in what order of importance?
Answer: (1) technical approach (significantly more important than price); (2) price (more important than past performance); (3) past performance. [BACKGROUND]

q019. Give the full citation string the decision uses for the proposition that GAO will not reevaluate quotations.
Answer: NCI Info. Sys., Inc., B-412870.2, Oct. 14, 2016, 2016 CPD ¶ 310 at 11. [DISCUSSION]

q020. What agency-report exhibit and page coordinate is cited for the evaluation summary (the ratings/price table)?
Answer: AR, Exh. 9, Summary of Award at 3-4. [BACKGROUND]

q021. Quote the solicitation's exact warning about "[s]imple statements of compliance."
Answer: "[s]imple statements of compliance (i.e. 'understood,' 'will comply') without a detailed description of how compliance will be achieved will not be considered sufficient evidence that the proposed services can technically meet the requirements." [BACKGROUND]

q022. The TET assessed a weakness for missing "timeline details." What did GAO find about the SOW's requirements, and what related term did the solicitation actually list?
Answer: GAO found the SOW did not include any requirement for timelines (RFQ at 8-12); the solicitation listed "[t]imeliness" (not timelines) as a methodology element, and the record confirmed Castro's quotation addressed timeliness (AR, Exh. 4 at 12). [TECHNICAL APPROACH EVALUATION + FOOTNOTES fn 9 + BACKGROUND methodology list]

q023. Which protest argument did GAO treat as abandoned, and why?
Answer: Castro's argument that the agency unreasonably assessed a deficiency for the firm's key personnel lacking experience; it was abandoned because the protester did not substantively address the agency's response in its comments. [FOOTNOTES fn 8]

q024. On how many and which grounds did GAO sustain the protest?
Answer: Three grounds: (1) the OCI consideration was unreasonable and inadequately documented; (2) the technical approach evaluation was unsupported and inadequately documented; and (3) the best-value tradeoff decision was unreasonable and inadequately documented. [DIGEST 1-3 + the three DISCUSSION subsections + CONCLUSION]

q025. Castro offered the lowest price of all seven quotations, yet how was its quotation treated in the tradeoff, and what term did the solicitation not define that bears on this?
Answer: Castro's quotation — the lowest-priced of all seven, rated "marginal" but not "unacceptable" — was excluded from the documented tradeoff without any explanation; the solicitation did not define the term "deficiency," and a marginal rating did not render a quotation ineligible for award. [BACKGROUND table + BEST-VALUE TRADEOFF ANALYSIS + FOOTNOTES fn 8]
