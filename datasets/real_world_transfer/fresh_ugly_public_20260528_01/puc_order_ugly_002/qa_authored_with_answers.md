# QA with reference answers — puc_order_ugly_002

Source: CPUC Resolution ALJ-445, Resolving Request for Hearing H.22-07-010 (PG&E 2020 PSPS Administrative Enforcement), issued October 16, 2023.

q001. What is the resolution number, and which Commission division issued it?
Answer: Resolution ALJ-445; the Administrative Law Judge Division of the California Public Utilities Commission. [CAPTION]

q002. What is the Request for Hearing (proceeding) number resolved by this resolution?
Answer: H.22-07-010 (Request for Hearing (H.) 22-07-010). [CAPTION]

q003. Who are the two parties whose Joint Motion the resolution grants?
Answer: The Safety and Enforcement Division (SED) and Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E). [SUMMARY]/[BACKGROUND]

q004. Who was the assigned Administrative Law Judge?
Answer: Robert W. Haga. [BACKGROUND]

q005. Who certified the resolution and with what title, and who is the Commission President?
Answer: Rachel Peterson, Executive Director, certified the resolution; Alice Reynolds is the President. [VOTE]

q006. Which Commissioner was necessarily absent and did not participate in the vote?
Answer: Commissioner Darcie L. Houck. [VOTE]

q007. What is the date of issuance, and on what date was the resolution adopted at conference?
Answer: Date of issuance: October 16, 2023 (10/16/2023); adopted at a conference held October 12, 2023. [CAPTION]/[VOTE]

q008. On what date did SED issue the proposed Administrative Enforcement Order?
Answer: June 15, 2022. [BACKGROUND]

q009. On what date did PG&E submit its Request for Hearing on the Proposed Order?
Answer: July 15, 2022. [BACKGROUND]

q010. On what date did the Parties file their Joint Motion for Approval of Settlement Agreement?
Answer: October 13, 2022. [THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]

q011. On what date was the Commission Enforcement Policy (Resolution M-4846) adopted?
Answer: November 6, 2020. [DISCUSSION — PENALTY ASSESSMENT METHODOLOGY]

q012. Within how many days of the effective date must PG&E pay the $500,000 fine?
Answer: Within 30 days of the effective date of the Resolution (the Settlement Agreement states within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date). [THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]/[ORDER] 2

q013. Place these events in chronological order: the Joint Motion for Approval of Settlement; the proposed Administrative Enforcement Order; PG&E's Request for Hearing; the Joint Motion for Stay.
Answer: (1) proposed Administrative Enforcement Order (June 15, 2022); (2) PG&E's Request for Hearing (July 15, 2022); (3) Joint Motion for Stay (July 21, 2022); (4) Joint Motion for Approval of Settlement (October 13, 2022). [BACKGROUND]+[THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]

q014. Over what period must PG&E fund the additional ISM amount, and how does it relate to PG&E's existing ISM funding?
Answer: PG&E must fund an additional $7,500,000 to the Independent Safety Monitor during the period 2023 to 2026 with shareholder funds; this is in addition to the $5 million per year that PG&E already funds with shareholder funds pursuant to Resolution M-4855. [THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]/[ORDER] 3

q015. The Proposed Order alleged violations of which three Commission authorities, and how many of each?
Answer: Three alleged violations of Resolution ESRB-8; eight alleged violations of Decision (D.) 19-05-042; and one alleged violation of D.20-05-051. [BACKGROUND]/[FINDINGS] 3

q016. What total penalty did the Proposed Order direct, and what total does the Settlement provide (with its breakdown)?
Answer: The Proposed Order directed penalties totaling $12,000,000; the Settlement provides an $8 million total — a $500,000 monetary penalty to the California State General Fund plus $7,500,000 in additional shareholder-funded ISM funding. [BACKGROUND]+[THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]

q017. What are the five factors of the Penalty Assessment Methodology?
Answer: (1) severity or gravity of the offense(s); (2) conduct of the regulated entity; (3) financial resources of the regulated entity; (4) totality of the circumstances in furtherance of the public interest; and (5) the role of precedent. [DISCUSSION — PENALTY ASSESSMENT METHODOLOGY]

q018. What two precedent PSPS penalties did the Parties discuss, with amounts and citations?
Answer: A $24,000 penalty to SDG&E for its 2020 PSPS events (Res. M-4862); and a $106 million penalty (offset against $86 million in bill credits) to PG&E for violating guidelines during fall 2019 PSPS events (D.21-09-026). [DISCUSSION — ... PRECEDENT]

q019. To whom and to what address must PG&E send the $500,000 fine, and what must it write on the check?
Answer: Payable to the California Public Utilities Commission, mailed or delivered to the Commission's Fiscal Office at 505 Van Ness Avenue, Room 3000, San Francisco, CA 94102; PG&E shall write on the face of the check or money order "For deposit to the General Fund pursuant to Resolution ALJ-445." [ORDER] 2

q020. Where is the Settlement Agreement located in the resolution?
Answer: It is attached as Appendix A (Attachment 1: H2207010 Settlement Agreement.pdf). [SUMMARY]/[APPENDIX A]

q021. Was public review or comment required for this resolution, and on what basis?
Answer: No. Public review and comment were waived pursuant to Rule 14.6(c)(2) of the Commission's Rules of Practice and Procedure. [COMMENTS]

q022. What did the Settlement Agreement state about Rule 1.1 violations, other ethical violations, or deliberate misconduct?
Answer: It stated there were no allegations of Rule 1.1 violations and no allegations of other ethical violations or any deliberate misconduct associated with these actions. [DISCUSSION — SEVERITY OF THE OFFENSE]

q023. Who funds the settlement amounts, and may PG&E recover them in rates?
Answer: PG&E shareholders fund the amounts; PG&E shall not seek to recover any of these costs in rates. [THE SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT]

q024. What did the Parties conclude about whether the precedent cases were comparable to this matter?
Answer: They determined the factual circumstances presented here are not reasonably comparable to other cases where penalties were assessed (although the precedents bore some similarities). [DISCUSSION — ... PRECEDENT]

q025. On the severity factor, how did SED's position and PG&E's position differ regarding customer notification?
Answer: SED claimed no notifications to any customers were made at de-energization (with a troubling number of customers affected by the lack of reporting), while PG&E argued it provided multiple prior notifications approximately 48 and 24 hours before de-energization and that more than 99 percent of the 650,000 customers de-energized during 2020 PSPS events received advance notice. [DISCUSSION — SEVERITY OF THE OFFENSE]
