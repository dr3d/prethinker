DECISION AND ORDER
During the early morning hours of November 12, 2022, a Precision Refractory Services, LLC, (PRS) employee was seriously injured when a scaffold component rolled off the upper level of the scaffold under which he had been working and struck his arm. The blow fractured his wrist. PRS reported the injury to OSHA and the Mobile, Alabama, Area Office dispatched a CSHO to the worksite to investigate the accident.
As a result of that investigation, the Secretary of Labor issued PRS a citation alleging two serious violations of regulations promulgated under § 5(a)(2) of the Act, 29 U.S.C. §§ 651-678 (2014). The Secretary alleged PRS violated 29 C.F.R. §§ 1926.451(f)(7) (Item 1a) and 1926.451(h)(2)(i)[1] (Item 1b), both of which address scaffold safety. The Secretary proposes a total penalty of $8702 for the violations. PRS timely contested the citation pursuant to § 10(c) of the Act.
The undersigned held a hearing on January 6, 2025, in Mobile, Alabama. The parties filed post-hearing briefs on March 7, 2025.[2]
For the reasons discussed below, Item 1a alleging a violation of § 1926.451(f)(7) is vacated, and Item 1b alleging a violation of § 1926.451(h)(2)(i) is affirmed with a penalty of $7000.

Findings of Fact
PRS performs refractory and fireproofing work. PRS has been in business since 2021 and employs approximately 100 individuals. In 2022, PRS had a contract to perform refractory work inside a furnace on the property of Ascend Performance Materials (Ascend) in Cantonment, Florida. The general contractor was Koch.[4] Koch contracted with Coastal Industrial Services, Inc. (Coastal) to erect, maintain, and dismantle the scaffold. The scaffolds were eight decks high with six feet between each deck. On the day of the accident, Domingo Rocha, a PRS foreman, and Jones Smith, a PRS laborer, were stacking brick at a height of approximately 12 to 14 feet above ground level. Jose Bustos, an onsite supervisor, had assigned the injured employee to work on the ground level below the scaffold. A scaffold component, described as a three-inch diameter pipe, fell off the scaffold and struck the injured employee. Andrew Castillo, the job superintendent, later transported the injured employee to a hospital where he was treated for an open fracture and laceration to his hand.
The Mobile Area Office assigned CSHO William Kitchen to inspect the worksite and investigate the accident.

DISCUSSION
To prove a violation the Secretary must show (1) the cited standard applies; (2) the employer failed to comply; (3) employees had access to the violative condition; and (4) the employer knew or could have known with reasonable diligence of the condition. JPC Grp., Inc., No. 05-1907, 2009 WL 2567337, at *2 (OSHRC Aug. 11, 2009).
Item 1a, Citation 1 (§ 1926.451(f)(7)): PRS conceded it did not have a competent person on site. The Secretary relied on hearsay statements from Koch and Coastal employees which the court excluded under Fed. R. Evid. 802/807. See Rivers v. United States, 777 F.3d 1306, 1312 (11th Cir. 2015). The Secretary failed to meet her burden. Item 1a is vacated.
Item 1b, Citation 1 (§ 1926.451(h)(2)(i)): The standard provides alternative means of protecting employees from falling objects. PRS did not barricade the area below and allowed employees to enter it. The court rejected PRS's argument that only the toeboard subpart applied. Supervisor Bustos and Foreman Rocha knew an employee worked below and that objects could fall; Bustos had identified a falling-object hazard in his November 11th and 12th JSAs and knew the scaffolds lacked toeboards. Item 1b is affirmed.

Characterization: A violation is serious under 29 U.S.C. § 666(k). The wrist fracture evinces the serious nature of the hazard. The violation is serious.

Penalty: The Secretary grouped the two violations and proposed a penalty of $8706. Because Item 1a is vacated, the court assesses a gravity-based penalty for Item 1b. The gravity is high. PRS is a small employer with no significant history but no documentary evidence of training. The court finds a penalty of $7000, approximately half the maximum penalty for a serious violation,[11] appropriate.

ORDER
Item 1a, Citation 1, is vacated; Item 1b, Citation 1, is affirmed as a serious violation with a penalty of $7000 assessed. SO ORDERED.
