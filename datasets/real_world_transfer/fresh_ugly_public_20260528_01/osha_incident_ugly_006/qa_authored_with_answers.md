q021. What four elements must the Secretary prove to establish a violation, and what case is cited for them?
Answer: (1) the cited standard applies; (2) the employer failed to comply with its terms; (3) employees had access to the violative condition; and (4) the employer knew or could have known with reasonable diligence of the condition. JPC Grp., Inc., No. 05-1907, 2009 WL 2567337, at *2 (OSHRC Aug. 11, 2009). [DISCUSSION — STANDARD]

q022. Why did the court vacate Item 1a?
Answer: Because the Secretary failed to present persuasive, admissible evidence that PRS employees altered the scaffold; the key third-party (Koch/Coastal) statements were excluded as inadmissible hearsay that did not qualify under the Rule 807 residual exception, and the remaining admitted evidence did not establish noncompliance. [DISCUSSION — ITEM 1A]

q023. What did PRS concede it did not have on site, relevant to Item 1a?
Answer: PRS conceded it did not have a competent person on site to oversee construction or alteration of the scaffolds. [DISCUSSION — ITEM 1A]

q024. How did the court treat the affirmative defense of unpreventable employee misconduct?
Answer: PRS did not address it in its post-hearing brief, so the court deemed it waived/abandoned (footnote 2). [FOOTNOTES] fn 2

q025. How is the PRS foreman's first name rendered in the stipulated facts versus in the findings of fact?
Answer: Inconsistently: Stipulated Fact 17 names him "Dominga Rocha," while the Findings of Fact refer to "Domingo Rocha," a PRS foreman. [BACKGROUND — STIPULATED FACTS] #17 vs [FINDINGS OF FACT]
