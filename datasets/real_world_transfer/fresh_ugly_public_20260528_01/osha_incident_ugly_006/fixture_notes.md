# Fixture notes — osha_incident_ugly_006

## Why this document is messy
It is an OSHRC administrative-law-judge decision arising from a workplace accident, blending an incident narrative with inspection metadata, a 19-item numbered stipulated-facts list, formal findings of fact, two separately analyzed citation items with different outcomes, an evidentiary (hearsay) ruling, a multi-factor penalty analysis, four footnotes, and an order. Multiple named individuals (a foreman, an onsite supervisor, a laborer, a job superintendent, a CSHO, and counsel for each side) and multiple companies (the respondent, the general contractor, the scaffolding contractor, and the site owner) appear, and the injured employee's name is redacted.

## What structures it pressures
- Identifier fidelity across many proper names, a docket number, CFR standards (§ 1926.451(f)(7); § 1926.451(h)(2)(i); the amended-from § 1926.451(f)(13)), and a precise worksite address.
- Date/sequence: an accident date, two inspection dates, an investigation-opened date, an amendment date, a hearing date, a briefing date, and a decision date.
- Table/list/coordinate: the 19 numbered stipulated facts function as discrete, individually citable rows.
- Source-state/limitation: the vacatur of Item 1a turns on what the record did NOT admissibly establish; the penalty turns on what good-faith evidence was absent; a defense was waived.
- Cross-section: the proposed penalty (stated differently in two sections) versus the assessed penalty in the order; the stipulated facts versus the findings of fact.
