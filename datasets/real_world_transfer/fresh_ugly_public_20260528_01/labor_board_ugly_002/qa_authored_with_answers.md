# QA with reference answers — labor_board_ugly_002

Source: PERB Decision No. 2262, Mutual Organization of Supervisors v. Fairfield-Suisun Unified School District, Case No. SF-CE-2806-E (May 8, 2012).

q001. What is the case number and the PERB decision number?
Answer: Case No. SF-CE-2806-E; PERB Decision No. 2262. [CAPTION / APPEARANCES]

q002. Who is the charging party and who is the respondent?
Answer: Charging Party: Mutual Organization of Supervisors (MOS); Respondent: Fairfield-Suisun Unified School District. [CAPTION / APPEARANCES]

q003. Who appeared for each party?
Answer: For MOS: Rick Lemke, Job Steward. For the District: Littler Mendelson by Bruce Sarchet, Attorney. [CAPTION / APPEARANCES]

q004. Which Board members decided the case, who authored the decision, and who concurred?
Answer: Martinez (Chair), Dowdin Calvillo and Huguenin (Members); Member Huguenin authored the decision, and Member Dowdin Calvillo wrote a concurrence (Chair Martinez joined the decision). [CAPTION / APPEARANCES]+[CONCURRENCE]

q005. What is the decision date?
Answer: May 8, 2012. [CAPTION / APPEARANCES]

q006. Who was the terminated employee, and what positions did he hold?
Answer: Keith A. Campbell, hired as a school bus driver in or about 2001 and promoted to transportation supervisor in 2003. [FINDINGS OF FACT — CAMPBELL TERMINATION]

q007. Which statute did the Board conclude the District violated?
Answer: EERA (Educational Employment Relations Act) section 3543.5(a), (b) and (c). [CONCLUSION]/[ORDER]

q008. On what date did MOS file its unfair practice charge, according to the Procedural History?
Answer: October 19, 2009. [PROCEDURAL HISTORY]

q009. On what date did the Office of the General Counsel issue the complaint?
Answer: November 12, 2009. [PROCEDURAL HISTORY]

q010. On what date was the formal hearing held?
Answer: August 24, 2010. [PROCEDURAL HISTORY]

q011. On what date did the Hearing Officer issue the proposed decision?
Answer: January 25, 2011. [PROCEDURAL HISTORY]

q012. On what date was Campbell terminated, and from what date does the backpay interest run?
Answer: Campbell was terminated on March 25, 2010; interest runs from the date of termination (March 25, 2010). [FINDINGS OF FACT — CAMPBELL TERMINATION]/[REMEDY]

q013. Place these events in chronological order: the formal hearing; MOS files its charge; the proposed decision; the complaint issues.
Answer: (1) MOS files its charge (October 19, 2009); (2) the complaint issues (November 12, 2009); (3) the formal hearing (August 24, 2010); (4) the proposed decision (January 25, 2011). [PROCEDURAL HISTORY]

q014. The decision gives the charge-filing date in two places. What two dates appear, and where does each appear?
Answer: "October 19, 2009" in the Procedural History, and "October 12, 2009" in the statute-of-limitations analysis (District Defenses). The two dates are inconsistent in the source. [PROCEDURAL HISTORY] vs [DISTRICT DEFENSES]

q015. What four elements must a charging party establish to prove a unilateral change, and what two decisions are cited?
Answer: (1) the employer took action to change policy; (2) the change concerns a matter within the scope of representation; (3) the action was taken without giving the exclusive representative notice or opportunity to bargain; (4) the action had a generalized effect or continuing impact. Walnut Valley Unified School District (1981) PERB Decision No. 160; Grant Joint Union High School District (1982) PERB Decision No. 196. [CONCLUSIONS OF LAW — UNILATERAL CHANGE TEST]

q016. List, in order, the progressive discipline steps set out in Article 11.
Answer: Verbal reprimand; Written reprimand; Discipline less than dismissal; Dismissal. [FINDINGS OF FACT — PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE]

q017. What are the two exceptions to progressive discipline in Article 11?
Answer: (1) where the unit member's conduct endangers the safety of students, employees, or District property (the Superintendent or designee may suspend with pay rather than follow the progressive steps); and (2) in cases of emergency, for reasons stated in writing and sent to MOS, the Superintendent or designee may skip one or more steps. [FINDINGS OF FACT — PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE]

q018. What federal regulation is cited as requiring random drug/alcohol testing of commercial drivers?
Answer: 49 C.F.R. § 382 et seq. (specifically § 382.211). [FINDINGS OF FACT — FEDERAL DRUG TESTING]

q019. What is the number and title of the District regulation that established the drug-testing procedure?
Answer: District Administrative Regulation 4112.41, "Drug and Alcohol Testing For School Bus Drivers." [FINDINGS OF FACT — FEDERAL DRUG TESTING / ADOPTION OF PROCEDURE]

q020. List the three cease-and-desist provisions of the Order (Part A).
Answer: (1) establishing and enforcing a zero tolerance policy with respect to any MOS unit employee's alleged refusal to submit to a random controlled substance and/or alcohol test without meeting and negotiating with MOS; (2) interfering with the rights of employees to be represented by MOS; and (3) denying MOS its right to represent its unit members guaranteed by EERA. [ORDER] Part A

q021. Why did the Board reject the District's "waiver by contract" defense?
Answer: Because waivers of the right to bargain are disfavored and must be shown by "clear and unmistakable" language, with the matter "fully discussed" and the union having "consciously yielded"; the safety-exception language of Article 11 did not effectuate such a waiver. [DISTRICT DEFENSES]

q022. Why did the Board find MOS's charge timely under the six-month limitations period?
Answer: Because MOS first learned of the District's application of the zero tolerance provision to MOS unit members on May 15, 2009 (Campbell's termination notice), and filed its charge within six months of that date. [DISTRICT DEFENSES]

q023. On what basis did Member Dowdin Calvillo decline to join part of the majority's reasoning?
Answer: She found it unnecessary to decide whether Campbell's conduct fell within the Article 11 "safety" exception, because the District did not rely on Article 11 in terminating Campbell (it relied on the zero tolerance policy) and only claimed the safety exception after the charge was filed. [CONCURRENCE — DOWDIN CALVILLO]

q024. Within how many days must the District offer Campbell reinstatement, and does the decision state this timeframe consistently?
Answer: The Order (Part B.2) requires action within fifteen (15) days of service of the final decision; the narrative Remedy section instead says "within thirty (30) days of the effective date of this order." The two timeframes are inconsistent in the source. [REMEDY] vs [ORDER] Part B.2

q025. What conduct was Campbell disciplined for, and what did the Board conclude about whether it fell within the Article 11 "safety" exception?
Answer: Campbell was disciplined for refusing to comply with a directive from a testing technician (refusing to disrobe below the waist to permit inspection for a prosthetic device during a random drug test). The Board concluded this conduct fell outside both Article 11 exceptions: it did not threaten or expose to danger students, employees, or District property, and did not constitute an emergency. [FINDINGS OF FACT — CAMPBELL TERMINATION]+[CONCLUSIONS OF LAW]
