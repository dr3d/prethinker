# Fixture notes — sec_material_event_ugly_006

## Why this document is messy
It is a single Form 8-K that simultaneously reports several distinct corporate-officer events, layering multiple roles, multiple individuals, and multiple kinds of dates. Within one Item 5.02 it covers an incoming CEO (effective in the future), a new Board Chair (effective on a different date), a retiring incumbent who held three titles at once, and a sitting CFO who takes on an interim CEO role for a bridging period. The cover page packs several identifier systems into small tables, and the filing distinguishes "filed" Item 5.02/9.01 content from "furnished" Item 7.01 content.

## What structures it pressures
- Date disambiguation: Date of Report (Dec 31, 2025) vs. the CEO effective/"Commencement Date" (no later than March 31, 2026) vs. the Chair effective date (Jan 1, 2026) vs. the Interim-CEO start (Jan 1, 2026) vs. the Offer Letter date (Dec 23, 2025) vs. the future equity-grant date (Jan 1, 2027) vs. the signature date.
- Identifier fidelity: Commission File Number (001-36863), IRS EIN (13-3060083), trading symbol (CABO), par value ($0.01).
- Role allocation: the retiring incumbent's three titles (Chair, President, CEO) are split among three people, requiring a reader to track who gets which.
- Table/list extraction: the three-row exhibit table and the five-part compensation list, plus the committee roster.
- Source-state/limitation: "no arrangement or understanding," "no family relationships," the Section 18 "furnished not filed" qualifier, and the conditional Foregone-Bonus refund clause.
- Cross-section reasoning: the Interim-CEO term is defined only by reference to the separately defined "Commencement Date"; the Offer Letter's date and its effectiveness are stated in different places.

## Known ambiguities in the source
- The CEO appointment is "effective no later than March 31, 2026," not a fixed date; the exact Commencement Date is open.
- Two different events share the January 1, 2026 effective date (Meduski as Chair; Koetje as Interim CEO), which can invite conflation.
- The source contains a verbatim typo ("Nominating and Governance and Committee"); answers follow the published wording.
