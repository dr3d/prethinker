BEFORE THE PUBLIC SERVICE COMMISSION OF UTAH
Application of Rocky Mountain Power for Authority to Increase its Retail Electric Utility Service Rates in Utah and for Approval of its Proposed Electric Service Schedules and Electric Service Regulations.
DOCKET NO. 24-035-04.
ORDER MEMORIALIZING DECISION.
ISSUED: June 24, 2025.

[BACKGROUND]
On December 10, 2024, Rocky Mountain Power (RMP) and the Utah Large Customer Group (UTLCG, collectively with RMP, the "Settling Parties") filed a Partial Settlement Stipulation ("Settlement"), wherein UTLCG agreed to withdraw certain recommendations it raised in this proceeding, RMP agreed to provide individual and power quality data on a quarterly basis to certain customers receiving service under Schedule 9 of its tariff for a period of one year, and the Settling Parties agreed to meet after one year to discuss resolution of any identified chronic, high-impact, or high-risk issues.
The Public Service Commission (PSC) heard testimony regarding the Settlement during the Phase I hearings, specifically on December 16, 2024. RMP and UTLCG provided testimony in support of the Settlement. No party testified in opposition to the PSC's approval of the Settlement or otherwise opposed it.
After hearing the testimony, the PSC found the Settlement to be "just and reasonable in result" and "in the public interest." [Footnote 1: Dec. 16, 2024, Hr'g Tr. at 1364:22-1365:7.] The PSC also indicated it would memorialize this decision in its final order. However, in the lengthy order the PSC issued on April 25, 2025, addressing the myriad issues raised in this docket, the PSC neglected to include language memorializing its approval of the Settlement. The PSC apologizes for the oversight.
On May 22, 2025, RMP and UTLCG filed a Motion for a Written Order Memorializing the Approval of the Settlement ("Motion"). No party filed a response or otherwise objected to the Motion.

[DISCUSSION]
Stipulated agreements that resolve matters pending before the PSC are statutorily encouraged. Utah Code § 54-7-1. The PSC may adopt "any settlement proposal entered into by two or more . . . parties" provided the evidence supports the PSC finding the settlement proposal is just and reasonable in result. Id. at § 54-7-1(2)-(3).

[ORDER]
As the PSC announced at hearing, the PSC found the evidence in the record supported its finding that the Settlement is just and reasonable in result. Consistent with the PSC's decision at hearing, this order memorializes the PSC's approval of the Settlement.

Pursuant to Utah Code Ann. §§ 63G-4-301 and 54-7-15, a party may seek agency review or rehearing of this written order by filing a request for review or rehearing with the PSC within 30 days after the issuance of the order. Responses to a request for agency review or rehearing must be filed within 15 days of the filing of the request for review or rehearing. If the PSC fails to grant a request for review or rehearing within 30 days after the filing of a request for review or rehearing, it is deemed denied. Judicial review of the PSC's final agency action may be obtained by filing a Petition for Review with the Utah Supreme Court within 30 days after final agency action.
