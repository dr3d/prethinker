## Section 1. Recall Bulletin (Source Excerpt)

The following is reproduced from the manufacturer recall bulletin received
2026-04-12.

> **MEDIVOLT PHARMA SYSTEMS — RECALL NOTICE MV-2026-04**
> **Issued:** 2026-04-12
> **Product:** Medivolt MP-450 ambulatory infusion pump
> **Scope (initial):** All MP-450 units with serial numbers in the range
> 4501-AA-100100 through 4501-AA-100200, manufactured in batch B-2024-Q3.
> **Reason:** Intermittent failure of the secondary occlusion sensor under
> high-viscosity infusates. Failure rate observed in field returns: 0.7 per
> 1,000 hours of use.
> **Required action:** Clinics must quarantine in-scope devices until
> manufacturer technician completes the in-clinic verification procedure
> MV-VP-04-A. Devices in active patient use may be retained under the
> patient-use exception protocol; see Section 4.
> **Manufacturer contact:** K. Halberg, Regional Liaison, Eastern Network.

A supersession notice was issued by the manufacturer on 2026-04-16. It is
reproduced as Section 2.

## Section 2. Recall Supersession Notice (Source Excerpt)

## Section 7. Unresolved Item — Firmware Inquiry

The manufacturer's recall bulletin and supersession notice both address
the secondary occlusion sensor module. Neither addresses whether MP-450
units running firmware version 4.2.1 require an additional correction
even when the sensor module passes the MV-VP-04-A procedure.

K. Halberg (Manufacturer Regional Liaison) was queried on this point by
email on 2026-04-17. Halberg's reply, also dated 2026-04-17, states:

> "We are awaiting determination from Medivolt engineering on whether
> firmware 4.2.1 introduces an additional failure pathway. Anticipated
> response by 2026-04-25."

As of packet compilation (2026-04-19), no determination has been received.
This item is open. The missing evidence is the Medivolt engineering
determination on firmware 4.2.1.

The MP-450 firmware version on each device is recorded in the device's
service plate but is not duplicated in the inventory table; the firmware
state of network devices is not addressed by this packet.

## Section 10. Glossary

- **Active**: device is in inventory and approved for clinical use.
- **Active (exception)**: device is in inventory, would otherwise be
  quarantined per the recall, and is in clinical use under written
  authority of the network medical director.
- **Quarantined**: device is in inventory, physically secured, and
  removed from clinical service.
- **Withdrawn (inventory)**: device is no longer carried on the network's
  active inventory roll. This status is administered by the receiving
  site and may be the result of decommission, loss, or transfer.
- **Repair Verified — Yes**: manufacturer technician has completed the
  MV-VP-04-A procedure and the device passed.
- **Repair Verified — No**: manufacturer technician has not completed
  the MV-VP-04-A procedure on the device.
- **Repair Verified — Scheduled**: a verification visit is on the
  manufacturer technician calendar but has not occurred.
- **Repair Verified — N/A**: the device is administratively out of scope
  for the recall, whether by supersession, withdrawal, or any other
  cause; the verification procedure does not apply.

End of packet.
