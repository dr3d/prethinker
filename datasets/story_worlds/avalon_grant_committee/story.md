# The Avalon Grant Committee

## Source Document: Grant Eligibility Rules, Applicant Records, and Committee Deliberation Notes

### SECTION A — Avalon Foundation Grant Eligibility Rules (2026 Cycle)

**Rule 1 — Residency.**
An applicant must have been a resident of Avalon County for at least 2
continuous years at the time of application to be eligible. Residency is
determined by primary address on tax records.

**Rule 2 — Prior Funding.**
An applicant who has received an Avalon Foundation grant in any of the
preceding 3 fiscal years is ineligible for a new grant, unless the
applicant's prior grant was returned unused before the end of its fiscal
year. A returned grant resets the waiting period.

**Rule 3 — Organizational Status.**
Grants may be awarded to individuals or to registered nonprofit organizations.
A for-profit entity is ineligible. An individual who applies on behalf of a
for-profit entity is also ineligible, even if the individual personally meets
all other criteria.

**Rule 4 — Project Category.**
Grants are restricted to projects in the following categories: Community
Health, Arts & Culture, Environmental Stewardship, and Youth Development.
A project that spans multiple eligible categories is permitted. A project
that includes any ineligible component is ineligible in its entirety, unless
the ineligible component can be severed and the applicant submits a revised
budget excluding the severed component.

**Rule 5 — Matching Funds.**
Projects requesting more than $25,000 must demonstrate matching funds from
a non-Avalon source equal to at least 30% of the requested amount. Projects
requesting $25,000 or less are exempt from the matching-funds requirement.

**Rule 6 — Committee Quorum.**
A grant may be approved only by a vote of the full Grant Committee at a
meeting where at least 4 of the 5 members are present. A vote taken without
quorum is void.

**Rule 7 — Conflict of Interest.**
A committee member who has a financial or personal relationship with an
applicant must recuse from deliberation and voting on that application. A
recused member does not count toward the quorum calculation for that vote.
If recusal reduces the available members below 4, the vote must be deferred
until a temporary replacement is appointed by the Foundation Director.

**Rule 8 — Conditional Approval.**
The committee may grant conditional approval, meaning the grant is approved
subject to the applicant satisfying one or more conditions (such as
submitting a revised budget, providing matching-fund documentation, or
demonstrating residency) within 30 days. If the conditions are not met
within 30 days, the approval expires automatically. The Foundation Director
may grant a single 15-day extension.

**Rule 9 — Appeal.**
An applicant whose application is denied may appeal within 10 business days
of receiving the denial. The appeal is heard by a separate Review Panel,
not by the Grant Committee. The Review Panel may uphold the denial, reverse
it, or remand it to the committee with instructions. The Review Panel's
decision is final.

**Rule 10 — Fiscal Year Boundary.**
All grants must be disbursed within the fiscal year in which they are
approved (July 1 to June 30). An approved grant that is not disbursed by
June 30 lapses automatically. The committee cannot extend the disbursement
deadline beyond June 30.

### SECTION C — Committee Deliberation Notes (Meeting of April 3, 2026)

**Committee members present:** Chair Miriam Sokolova, Dr. James Worthington,
Lucia Bianchi, Kenji Oda. **Absent:** Franklin Dubois (travel).

**Quorum check:** 4 of 5 members present. Quorum met.

**Item 1: Clara Mendes — "Wellness Walks"**

Discussion: Mendes received a grant in FY2024, which is within the preceding
3 fiscal years (FY2024, FY2025, FY2026). The grant was not returned unused.
Under Rule 2, she is ineligible.

Worthington asks: "If FY2024 means the fiscal year starting July 1, 2023,
and the current fiscal year is FY2026 starting July 1, 2025, then the
preceding 3 fiscal years are FY2025, FY2024, and FY2023. FY2024 falls
within that window. Is that correct?"

Chair Sokolova confirms: "Yes. She is ineligible under Rule 2."

Vote: Denied unanimously (4-0). Mendes notified April 5, 2026.

**Item 2: Northshore Youth Alliance — "Code Campfire"**

Discussion: NYA received a grant in FY2025, which is within the preceding
3 fiscal years. However, $12,000 was returned unused before the end of
FY2025. Under Rule 2, a returned grant resets the waiting period.

Bianchi asks: "The rule says 'returned unused.' NYA returned $12,000 of a
$40,000 grant. Does a partial return count as a 'returned grant' that
resets the waiting period, or must the entire grant be returned?"

The committee cannot agree on the interpretation. Chair Sokolova notes that
the rule text says "the applicant's prior grant was returned unused" without
specifying whole or partial.

**Action: The committee defers the eligibility determination on NYA pending
a written interpretation from the Foundation Director on whether partial
return satisfies Rule 2.**

Foundation Director's written interpretation (April 8, 2026): "A partial
return does not constitute a 'returned grant' under Rule 2. The phrase
'prior grant was returned unused' requires the full grant amount to have
been returned. NYA used $28,000 of the $40,000 grant; this is not a return
of the grant. NYA remains subject to the 3-fiscal-year waiting period and
is ineligible for the 2026 cycle."

NYA notified of denial April 10, 2026.

**Item 3: Desmond Achebe — "Pollinator Corridors"**

Discussion: Achebe has been resident for 1 year 2 months, which is less
than the 2-year continuous residency requirement. Under Rule 1, he is
ineligible on residency grounds.

Additionally, Oda raises Rule 3: "The project is operated through Greenway
Landscapes LLC, a for-profit entity. Even though Achebe is applying as an
individual, Rule 3 says an individual who applies on behalf of a for-profit
entity is ineligible."

Achebe's application letter states: "While Greenway Landscapes will provide
equipment and labor for the project, the grant funds would go directly to
the county parks department for native plant materials. Greenway is
providing in-kind support, not receiving grant funds."

Worthington: "Ist er disqualifiziert, weil er als Einzelperson im Namen
eines gewinnorientierten Unternehmens bewirbt, oder bewirbt er sich als
Einzelperson, die die Unterstützung eines Unternehmens nutzt? Die Regel
ist zweideutig."
[Translation: Is he disqualified because he's applying as an individual on
behalf of a for-profit entity, or is he applying as an individual who
happens to use a company's support? The rule is ambiguous.]

Chair Sokolova: "He is clearly ineligible on residency (Rule 1). We deny
on residency without reaching the Rule 3 question."

Vote: Denied on residency grounds (4-0). The Rule 3 for-profit question is
noted but not decided. Achebe notified April 5, 2026.

**Item 4: Anya Petrov — "Roots & Rhythms"**

Discussion: Petrov meets residency (2.5 years). No prior funding. Individual
applicant.

Category question: The project spans Community Health (garden), Arts &
Culture (music series), and includes a commercial food-truck bazaar.
Bianchi: "The food-truck bazaar is a commercial activity. Under Rule 4, a
project that includes any ineligible component is ineligible in its
entirety, unless the component can be severed."

Oda: "Can the food-truck component be severed?"

Petrov has not submitted a revised budget excluding the food-truck component.

**Action: Conditional approval under Rule 8 — Petrov must submit a revised
budget excluding the food-truck bazaar component within 30 days (by May 3,
2026) and provide documentation of matching funds from the Avalon Arts
Council.**

Matching funds question: Petrov requests $30,000 and declares $10,000 in
matching funds. Rule 5 requires matching funds of at least 30% of the
requested amount for projects over $25,000. 30% of $30,000 = $9,000.
Petrov's $10,000 exceeds the $9,000 minimum, but only if the matching-fund
documentation is verified.

Vote: Conditional approval (4-0). Conditions: (1) revised budget excluding
food-truck bazaar, (2) verified matching-fund documentation from Avalon
Arts Council. Deadline: May 3, 2026.

**May 1, 2026:** Petrov submits a revised budget excluding the food-truck
bazaar. The revised project total is $26,500 (reduced from $30,000).
However, the revised request amount is $26,500, not $30,000. The matching-
fund requirement at 30% of $26,500 is $7,950, and the $10,000 commitment
from the Avalon Arts Council exceeds this.

**May 1, 2026:** Avalon Arts Council provides a signed commitment letter
confirming the $10,000 match.

**May 5, 2026:** Foundation Director reviews the conditional satisfaction.
The revised budget was submitted on May 1, within the 30-day deadline.
Both conditions are met. The grant is approved at the revised amount of
$26,500.

**Item 5: Sunrise Community Land Trust — "Affordable Housing Navigator"**

Discussion: SCLT meets residency. SCLT received a grant in FY2024, which
is within the 3-year window. The grant was used in full and was not returned.
Under Rule 2, SCLT is ineligible.

However, Bianchi recuses herself from this vote because she is a board
member of SCLT. After recusal, 3 members remain (Sokolova, Worthington,
Oda).

Rule 7: The quorum requirement is 4 members, and recused members do not
count toward quorum. With 3 available members and quorum at 4, the vote
cannot proceed.

**Action: Vote deferred until the Foundation Director appoints a temporary
replacement for Bianchi on this item.**

Foundation Director appoints Dr. Rosa Gutierrez (Avalon County Community
College) as temporary member on April 10, 2026. Available members:
Sokolova, Worthington, Oda, Gutierrez = 4. Quorum met.

**Reconvened vote (April 15, 2026):** SCLT denied on Rule 2 grounds (4-0).

However: SCLT's FY2024 grant was disbursed in September 2023, and the
current fiscal year is FY2026 (beginning July 1, 2025). SCLT argues that
the "preceding 3 fiscal years" should be counted as FY2026 (current),
FY2025, and FY2024 — making FY2024 the oldest year in the window and
FY2023 (when the grant was actually disbursed) outside the window.

Sokolova: "The rule says 'received a grant in any of the preceding 3 fiscal
years.' The grant was awarded in FY2024. The disbursement date is
September 2023, which is within the FY2024 period (July 2023 – June 2024).
FY2024 is within the 3-year window regardless of the disbursement date."

SCLT notified of denial April 17, 2026.

**April 27, 2026:** SCLT files an appeal with the Review Panel within 10
business days of the denial notification (April 17 to April 27 = 8 business
days, within the 10-business-day window).

**May 15, 2026:** Review Panel decision — denial upheld. The Review Panel
agrees with Sokolova's interpretation that FY2024 is within the 3-year
window. The decision is final.
