## 1. Cycle context

The Northeast Regional Arts Foundation (NERAF) administers the Emerging Artists Fellowship, an annual $500,000 program awarding $25,000 fellowships to early-career artists. The 2026 cycle has a funding threshold of **75.0/100** on the panel's weighted scoring rubric. As of packet date, fellowships have been awarded as detailed in the cycle fiscal ledger (§10).

## 2. Panel composition and roster

The 2026 review panel consists of five voting members and one alternate:

- Dr. Aida Marchetti, Panel Chair (curator, Westmont Center)
- Marcus Chen (composer)
- Imelda Tovar (photographer)
- Felipe Aragon (sculptor)
- Beatrice Rooke (poet)
- Wenjing Lu (alternate; printmaker)

## 3. Original panel meeting — March 14, 2026

The panel convened in-person on March 14, 2026, 09:00–17:30 EDT, to review 47 applications. Each application was scored independently by all five voting members along four weighted criteria:

- Artistic Merit (max 40)
- Career Stage (max 20)
- Project Plan (max 25)
- Community Impact (max 15)

Panelist totals are summed per panelist, and the application's score is the arithmetic mean of all eligible panelist totals; the threshold for funding is 75.0.

## 6. Score correction — May 2, 2026 audit memo

On May 2, 2026, during a routine post-cycle audit of the original score sheets, Panel Coordinator Lin Whitley identified a discrepancy in Felipe Aragon's score for application NERAF-EAF-2026-0147:

- Official tally entered into the panel's scoring system on March 14: Artistic Merit = **26**.
- Aragon's signed bench-note score sheet (handwritten, signed by Aragon at the panel meeting): Artistic Merit = **38**.

Aragon's other scores (Career Stage 12, Project Plan 17, Community Impact 11) match between the bench notes and the official tally. The discrepancy is isolated to a single value in a single field. Aragon, contacted by Whitley on May 2, confirmed that 38 was his intended score and that the bench-note value is correct.

Corrected score for Aragon: 38 + 12 + 17 + 11 = **78**.

## 7. Counsel note on §IV.B.3 — May 4, 2026

Counsel Beatriz Romero, Esq., reviewed the May 2 audit memo and issued the following note on May 4, 2026:

"Per NERAF Bylaws §IV.B.3 (Correction of Scrivener's Error), a transcription error in the score-tally entry that is documented by a contemporaneous, signed panelist bench note may be corrected in-cycle without convening a new panel. The correction shall be reflected in the official score sheet of record. The correction does not itself adjudicate the underlying funding question; the panel or reconsideration docket retains authority over award determinations."

## 9. Recusal scenario per §IV.B.5

Per NERAF Bylaws §IV.B.5: "If a panelist is determined to have been ineligible to score, the corresponding score is removed and the average is recomputed on remaining panelists; the alternate panelist may be invited to score on reconsideration."

If Tovar is determined to have been ineligible to score on application NERAF-EAF-2026-0147:

- Tovar's score (76) is removed.
- Remaining four panelists' corrected totals: 79 + 70 + 78 + 74 = 301; recomputed average = 301 / 4 = **75.25**.
- 75.25 still exceeds the 75.0 threshold.
- Alternate Wenjing Lu may be invited to score on reconsideration; if she does, the average would be recomputed including her score. The bylaws do not require Lu to score; that is at the docket's discretion.
