## 1. Operative rules

The following rules govern eligibility and grant amount for the 2026 Round 1 program.

### 1.1 Eligibility thresholds (§2)

**§2.1 — Operating-history requirement.** An eligible applicant must be an incorporated business that has operated continuously in Borden County for at least twelve (12) months as of the application date. The clock runs from the date of Borden County incorporation, subject to §4.1.

**§2.2 — Revenue ceiling.** Gross revenue in the applicant's most recently completed fiscal year must be no more than two million five hundred thousand dollars ($2,500,000).

**§2.3 — Revenue floor.** Gross revenue in the applicant's most recently completed fiscal year must be at least fifty thousand dollars ($50,000).

**§2.4 — Workforce range.** As of the application date, the applicant must have a full-time-equivalent (FTE) employee count of at least two (2) and no more than twenty-five (25). The lower bound may be waived under §4.2.

**§2.5 — Tax-compliance threshold.** As of the application date, the applicant must have no outstanding state or county tax liability greater than one thousand dollars ($1,000). A violation may be cured under §4.3.

### 1.2 Bonus rules (§3)

**§3.1 — Hospitality bonus.** Applicants whose primary business falls within North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) major group 72 (Accommodation and Food Services) receive a bonus of twenty-five percent (+25%) on the base grant.

**§3.2 — Underrepresented-owner credit.** Applicants whose majority owner qualifies as women-owned, minority-owned, or veteran-owned per the program's documentation standard (§6) receive a bonus of fifteen percent (+15%) on the base grant.

**§3.3 — Bonus computation.** Bonuses under §3.1 and §3.2 are applied to the base grant defined in §5.1; they stack additively.

**§3.4 — Combined bonus cap.** The combined bonus from §3.1 and §3.2 may not exceed thirty-five percent (+35%) of the base grant, regardless of the applicant's qualifications under both rules.

### 1.3 Exceptions (§4)

**§4.1 — Cross-county relocation.** An applicant that relocated to Borden County from an adjacent county within the last twelve months may substitute the prior county's continuous-incorporation date for purposes of §2.1, provided the relocation was completed on or before 2025-12-31. The §2.1 clock then runs continuously across the relocation.

**§4.2 — Pandemic-recovery waiver for hospitality sole proprietors.** The §2.4 lower bound (2 FTE) is waived for applicants whose primary business is sole proprietorship in NAICS major group 72 (Accommodation and Food Services), provided the applicant can document at least eighteen (18) months of continuous operation. §4.2 activates only after §2.1 is satisfied at the higher 18-month threshold; satisfying §2.1 at the baseline 12-month threshold is not sufficient to invoke §4.2.

**§4.3 — Tax-liability cure.** An applicant in violation of §2.5 may cure the violation by entering into a payment plan with the Borden County Tax Office at least thirty (30) days before the application deadline. Once the applicant is on a payment plan and documentation is on file, §2.5 is treated as satisfied for the round in which the cure was completed.

### 1.4 Base grant (§5)

**§5.1 — Base grant amount.** Each approved application receives a base grant of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000) before bonuses under §3.

### 1.5 Underrepresented-owner documentation (§6)

**§6 — Documentation standard.** An applicant claiming an underrepresented-owner credit under §3.2 must provide the program with documentation evidencing the majority owner's qualifying status. Acceptable documentation is enumerated in the program's intake form and is not reproduced here. The Program Officer's determination on §3.2 status is recorded per application below.

| Gross revenue (FY2025) | $740,000 |
| Outstanding tax liability | $0 |
| Application date | 2026-04-12 |

**Determination.** All §2 thresholds are satisfied: §2.1 (operating history 25 months, well over 12), §2.2 ($740,000 < $2,500,000), §2.3 ($740,000 > $50,000), §2.4 (4 FTE within 2–25), §2.5 (no liability). §3.1 applies (NAICS 722 ⊂ NAICS 72). §3.2 applies (women-owned). **Grant amount.** $25,000 × (1 + 0.35) = **$33,750.** Status: **approved**.

The Tax Office has not transmitted payment-plan documentation for Cale Industrial as of packet close. The applicant claims a payment plan was entered on 2026-03-28 (within the §4.3 window) but documentation has not been received by the Program Office.

**Status: pending determination.** Final eligibility depends on Tax Office confirmation. If §4.3 applies, applicant is approved at base grant only ($25,000; no §3.1 or §3.2 qualification). If §4.3 does not apply, applicant is denied on §2.5.
