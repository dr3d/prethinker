# The Veridia-9 Supply Chain & Patent Integrity Dispute

## Source Document: Combined Record of Pharmaceutical Liability Dispute, Lab Audits, Distribution Logs, Patent Tribunal Proceedings, and Regulatory Correspondence

### SECTION A -- Backing and Liability Structure

#### A.1 -- The Product
The Veridia-9 (V9) is a synthetic biological catalyst, Batch V9-402, manufactured in 2024, cold-chain sensitive, registered with the Federal Drug Registry (FDR), managed by Biogenix Systems Ltd (Singapore), and beneficially owned by Aether Bio-Ventures SA (Luxembourg). The product's Regulatory Integrity Record (RIR) was current as of September 20, 2025 (annual audit, no safety violations outstanding).

#### A.2 -- Product Liability Policy
The V9 production is covered under a Supply Chain Liability (SCL) policy, Policy No. SCL-V9-2025-0812, written on the International Bio-Pharma Clauses (IBC) 2012 form. The policy period runs from January 1, 2025, to December 31, 2025. The agreed insured value per major batch is USD 42,000,000.

The lead underwriter is Zenith Underwriting 1902 at Lloyd's (50% line). Following underwriters are Northern Bio-Secure Ltd, Stockholm (30% line) and Mediterranean Pharma Mutual, Valletta (20% line).

The policy contains a Compliance Clause requiring the batch to maintain storage within a +/-2 degrees C variance throughout the transit period. Loss of compliance or failure to report a variance within 48 hours suspends cover until a re-certification survey is completed.

#### A.3 -- Reinsurance Layers
Zenith Underwriting is re-insured for 80% of its exposure under a Quota Share Treaty with Global Re-Ventures (GRV). The treaty requires "immediate notice of any event likely to exceed 10% of the insured value."

### SECTION B -- The Contamination Incident

#### B.1 -- The Event
On October 15, 2025, during transit between the Zurich manufacturing facility and the Rotterdam distribution hub, a refrigeration failure occurred in Transport Unit T-88. Initial sensor data suggested a 4 degrees C rise for 12 minutes.

Initial report (October 16, 09:00 UTC): Unit T-88 driver, L. Vance, reported a manual reset of the cooling system at 04:15 UTC. He claimed the temperature stayed within safety limits.

Revised report (October 18): Data extraction from the secondary "black box" sensor shows the temperature breach began at 03:55 UTC and lasted until 04:30 UTC (35 minutes), peaking at +6.2 degrees C.

#### B.2 -- Competing Laboratory Accounts
**Biogenix Internal Lab (Nov 2):** Claims the V9-402 molecular structure remained stable despite the +6 degrees C spike. They cite a "rapid recovery" protocol.

**Independent Audit (Nov 10 - Helix Analytics):** Claims the "rapid recovery" protocol was not approved by the FDR. They found significant protein degradation in 15% of the samples. Helix concludes the batch is "commercially non-viable."

### SECTION C -- Distribution and Breach

On October 22, 2025, while the contamination dispute was ongoing, Biogenix authorized the release of 5,000 units from Batch V9-402 to the African regional hub (Nairobi).

The FDR issued a "Hold and Sequestration" order on October 21 at 23:30 UTC. Biogenix claims they did not receive the order until October 23. The Nairobi hub manager, T. Obasi, confirmed receipt of the units on October 24.

### SECTION D -- Patent Integrity and Trading Warranty

The V9 product is subject to a Trading Warranty: it must not be sold in jurisdictions where a "Stay of Patent" is active. On October 1, 2025, the Nairobi Patent Tribunal issued a temporary stay on V9 following a challenge by a local generic manufacturer.

Zenith (Underwriters) argue that by shipping to Nairobi on October 22, Biogenix breached the Trading Warranty, discharging the underwriters from liability for any subsequent losses, including the contamination claim.

### SECTION E -- Financial Positions and Deductibles

The policy has a USD 2,500,000 "Self-Insured Retention" (deductible). Biogenix claims the total loss for Batch V9-402 is USD 38,000,000 (production cost plus lost contracts). Zenith argues the loss is limited to USD 12,000,000 (market value of raw materials only, as the batch was already "at risk" due to the prior October 15 failure).

### SECTION F -- Corrections and Clarifications

**Correction 1 (Nov 12, 2025):** The initial driver report stated the reset occurred at 04:15 UTC. The VDR shows the actual power-loss event began at 03:55 UTC. The 04:15 time was the driver's first attempt to restart the engine, not the cooling failure itself.

**Correction 2 (Nov 25, 2025):** Helix Analytics initially stated degradation was "approximately 22%." Their final report corrects this to "15.4% based on high-resolution chromatography."

**Clarification 1 (Jan 5, 2026):** Zenith clarifies that the "immediate notice" to reinsurers was sent on October 20, 2025, five days after the event. GRV (Reinsurers) are disputing this as "late notice."
