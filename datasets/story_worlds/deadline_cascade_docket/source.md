# Millhaven Regional Water Board — Enforcement Docket 2025-EF-0093

## Docket Summary

On June 2, 2025, the Millhaven Regional Water Board issued a Notice of Violation (NOV) to Granger Industrial Park LLC for exceeding permitted discharge limits at Outfall 7 during the period April 14–April 28, 2025. The NOV triggered a cascade of response deadlines, compliance milestones, and appeal windows governed by the Board's Enforcement Procedures Manual (EPM), Revision 12.

This record documents the docket timeline through September 30, 2025.

## Enforcement Procedures Manual — Relevant Deadline Rules

**Rule D-1: Response Deadline.** The respondent must file a written response within 30 calendar days of NOV issuance. If the 30th day falls on a weekend or Board holiday, the deadline extends to the next business day.

**Rule D-2: Compliance Plan Deadline.** If the respondent's response includes a request for a compliance schedule, the respondent must submit a Compliance Plan within 45 calendar days of the response filing date.

**Rule D-3: Board Review Period.** The Board has 21 calendar days from Compliance Plan receipt to approve, reject, or request revision. If no action is taken within 21 days, the plan is deemed provisionally approved.

**Rule D-4: Appeal Window.** The respondent may appeal a Board rejection within 14 business days of the rejection date. Business days exclude weekends and Board holidays. The Board's holiday calendar lists July 4, September 1 (Labor Day), and November 27 (Thanksgiving) as 2025 holidays.

**Rule D-5: Penalty Assessment Deadline.** If no compliant response is filed by the response deadline, the Board must issue a Penalty Assessment within 60 calendar days of the missed deadline. The penalty clock does not start until the response deadline passes.

**Rule D-6: Tolling.** Filing a timely appeal tolls the penalty assessment clock for the duration of the appeal. The clock resumes on the calendar day after the appeal decision is issued.

**Rule D-7: Extension Requests.** The respondent may request a single 15-calendar-day extension of the response deadline. The request must be filed at least 7 calendar days before the original deadline. The Executive Director may grant or deny without Board vote.

**Rule D-8: Compliance Plan Revision.** If the Board requests revision, the respondent has 14 calendar days from the revision request to resubmit. The Board then has another 21 calendar days to act on the revised plan.

## Docket Timeline

**June 2, 2025**: NOV issued to Granger Industrial Park LLC. Response deadline: July 2, 2025 (30 calendar days).

**June 20, 2025**: Granger filed an extension request under Rule D-7. The original response deadline is July 2; the request was filed 12 days before the deadline, satisfying the 7-day advance requirement.

**June 23, 2025**: Executive Director Mira Tan granted the extension. New response deadline: July 17, 2025 (15 calendar days added to the original July 2 deadline).

**July 17, 2025**: Granger filed its written response. The response included a request for a compliance schedule. Compliance Plan deadline under Rule D-2: 45 calendar days from July 17 = August 31, 2025. August 31 is a Sunday. The EPM does not contain a weekend-rollover rule for compliance plan deadlines (Rule D-1's rollover applies only to the response deadline). The compliance plan deadline is August 31 as stated.

**August 29, 2025 (Friday)**: Granger submitted its Compliance Plan, two days before the August 31 deadline. Board review period under Rule D-3: 21 calendar days from August 29 = September 19, 2025.

**September 15, 2025**: The Board rejected the Compliance Plan. Rejection reason: "The proposed monitoring frequency is insufficient. Weekly sampling is required; the plan proposes monthly." Appeal window under Rule D-4: 14 business days from September 15.

Computing the appeal deadline: September 15 is a Monday. Counting 14 business days from September 15 (not including September 15 itself): September 16–19 (4), September 22–26 (9), September 29–30 (11), October 1 (12). Note: September 1 (Labor Day) is a Board holiday but falls before the rejection date. No holidays fall within the September 16 – October 3 window. The appeal deadline is October 3, 2025 (Friday).

**September 18, 2025**: Granger filed a revised Compliance Plan under Rule D-8 (14 calendar days from September 15 = September 29 deadline). The revision increased monitoring frequency to biweekly, not weekly.

**September 22, 2025**: Granger also filed an appeal of the Board rejection under Rule D-4, arguing that biweekly monitoring meets the EPM's "adequate frequency" standard and that the Board's insistence on weekly sampling exceeds the permit requirements.

**Filing overlap note**: Granger now has both a revised Compliance Plan (filed September 18) and a pending appeal (filed September 22) active simultaneously. The EPM does not explicitly address this overlap. Executive Director Tan noted for the record: "The revised plan and the appeal are separate proceedings. The Board will review the revised plan under Rule D-8's 21-day window. The appeal proceeds on its own track."

**September 22, 2025 — Tolling effect**: The appeal filing tolls the penalty assessment clock under Rule D-6. However, no penalty clock has started because Granger filed a timely response on July 17 (Rule D-5 only activates if no response is filed by the response deadline).

**September 30, 2025 — Docket status snapshot**:

- NOV: issued June 2. Active.
- Response: filed July 17. Timely (extended deadline was July 17).
- Compliance Plan (original): submitted August 29. Rejected September 15.
- Compliance Plan (revised): submitted September 18. Board has 21 calendar days to act = deadline October 9, 2025. No action yet.
- Appeal: filed September 22. Pending. Appeal deadline was October 3; the appeal is timely.
- Penalty assessment: not triggered. Granger filed a timely response, so Rule D-5's clock never started.
- Tolling: the appeal tolls the penalty clock, but the penalty clock is not running, so tolling has no current practical effect.
- Compliance Plan revision deadline: Granger's deadline to submit the revision was September 29. The revision was submitted September 18, which is timely.
- Board action on revised plan: due by October 9. If no action is taken by October 9, the revised plan is deemed provisionally approved under Rule D-3.
- If the Board rejects the revised plan, a new appeal window would open.
- The existing appeal of the original rejection remains pending regardless of the revised plan outcome.

## Monitoring Note — Inspector Oren Baek

Inspector Baek visited Outfall 7 on September 25, 2025 and noted that Granger had already begun biweekly sampling on its own initiative as of September 1. He recorded: "Sampling frequency appears adequate for interim compliance. Formal compliance status depends on Board action on the revised plan."

Baek's note is an observation, not a compliance determination. Only the Board or the Executive Director can issue a formal compliance determination.
