## 3. Charter rules and anti-rules

### Charter Rule F: Dock permit filing

A dock permit is timely if it is filed within 36 hours after the beacon scan.

If a storm waiver is signed by the Port Clerk within 12 hours after the beacon scan, the permit deadline extends to 60 hours after the beacon scan.

A verbal waiver does not extend the deadline.

A waiver signed by the Deputy Port Clerk does not extend the deadline unless the Port Clerk later ratifies it in writing.

### Charter Rule T: Cargo harbor tax

Cargo is taxable if its declared value is greater than 500.

Cargo is exempt if its declared value is at most 500.

Cargo is also exempt if it is certified as relief cargo by the Relief-Cargo Certifier.

A counterfeit relief certificate voids relief status.

A witness saying "cargo de ayuda" means "relief cargo" as a translation claim only; it does not certify relief status.

### Charter Rule Q: Quarantine clearance

A crew member may disembark only if:

1. the crew member has two negative tests at least eight hours apart; and
2. the crew member has no recorded fever within six hours before clearance.

A single negative rapid test never clears a crew member.

A translated radio statement saying "sin fiebre" means "without fever" only as a source claim unless the Quarantine Officer adopts it.

### Charter Rule P: Repair payment authorization

A repair payment is authorized only if the Tide Engineer approves the repair packet and the Treasurer releases the funds.

The Pilot's signature alone does not authorize repair payment.

The Contractor Lead's signature alone does not authorize repair payment.

An emergency stabilization may begin if the Port Clerk and the Acting Watch Captain both sign the emergency start order. This does not authorize full repair payment.

### Charter Rule E: Evidence boundary

A witness statement, staff memo, contractor letter, radio translation, or minority note is a claim unless the Dock Tribunal adopts it as a finding.

A claim that someone caused an event must not be stored as a tribunal finding unless adopted.

### Charter Rule Z: Priority and safety hold

A safety hold overrides payment release and cargo transfer.

No full repair payment may be released while a safety hold is active.

Temporary stabilization may proceed during a safety hold only if the emergency start order was signed by the Port Clerk and the Acting Watch Captain.

### Charter Rule R: Corrections and retractions

A correction replaces an old fact only when the target old fact is named or uniquely inferable from the immediate context.

A vague correction such as "No, it was that one" must be held for clarification.

A correction from a witness is a correction claim, not an official correction, unless the Dock Tribunal or relevant officer adopts it.

## 4. Timeline of clear records

### May 3: beacon and permit

1. On May 3 at 08:10, Beacon Scan BS-19 was logged for the Lantern Finch at Dock 9.
2. On May 3 at 19:20, Asha Rin signed Storm Waiver SW-2.
3. On May 3 at 21:00, Ari Lin said on radio, "waiver ok"; this was a verbal statement and did not itself extend the deadline.
4. On May 5 at 04:00, Kai Morano filed Dock Permit DP-44.
5. On May 5 at 06:00, Asha Rin ratified the filing in writing and recorded the permit as timely under Charter Rule F.

### May 4-6: repair packet and safety hold

6. On May 4 at 14:00, Marta Sol approved Repair Packet RP-31.
7. On May 4 at 16:00, Kai Moreno signed the repair packet as Pilot.
8. On May 4 at 17:00, Kai Morano signed the repair packet as Contractor Lead.
9. On May 5 at 09:00, Asha Rin and Oren Hale signed Emergency Start Order ESO-7.
10. On May 5 at 10:00, a safety hold began after two mooring alarms within three hours.
11. On May 5 at 12:00, temporary stabilization began under ESO-7.
12. On May 6 at 11:00, the safety hold was lifted by Bea Thorne.
13. On May 6 at 13:00, Lena Park released repair funds.
14. On May 6 at 13:10, the file records that full repair payment became authorized.

### May 5-7: quarantine

15. Dr. Noor Vale recorded Juno Mare as a crew member.
16. Juno Mare had a negative test at May 5 08:00.
17. Juno Mare had a negative test at May 5 16:30.
18. Juno Mare had no fever recorded between May 5 10:30 and May 5 16:30.
19. Dr. Noor Vale cleared Juno Mare to disembark on May 5 at 17:00.
20. Pavi Chen had a negative test at May 5 09:00.
21. Pavi Chen had a negative test at May 5 16:00.
22. Pavi Chen had a fever recorded at May 5 15:00.
23. Dr. Noor Vale did not clear Pavi Chen on May 5.
24. The translated radio note by Noor Vail said "Pavi sin fiebre", but the Quarantine Officer did not adopt that note.

### May 6-8: cargo and tax

25. Cargo Manifest CM-8 listed:
    - Glass Eels, value 900, not relief cargo.
    - Seed Crystals, value 300, not relief cargo.
    - Lamp Rice, value 820, relief cargo certified by Lena Pierce.
    - Mirror Seeds, value 650, relief certificate RC-4 later found counterfeit.
26. Lena Pierce certified Lamp Rice as relief cargo on May 6.
27. RC-4 for Mirror Seeds was found counterfeit on May 7.
28. On May 8, Asha Rin recorded:
    - Glass Eels are taxable.
    - Seed Crystals are exempt.
    - Lamp Rice is exempt.
    - Mirror Seeds are taxable.

### May 7-9: salvage and crates

29. At May 7 21:10, Tomas Reed recovered Crate C-17 from the water.
30. At May 7 21:25, Nell Quill recovered Crate C-71 from the water.
31. The first salvage sheet said "blue crate abandoned" without an id.
32. On May 8, the Dock Tribunal corrected the salvage sheet: Crate C-17 was abandoned and not sacred; Crate C-71 was sacred and not abandoned.
33. A witness note by Eli Kest says, "the blue crate was not abandoned"; the tribunal did not adopt this statement.
34. Under the salvage rule read into the file, a diver earns a salvage reward for abandoned non-sacred cargo recovered from water. A diver earns no salvage reward for sacred cargo.
35. The tribunal recorded that Tomas Reed earned a salvage reward for Crate C-17.
36. The tribunal recorded that Nell Quill did not earn a salvage reward for Crate C-71.

### May 9-11: spring claim and responsibility

37. On May 9, Samir Cho opened Claim SC-88 for alleged lost dock revenue.
38. On May 10, Samir Cho marked Claim SC-88 as disputed, not approved.
39. A staff memo by Ivo Kest claims that Kai Morano caused the mooring alarm confusion.
40. A witness statement by Eli Kest claims that the harbor office caused the mooring alarm confusion.
41. A contractor letter by Kai Morano claims that the Pilot, Kai Moreno, caused the confusion.
42. On May 11, the Dock Tribunal adopted only one finding about responsibility: the alarm log was overwritten before review.
43. The Dock Tribunal did not adopt any finding that Kai Morano, Kai Moreno, Ivo Kest, Eli Kest, or the harbor office caused the confusion.
44. On May 11, the Dock Tribunal closed the responsibility section without assigning a responsible person.

### May 12: messy language and correction cluster

45. A Spanish note said, "Asha aprobó el paquete." The translator wrote "Asha approved the packet," but the Port Clerk later clarified that she meant "Asha verified receipt," not payment approval.
46. A French note said, "la règle s'applique si le capitaine intérimaire signe." The translator wrote "the rule applies if the acting captain signs," but did not specify whether this referred to Charter Rule P or Charter Rule Z.
47. A damaged text message said, "Lena approved it." The file does not say whether this was Lena Park releasing funds or Lena Pierce certifying relief cargo.
48. An oral correction said, "No, it was Orin, not Oren." The tribunal held the correction for clarification because the target record was not identified.
49. A later written correction said, "The Acting Watch Captain who signed ESO-7 on May 5 was Oren Hale, not Orin Hale." The tribunal adopted this written correction.
50. A final summary states: "The dock permit was timely; the full repair payment was authorized only after the safety hold was lifted and funds were released; Juno Mare was cleared; Pavi Chen was not cleared; Lamp Rice was exempt; Mirror Seeds were taxable; the tribunal assigned no responsibility for the mooring alarm confusion."
