# Thornvale Autumn Festival — Permit and Licensing Record

## Overview

The Thornvale Autumn Festival runs from October 10 to October 19, 2025. It requires five distinct permit types, each with its own issuing authority, timeline, conditions, and renewal or extension rules. This record compiles the permit status for the 2025 festival as of October 16, the date of an emergency review meeting called by the Town Clerk.

## Permit Types

**A — Ground Use Permit**: Issued by Parks & Recreation. Covers use of Thornvale Common for tents, stages, and vendor stalls. Valid for the stated festival dates. Extension requires a written request filed at least 72 hours before expiration, approved by the Parks Director. Fee: £400.

**B — Temporary Food Service License**: Issued by Environmental Health. Covers all food vendors. Valid for 14 calendar days from issue. Renewal requires a reinspection within 48 hours of expiration. If reinspection fails, the license is suspended immediately and vendors must cease service. Fee: £250 per vendor.

**C — Amplified Sound Permit**: Issued by the Noise Control Office. Valid from 10:00 to 22:00 daily. Extension past 22:00 requires a one-time exemption approved by the Town Council at least 7 calendar days before the event. Violations trigger an automatic 24-hour suspension of the permit. Fee: £150.

**D — Fireworks Display License**: Issued by the Fire Service. Requires a site safety inspection within 5 days before each display. The license covers one display per approved date. Each additional date requires a new inspection. The license expires at the end of each approved display. Fee: £600 per display.

**E — Alcohol Service License**: Issued by the Licensing Authority. Valid for the stated festival dates. The license contains a condition: if a "serious incident" is reported by police, the Licensing Authority may impose a temporary restriction (reduced hours or suspension) without a hearing, effective immediately. The licensee may appeal within 5 business days. Fee: £500.

## Permit Status Record

### Permit A — Ground Use

- Issued: September 15, 2025. Valid October 10–19.
- Extension request filed October 13 by festival director Marcus Holt for an additional day (October 20) to allow teardown.
- Parks Director Leona Chu approved the extension on October 14: "Extension granted to October 20 for teardown only, not public events."
- Status as of October 16: **Active through October 20. Public events end October 19. Teardown only on October 20.**

### Permit B — Temporary Food Service

- Issued: October 3, 2025. Valid 14 calendar days: October 3–16.
- Reinspection scheduled for October 15.
- Environmental Health Officer Dara Salih conducted the reinspection on October 15 and failed three of eleven vendors: Grill House (grease trap), Noodle Cart (handwashing station), and Bake Tent (temperature log). The other eight vendors passed.
- Salih's report (filed October 15): "Three vendors fail reinspection. Their licenses are suspended effective immediately. They may not serve food until a follow-up inspection clears each deficiency. The eight passing vendors are renewed for 14 calendar days from October 16."
- Grill House corrected the deficiency and passed a follow-up inspection on October 16 at 09:00. Grill House's license was reinstated.
- Noodle Cart and Bake Tent have not yet been reinspected as of October 16.
- Status as of October 16: **Eight vendors renewed (valid through October 29). Grill House reinstated October 16 (valid through October 29). Noodle Cart and Bake Tent suspended, not serving.**

### Permit C — Amplified Sound

- Issued: September 28, 2025. Valid October 10–19, 10:00–22:00 daily.
- On October 12, a band played until 22:47. Noise Control Officer Jin Morrow filed a violation report.
- Per the permit terms, the sound permit was automatically suspended for 24 hours: from 22:47 October 12 through 22:47 October 13.
- Festival director Holt requested a one-time late-night exemption for the closing concert on October 18 (until midnight). The request was filed October 9.
- The Town Council discussed the request at its October 6 meeting (before the festival began) and approved: "Exemption granted for October 18 only, sound permitted until 00:00 October 19."
- A second violation occurred on October 15 at 22:15. Morrow filed a second violation report.
- The permit was automatically suspended for another 24 hours: from 22:15 October 15 through 22:15 October 16.
- Status as of October 16: **Suspended until 22:15 October 16. After that, active through October 19 with the October 18 late-night exemption still valid.**
- Town Clerk's note: "Two violations in one festival. If a third occurs, I will recommend permanent revocation to the Council."

### Permit D — Fireworks Display

- Approved dates: October 12 and October 18.
- Site inspection for October 12 display: conducted October 8 by Fire Inspector Callum Yates. Passed.
- October 12 display: completed without incident.
- Site inspection for October 18 display: not yet conducted as of October 16. Must be conducted by October 17 (5 days before = October 13, but inspection is required within 5 days *before* each display, so the window for October 18 is October 13–17).
- Status as of October 16: **October 12 display license expired (display completed). October 18 display license pending — requires inspection by October 17.**

### Permit E — Alcohol Service

- Issued: September 20, 2025. Valid October 10–19.
- On October 14 at 23:30, police responded to a disturbance at the beer tent. PC Ellen Farrow filed an incident report classifying it as a "serious incident."
- The Licensing Authority imposed an immediate temporary restriction on October 15: "Alcohol service hours reduced to 12:00–20:00 for the remainder of the festival, effective immediately."
- Festival director Holt filed an appeal on October 15.
- The Licensing Authority acknowledged receipt and scheduled the appeal hearing for October 20 — one day after the festival ends.
- Status as of October 16: **Active but restricted to 12:00–20:00. Appeal filed but hearing not scheduled until after the festival ends. The restriction is effective during the appeal period.**

## Emergency Review Meeting — October 16

Town Clerk Agnes Burr called the meeting to address the accumulation of permit issues. Attendees: Burr, Holt, Chu, Salih, Morrow, and a representative from the Licensing Authority.

Burr's summary: "We have two sound violations with a third risking permanent revocation, two food vendors still suspended, an alcohol restriction that effectively ends evening service, and a fireworks inspection deadline tomorrow. The festival can continue but the remaining days are significantly constrained."

Holt's response: "The sound violations were honest mistakes — the first by 47 minutes, the second by 15 minutes. The food vendor suspensions are being resolved. The alcohol restriction is disproportionate to a single incident. I am asking for practical accommodation, not rule changes."

Burr's ruling: "The permit terms are clear. I have no authority to override the automatic sound suspension, the food reinspection requirements, or the Licensing Authority's restriction. I can only note Mr. Holt's position and recommend that the October 18 fireworks inspection be prioritized."

No permit terms were changed at the meeting.
