### DOCKET RULES (Copperfall County Local Rule 7)

**LR 7.1 — Answer Deadline.** The defendant must file an answer within 30 calendar days of service. If the defendant files a motion to dismiss instead of an answer, the answer deadline is tolled until the motion is resolved. If the motion is denied, the defendant has 14 calendar days from the denial order to file an answer.

**LR 7.2 — Discovery Deadline.** Discovery opens on the date the answer is filed (or the date a motion to dismiss is denied and the answer period begins, whichever is later) and closes 120 calendar days later. The court may extend the discovery deadline by up to 60 additional days upon a showing of good cause by either party. Only the judge may grant a discovery extension; stipulations between the parties are insufficient without a court order.

**LR 7.3 — Dispositive Motion Deadline.** Motions for summary judgment or other dispositive motions must be filed within 30 calendar days of the close of discovery. The opposing party has 21 calendar days to respond. The moving party has 14 calendar days to reply.

**LR 7.4 — Trial Setting.** If no dispositive motion is filed or if the motion is denied, the court sets a trial date no sooner than 45 calendar days and no later than 90 calendar days from the dispositive motion deadline or from the denial of the motion, whichever is later.

**LR 7.5 — Continuances.** A continuance of any deadline may be granted by the court upon a showing of good cause. A continuance request must be filed at least 5 business days before the deadline to be continued. A request filed fewer than 5 business days before the deadline is presumptively denied unless the movant demonstrates extraordinary circumstances.

**LR 7.6 — Status Designations.** Each case has a single active status:
- **Active-Pleadings**: from filing through the close of pleadings
- **Active-Discovery**: from the opening of discovery through its close
- **Active-Dispositive**: from the close of discovery through resolution of any dispositive motions
- **Trial-Set**: from the setting of the trial date through the trial
- **Stayed**: when the court issues a stay order; all deadlines are tolled during a stay
- **Closed**: after final judgment or voluntary dismissal

Status transitions are recorded by the Clerk's Office. Only the judge may order a stay or close a case.
