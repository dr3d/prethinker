# Lantern Hill School Coastal Field Trip — Administrative Record

## Trip Overview

From October 7 to October 9, 2025, thirty-two Year 8 students from Lantern Hill School participated in a three-day coastal ecology field trip along the Pembury Shore. The trip was organized by lead teacher Ms. Naomi Strand and supported by three additional chaperones: Mr. Edgar Tullis, Ms. Pia Okafor, and parent volunteer Mr. Henrik Jostad.

Students were divided into four groups at departure:

- **Red Group** (chaperone: Ms. Strand): Arden, Bettina, Cosmo, Daliah, Emmett, Felix, Gail, Hana
- **Blue Group** (chaperone: Mr. Tullis): Ira, Juno, Kael, Lotte, Maeve, Niles, Odette, Priya
- **Green Group** (chaperone: Ms. Okafor): Quinn, Rafe, Suki, Tamsin, Uriel, Vera, Willem, Xiomara
- **Yellow Group** (chaperone: Mr. Jostad): Yara, Ziv, Arden B., Brigid, Cleo, Dion, Elio, Freya

Note: There are two students named Arden. Arden (Red Group) is Arden Marsh. Arden B. (Yellow Group) is Arden Banerjee.

## Day 1 — October 7: Salt Marsh and Dune Walk

All four groups departed the coach park at 9:15 and walked the salt marsh trail together until the first split point at 10:30, where Red and Blue groups took the northern dune path and Green and Yellow groups took the southern estuary path.

At 11:00, Cosmo (Red) reported feeling unwell. Ms. Strand sent Cosmo back to the coach with Mr. Tullis, who transferred Blue Group to Ms. Strand's supervision for the remainder of the morning. Blue Group therefore walked the northern dune path under Ms. Strand's supervision from 11:00 onward, while Mr. Tullis stayed with Cosmo at the coach until lunch.

At lunch (12:30), all groups reunited at the Pembury Shore visitor center. Cosmo remained at the coach and did not attend the afternoon session. Mr. Tullis resumed supervision of Blue Group.

The afternoon session (13:30–16:00) was a guided beach survey. All 31 attending students participated in mixed-group pairs assigned by the beach ranger. Group designations were suspended during this session. Hana (Red) and Niles (Blue) were paired together. Suki (Green) and Yara (Yellow) were paired together.

## Day 2 — October 8: Tide Pools and Cliff Walk

Cosmo returned to participation on Day 2 and rejoined Red Group.

At 9:00, Mr. Jostad informed Ms. Strand that he had a family emergency and needed to leave by noon. Ms. Strand reassigned Yellow Group: Yara, Ziv, and Arden B. joined Blue Group under Mr. Tullis, while Brigid, Cleo, Dion, Elio, and Freya joined Green Group under Ms. Okafor.

At 10:15, all groups arrived at the Pembury tide pools. Green Group (now 13 students) was too large for a single tide pool station, so Ms. Okafor split them: Quinn, Rafe, Suki, Tamsin, Uriel, Vera, Willem, and Xiomara at Station A; Brigid, Cleo, Dion, Elio, and Freya at Station B. Ms. Okafor supervised Station A and asked Freya, the oldest student present, to keep watch at Station B.

### The Tide Pool Incident

At approximately 10:45, Elio slipped on wet rocks at Station B and cut his hand. Two incident reports were filed:

**Freya's report** (filed 11:00): "Elio was reaching for a starfish when he slipped. Dion tried to grab him but missed. I applied the first-aid kit bandage. Cleo went to get Ms. Okafor. Brigid stayed with Elio."

**Brigid's report** (filed 11:30): "Elio was trying to catch a crab, not a starfish. Dion pushed him as a joke and then tried to grab him after he slipped. I applied the bandage, not Freya. Freya was facing away and turned around after Elio yelled."

Ms. Okafor arrived at Station B at 10:52. She noted that Elio's hand was already bandaged when she arrived and that both Freya and Brigid were next to him. She did not determine who applied the bandage. She recorded the injury as "minor laceration, left palm, treated with adhesive bandage from field kit."

Ms. Okafor's own report states: "I cannot confirm whether Elio was reaching for a starfish or a crab, or whether Dion's contact was accidental or intentional. I only observed the aftermath."

Mr. Jostad departed at noon as planned. He was not present for the tide pool incident.

### Afternoon — Cliff Walk

The afternoon cliff walk (14:00–16:30) proceeded with three groups:

- Red Group (8 students, Ms. Strand)
- Blue Group (11 students, Mr. Tullis) — original Blue plus Yara, Ziv, Arden B.
- Green Group (13 students, Ms. Okafor) — original Green plus Brigid, Cleo, Dion, Elio, Freya

Lotte (Blue) twisted her ankle on the cliff path at 14:40 and was escorted back to the coach by Mr. Tullis. Ms. Strand took over Blue Group supervision for the remainder of the afternoon. Mr. Tullis stayed with Lotte.

At 15:30, Maeve (Blue, now under Strand) and Vera (Green, under Okafor) asked to swap groups for the final observation point because Vera wanted to see the cliff nesting site and Maeve wanted to sketch the rock pools. Ms. Strand and Ms. Okafor agreed. For the final hour, Maeve was with Green Group and Vera was with Blue Group.

## Day 3 — October 9: Beach Clean and Departure

Mr. Jostad did not return. Three chaperones supervised all 32 students.

Ms. Strand reorganized for the morning beach clean: Red and Blue students formed "Shore Team" (19 students), Green students formed "Dune Team" (13 students). Maeve returned to Blue Group and Vera returned to Green Group for this division.

Lotte participated with a wrapped ankle and was assigned the recording clipboard instead of active collection.

At 10:30, Shore Team found a sealed container marked "CAUTION — AGRICULTURAL" washed up near the high-tide line. Ms. Strand instructed students to stay back and contacted the coast guard. The container was collected at 11:15 by a coast guard crew who confirmed it was an empty fertilizer drum and posed no hazard. No students touched the container.

Departure was at 13:00. All 32 students were accounted for on the return coach. Ms. Strand filed a trip completion report noting: one illness (Cosmo, Day 1 morning), one minor injury (Elio, Day 2 tide pool), one sprained ankle (Lotte, Day 2 cliff), one chaperone early departure (Jostad, Day 2 noon), one hazardous material report (empty drum, Day 3), and two unresolved incident report discrepancies (Freya vs Brigid accounts of the Elio injury).
