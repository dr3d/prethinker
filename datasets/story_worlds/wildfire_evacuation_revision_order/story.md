## 1. Incident summary

The Coyote Fork Fire ignited in the Lassen National Forest at approximately 16:14 PDT on April 14, 2026. By 18:00 PDT it had crossed onto private and county lands in eastern Tehama County. CAL FIRE Tehama-Glenn Unit assumed unified command with USFS Lassen National Forest at 18:22 PDT. Estimated acreage at packet time: 4,820 acres. Containment: 3 percent. Cause is under investigation; the suspected ignition area is approximately 0.4 miles northeast of Coyote Fork Trailhead.

## 4. Map layer correction MC-2026-014-01

Filed at April 15, 2026, 09:47 PDT, by Lisette Boyer, GIS Analyst II, Tehama County.

Issue: TCSO_Evac_Layers_v1.1 incorrectly drew the boundary between zones TC-9 and TC-11. The TC-11 polygon as published at 04:12 extended approximately 0.6 miles farther east than intended, encroaching on the eastern edge of the Manton Knolls subdivision (parcels 074-220-001 through 074-220-046). Those 46 parcels were inside TC-9's boundary in v1.0 and should remain inside TC-9 under v1.1.

Correction: **TCSO_Evac_Layers_v1.1.1** redraws the TC-9 / TC-11 line along Cold Spring Road. The 46 parcels of Manton Knolls subdivision are restored to TC-9.

Effect on orders: Manton Knolls subdivision (parcels 074-220-001 through 074-220-046) is now under MANDATORY EVACUATION (TC-9), not EVACUATION WARNING. The corrected order is what was always intended; the v1.1 polygon was a drafting error.

Public alert correction via WEA sent at 09:51 PDT, with text: "Manton Knolls residents — your zone is TC-9 MANDATORY EVACUATION. Earlier alert was a mapping error. Evacuate now."

Residents who saw the 04:18 WEA alert and believed they were in TC-11 (Evacuation Warning) were under an erroneously published order between 04:12 and 09:51. The 09:51 correction supersedes that interim published status. GIS layer of record after correction: **TCSO_Evac_Layers_v1.1.1**.

## 7. Conditional re-entry framework

Re-entry to evacuated zones requires the conditions below **and** a Sheriff's signed re-entry authorization (Form **TCSO-RE-04**).

**TC-7 (currently Evacuation Warning):** Not applicable; no mandatory evacuation in force as of EO-2026-014B.

**TC-8 (Mandatory Evacuation):**
1. Fire perimeter located more than 2 miles from any TC-8 boundary, confirmed by IC.
2. PG&E confirmation of gas line integrity in zone.
3. Hazard tree assessment complete on all primary access roads.
4. Sheriff's signed re-entry authorization.

**TC-9 (Mandatory Evacuation):**
1. Damage assessment complete.
2. PG&E gas and electric restoration or confirmation of safe de-energized state.
3. Hazard tree assessment complete.
4. Sheriff's signed re-entry authorization.

**TC-10 / TC-11 (Evacuation Warning):** No re-entry authorization required; warning permits voluntary movement.

As of packet time, **no** re-entry authorizations have been signed for any zone.

## 10. Cross-reference: GIS evacuation polygons vs road-jurisdiction layer

The GIS layers v1.0, v1.1, and v1.1.1 are evacuation polygons; they do not encode road-jurisdiction information. Road jurisdiction is encoded in a separate layer maintained by Tehama County Public Works, layer name **TCPW_Roads_v2025.4**. That layer marks Forest Road 32N17 as "Federal — USFS Lassen NF" and Highway Spur 36 as "County — TCPW maintained."

Where the radio log differs from the road-jurisdiction layer (e.g., the 04:48 reference to civilians using Forest Road 32N17), the road-jurisdiction layer is the authoritative description of who controls the gate; the radio log describes events that occurred on a road, not the jurisdiction of the road.
