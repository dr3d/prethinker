# Estate of Aldric Pethwick, Clockmaker — Executor's Working File

## Background

Aldric Pethwick operated a clock and watch repair shop at 14 Chandler Lane, Thornbury, from 1981 until his death on February 9, 2025. His niece, Esme Pethwick-Dorsey, is the sole executor of his estate. Among the shop's contents are three handwritten ledgers covering different aspects of the business. The ledgers are undated on their covers and were found in separate locations: the accounts ledger in the front desk, the client ledger in a filing cabinet, and the parts ledger in the workshop.

Esme retained Marion Keates, a forensic document examiner, to assess the ledgers. Keates confirmed all three are in Aldric's hand but noted that marginal corrections appear in at least two different inks: the original entries in blue ballpoint, and later corrections in black gel ink. One set of corrections in the parts ledger appears in a third hand — possibly a shop assistant's — but Keates could not confirm the writer's identity.

## The Accounts Ledger

The accounts ledger covers financial transactions. Key entries relevant to the estate:

**Entry A-041** (dated June 2019): "Received Whitford bracket clock for restoration. Client: Greta Lundquist. Estimated fee: £1,400. Deposit paid: £700."

**Entry A-058** (dated November 2020): "Whitford bracket clock restoration complete. Balance due: £700. Client notified November 3."

**Entry A-062** (dated January 2021): "Whitford bracket clock — balance paid in full. Clock held for client pickup."

**Entry A-089** (dated March 2023): "Consignment: Dearborn regulator. Owner: Clive Maughton. Retail price if sold: £3,200. Commission: 15%."

**Entry A-093** (dated June 2023): "Dearborn regulator sold to Barnett. Proceeds: £3,200. Commission retained: £480. Due to Maughton: £2,720." A marginal note in black ink reads: "Error — commission should be 20% per revised agreement. Due to Maughton: £2,560. Difference owed by estate: £160."

**Entry A-107** (dated September 2024): "Received Hartley marine chronometer for assessment. Client: Oona Selkirk. No fee quoted pending assessment."

**Entry A-112** (dated December 2024): "Hartley marine chronometer assessment complete. Recommended against restoration — escapement damage too extensive. Client advised. Clock in storage pending client instruction."

A marginal note on A-112 in black ink reads: "Selkirk called January 14. Wants clock returned. Ship by registered post. Do NOT attempt repair."

## The Client Ledger

The client ledger records client details and item tracking. Key entries:

**Entry C-017**: "Greta Lundquist, 28 Lime Close, Thornbury. Whitford bracket clock. Received June 2019. Completed November 2020. Picked up March 2021."

**Entry C-024**: "Clive Maughton, The Beeches, Dursley. Dearborn regulator. Consigned March 2023. Commission rate: 15%."

A marginal note on C-024 in black ink reads: "Maughton called September 2023. Disputes commission rate. Claims verbal agreement for 20%. No written record of change. Aldric's note: keep at 15% until written confirmation."

**Entry C-031**: "Oona Selkirk, Flat 4, Quayside Mews, Bristol. Hartley marine chronometer. Received September 2024."

**Entry C-033**: "Denny Vickers, 7 Plough Road, Thornbury. Ansonia shelf clock. Received October 2024. Movement cleaning. Estimated fee: £280."

**Entry C-033 marginal note** in black ink: "Movement cleaning complete December 2024. Clock running but gains 4 minutes per day. Advised Vickers — acceptable for decorative use, not precision. Vickers accepted. Fee paid: £280."

## The Parts Ledger

The parts ledger records workshop inventory and parts usage. Key entries:

**Entry P-088** (dated April 2023): "Installed replacement pendulum bob in Dearborn regulator. Source: NOS Dearborn part from Haverstock auction lot 12. Cost: £85."

**Entry P-094** (dated August 2023): "Whitford bracket clock — client pickup note: clock returned to Greta Lundquist March 2021 per C-017." This entry is crossed out with a marginal note in the third hand (unidentified writer): "Wrong. Clock still in shop as of August 2023. Lundquist never collected."

**Entry P-101** (dated July 2024): "Ordered replacement balance spring for Hartley marine chronometer. Supplier: Cousins UK. Cost: £42. Arrived August 2024."

**Entry P-101 marginal note** in black ink (Aldric's hand per Keates): "Do NOT install. Assessment concluded escapement damage too extensive. Spring stored in parts drawer H-7."

**Entry P-108** (dated November 2024): "Ansonia shelf clock movement cleaned. Replaced mainspring. Source: generic replacement, Meadows catalog. Cost: £18."

**Entry P-112** (dated January 2025): "Inventory check — Dearborn regulator: SOLD per A-093. Whitford bracket clock: IN SHOP per P-094 correction. Hartley marine chronometer: IN STORAGE, return to client pending. Ansonia shelf clock: COMPLETE, awaiting pickup."

## Marion Keates's Assessment Summary

Keates provided the following opinions:

1. The accounts ledger commission correction (A-093 marginal note changing commission from 15% to 20%) is in Aldric's hand and black gel ink, consistent with other late-period corrections. However, the client ledger entry C-024 contains Aldric's own note rejecting Maughton's 20% claim and insisting on 15% until written confirmation. The two corrections contradict each other. Keates recommends treating the client ledger note as the later and more considered position because it references the dispute explicitly, while the accounts marginal note reads as an initial concession that Aldric later reconsidered.

2. The parts ledger correction on P-094 (claiming the Whitford clock was never collected by Lundquist) is in an unidentified third hand. Keates cannot verify its accuracy. The accounts ledger says the balance was paid (A-062), and the client ledger says the clock was picked up in March 2021 (C-017). The unidentified correction is the only document claiming otherwise.

3. The Hartley chronometer balance spring (P-101) was ordered before the assessment concluded against restoration. Aldric's own marginal note instructs not to install it.

## Executor's Open Questions

Esme has flagged the following as unresolved:

- Whether the Whitford bracket clock is in the shop or was collected by Lundquist
- Whether Maughton is owed £2,720 (15% commission) or £2,560 (20% commission)
- Whether the balance spring should be returned to the supplier, kept as estate inventory, or offered to Selkirk
- Whether the Ansonia shelf clock has been collected by Vickers
- Whether the Hartley chronometer has been shipped to Selkirk per the January 14 instruction
