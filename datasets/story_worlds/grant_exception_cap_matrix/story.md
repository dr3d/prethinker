## Section 2. Eligibility Rules

The following rules apply to all applications. All rules must be
satisfied for an application to proceed to scoring. Failure on any
eligibility rule terminates review at the eligibility stage; the
application is not scored, and reviewer time is not assigned.

- **ER-1: Tax status.** Applicant must be a current 501(c)(3) public
  charity in good standing with the IRS, or a fiscally sponsored
  project of a 501(c)(3) with a current memorandum of understanding
  on file. Documentation of tax status must be submitted with the
  application.
- **ER-2: Service area.** Applicant's primary service area must
  include at least one of the three priority counties: Briar, Wood, or
  Stone. Service in non-priority counties is permitted but does not by
  itself satisfy ER-2; the applicant must be able to demonstrate that
  a priority county is in the *primary* service area, not merely an
  incidental one.
- **ER-3: Compliance history.** Applicant must have completed at least
  one prior Briarwood grant cycle without an open compliance finding.
  An "open compliance finding" is defined in the cycle procedure manual
  and includes any unresolved financial reporting issue from a prior
  cycle. See EX-A below for the first-time-applicant exception.

## Section 3. Exceptions

- **EX-A: First-time applicant exception.** An applicant that has
  never applied for a Briarwood grant before is exempt from ER-3.
  EX-A does not exempt the applicant from ER-1 or ER-2.
- **EX-B: Rural service-area bonus.** An applicant whose primary
  service area is more than 50% (by population, per the most recent
  decennial census-designated rural blocks) in rural blocks may
  receive a +10% bonus on the requested award amount. The bonus is
  applied before the per-grant cap of $25,000. EX-B is a bonus, not
  an eligibility exception; it does not waive any of ER-1 through
  ER-3.

Reviewers use criteria C-1 (project clarity), C-2 (community fit), and
C-3 (feasibility). The composite is the simple average of the three

A recusal removes the recused member from voting on the named item
only; it does not change the member's voting eligibility on other
items, and **does not automatically decide the named item in any
direction.** A recused member does not count toward the quorum for
the recused item.

The committee has 7 voting members. With one recusal, 6 members vote
on the recused item; with two recusals on different items, the
committee size for any given item is 7 minus the number of recusals
filed for that specific item.
