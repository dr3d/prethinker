# The Harfield Lending Library — Incident and Inventory Discrepancy Report

## Background

The Harfield Lending Library is a small community library in a converted chapel. Librarian June Odell filed this report on November 8, 2025, after discovering that events described in recently acquired novels appeared to mirror — but did not match — real incidents at the library.

The report is intended for the Library Board to clarify the factual record versus fictional content that may confuse future auditors or researchers.

## Real Incident Log

The following events are verified entries in the library's operational log, signed by staff:

**RI-1 (September 3, 2025)**: A water pipe burst in the Reference Room at 14:20. Volunteer Gary Sims discovered the leak. Approximately forty books on the lower shelves sustained water damage. The damaged titles were moved to the Drying Room (formerly the vestry). Insurance assessor Wendy Cade inspected September 5 and classified the damage as "moderate — restorable."

**RI-2 (September 18, 2025)**: Patron Della Vickers reported that a first edition of "Compass and Wheel" by Morton Hale was missing from the Rare Shelf. A search of the library, including the Drying Room, did not locate the book. The police were notified. The book has not been recovered as of this report. Its insured value is £1,800.

**RI-3 (October 2, 2025)**: The heating system failed. The Library Board authorized emergency repair. Contractor Tobin Greaves completed the repair on October 4. Total cost: £2,300. During the repair, the boiler room was inaccessible for 48 hours.

**RI-4 (October 20, 2025)**: During the annual volunteer appreciation event, patron Reggie Lam fell on the entrance steps and fractured his wrist. An ambulance was called. Lam was treated at Harfield General Hospital. He has not filed a liability claim as of this report.

**RI-5 (November 1, 2025)**: Librarian Odell discovered that the inventory system showed 4,312 titles, but a physical shelf count found 4,287 titles — a discrepancy of 25 books. The discrepancy is under investigation. Odell notes that some of the missing titles may be among the water-damaged books moved to the Drying Room and not yet re-cataloged.

## Recently Acquired Novels With Library Settings

Three novels acquired in the autumn 2025 purchase contain library settings with events that superficially resemble real incidents. June Odell flags these to prevent confusion.

### Novel A: "The Flooded Archive" by Clara Ng (2024)

Set in a fictional library called the Whitmore Archive. Chapter 3 describes a burst pipe that destroys "hundreds" of rare manuscripts. The fictional librarian, Philip, discovers that the pipe was deliberately sabotaged by a disgruntled patron named Gerald who wanted to destroy a genealogy record that proved he was disinherited.

**Odell's note**: "The pipe burst at Harfield (RI-1) was caused by age and corrosion, not sabotage. Approximately forty books were damaged, not hundreds. They were standard lending stock, not rare manuscripts. There is no patron named Gerald at Harfield and no evidence of sabotage. The resemblance is coincidental."

### Novel B: "Midnight in the Stacks" by P. Ashworth (2025)

Set in a fictional library called Greyminster. The plot involves a first edition of a book called "Compass and Wheel" being stolen from a locked display case by the library's own volunteer, who sells it to fund his daughter's medical treatment. The volunteer is named Gary.

**Odell's note**: "We do have a missing first edition of 'Compass and Wheel' (RI-2) and we do have a volunteer named Gary (Gary Sims, who discovered the pipe leak in RI-1). These are coincidences. Gary Sims is not a suspect in the book's disappearance. The police investigation has not identified any suspect. The novel was published in January 2025, before our copy went missing in September. The fictional 'Compass and Wheel' in the novel is described as a navigation manual; our real copy is a poetry collection by Morton Hale."

**Additional note**: "I contacted the publisher to ask whether Ashworth had any connection to Harfield. The publisher confirmed that Ashworth has never visited the library and that the manuscript was completed in 2023, before any of the real incidents occurred."

### Novel C: "The Librarian's Confession" by R. Thorn (2025)

Set in a fictional library called Dunbarton House. The plot involves a librarian who fabricates an inventory discrepancy to cover up books she has been secretly selling. The fictional discrepancy is exactly 25 books. The librarian character is named June.

**Odell's note**: "The real inventory discrepancy (RI-5) is also 25 books, and my name is June. This is the most alarming coincidence. However: the novel was published in March 2025. The real discrepancy was discovered November 1, 2025 — eight months after publication. I did not discover the novel's plot until a patron mentioned it on November 3. I have asked the Library Board to appoint an independent auditor to verify the inventory count and confirm that the discrepancy is explainable by the water damage event and re-cataloging delay."

**Board response (November 6)**: "The Board acknowledges Ms. Odell's disclosure. An independent auditor, Frances Kemp, has been appointed. The audit will begin November 15. No finding of misconduct has been made. The Board notes that Ms. Odell voluntarily disclosed the coincidence, which weighs in her favor."

## Odell's Summary

"Three novels. Three coincidences. None of them describe what actually happened at Harfield. The pipe was not sabotage. Gary is not a thief. The discrepancy is not a cover-up. But I understand that someone reading these novels and then reading our incident log might draw wrong conclusions. This report exists to make the boundary between fiction and fact explicit."

## Current Status

- RI-1 (pipe burst): Damage restoration in progress. Not yet complete.
- RI-2 (missing book): Unresolved. No suspect identified. Police case open.
- RI-3 (heating repair): Complete.
- RI-4 (patron fall): No liability claim filed.
- RI-5 (inventory discrepancy): Under investigation. Independent audit begins November 15.
- Novel claims: None of the fictional events are part of the factual record. The Board has made no finding linking any novel to any real incident.
