## 1. District Field Trip Policy — operative excerpts

The following excerpts from the Whitfield Unified School District Field Trip Policy (revision 2024-08) are operative for this trip.

**§3.2 — Chaperone-to-student ratio (water-access trips).** For any school-sponsored field trip to a destination at which students will have access to a body of water (river, lake, ocean, swimming pool, harbor, or other open water), the school shall provide a minimum of one (1) qualifying chaperone for every ten (10) students on the trip-day manifest. The chaperone count is computed as ⌈students ÷ 10⌉.

**§3.4 — Medical-staff exclusion.** A registered nurse, certified emergency medical technician, or other medical professional accompanying a trip in a medical capacity is not counted as a qualifying chaperone for purposes of §3.2. Medical personnel are listed on the trip manifest in a separate role.

**§3.5 — Standby chaperones.** A standby chaperone may be designated for a trip but is not counted as a qualifying chaperone under §3.2 unless they appear on the trip-day manifest. A standby is not a substitute for the §3.2 minimum at the time the manifest is locked.

**§4.1 — Roster correction.** A trip roster may be corrected by the issuing school office before the trip-day manifest is locked. Each correction is issued as a numbered Correction Notice referencing the prior roster version. The trip-day manifest is locked at 06:30 on the trip date.

**§4.2 — Permission-slip deadline.** A signed permission slip from a parent or guardian is required for each student on the trip-day manifest. The deadline for receipt is 17:00 on the day before the trip date. Students without a signed slip on file at the deadline are removed from the trip-day manifest.

The Lakeport Marine Science Center includes shoreline access on Lake Lakeport; §3.2 therefore applies to this trip.
