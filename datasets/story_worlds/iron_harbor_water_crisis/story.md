# The Iron Harbor Water Crisis

## Source Document: Combined Incident Log, Policy Extract, and Witness Statements

### SECTION A -- Standing Policy (Iron Harbor Municipal Water Authority, Bylaw 9.4)

Any surface-water intake supplying a residential distribution zone must be tested
for coliform bacteria no less than every 72 hours during normal operations.

During a declared contamination advisory, the testing interval drops to every
12 hours and remains at that frequency until the advisory is formally lifted by
the Chief Water Officer.

A contamination advisory is automatically triggered when any single coliform
reading exceeds 400 CFU/100mL at any monitored intake point.

No residential zone may receive untreated surface water. All surface water must
pass through the Eastgate Treatment Facility or, in emergency bypass, through
the portable chlorination unit maintained at Pier 7.

If the Eastgate facility is offline for more than 6 hours during a contamination
advisory, the Chief Water Officer must issue a boil-water notice to all
downstream residential zones within 1 hour of the 6-hour threshold being
reached.

A boil-water notice remains in effect until two consecutive coliform readings at
the affected intake, taken at least 12 hours apart, both fall below 50
CFU/100mL. The Chief Water Officer must then formally lift the notice within
4 hours of the second clean reading.

Emergency bypass through the Pier 7 chlorination unit requires written
authorization from both the Chief Water Officer and the Harbor Master. Neither
officer acting alone may authorize the bypass.

The Harbor Master's authorization is only valid if the Harbor Master has
physically inspected the Pier 7 unit within the preceding 30 days. An
inspection older than 30 days does not count as current.

Residential zones downstream of the Eastgate facility are: Millbrook, Linden
Terrace, and Old Harbor. The industrial zone (Foundry Row) is not a residential
zone and is not subject to the boil-water notice requirement, but must be
notified separately within 2 hours of any contamination advisory.

### SECTION D -- Corrections and Addenda (filed March 8, 2026)

**Correction 1:** The March 3, 14:00 coliform reading was initially recorded as
120 CFU/100mL. R. Vasquez's lab notebook shows the actual reading was 180
CFU/100mL. The incident log is corrected accordingly. The original 120 value
was a transcription error.

**Correction 2:** M. Okonkwo's statement says arrival at 01:45. The access
badge log shows entry at 01:52. The badge log is treated as authoritative for
timeline reconstruction.

**Correction 3:** Ferreira initially stated his last Pier 7 inspection was
January 28. He then corrected himself to February 1. His written inspection
log confirms February 1. The January 28 date is retracted.

**Addendum:** During the post-incident review, Cheng disclosed that she was
aware of the Eastgate pump's deteriorating condition as early as February 20
but did not order preventive maintenance. This is noted for the compliance
record. It is Cheng's statement, not a finding of the review board.
