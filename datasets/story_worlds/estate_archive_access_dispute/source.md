## 1. Decedent and estate summary

Walter Hawthorne Hennessy, of Brookline, Massachusetts, died on November 12, 2025, at age 91. He was a historian and collector. His will, executed January 14, 2017, names his eldest daughter **Katherine Hennessy-Brown** as executor of his estate. The will is admitted to probate, Docket No. P-26-1247.

Decedent is survived by three adult children: Katherine Hennessy-Brown (eldest, executor), Daniel Hennessy, and Sophia Hennessy-Marchetti (youngest). Decedent's wife Emiko Hennessy predeceased him in 2018.

## 2. The Hennessy Archive

The Hennessy Archive comprises decedent's personal and professional papers spanning his career from 1958 to his death. The bulk of the archive — approximately 38 linear feet of correspondence, manuscripts, research notes, and photographs — was conveyed to Hartwell College Special Collections Library by deed of gift in two stages, described in §3 and §4 below.

The "Crimson Notebooks" are a discrete subset of the archive: three bound notebooks in red cloth boards (volumes I, II, III), containing handwritten research notes from Hennessy's 1968–1972 monograph *Patterns of Dissent*. The notebooks are catalogued in the Hartwell Special Collections finding aid as **MS-Hennessy-068**.

## 3. 2019 Deed of Gift and Intake Receipt #ARC-2019-0834

On October 7, 2019, Walter H. Hennessy executed a Deed of Gift to Hartwell College Special Collections Library. The deed's operative language is:

"I, Walter Hawthorne Hennessy, do hereby give, transfer, and convey to Hartwell College Special Collections Library all my personal papers, manuscripts, and correspondence presently located at 247 Old Cobb Lane, Brookline, Massachusetts, with the exception of items specifically retained on the attached Schedule A."

Schedule A listed 14 specifically retained items, including correspondence with the Hennessy family, certain personal photographs, and the Crimson Notebooks. **Schedule A item #11** reads: "Three bound research notebooks, red cloth boards, c. 1968–1972, retained for ongoing scholarly use."

Hartwell intake receipt **#ARC-2019-0834**, dated October 7, 2019, lists 38 linear feet of materials received at the Special Collections loading dock. The Crimson Notebooks are not on the 2019 intake list, consistent with their explicit retention in Schedule A.

## 4. 2022 Supplementary Deed of Gift and Intake Receipt #ARC-2022-0247

On April 18, 2022, Walter H. Hennessy executed a Supplementary Deed of Gift to Hartwell College Special Collections Library. The supplementary deed states:

"I, Walter Hawthorne Hennessy, in supplement to my Deed of Gift dated October 7, 2019, do hereby give, transfer, and convey the following items previously retained under Schedule A: items #1, #4, and #11. Schedule A is amended to remove these items."

Schedule A item #11 is "Three bound research notebooks, red cloth boards, c. 1968–1972" — i.e., the Crimson Notebooks, MS-Hennessy-068.

Hartwell intake receipt **#ARC-2022-0247**, dated April 21, 2022, lists three items received: (a) a bound manuscript of decedent's 1972 monograph corrections; (b) an album of family photographs from his 1965 trip to Japan; and (c) **"Three bound research notebooks, red cloth boards, c. 1968–1972, MS-Hennessy-068."** The receipt is signed by Hartwell's Curator of Manuscripts, **Dr. Aiden Trevathan**, and counter-signed by W.H. Hennessy.

## 6. October 12, 2024 codicil to will

On October 12, 2024, Walter H. Hennessy executed a codicil to his will of January 14, 2017. The codicil reads (operative provisions):

"I hereby revoke any prior conveyance of the three bound research notebooks in red cloth boards bearing the catalogue identifier MS-Hennessy-068, also known as the Crimson Notebooks, including without limitation the conveyance to Hartwell College Special Collections Library by Supplementary Deed of Gift dated April 18, 2022. I hereby bequeath the said three notebooks to my three children, Katherine Hennessy-Brown, Daniel Hennessy, and Sophia Hennessy-Marchetti, jointly and equally."

The codicil is signed by Hennessy and witnessed by two attesting witnesses (Lawrence Petrov and Jamilah Otieno) and notarized by Notary Public Carlos Mendoza. It is on file with the Probate Court as **Exhibit C-1**.

## 7. Withdrawn September 2024 codicil draft

A draft codicil dated September 24, 2024 was prepared by Hennessy's prior counsel, Mr. Theodore Marchetti (no relation to Sophia Hennessy-Marchetti's married name). The September 24 draft contained substantively similar language to the October 12 signed codicil but was **never signed** by Hennessy. The draft is retained in the estate file as **Exhibit D-1** for procedural reference only and is not operative. The October 12 signed codicil supersedes the September 24 draft.

## 8. Capacity question

Counsel for Hartwell College has raised, in a brief filed April 22, 2026, the question of whether decedent had testamentary capacity at the time of the October 12, 2024 codicil. Hartwell's brief notes that decedent was diagnosed with mild cognitive impairment by his physician, Dr. Helena Yamamoto, on August 15, 2024, approximately two months before the codicil was signed. Hartwell argues this diagnosis raises a substantial question requiring formal capacity adjudication.

Counsel for the estate has filed a responsive brief on April 28, 2026, arguing that mild cognitive impairment does not by itself preclude testamentary capacity, and that the codicil's witnessing by two attesting witnesses and notarization satisfies the formal requirements of **M.G.L. c. 190B § 2-502**. The estate's brief also notes that Dr. Yamamoto's August 15, 2024 evaluation expressly stated that decedent "retained understanding of his finances, family, and personal affairs at the level required for routine estate decisions."

The capacity question is **pending Probate Court determination** at the May 18, 2026 hearing.

## 9. Withdrawn researcher authorization (procedural reference)

In a separate matter, on April 11, 2024, Hennessy issued a written instruction to Hartwell Special Collections **withdrawing access permission for one specific researcher, Dr. Peter Calverley**, who had previously been authorized to consult the Hennessy papers. The withdrawal of access did not affect any item disposition; it concerned reading-room access only. Dr. Calverley filed an appeal which was dismissed by Hartwell's Director of Special Collections on May 14, 2024.

This withdrawn authorization is referenced in the present packet only as procedural context for the access framework; it is unrelated to the Crimson Notebooks dispute.

## 10. Estate inventory and Hartwell catalog status

Estate inventory filed by executor Katherine Hennessy-Brown on March 4, 2026, lists the Crimson Notebooks (MS-Hennessy-068) as an estate asset, citing the October 12, 2024 codicil. The inventory is on file as **Exhibit E-1**.

Hartwell College Special Collections catalog as of packet date lists MS-Hennessy-068 as part of the Hennessy Archive, accession date April 21, 2022, with the following status note (added April 30, 2026): "**Reading room access only; physical removal restricted pending probate court determination.**"

The Crimson Notebooks are currently in the **physical possession** of Hartwell College Special Collections, in the climate-controlled stacks at the Reardon Manuscript Vault, **Vault Bay 14**. Legal ownership is disputed.

## 11. Counsel memos on file

- **Exhibit M-1**: Memo from estate counsel Bernard Tovar, Esq., dated April 16, 2026, asserting that the October 12, 2024 codicil revokes the 2022 supplementary deed and that the Crimson Notebooks are estate property to be distributed jointly to the three children per the codicil.
- **Exhibit M-2**: Memo from college counsel Helen Ng, Esq., dated April 22, 2026, asserting that the 2022 supplementary deed was a completed inter vivos gift, that title passed at the moment of delivery on April 21, 2022, and that a subsequent codicil cannot revoke a completed transfer of personal property. Ng's memo notes the capacity question raised in §8 above as a separate (and in Hartwell's view ancillary) question.
- **Exhibit M-3**: Reply memo from Bernard Tovar dated April 28, 2026, contesting the "completed inter vivos gift" theory on the grounds that the supplementary deed contained a reservation of access for Hennessy's lifetime and arguing that the gift was therefore conditional or incomplete. Hartwell's reply on this point is not yet filed.

The court has not yet ruled on either party's legal theory.
