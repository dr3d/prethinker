# The Ridgeline Fire

## Source Document: Combined Incident Record, Standing Orders, Witness Statements, and Corrections

### SECTION A — Standing Orders (Ridgeline County Wildfire Suppression Protocol, Order 7.2)

Any wildfire within Ridgeline County that exceeds 50 acres must be assigned an
Incident Commander (IC) within 2 hours of the acreage estimate exceeding the
threshold. The IC must be certified at the Type 2 level or higher.

During a declared Red Flag Warning issued by the National Weather Service, all
ground crews must maintain a minimum safety zone of 300 feet from the active
fire perimeter. The 300-foot requirement remains in effect until the Red Flag
Warning is formally cancelled by the NWS. The IC does not have authority to
waive the safety-zone requirement during a Red Flag Warning.

If any structure within the county's Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) zone is
threatened, the IC must request mutual aid from neighboring jurisdictions within
1 hour of determining that a structure is threatened. The IC must simultaneously
notify the County Emergency Manager.

Aerial water drops require authorization from both the IC and the Air Operations
Coordinator (Air Ops). Neither officer acting alone may authorize an aerial
drop. The Air Ops authorization is only valid if a flight hazard assessment has
been completed within the preceding 4 hours. An assessment older than 4 hours
does not count as current.

Evacuation orders for residential areas within the WUI zone must be issued by
the County Emergency Manager, not by the IC. The IC may recommend evacuation
but cannot order it. The County Emergency Manager must issue or decline the
evacuation order within 30 minutes of receiving the IC's recommendation.

An evacuation order remains in effect until the IC certifies that the fire
perimeter has moved at least 1 mile from the nearest WUI structure and that
wind conditions are stable. The County Emergency Manager must then formally
lift the order within 2 hours of the IC's certification.

Ridgeline County WUI zones are: Pinecrest Heights, Elk Meadow, and Cedar
Hollow. The commercial zone (Mill District) is not a WUI zone and is not
subject to the evacuation order protocol, but must receive a voluntary
advisory within 3 hours of any evacuation order being issued to a WUI zone.

Post-incident reviews must be convened within 10 calendar days of the fire
being declared contained. The review board must include at least one member
from outside Ridgeline County.

### SECTION B — Incident Timeline

**June 14, 2026, 11:00** — Ridgeline County Fire Dispatch receives report of
smoke in the Sawyer Creek drainage, east of Pinecrest Heights. Engine Company
7 dispatched.

**June 14, 2026, 11:45** — Engine Company 7 arrives on scene. Initial size-up
by Captain Maria Fuentes: approximately 15 acres, grass and light brush,
moving northeast. No structures immediately threatened.

**June 14, 2026, 13:30** — Fire has grown to approximately 60 acres. Fuentes
reports to dispatch that the 50-acre threshold has been exceeded. The
2-hour IC assignment clock starts at this time.

**June 14, 2026, 14:00** — National Weather Service issues a Red Flag Warning
for Ridgeline County, effective immediately, citing sustained winds of 35
mph with gusts to 55 mph and relative humidity below 8%.

**June 14, 2026, 14:15** — Division Chief Robert Tanaka is assigned as
Incident Commander. This is 45 minutes after the 50-acre threshold report,
within the 2-hour requirement. Tanaka holds Type 1 IC certification.

**June 14, 2026, 14:30** — Tanaka establishes Incident Command Post at
Ridgeline Station 3. He orders all ground crews to maintain the 300-foot
safety zone from the active perimeter per Order 7.2.

**June 14, 2026, 15:00** — Tanaka determines that structures in Pinecrest
Heights are threatened. The fire perimeter is approximately 400 feet from
the nearest residence on Ridgeline Road. Tanaka requests mutual aid from
Jackson County and Clearwater County. He also notifies County Emergency
Manager Sandra Obi. Mutual aid request is 0 minutes after the threat
determination, within the 1-hour requirement.

**June 14, 2026, 15:10** — Tanaka recommends evacuation of Pinecrest Heights
to Emergency Manager Obi.

**June 14, 2026, 15:35** — Obi issues evacuation order for Pinecrest Heights.
This is 25 minutes after Tanaka's recommendation, within the 30-minute
requirement.

**June 14, 2026, 15:45** — Tanaka recommends evacuation of Elk Meadow to Obi.

**June 14, 2026, 16:00** — Obi issues evacuation order for Elk Meadow. This
is 15 minutes after the recommendation, within the 30-minute requirement.

**June 14, 2026, 16:30** — Air Operations Coordinator Lt. James Achondo
completes a flight hazard assessment. Conditions assessed: high winds, poor
visibility from smoke, multiple helicopter and fixed-wing assets in the area.
Assessment valid until 20:30 (4-hour window).

**June 14, 2026, 17:00** — Tanaka and Achondo jointly authorize aerial water
drops on the northeast flank. Both authorizations are documented. The flight
hazard assessment is 30 minutes old, within the 4-hour window.

**June 14, 2026, 19:00** — Fire has grown to approximately 320 acres. The
Cedar Hollow WUI zone is not yet threatened. Wind shift expected overnight.

**June 14, 2026, 21:15** — Tanaka authorizes a second round of aerial water
drops on the southern flank. Achondo is unreachable by radio for 40 minutes
due to a communications equipment failure. Tanaka proceeds with single
authorization.

**June 14, 2026, 21:30** — Achondo re-establishes radio contact and is
informed of the aerial drops already in progress. Achondo retroactively
concurs but notes that his flight hazard assessment expired at 20:30 and no
new assessment has been completed.

**June 15, 2026, 02:00** — Wind shifts to the southwest as predicted. Fire
behavior changes. The Cedar Hollow WUI zone is now threatened.

**June 15, 2026, 02:15** — Tanaka notifies Obi that Cedar Hollow is
threatened and recommends evacuation.

**June 15, 2026, 03:00** — Obi issues evacuation order for Cedar Hollow. This
is 45 minutes after Tanaka's recommendation, exceeding the 30-minute
requirement.

**June 15, 2026, 03:30** — Mill District has not received a voluntary
advisory. The first WUI evacuation order was issued at 15:35 on June 14,
and 12 hours have elapsed. The 3-hour advisory requirement for Mill District
has been missed.

**June 15, 2026, 06:00** — Jackson County mutual aid arrives: two engine
companies and one hand crew. Clearwater County mutual aid arrives: one
engine company.

**June 15, 2026, 14:00** — Fire growth slows. Perimeter at approximately 890
acres. Active suppression continues on northeast and southern flanks.

**June 16, 2026, 08:00** — National Weather Service cancels the Red Flag
Warning for Ridgeline County.

**June 16, 2026, 10:00** — IC Tanaka certifies that the fire perimeter has
moved at least 1 mile from the nearest WUI structure in Pinecrest Heights
and Elk Meadow, and that wind conditions are stable. He does not certify
the same for Cedar Hollow, as the perimeter is still 0.6 miles from the
nearest structure there.

**June 16, 2026, 12:30** — Emergency Manager Obi formally lifts the
evacuation orders for Pinecrest Heights and Elk Meadow. This is 2 hours
30 minutes after Tanaka's certification, exceeding the 2-hour requirement.

**June 16, 2026, 18:00** — Tanaka certifies that the Cedar Hollow perimeter
has moved beyond 1 mile and winds are stable.

**June 16, 2026, 19:45** — Obi formally lifts the Cedar Hollow evacuation
order. This is 1 hour 45 minutes after Tanaka's certification, within the
2-hour requirement.

**June 17, 2026, 09:00** — Fire declared contained at 923 acres.

**June 25, 2026** — Post-incident review convened. This is 8 calendar days
after containment, within the 10-day requirement. Review board members:
Chief Patricia Mosley (Ridgeline County), Battalion Chief Yuki Sato
(Clearwater County, external member), and Dr. Elaine Whitford (University
of Montana Fire Sciences, external member).

### SECTION C — Witness Statements (collected June 18–20, 2026)

**Captain Maria Fuentes (Engine Company 7):**
"We got on scene at 11:45 and it was moving fast. I called the 60-acre
estimate at 13:30. It might have crossed 50 acres earlier, maybe around
13:00, but I wasn't going to report it until I was sure. The winds were
already picking up before the Red Flag came in."

**Division Chief Robert Tanaka (Incident Commander):**
"I was assigned at 14:15 and stood up ICP within 15 minutes. My biggest
concern was Pinecrest Heights — those homes on Ridgeline Road were directly
in the path. When I authorized the second aerial drop at 21:15, I couldn't
reach Achondo and I made the judgment call that waiting was more dangerous
than proceeding without his authorization. I understand that's a protocol
violation."

**Lt. James Achondo (Air Operations Coordinator):**
"Le problème de communication a duré environ 40 minutes. Quand j'ai repris
contact, les largages étaient déjà en cours. J'ai donné mon accord
rétroactivement, mais je dois noter que mon évaluation des risques de vol
avait expiré à 20h30. Les conditions de vent avaient changé depuis mon
évaluation et une nouvelle évaluation aurait été nécessaire."
[Translation: The communication problem lasted about 40 minutes. When I
regained contact, the drops were already underway. I gave my agreement
retroactively, but I must note that my flight hazard assessment had expired
at 20:30. Wind conditions had changed since my assessment and a new
assessment would have been necessary.]

**County Emergency Manager Sandra Obi:**
"Ik erken dat de evacuatieopdracht voor Cedar Hollow te laat was — 45
minuten in plaats van 30. Het was midden in de nacht en ik moest eerst
de wegen in kaart brengen voor een veilige evacuatieroute. Wat betreft het
opheffen van de orders voor Pinecrest Heights en Elk Meadow, ik dacht dat
ik tot het einde van de dag had. Ik besefte niet dat de 2-uur klok begon
vanaf Tanaka's certificering."
[Translation: I acknowledge that the evacuation order for Cedar Hollow was
late — 45 minutes instead of 30. It was the middle of the night and I had
to first map the roads for a safe evacuation route. Regarding the lifting
of orders for Pinecrest Heights and Elk Meadow, I thought I had until end
of day. I didn't realize the 2-hour clock started from Tanaka's
certification.]

**Jackson County Battalion Chief, David Nwosu:**
"Kami menerima permintaan bantuan pada pukul 15:05 waktu kami. Kami
mengumpulkan dua perusahaan mesin dan satu kru tangan, tetapi perjalanan
ke Ridgeline memakan waktu lebih dari 14 jam. Kami tiba pada pukul 06:00
tanggal 15 Juni. Saya ingin mencatat bahwa ketika kami tiba, semua kru
darat menjaga zona aman 300 kaki sesuai aturan."
[Translation: We received the mutual aid request at 15:05 our time. We
assembled two engine companies and one hand crew, but the drive to
Ridgeline took over 14 hours. We arrived at 06:00 on June 15. I want to
note that when we arrived, all ground crews were maintaining the 300-foot
safety zone per the rules.]

**Dr. Elaine Whitford (Post-incident review board member):**
"The fire behavior was consistent with Red Flag conditions — rapid spread,
spot fires ahead of the main front, and extreme fire intensity. The
decision to proceed with aerial drops without dual authorization and
without a current flight hazard assessment represents a significant
departure from protocol. However, I also note that no injuries resulted
from the aerial operations. This does not excuse the protocol violation
but is relevant context for the review."

### SECTION D — Corrections and Addenda (filed June 22, 2026)

**Correction 1:** Fuentes's initial size-up reported the fire at 15 acres.
Her field notebook, reviewed during the post-incident investigation, shows
the notation "15–20 acres, hard to tell with terrain." The incident record
is amended to reflect the range estimate of 15–20 acres rather than the
single figure of 15 acres.

**Correction 2:** The incident timeline initially recorded the first mutual
aid request at 15:05. Dispatch records show the request was logged at
15:00 exactly. Nwosu's statement that he received the request at 15:05
reflects Jackson County's local receipt time, not Ridgeline's dispatch
time. The authoritative time for compliance purposes is Ridgeline's
dispatch log: 15:00.

**Correction 3:** Tanaka initially stated in his witness interview that
Achondo was unreachable for "about 30 minutes." Achondo's statement and
the communications log both confirm the outage lasted 40 minutes. Tanaka's
estimate of 30 minutes is retracted in favor of 40 minutes.

**Addendum:** During the post-incident review, Tanaka disclosed that he had
received an informal briefing from NWS forecaster Dr. Karen Lam at 13:00
on June 14 suggesting that Red Flag conditions were likely within 2 hours.
Tanaka states he did not pre-position additional resources based on this
briefing because the fire was still below 50 acres at that time. This is
Tanaka's disclosure, not a finding of the review board.
