## Answers

1. 15-20 acres (corrected from initially reported 15 acres; Fuentes's field notebook shows '15-20 acres, hard to tell with terrain')
2. Approximately 13:30 on June 14, when Fuentes reported approximately 60 acres. Fuentes's witness statement suggests the fire may have crossed 50 acres around 13:00, but the official threshold report was at 13:30.
3. Yes — the 2-hour clock started at 13:30 (threshold report) and Tanaka was assigned at 14:15, which is 45 minutes later, within the 2-hour requirement
4. Type 1 IC certification, which exceeds the minimum Type 2 requirement
5. The County Emergency Manager (Sandra Obi), not the IC. The IC may recommend evacuation but cannot order it.
6. No — Mill District is a commercial zone and is not subject to the evacuation order protocol
7. Yes — Mill District must receive a voluntary advisory within 3 hours of any evacuation order being issued to a WUI zone
8. No — the first WUI evacuation order was issued at 15:35 on June 14, giving a 3-hour deadline of 18:35. As of 03:30 on June 15 (12 hours later), Mill District had still not received an advisory. This is a violation.
9. No — two violations: (1) Tanaka authorized without Achondo's co-authorization, because Achondo was unreachable due to communications failure; (2) The flight hazard assessment had expired at 20:30, 45 minutes before the authorization. Achondo's retroactive concurrence does not cure either violation.
10. No — the IC does not have authority to waive the safety-zone requirement during a Red Flag Warning
11. Issued June 14 at 14:00, cancelled June 16 at 08:00
12. No — Tanaka recommended evacuation at 02:15 on June 15. Obi issued the order at 03:00, which is 45 minutes later, exceeding the 30-minute requirement by 15 minutes.
13. Yes — Tanaka recommended at 15:10, Obi issued at 15:35, which is 25 minutes later, within the 30-minute requirement
14. Yes — Tanaka recommended at 15:45, Obi issued at 16:00, which is 15 minutes later, within the 30-minute requirement
15. No — Tanaka certified perimeter clearance at 10:00 on June 16, giving a 2-hour deadline of 12:00. Obi lifted the orders at 12:30, which is 30 minutes late.
16. Yes — Tanaka certified at 18:00 on June 16, Obi lifted at 19:45, which is 1 hour 45 minutes later, within the 2-hour requirement
17. Authorization from both the Incident Commander and the Air Operations Coordinator. Neither officer acting alone may authorize an aerial drop.
18. A flight hazard assessment must have been completed within the preceding 4 hours. An assessment older than 4 hours does not count as current.
19. Yes — both Tanaka and Achondo authorized it, and the flight hazard assessment completed at 16:30 was 30 minutes old, within the 4-hour window
20. No — the policy requires authorization from both officers, not retroactive concurrence after operations have begun. Additionally, the flight hazard assessment had expired regardless of Achondo's concurrence.
21. 45 minutes — the assessment expired at 20:30 and Tanaka authorized at 21:15
22. 40 minutes, corrected from Tanaka's initial estimate of 30 minutes. Both Achondo's statement and the communications log confirm 40 minutes.
23. 15:00, per Ridgeline's dispatch log. Nwosu's statement that he received the request at 15:05 reflects Jackson County's local receipt time. The dispatch log is authoritative for compliance purposes.
24. No — it is Fuentes's retrospective estimate from her witness statement. The official threshold report was at 13:30. The 13:00 time is a claim, not a fact.
25. The document does not establish this as a policy violation. It is Tanaka's disclosure, not a finding of the review board.
26. He said (in French) that his flight hazard assessment had expired at 20:30, wind conditions had changed since his assessment, and a new assessment would have been necessary
27. She said (in Dutch) that she acknowledges the order was late — 45 minutes instead of 30 — and that she had to map roads for a safe evacuation route because it was the middle of the night
28. He said (in Indonesian) that when his crews arrived at 06:00 on June 15, all ground crews were maintaining the 300-foot safety zone per the rules
29. Pinecrest Heights (15:35 June 14), Elk Meadow (16:00 June 14), and Cedar Hollow (03:00 June 15)
30. Jackson County: two engine companies and one hand crew (arrived 06:00 June 15). Clearwater County: one engine company (arrived 06:00 June 15).
31. 923 acres, declared contained on June 17 at 09:00
32. Yes — the fire was declared contained on June 17 and the review was convened on June 25, which is 8 calendar days later, within the 10-day requirement
33. The board must include at least one member from outside Ridgeline County
34. Yes — Battalion Chief Yuki Sato is from Clearwater County and Dr. Elaine Whitford is from the University of Montana. Both are external members.
35. 1) Aerial drop at 21:15 authorized by Tanaka alone without Achondo's co-authorization; 2) Aerial drop at 21:15 authorized with an expired flight hazard assessment (expired 20:30, 45 minutes prior); 3) Cedar Hollow evacuation order issued 45 minutes after recommendation, exceeding 30-minute requirement; 4) Mill District voluntary advisory never issued, violating 3-hour requirement; 5) Pinecrest Heights and Elk Meadow evacuation orders lifted 2.5 hours after IC certification, exceeding 2-hour requirement
36. No — mutual aid was requested at 15:00, simultaneously with the threat determination, which is 0 minutes after and within the 1-hour requirement
37. No — the document records that Tanaka ordered all ground crews to maintain the 300-foot safety zone, and Nwosu confirmed compliance when mutual aid arrived. No violation of the safety-zone requirement is documented.
38. No — Whitford explicitly states that the absence of injuries 'does not excuse the protocol violation but is relevant context for the review.' The observation is context, not exoneration.
39. 1) 15:35 June 14 — Pinecrest Heights evacuation order issued; 2) 16:00 June 14 — Elk Meadow evacuation order issued; 3) 03:00 June 15 — Cedar Hollow evacuation order issued; 4) 12:30 June 16 — Pinecrest Heights and Elk Meadow orders lifted; 5) 19:45 June 16 — Cedar Hollow order lifted
40. Two would remain: (1) the aerial drop at 21:15 without Achondo's co-authorization, and (2) the aerial drop at 21:15 with an expired flight hazard assessment. The Cedar Hollow timing, the order-lifting timing, and the Mill District advisory violations would all be resolved.
