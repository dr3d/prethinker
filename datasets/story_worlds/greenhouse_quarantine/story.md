# Cranesbrook Botanical Nursery — Quarantine and Status Record

## Background

Cranesbrook Botanical Nursery operates eight greenhouses (GH-1 through GH-8) growing ornamental plants for wholesale distribution. On August 12, 2025, Plant Health Inspector Margit Solberg identified suspected citrus canker (Xanthomonas citri) on specimens in GH-3 during a routine inspection. This triggered a quarantine and monitoring protocol that expanded over the following weeks.

This record documents the quarantine status of all affected lots through October 1, 2025.

## Initial Quarantine — August 12

Inspector Solberg's initial report:

- **GH-3, Lot 3A (Meyer lemon, 40 plants)**: Visible lesions on 6 plants. Classified as **suspect**. All 40 plants placed under quarantine hold. No movement permitted.
- **GH-3, Lot 3B (Calamondin, 25 plants)**: No visible symptoms. Classified as **precautionary hold** due to proximity to Lot 3A within the same greenhouse. No movement permitted pending lab results.

Solberg sent leaf samples from Lot 3A to the Regional Plant Health Laboratory. Results expected within 10 business days.

## Lab Result — August 26

The laboratory report (ref: RPHL-2025-4471) confirmed citrus canker in 4 of the 6 sampled plants from Lot 3A. The other 2 samples were negative.

Solberg's updated classifications:

- **Lot 3A**: Reclassified from **suspect** to **confirmed positive**. Destruction order issued for all 40 plants. Destruction must be completed within 14 calendar days of the order (by September 9).
- **Lot 3B**: Remains on **precautionary hold**. Solberg ordered additional testing of Lot 3B (5 random samples). Results expected by September 8.

## Scope Expansion — August 28

Nursery manager Devon Hartwell reported to Solberg that Lot 3A plants had been temporarily moved to GH-5 on July 30 for repotting, then returned to GH-3 on August 2. This movement was not disclosed during the initial inspection.

Solberg's response:

- **GH-5, Lot 5A (Kumquat, 30 plants)**: Classified as **suspect** due to shared greenhouse exposure during July 30 – August 2. Quarantine hold applied. Sampling ordered (5 plants).
- **GH-5, Lot 5B (Satsuma, 20 plants)**: Classified as **precautionary hold**. Lot 5B was in GH-5 during the overlap period but on a separate bench with no shared irrigation. Hold applied pending Lot 5A results.
- **GH-5, Lot 5C (Blood orange, 15 plants)**: **Not quarantined**. Lot 5C arrived in GH-5 on August 5, after the Lot 3A plants were returned to GH-3. No overlap exposure.

Solberg's note: "The scope expansion to GH-5 is based on the undisclosed July 30 movement. GH-1, GH-2, GH-4, GH-6, GH-7, and GH-8 are not affected by this quarantine unless further movements are disclosed."

## Lot 3B Lab Result — September 5

All 5 samples from Lot 3B tested negative.

Solberg's updated classification:

- **Lot 3B**: Reclassified from **precautionary hold** to **cleared**. Quarantine lifted. Lot 3B may resume normal operations, including distribution, effective immediately.

## Lot 3A Destruction — September 8

Nursery records confirm all 40 Lot 3A plants were destroyed on September 8, one day before the deadline. Destruction was supervised by nursery foreman Ola Nyberg and witnessed by Inspector Solberg.

Lot 3A status: **destroyed**.

## Lot 5A Lab Result — September 10

3 of 5 samples from Lot 5A tested positive for citrus canker.

Solberg's updated classifications:

- **Lot 5A**: Reclassified from **suspect** to **confirmed positive**. Destruction order issued. Deadline: September 24 (14 calendar days).
- **Lot 5B**: Elevated from **precautionary hold** to **suspect**. Solberg ordered additional sampling (8 plants instead of the standard 5) because Lot 5B shared GH-5 with the now-confirmed Lot 5A for the entire period since August 28. Note: the "separate bench, no shared irrigation" fact from the August 28 assessment is not retracted, but Solberg states: "Proximity alone warrants elevated sampling given the confirmed status of the adjacent lot."

## Mistaken Inclusion — September 15

Nursery manager Hartwell contacted Solberg to report that 5 plants from Lot 5C had been mistakenly moved to the Lot 5B bench on September 12 by a seasonal worker who misread the lot tags. The plants were returned to Lot 5C on September 14 when Hartwell noticed the error.

Solberg's response:

- **Lot 5C (5 affected plants)**: The 5 moved plants are reclassified as **suspect** and separated from the remaining 10 Lot 5C plants. They are placed under quarantine hold. Sampling ordered.
- **Lot 5C (remaining 10 plants)**: Retain their **not quarantined** status. They were never in contact with quarantined lots.

Solberg's note: "This is a targeted action for the 5 misplaced plants only. It does not change the status of the 10 unmoved Lot 5C plants or any other greenhouse."

## Lot 5B Lab Result — September 22

All 8 samples from Lot 5B tested negative.

Solberg's updated classification:

- **Lot 5B**: Reclassified from **suspect** to **cleared**. Quarantine lifted effective immediately.

## Lot 5A Destruction — September 23

All 30 Lot 5A plants were destroyed on September 23, one day before the deadline. Supervised by Nyberg, witnessed by Solberg.

Lot 5A status: **destroyed**.

## Lot 5C Subset Lab Result — September 29

All 5 samples from the mistakenly moved Lot 5C subset tested negative.

Solberg's updated classification:

- **Lot 5C (5 previously suspect plants)**: Reclassified from **suspect** to **cleared**. Returned to the main Lot 5C bench. Quarantine lifted.

The Lot 5C subset quarantine was justified by the mistaken September 12 movement. No further action is recommended unless new symptoms are reported."
