# Fenmore Seed Bank Conservation Ledger

## Overview

The Fenmore Regional Seed Bank is a conservation facility that preserves seeds
of native plant species from the Fenmore watershed. This ledger covers the
period January through April 2026. It records accessions, viability tests,
storage assignments, transfers, and deaccessions.

## Accession Protocols

Each seed lot entering the bank receives an accession number in the format
FB-YYYY-NNN. The accession record must include: species name, collector name,
collection site, collection date, estimated seed count, and initial condition
assessment (rated A through D, where A is excellent and D is non-viable).

Seeds rated D at accession are not stored. They are logged for the record and
deaccessioned immediately with reason "non-viable at intake."

Seeds rated A, B, or C are assigned to a storage vault. Vault assignment
depends on species type: Vault 1 (temperate grasses), Vault 2 (wildflowers
and forbs), Vault 3 (woody species — trees and shrubs). Seeds requiring
cryogenic storage are assigned to Vault 4 regardless of species type.

Viability testing is performed on each stored lot within 90 days of accession.
The test measures germination rate over a 21-day trial. A lot with germination
rate below 40% is reclassified to condition D and scheduled for deaccession.
A lot between 40% and 65% is reclassified to condition C. Above 65% is
confirmed at its current rating or upgraded.

**Transfer T-002 (April 10, 2026):**
Request from the University of Fenmore Botany Department for 200 seeds of
FB-2026-001 (Elymus canadensis) for a germination study. Authorized by
Bank Director Voss. Remaining count in Vault 1: approximately 4,000.

**Transfer T-003 (April 18, 2026):**
Request from Ridgetop Garden Club for 1,000 seeds of FB-2026-004 (Asclepias
tuberosa) for a community pollinator garden. Director Voss declined the
request: lot FB-2026-004 has only 2,100 seeds and a condition C rating.
Bank policy requires maintaining at least 1,500 seeds in storage for any
lot rated C or below to ensure long-term viability. Transferring 1,000
would leave only 1,100, below the threshold. Request denied.
