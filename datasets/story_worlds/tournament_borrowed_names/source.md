# The Whitburn Autumn Archery Tournament — Marshal's Record

## Tournament Structure

The Whitburn Autumn Archery Tournament is held annually on the village green. The 2025 tournament took place September 27–28. Competitors enter as individuals but compete under inherited team banners. This year, six team banners were active: Stag, Heron, Fox, Owl, Raven, and Kingfisher.

Team banners pass from one competitor to the next by tradition, not by family. The current banner holder chooses a successor at the end of each season. A competitor is addressed by their banner name during competition (e.g., "the Stag") but retains their given name in all administrative records.

This is the Marshal's record for scoring, disputes, and administrative matters. The Marshal for 2025 is Edgar Whittle.

Neither Emmet nor Venn competes on Day 2.

## Day 2 — September 28: Elimination Round

**Semifinal 1: Fox (Jupp) vs Raven (Mace)**

12 arrows at 70m, alternating.

Result: Fox 104, Raven 98. Fox advances.

**Semifinal 2: Stag (Calloway) vs Heron (Darke)**

12 arrows at 70m, alternating.

During this match, the wind shifted. Darke's 8th arrow was released during a gust and struck the target boss frame. Marshal Whittle ruled it a valid shot scoring zero, not a misfire. Darke protested, arguing that safety protocol required a hold call from the range officer when wind exceeded the safety threshold. Assistant range officer Bryce Emmet, who was on duty during the shot, stated: "The gust was sudden. I did not call a hold because it arrived during the release, not before."

Whittle ruled: "The arrow was released before any hold could be called. The shot stands as scored. Mr. Darke's protest is noted but denied."

Result: Stag 101, Heron 94. Stag advances.

**Final: Fox (Jupp) vs Stag (Calloway)**

12 arrows at 70m, alternating.

Result: Fox 108, Stag 106. **Fox wins the 2025 tournament.**

Substitute scorer Orla Venn recorded the final match scores and certified them.
