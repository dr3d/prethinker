# Portmere Harbor — Collision Investigation Summary

## Incident Overview

At approximately 14:35 on August 7, 2025, the motor vessel Heron's Wake (registered owner: Callister Marine Ltd) collided with the anchored barge Foundry Six (registered owner: Deepwater Logistics) in the inner harbor of Portmere. The collision caused hull damage to both vessels, a fuel spill from Heron's Wake estimated at 80–120 liters, and a minor injury to Foundry Six deckhand Pavel Novak (bruised ribs, treated at Portmere General Hospital, released same day).

Harbor Master Captain Elise Drummond convened an investigation under Harbor Regulation 17. Four sources submitted written statements. The investigation summary below reflects the Harbor Master's assessment of each source, the points of agreement, the points of conflict, and the preliminary findings.

## Source A: Captain Leo Vickers, Master of Heron's Wake

Vickers states that Heron's Wake was transiting the inner harbor at 5 knots, within the posted 6-knot speed limit. He states that Foundry Six was anchored without displaying the required anchor ball (a black spherical day shape required under International Regulations for Preventing Collisions at Sea, Rule 30). He claims he did not see Foundry Six until approximately 200 meters, at which point he reversed engines but could not stop in time.

Vickers attributes the collision to Foundry Six's failure to display proper day shapes: "If the anchor ball had been displayed, I would have identified the barge as stationary much earlier and altered course."

He denies that Heron's Wake was traveling above the speed limit and states that his GPS log will confirm 5 knots. He has not yet submitted the GPS log.

## Source B: Deckhand Pavel Novak, Foundry Six

Novak states that the anchor ball was displayed. He personally hoisted it when Foundry Six anchored at 11:20 that morning. He states: "The ball was up. I checked it at noon when I ate lunch on deck. It was still up at 14:00 when I went below to the engine room."

Novak estimates that Heron's Wake was traveling "much faster than usual, maybe 8 or 9 knots." He states he heard no horn signal before the collision.

Novak acknowledges he was below deck at the time of collision and did not directly observe the approach or the anchor ball status at the moment of impact.

## Source C: Harbor Pilot Yusuf Ghali

Ghali was aboard the tug Kestrel, approximately 300 meters east of the collision point, guiding an inbound container ship. He witnessed the collision from that distance.

Ghali states: "I saw Heron's Wake approaching from the north. She appeared to be traveling fast for the inner harbor — I would estimate 7 knots, maybe more. I did not observe an anchor ball on Foundry Six, but I was focused on my own vessel and the container ship. I cannot say the ball was absent; I simply did not look for it."

Ghali also states he heard one short horn blast from Heron's Wake approximately 10 seconds before the collision, inconsistent with Novak's claim that no horn signal was given.

## Source D: CCTV Footage (Harbor Camera 4)

Harbor Camera 4 recorded the inner harbor from 14:30 to 14:40. The footage was reviewed by Harbor Master Drummond and Harbor Technician Owen Searle.

Drummond's assessment of the footage: "The camera angle does not show the anchor ball position on Foundry Six. It does show Heron's Wake approaching from the north. Comparing the vessel's progress against fixed harbor markers, Searle estimates Heron's Wake speed at 6.5 to 7 knots. The footage shows a brief wake disturbance consistent with engine reversal approximately 8 seconds before contact."

Searle's technical note: "The 6.5-7 knot estimate has a margin of error of approximately ±1 knot due to camera angle and tide. A speed of 5.5 to 8 knots is within the plausible range. The footage cannot definitively confirm or exclude either party's speed claim."

## Points of Agreement

All sources agree:
- The collision occurred at approximately 14:35.
- Heron's Wake was approaching from the north.
- Foundry Six was stationary in the inner harbor.
- There was hull damage to both vessels.
- A fuel spill occurred from Heron's Wake.
- Novak was injured (bruised ribs).

## Points of Conflict

**Speed of Heron's Wake:**
- Vickers claims 5 knots (within the 6-knot limit).
- Novak estimates 8-9 knots (above the limit).
- Ghali estimates 7 knots (above the limit).
- CCTV analysis estimates 6.5-7 knots with ±1 knot margin (inconclusive: 5.5-8 knots).

**Anchor ball on Foundry Six:**
- Vickers claims no anchor ball was displayed.
- Novak claims it was displayed (he hoisted it, checked it at noon and 14:00).
- Ghali did not observe it but cannot confirm absence.
- CCTV cannot resolve it due to camera angle.

**Horn signal:**
- Novak claims no horn signal was given.
- Ghali states he heard one short blast approximately 10 seconds before collision.
- Vickers does not address horn signals in his statement.
- CCTV is a visual-only recording.

## Harbor Master Preliminary Findings

Captain Drummond issued the following preliminary findings on August 21, 2025:

**Finding 1 — Speed:** "Heron's Wake was likely traveling above the 6-knot speed limit. The CCTV estimate of 6.5-7 knots is supported by Ghali's observation. Vickers's claim of 5 knots is not supported by the available evidence. However, the CCTV margin of error means I cannot determine the speed to a legal standard of certainty. I note the probable exceedance but do not issue a speed violation at this time."

**Finding 2 — Anchor ball:** "The anchor ball question is unresolved. Novak's testimony is credible but he was below deck at the time of collision. Vickers's claim is self-interested but not disproven. Ghali's testimony is neutral but inconclusive. The CCTV does not resolve it. I make no finding on this point."

**Finding 3 — Horn signal:** "Ghali's testimony that he heard one short blast is more credible than Novak's denial, because Novak was below deck and engine-room noise would reduce his ability to hear an external signal. However, one short blast is an improper signal for the situation (it indicates altering course to starboard, not an emergency warning). Vickers should have sounded five or more short blasts (the danger signal). I find that a horn signal was likely given but was not the correct signal."

**Finding 4 — Fuel spill:** "The spill is confirmed. The volume is estimated at 80-120 liters based on cleanup records. Responsibility for spill costs is assigned to Callister Marine Ltd as the vessel owner, pending final determination of liability."

**Finding 5 — Injury:** "Novak's injury is confirmed. It is classified as minor and non-disabling."

**Finding 6 — Liability:** "Preliminary liability is shared. Heron's Wake was likely exceeding the speed limit and used an improper horn signal. The anchor ball question is unresolved and, if Foundry Six failed to display it, that would constitute contributory fault. Final liability apportionment is deferred pending Vickers's GPS log submission and any additional evidence."

**Status:** The investigation remains open. No final liability determination has been made. Vickers has been directed to submit the GPS log within 14 days (by September 4). As of August 28, the log has not been received.
