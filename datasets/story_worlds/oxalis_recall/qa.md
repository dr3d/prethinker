## Answers

1. [OX-001] Oxalis Model 7200 Infusion Pump, manufactured by Veridian Medical Systems
2. [OX-002] 6 lots: 7200-2024-A through 7200-2024-F
3. [OX-003] 4,200 units, corrected from initially reported 4,800. The 4,800 figure incorrectly included 600 units from Lot G, which was manufactured with corrected firmware.
4. [OX-004] Class II, classified by FDA on January 22, 2026
5. [OX-005] Class I, reclassified by FDA on February 3, 2026
6. [OX-006] 24 hours from FDA classification
7. [OX-007] 72 hours from FDA classification
8. [OX-008] The notification clock reset from the date of reclassification (February 3). A new 24-hour deadline applied. Distributors who had already acknowledged under Class II had to re-acknowledge under Class I within 5 business days of the new notification.
9. [OX-009] Yes — FDA classified on January 22, Veridian notified all 14 distributors on January 23 (24 hours later), within the 72-hour Class II requirement
10. [OX-010] Yes — FDA reclassified on February 3, Veridian completed all 14 re-notifications by 22:00 EST the same day (8 hours elapsed), within the 24-hour requirement
11. [OX-011] Yes — re-notification was February 3, acknowledgment was February 10, which is 5 business days later (Feb 4, 5, 6, 7, 10), within the 5-business-day requirement
12. [OX-012] No — Cascade acknowledged on March 5, which is 22 business days after the February 3 re-notification. This exceeds the 5-business-day requirement by 17 business days.
13. [OX-013] Yes — Cascade acknowledged March 5 and submitted its reconciliation report March 20, which is 11 business days after acknowledgment, within the 15-business-day requirement
14. [OX-014] February 26, 2026 (15 business days from February 5)
15. [OX-015] Yes — all 12 submitted by February 26
16. [OX-016] 91.6% (3,847 of 4,200 units)
17. [OX-017] 353 units
18. [OX-018] 98.3% (4,127 of 4,200 units)
19. [OX-019] 2,190 returned to Veridian, 892 confirmed destroyed at distributor facilities, 765 confirmed still in hospital inventory with firmware updates applied
20. [OX-020] FDA determined that 353 unaccounted units (8.4%) exceeded the threshold for termination. Veridian was required to continue recall efforts.
21. [OX-021] No — Clarification 1 explicitly states that the denial is a regulatory action reflecting FDA's assessment that the reconciliation threshold was not met. It does not imply that Veridian's recall efforts have been inadequate.
22. [OX-022] Yes — the request was submitted April 1 and FDA responded April 25, which is 24 calendar days, within the 30-calendar-day response window
23. [OX-023] Yes — the request was submitted May 15 and FDA responded June 10, which is 26 calendar days, within the 30-calendar-day window
24. [OX-024] Class II — the recall was classified as Class II on January 22 and was not reclassified to Class I until February 3
25. [OX-025] Class I — the recall was reclassified from Class II to Class I on February 3
26. [OX-026] Active — the first termination request was denied on April 25, and the second request was not submitted until May 15
27. [OX-027] Terminated — FDA approved termination on June 10
28. [OX-028] Initially Lot C only (700 units, January 18). Expanded to all 6 lots A through F (4,200 units, January 30). The scope expansion preceded the reclassification from Class II to Class I (February 3).
29. [OX-029] A faulty stepper motor calibration routine in the firmware, affecting all lots manufactured between March and August 2024
30. [OX-030] MedLine initially reported the adverse events involved Lots A and E. Pharmacy records confirm the devices were from Lots A and D. The lot reference was corrected from E to D.
31. [OX-031] Cascade initially reported 190 units received, corrected to 180. The discrepancy was a duplicate entry in Cascade's inventory system.
32. [OX-032] Molina said (in Spanish) that they received the re-notification February 3, acknowledged February 10, submitted their reconciliation report February 28, all deadlines were met, and of 310 units received they returned 285 and confirmed destruction of 25
33. [OX-033] Hale said (in Dutch) that the email went to the spam filter and the phone number Veridian used was a discontinued number. He apologized and reported all 180 units accounted for: 120 returned, 40 destroyed, 20 updated with firmware in the field
34. [OX-034] Tanaka said (in Japanese) that the root cause was the firmware stepper motor calibration routine, all lots manufactured March through August 2024 were affected, the corrective firmware was ready February 12, and was applied to all located units by March 15
35. [OX-035] All 310: 285 returned and 25 destroyed
36. [OX-036] All 180 (corrected from 190): 120 returned, 40 destroyed, 20 updated with firmware
37. [OX-037] No — Lot G was manufactured in September 2024 with corrected firmware and is not affected. It was erroneously included in the initial unit count of 4,800.
38. [OX-038] Jan 18: voluntary recall of Lot C initiated. Jan 22: FDA classifies as Class II. Jan 30: recall expanded to all 6 lots. Feb 3: FDA reclassifies to Class I. Apr 1: first termination request submitted. Apr 25: first termination denied. May 15: second termination request submitted. Jun 10: termination approved.
39. [OX-039] 72 hours (the Class II deadline), not 24 hours. Distributors would have had until January 25 to receive the original notification. No re-notification or re-acknowledgment would have been required.
40. [OX-040] Met: initial Class II notification (all 14 within 72 hours); Class I re-notification (all 14 within 24 hours); 13 of 14 acknowledgments within 5 business days; all 14 reconciliation reports within 15 business days of their respective acknowledgments. Missed: Cascade Medical's acknowledgment (22 business days, exceeding the 5-business-day requirement). Only one deadline was missed across the entire recall.
