# The Oxalis Recall

## Source Document: Medical Device Recall Tracking Record, FDA Correspondence, Distributor Compliance Reports, and Corrective Action Log

### SECTION A — Recall Classification Rules (21 CFR Part 7, simplified)

**Class I Recall:** A situation in which there is a reasonable probability
that the use of or exposure to a violative product will cause serious
adverse health consequences or death.

**Class II Recall:** A situation in which use of or exposure to a violative
product may cause temporary or medically reversible adverse health
consequences, or where the probability of serious adverse health
consequences is remote.

**Class III Recall:** A situation in which use of or exposure to a violative
product is not likely to cause adverse health consequences.

The manufacturer must notify all affected distributors within 24 hours of
an FDA recall classification for Class I, within 72 hours for Class II,
and within 10 business days for Class III.

After notification, distributors must acknowledge receipt within 5 business
days and provide a reconciliation report (accounting for all units received,
sold, returned, and remaining in inventory) within 15 business days of
acknowledgment.

If a recall is reclassified from a lower class to a higher class (e.g.,
Class III to Class II, or Class II to Class I), the notification clock
resets from the date of reclassification. Distributors who have already
acknowledged under the lower classification must re-acknowledge under the
higher classification within the applicable new deadline.

A recall is terminated when the FDA determines that all reasonable efforts
have been made to remove or correct the product and that the product
remaining in the market presents no significant risk. The manufacturer
must request termination in writing. FDA must respond within 30 calendar
days.

### SECTION B — Product Information

**Product:** Oxalis Model 7200 Infusion Pump
**Manufacturer:** Veridian Medical Systems, Inc. (Austin, TX)
**FDA Registration:** 3009847201
**Product Code:** FRN (Infusion Pump, general)
**Lot Numbers Affected:** 7200-2024-A through 7200-2024-F (6 lots)
**Units Manufactured:** 4,200 units across 6 lots
**Distribution:** Sold to 14 distributors across 9 states

### SECTION C — Recall Timeline

**January 15, 2026** — Veridian receives 3 adverse event reports (MDRs)
from hospitals reporting that the Oxalis 7200 intermittently delivers
doses exceeding the programmed rate by 15-40%. All events involved
Lot 7200-2024-C. No patient deaths; 2 patients required ICU observation.

**January 18, 2026** — Veridian initiates a voluntary recall of Lot
7200-2024-C (700 units). Veridian reports to FDA.

**January 22, 2026** — FDA classifies the recall as **Class II**. The
72-hour distributor notification clock starts.

**January 23, 2026** — Veridian notifies all 14 distributors of the
Class II recall by certified letter. This is 24 hours after
classification, within the 72-hour requirement.

**January 28, 2026** — Distributor MedLine West (San Francisco) reports
that 2 additional adverse events have occurred involving Lots 7200-2024-A
and 7200-2024-D, not just Lot C. Both events involved dose exceedance
of 20-30%.

**January 30, 2026** — Veridian expands the recall to include all 6 lots
(7200-2024-A through F, 4,200 total units). Reports expansion to FDA.

**February 3, 2026** — FDA reclassifies the recall from **Class II to
Class I**, citing the expanded scope, the pattern of dose exceedance across
multiple lots suggesting a systemic manufacturing defect, and the ICU
observations. The 24-hour notification clock starts.

**February 3, 2026, 14:00 EST** — Veridian begins re-notification of all
14 distributors by phone and email. Notifications completed by February 3,
22:00 EST. Total elapsed: 8 hours. Within the 24-hour requirement.

**February 5, 2026** — 12 of 14 distributors have acknowledged the Class I
re-notification. This is 2 business days after the re-notification,
within the 5-business-day requirement.

**February 7, 2026 (Friday)** — 13 of 14 distributors have acknowledged.
The remaining distributor, Pinnacle Health Supply (Denver), has not
responded.

**February 10, 2026 (Monday)** — Pinnacle Health Supply acknowledges. This
is 5 business days after the February 3 re-notification (Feb 3 = day 0;
Feb 4, 5, 6, 7, 10 = 5 business days). Within the 5-business-day
requirement.

**February 12, 2026** — Veridian's Quality Director, Dr. Keiko Tanaka,
reports to Veridian's CEO that root cause analysis has identified a
faulty stepper motor calibration routine in the firmware. The defect
affects all 6 lots manufactured between March and August 2024. A firmware
fix is available.

**February 20, 2026** — 10 of 14 distributors have submitted
reconciliation reports. The 15-business-day deadline from acknowledgment
is February 26 for the 12 distributors who acknowledged on February 5,
and February 28 for Pinnacle Health Supply (who acknowledged February 10).

**February 26, 2026** — 12 of 12 distributors who acknowledged February 5
have submitted reconciliation reports. On time.

**February 28, 2026** — Pinnacle Health Supply submits its reconciliation
report. On time (15 business days from February 10 acknowledgment).

**March 1, 2026** — The 14th distributor, Cascade Medical (Portland), has
still not acknowledged the Class I re-notification. It is now 26 calendar
days since the February 3 re-notification. Veridian escalates to FDA.

**March 5, 2026** — FDA contacts Cascade Medical directly. Cascade
responds the same day, stating that the re-notification email was caught
by a spam filter and the phone call went to a discontinued number.
Cascade acknowledges the recall on March 5.

**March 5, 2026** — Cascade Medical's acknowledgment is 30 calendar days
(22 business days) after the February 3 re-notification. This exceeds the
5-business-day requirement.

**March 15, 2026** — Veridian reports to FDA that 3,847 of 4,200 units
have been accounted for: 2,190 returned to Veridian, 892 confirmed
destroyed at distributor facilities, 765 confirmed still in hospital
inventory with firmware updates applied. 353 units are unaccounted for.

**March 20, 2026** — Cascade Medical submits its reconciliation report.
This is 15 calendar days after its March 5 acknowledgment, but only 11
business days. Within the 15-business-day requirement.

**April 1, 2026** — Veridian submits a written request to FDA for recall
termination, citing 91.6% unit reconciliation (3,847 of 4,200) and
completed firmware corrective action on all located units.

**April 25, 2026** — FDA responds to the termination request: **denied**.
FDA states that 353 unaccounted units (8.4%) exceeds the threshold for
termination and that Veridian must continue recall efforts. FDA's response
is 24 calendar days after the April 1 request, within the 30-calendar-day
response window.

**May 10, 2026** — Veridian locates 280 of the 353 unaccounted units
through secondary distributor tracking. 200 are returned; 80 have
firmware updates applied in the field. 73 units remain unaccounted for.

**May 15, 2026** — Veridian submits a second termination request, citing
98.3% unit reconciliation (4,127 of 4,200) and 73 units unaccounted.

**June 10, 2026** — FDA approves recall termination. FDA's response is 26
calendar days after the May 15 request, within the 30-day window. The
recall is officially terminated.

### SECTION E — Corrections and Clarifications

**Correction 1 (February 15, 2026):** Veridian's initial recall notice
stated 4,800 total units across the 6 lots. Veridian's manufacturing
records confirm 4,200 units. The 4,800 figure included 600 units from
Lot 7200-2024-G, which was manufactured in September 2024 with corrected
firmware and is not affected by the recall. The total is corrected to
4,200.

**Correction 2 (March 8, 2026):** MedLine West's January 28 report
initially stated that the adverse events involved Lots A and E. MedLine's
pharmacy records confirm the devices were from Lots A and D. The lot
reference is corrected from E to D.

**Correction 3 (March 22, 2026):** Cascade Medical initially reported 190
units received. Cascade's receiving records confirm 180 units. The
discrepancy is attributed to a duplicate entry in Cascade's inventory
system. Corrected to 180.

**Clarification 1 (April 28, 2026):** FDA's denial of the first
termination request is a regulatory action, not a finding of fault
against Veridian. The denial reflects FDA's assessment that the
reconciliation threshold has not been met. It does not imply that
Veridian's recall efforts have been inadequate.
