# Heronvale Arts Grant Program

## Program Rules (Heronvale Community Foundation, 2026 Cycle)

**Rule 1 — Eligibility.** An applicant must be a Heronvale resident or a
Heronvale-based organization. Residency is established by a current
utility bill or property tax record showing a Heronvale address.

**Rule 2 — Project Scope.** The project must be publicly accessible —
meaning open to the general public without membership, invitation, or
admission fee. A project open only to members of an organization is not
publicly accessible. A project with a suggested donation but no mandatory
fee is considered publicly accessible.

**Rule 3 — Prior Grant Restriction.** An applicant who received a
Heronvale Arts Grant in either of the two preceding fiscal years is
ineligible, unless the prior grant was for a different art form than the
current application. A painter who previously received a grant for painting
is ineligible; a painter who previously received a grant for sculpture
may apply for a painting grant.

**Rule 4 — Matching Requirement.** Projects requesting more than $5,000
must show matching support equal to at least 25% of the request from a
non-Foundation source. In-kind contributions count toward the match if
valued by an independent appraiser. Volunteer time does not count.

**Rule 5 — Community Impact Priority.** Among eligible applications, the
Foundation gives priority to projects serving underrepresented communities,
defined as communities where the median household income is below the
county median. Priority does not guarantee funding; it is a tiebreaker
when two otherwise equal applications compete for the same funding slot.

**Rule 6 — Environmental Exception.** A project that involves permanent
alteration of a public natural space (such as land art, installations in
parks, or tree removal for sculpture) requires additional approval from
the Parks Commission before the Foundation may disburse funds. The grant
may be conditionally approved pending Parks Commission approval, but
funds may not be released until Parks approval is received.

**Rule 7 — Disqualification.** An applicant who submits materially false
information in the application is permanently disqualified from all future
Heronvale Arts Grant cycles. A clerical error corrected before the review
deadline is not considered materially false.

**Rule 8 — Review Panel Composition.** The Review Panel consists of 3
members: one Foundation trustee, one practicing artist from the community,
and one at-large public member. A panelist who has a personal or financial
relationship with an applicant must recuse. If recusal reduces the panel
below 3, a substitute is appointed by the Foundation Director.

### APPLICATIONS

**Application A: Dara Okafor — "Murals for Main Street"**
- Residency: Heronvale (utility bill on file)
- Project: paint 5 public murals on building exteriors along Main Street
- Art form: painting (murals)
- Prior grant: received a $4,000 photography grant in FY2025
- Request: $7,500
- Matching: $2,000 cash from Main Street Business Alliance
- Public access: murals on public-facing exterior walls, no fee
- Community impact: Main Street is in a census tract below county median income
- Environmental: not applicable (building exteriors, not natural space)

**Application B: Whitebirch Folk Ensemble — "Summer Concert Series"**
- Residency: Heronvale-based nonprofit
- Project: 6 free outdoor concerts in Riverside Park, June through August
- Art form: music (folk)
- Prior grant: received a $6,000 music grant in FY2024
- Request: $8,000
- Matching: $3,500 cash from the Heronvale Merchants Association, plus $1,000
  in-kind (sound equipment loan, valued by independent appraiser)
- Public access: free, open-air, no fee or membership required
- Community impact: Riverside Park serves a mixed-income area, not classified
  as underrepresented
- Environmental: temporary stage setup in a park, removed after each concert.
  The Foundation previously ruled that temporary installations do not trigger
  Rule 6, but permanent fixtures do.

**Application C: Nadia Espinoza — "Watershed Sculptures"**
- Residency: Heronvale (property tax record on file)
- Project: install 3 permanent stone sculptures along the Heronvale Creek
  trail, incorporating native plantings around each base
- Art form: sculpture
- Prior grant: none
- Request: $12,000
- Matching: $4,000 cash from the Heronvale Land Trust, plus volunteer
  planting labor valued at $2,000
- Public access: on a public trail, no fee
- Community impact: the Creek trail runs through an underrepresented area
- Environmental: permanent installation in a natural space along a creek —
  Rule 6 triggered. Parks Commission approval required before disbursement.

**Application D: Leo Kwan — "Ceramics for Kids"**
- Residency: Kwan's studio is in Heronvale, but his home address is in
  neighboring Birchfield Township. His utility bill shows the Birchfield
  address. His Heronvale studio lease is on file but is a commercial lease,
  not a residential utility bill or property tax record.
- Project: after-school ceramics workshop for children ages 8-14
- Art form: ceramics
- Prior grant: none
- Request: $3,500
- Matching: not required (request is $5,000 or less)
- Public access: open to all children in the age range, no fee, but held at
  Kwan's private studio with limited enrollment (12 spots)
- Community impact: the workshop targets a low-income neighborhood

**Application E: Heronvale Poetry Circle — "Voices Unheard"**
- Residency: Heronvale-based informal group (not registered as nonprofit or
  any legal entity)
- Project: monthly spoken-word events at the public library, plus a printed
  anthology
- Art form: poetry (literary arts)
- Prior grant: none
- Request: $2,500
- Matching: not required (request is $5,000 or less)
- Public access: library events are free and open; the anthology would be
  distributed free
- Community impact: library is in a census tract below county median income

### REVIEW PANEL NOTES (April 10, 2026)

**Panelists:** Trustee Margaret Liu (Chair), Artist Carlos Reyes, Public
Member Jenna Whitfield.

**Application A (Okafor):** Eligible. Prior grant was for photography
(FY2025), not painting. Rule 3 exception applies — different art form.
Matching: $2,000 / $7,500 = 26.7%, exceeds 25%. Community impact priority
applies (Main Street is underrepresented). Recommended for approval.

**Application B (Whitebirch):** Prior grant was for music in FY2024. Current
application is also music. FY2024 is within the two preceding fiscal years.
Rule 3 applies — same art form. Panel notes this makes Whitebirch
ineligible unless the Rule 3 exception for different art forms applies.
It does not: both grants are for music. **Denied under Rule 3.**

**Application C (Espinoza):** Eligible. No prior grant. Matching: $4,000
cash counts; $2,000 volunteer time does not count per Rule 4. Effective
match: $4,000 / $12,000 = 33.3%, exceeds 25%. Community impact priority
applies. Environmental: Rule 6 triggered — permanent installation in
natural space. Conditionally approved pending Parks Commission approval.

Reyes recuses from voting on Application C because he has exhibited
sculpture at the same Heronvale Creek trail location. Liu and Whitfield
remain (2 panelists). A substitute is needed per Rule 8 because the panel
is below 3. Foundation Director appoints Dr. Amara Osei as substitute.
Vote proceeds with Liu, Whitfield, Osei: conditional approval, 3-0.

**Application D (Kwan):** Residency question. Kwan's home is in Birchfield.
His Heronvale studio is a commercial lease. Rule 1 requires a utility bill
or property tax record showing a Heronvale address. A commercial lease is
neither. Panel finds Kwan does not meet the residency requirement.
**Denied under Rule 1.**

Additionally, the panel notes but does not decide the public-access question:
the workshop has limited enrollment (12 spots) and is held at a private
studio. Whether this is "open to the general public" under Rule 2 is
ambiguous and was not reached because Rule 1 already disqualified Kwan.

**Application E (Poetry Circle):** Eligibility question. The Poetry Circle
is not a registered nonprofit or legal entity. Rule 1 says the applicant
must be a "Heronvale resident or a Heronvale-based organization." An
informal group may not qualify as an "organization." The panel defers to
the Foundation Director for interpretation.

Foundation Director's interpretation (April 15, 2026): "An informal group
without legal entity status does not qualify as a 'Heronvale-based
organization' under Rule 1. However, an individual member of the group who
is a Heronvale resident may apply as an individual on behalf of the project.
I recommend the Poetry Circle resubmit with a named individual applicant."

Poetry Circle has not yet resubmitted as of April 30, 2026.
