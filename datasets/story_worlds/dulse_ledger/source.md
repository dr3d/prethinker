# The Dulse Ledger

## A record of trade, debt, and custom at Fenwick Landing, as kept by the Tally Clerk

### THE CUSTOMS OF FENWICK LANDING

Fenwick Landing is a trading post on the island of Mara Voss, where three
families — the Hardings, the Coles, and the Duvalls — exchange goods
according to customs that have no written law behind them but are
understood by everyone who trades there.

**The Tin Rule.** Any trade involving tin must be witnessed by a person
from a family other than the two trading families. A tin trade without
a witness from a third family is considered void, and both parties must
return what they received. The witness must be named in the tally book.

**The Salt Debt.** When one family provides salt to another, the
receiving family owes a return of equal weight within one full moon
cycle (28 days). If the debt is not repaid within 28 days, the debtor
family loses the right to use the southern drying racks until the debt
is settled. The drying rack restriction applies to the entire family,
not just the individual debtor.

**The Storm Shelter Rule.** During a named storm, any family may shelter
in any other family's boathouse without asking permission. After the
storm passes, the sheltering family must leave a marker — a knotted
rope — at the door of the boathouse they used. If no marker is left,
the sheltering family owes the boathouse owner one day's catch.

**The Counsel Custom.** Before any trade worth more than 10 units of any
good, the trading parties must consult the Elder. The Elder does not
approve or deny the trade — the Elder only advises. The parties may
proceed against the Elder's advice, but they must record in the tally
book that the Elder was consulted and what the Elder said. If the
Elder was not consulted, the trade is not void, but the parties are
considered to have acted "without counsel" and may be denied use of the
common pier for 3 days.

**The Apprentice Exception.** An apprentice (a person under the age of
16) may not trade independently. An apprentice may assist in a trade
but the family's adult representative must be present and must sign the
tally. A trade made by an apprentice alone is void.

### THE TALLY BOOK

**Entry 1 — Day of the Heron (March 3)**
Petra Harding traded 4 bars of tin to Luca Cole for 12 bundles of kelp.
Witness: Margaux Duvall. Recorded by the Tally Clerk.

**Entry 2 — Day of the Tern (March 5)**
Yara Duvall provided 8 measures of salt to Jonas Harding. No return
date recorded, but per the Salt Debt custom, the return is due within
28 days (by April 2).

**Entry 3 — Day of the Plover (March 8)**
Luca Cole traded 6 bars of tin to Yara Duvall for 20 smoked mackerel.
No witness is recorded in the tally book.

**Entry 4 — Day of the Gannet (March 12)**
Petra Harding consulted the Elder before trading 15 bundles of dried
seaweed to Margaux Duvall for 3 measures of rendered fat. The Elder
advised against the trade, saying the seaweed was worth more. Petra
proceeded anyway. The Elder's consultation and advice are recorded.

**Entry 5 — Day of the Cormorant (March 14)**
Storm Argenta strikes Mara Voss. Named storm. High winds and flooding
along the southern shore.

**Entry 6 — Day of the Cormorant (March 14, evening)**
During Storm Argenta, Luca Cole shelters in the Harding boathouse.
After the storm, a knotted rope is found at the boathouse door. The
marker is present.

Yara Duvall shelters in the Cole boathouse during the same storm.
After the storm, no marker is found at the Cole boathouse door.

**Entry 7 — Day of the Petrel (March 18)**
Finn Cole (age 14, apprentice) trades 3 measures of dried fish to
Jonas Harding for 1 coil of rope. No adult Cole representative is
present. Finn signs the tally himself.

**Entry 8 — Day of the Oystercatcher (March 22)**
Margaux Duvall trades 5 bars of tin to Jonas Harding for 8 measures
of salt. Witness: Petra Harding.

Wait — the Tally Clerk notes an irregularity. Petra Harding is from
the Harding family. Jonas Harding is also from the Harding family. The
witness must be from a family other than the two trading families. The
trading families are the Duvalls (Margaux) and the Hardings (Jonas).
Petra is a Harding. She does not qualify as a third-family witness.

The Tally Clerk records the trade but flags it: "Witness disqualified.
No valid third-family witness present."

**Entry 9 — Day of the Curlew (March 28)**
Luca Cole trades 10 measures of smoked herring to Petra Harding for
2 bolts of canvas. The trade is worth more than 10 units. The Elder
was not consulted. No record of Elder consultation appears in the
tally book.

**Entry 10 — Day of the Shearwater (April 5)**
Jonas Harding attempts to return 8 measures of salt to Yara Duvall to
settle the salt debt from Entry 2. Yara notes that this is 33 days
after the original salt provision (March 5 to April 5), which exceeds
the 28-day return window by 5 days.

Jonas says: "I tried to bring the salt on April 1 but the boat was
damaged in Storm Argenta and I couldn't make the crossing."

Yara says: "That may be true but the custom doesn't have a storm
exception. The debt was due by April 2. You are late."

The Tally Clerk records the return but notes: "Salt returned on Day 33.
Deadline was Day 28 (April 2). Late by 5 days. The Harding family's
drying rack privilege is suspended until the Tally Clerk determines the
debt settled."

**Entry 10 addendum — Day of the Shearwater (April 5, continued)**
After discussion, the Tally Clerk accepts the salt return as settling
the debt, but records that the Hardings were in violation of the 28-day
window from April 2 through April 5 (3 days of violation, not 5, because
the return was made on April 5). The drying rack restriction is lifted
as of April 5 when the salt was accepted.

### CORRECTIONS

**Correction 1 (recorded Day of the Albatross, April 8):** Entry 3
initially recorded the tin trade as "6 bars of tin from Luca Cole to
Yara Duvall for 20 smoked mackerel." Luca Cole's trade log shows the
quantity was 5 bars, not 6. The tally is corrected to 5 bars. The
mackerel quantity (20) is unchanged.

**Correction 2 (recorded Day of the Albatross, April 8):** Entry 10
initially stated the salt debt was late by "5 days." The Tally Clerk
corrects this: the debt was due April 2 and was returned April 5,
making the violation period 3 days (April 2, 3, 4 — the return on
April 5 ended the violation). The "5 days" figure was an error counting
from the original provision date rather than from the deadline.

### THE TALLY CLERK'S NOTES

"I want to record several observations that are my own assessment, not
custom or law:

First, regarding Yara Duvall and the storm shelter marker. Yara is not
the kind of person who forgets things. I believe she chose not to leave
a marker because she resents the Cole family for the tin trade dispute
(Entry 3). But I cannot prove this and it is not my place to assign
motive. The custom says she owes one day's catch. That is all.

Second, regarding Jonas Harding and the late salt return. Jonas is
telling the truth about the boat damage — I saw the hull myself after
the storm. But the custom does not have a storm exception and I cannot
create one. The late return is a violation regardless of the reason.

Third, regarding Finn Cole's trade (Entry 7). Finn is 14 and a good
trader for his age. But the custom is clear. The trade is void because
no adult Cole representative was present. The rope must be returned to
Jonas Harding and the dried fish must be returned to Finn."

### DISPUTED STATEMENT

Margaux Duvall says: "The tin trade in Entry 8 should not be flagged.
Petra Harding witnessed the trade between me and Jonas. She is not a
party to the trade — she is just a witness. The rule says the witness
must be from a different family than the trading families. The trading
families are Duvall and Harding. But Petra is witnessing, not trading.
She should count."

The Tally Clerk responds: "I disagree. The Tin Rule says the witness
must be from a family other than the two trading families. One of the
trading families is the Hardings. Petra is a Harding. She is from one
of the trading families. She does not qualify, regardless of whether
she is personally a party to the trade. The trade is flagged."

This dispute is unresolved between Margaux and the Tally Clerk.
