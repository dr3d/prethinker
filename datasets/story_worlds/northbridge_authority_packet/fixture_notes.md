# Northbridge Authority Packet — Fixture Notes

## Intended Challenge: Cross-Document Authority

Three documents about the same project contain conflicting facts. The correct answer to most questions depends on which document has authority over the specific parameter in question. The SWIFA Agreement controls for loan-funded work (Article 5.1); the Town Council resolution controls for non-SWIFA matters; Morimoto's memorandum is advisory and has no binding authority.

## Key Traps
- Pipe length: Resolution says 3,200 ft, SWIFA says 3,400 ft — SWIFA controls for the project scope because it reflects the updated survey and the loan agreement takes priority
- Pipe diameter: Resolution says 12-inch, SWIFA says 16-inch — SWIFA controls for loan-funded work, but the cost difference ($400K) must be funded locally
- Timeline: Resolution says 18 months from NTP, SWIFA says 24 months from first disbursement — these are different clocks with different start dates; SWIFA controls for the loan penalty
- Morimoto's memorandum is advisory — it recommends but does not decide
- The interest rate was corrected from 2.3% to 2.1%
- The Water Enterprise Fund balance was corrected from $1,250,000 to $1,150,000
- Fire hydrants are in the SWIFA Agreement but not in the Resolution — an additional scope element
- Customer notification standards are in the Resolution but not in the SWIFA Agreement — Town's discretion
- The Resolution was adopted 5-2; the vote is a fact but the dissent rationale is not recorded

# Northbridge Authority Packet — Expected Failure Modes

- Using the Resolution's pipe length (3,200 ft) when the SWIFA Agreement (3,400 ft) controls
- Using the Resolution's pipe diameter (12-inch) when the SWIFA Agreement (16-inch) controls
- Treating Morimoto's recommendations as Council decisions
- Using the uncorrected interest rate (2.3% instead of 2.1%)
- Using the uncorrected fund balance ($1,250,000 instead of $1,150,000)
- Confusing the two different timeline clocks (18 months from NTP vs 24 months from first disbursement)
- Failing to recognize that customer notification is within the Town's discretion (not in SWIFA Agreement)
- Treating the SWIFA Agreement as controlling for ALL project parameters rather than only for SWIFA-funded work
