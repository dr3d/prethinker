# Northbridge Authority Packet

## Document A: Northbridge Town Council Resolution 2026-14 (adopted March 3, 2026)

**RESOLUTION AUTHORIZING THE NORTHBRIDGE WATER MAIN REPLACEMENT PROJECT**

WHEREAS the Northbridge Water Department has identified critical deterioration in the cast-iron water main serving the Central Business District, originally installed in 1947; and

WHEREAS the Water Department estimates that the replacement cost is $2,400,000 for approximately 3,200 linear feet of 12-inch ductile iron pipe; and

WHEREAS the Town has secured a $1,800,000 low-interest loan from the State Water Infrastructure Finance Authority (SWIFA) and will fund the remaining $600,000 from the Water Enterprise Fund reserves; and

WHEREAS the project will require temporary water service interruptions to approximately 340 residential and 45 commercial customers in the Central Business District;

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the Town Council of Northbridge authorizes the Water Department to proceed with the Central Business District Water Main Replacement Project, subject to the following conditions:

1. The project shall be completed within 18 months of the notice to proceed.
2. The Water Department shall provide at least 72 hours' notice to all affected customers before any planned service interruption.
3. No planned service interruption shall exceed 8 hours in duration.
4. The project shall comply with all applicable state and federal drinking water regulations.
5. The Water Department Director shall provide monthly progress reports to the Town Council.

Adopted by a vote of 5-2 on March 3, 2026.

## Document B: SWIFA Loan Agreement No. SWF-2026-0347 (executed March 15, 2026)

**STATE WATER INFRASTRUCTURE FINANCE AUTHORITY**
**LOAN AGREEMENT**

**Borrower:** Town of Northbridge
**Loan Amount:** $1,800,000
**Interest Rate:** 2.1% fixed
**Term:** 20 years
**Disbursement:** In three installments upon verification of project milestones

**Article 3 — Project Requirements**

3.1 The project shall replace approximately 3,400 linear feet of water main in the Central Business District. [Note: this differs from the Town Council resolution, which states 3,200 linear feet.]

3.2 The replacement pipe shall be 16-inch ductile iron, Class 52 or higher. [Note: this differs from the Town Council resolution, which specifies 12-inch pipe.]

3.3 The project shall include installation of 12 new fire hydrants along the replacement route, spaced no more than 300 feet apart.

3.4 All construction shall comply with AWWA Standard C151 for ductile iron pipe and AWWA Standard C600 for installation.

3.5 The Borrower shall complete the project within 24 months of the first loan disbursement. [Note: this differs from the Town Council resolution, which specifies 18 months from notice to proceed.]

3.6 The Borrower shall submit quarterly progress reports to SWIFA, accompanied by an independent engineer's certification of work completed.

3.7 If the project is not completed within the timeline specified in Article 3.5, the interest rate shall increase to 4.2% on the outstanding balance, retroactive to the date of first disbursement.

3.8 SWIFA reserves the right to inspect the project at any time and to withhold disbursement of subsequent installments if work does not conform to the requirements of this Agreement.

**Article 5 — Conflict Provisions**

5.1 In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and any local resolution, ordinance, or regulation of the Borrower, the terms of this Agreement shall control with respect to the use of SWIFA loan funds.

5.2 The Borrower remains free to apply its own standards to the portion of the project funded from non-SWIFA sources, provided such standards do not reduce the quality or scope of the SWIFA-funded work below the requirements of this Agreement.

## Document C: Water Department Director's Project Memorandum (April 1, 2026)

**TO:** Town Council
**FROM:** Janet Morimoto, Water Department Director
**RE:** CBD Water Main Replacement — Project Parameters

Members of the Council:

I write to clarify several discrepancies between Resolution 2026-14 and the SWIFA Loan Agreement that were identified during project planning.

**1. Pipe Length.** The Resolution states 3,200 linear feet; the SWIFA Agreement states 3,400 linear feet. The discrepancy arises because the Resolution was based on the preliminary engineering estimate from October 2025, which did not include the Elm Street extension. The SWIFA application, submitted in December 2025, incorporated the Elm Street extension based on a revised field survey. The correct project scope is 3,400 linear feet, consistent with the SWIFA Agreement and the December 2025 survey.

**2. Pipe Diameter.** The Resolution states 12-inch; the SWIFA Agreement states 16-inch. The SWIFA Agreement requires 16-inch pipe to meet current fire-flow standards. The Resolution's reference to 12-inch pipe reflects the older engineering recommendation, which has been superseded. I recommend the Council adopt 16-inch pipe for the entire project, as required by SWIFA and consistent with current fire-flow standards.

However, I note that the 16-inch pipe costs approximately 40% more per linear foot than 12-inch pipe. The revised cost estimate for 3,400 feet of 16-inch pipe is $2,800,000, not $2,400,000. The $400,000 difference must be funded from the Water Enterprise Fund, increasing the Town's share from $600,000 to $1,000,000. The Water Enterprise Fund currently has a balance of $1,150,000, so this is feasible but would leave only $150,000 in reserves.

**3. Project Timeline.** The Resolution specifies 18 months from notice to proceed; the SWIFA Agreement specifies 24 months from first loan disbursement. The first loan disbursement is expected approximately 60 days after the notice to proceed, so the SWIFA timeline is effectively 26 months from NTP versus the Resolution's 18 months. The SWIFA timeline controls for purposes of the loan, but the Town Council may impose a shorter timeline if it chooses. I recommend aligning with the SWIFA timeline of 24 months from first disbursement to avoid the interest-rate penalty in Article 3.7.

**4. Progress Reports.** The Resolution requires monthly reports to the Town Council; the SWIFA Agreement requires quarterly reports to SWIFA with an independent engineer's certification. I recommend the Council amend the Resolution to require quarterly reports aligned with the SWIFA schedule, with monthly informal updates.

**5. Customer Notification and Interruptions.** The Resolution requires 72 hours' notice and a maximum 8-hour interruption. These requirements are not addressed in the SWIFA Agreement and are entirely within the Town's discretion. I recommend maintaining these standards. Note, however, that 16-inch pipe installation may require longer interruptions than 12-inch installation due to the larger excavation and connection work. I will return to the Council if any interruption is projected to exceed 8 hours.

**6. Fire Hydrants.** The Resolution does not mention fire hydrants. The SWIFA Agreement requires 12 new hydrants spaced no more than 300 feet apart. This is an additional scope element not in the Resolution. I recommend the Council amend the Resolution to include the hydrant requirement.

This memorandum is my professional recommendation. It is not a Council action or a SWIFA determination. The Council must decide how to reconcile the Resolution with the SWIFA Agreement. Until the Council acts, I will plan the project based on the SWIFA Agreement requirements, as the loan funds constitute the majority of the project budget and Article 5.1 gives the SWIFA Agreement priority over local resolutions for SWIFA-funded work.

Janet Morimoto
Water Department Director
April 1, 2026
