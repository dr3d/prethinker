# The Heathwell Museum of Local History — Relabelling Audit Report

## Background

The Heathwell Museum of Local History occupies a converted Georgian townhouse with twelve exhibition rooms. In January 2025, incoming curator Dr. Petra Vane discovered that several room placards, object labels, and the printed visitor guide contained factual errors inherited from a 2019 renovation. She commissioned an internal audit comparing placard text, the printed visitor guide (2020 edition), the museum's digital catalog, and original acquisition records.

This report documents the audit findings as of March 2025. It covers rooms where at least one discrepancy was found.

## Room 3 — "The Weaving Gallery"

**Placard**: "Room 3 houses the Thornton Loom, donated by the Thornton family in 1962. The loom dates to approximately 1780 and was used in the Heathwell wool trade."

**Digital catalog**: The Thornton Loom was donated in 1958, not 1962. Its estimated date is 1790-1810, not 1780. The catalog notes it was used in linen production, not wool.

**Visitor guide**: Matches the placard (1962, 1780, wool). The guide was printed from placard text, not from the catalog.

**Acquisition record**: Confirms 1958 donation. Original assessment card reads: "Loom, probably late 18th or early 19th century. Linen production. Donated by H. Thornton, March 1958."

**Curator's note**: "The placard is wrong on all three points: date, period, and material. The visitor guide copied the placard errors. Correct to match the acquisition record and catalog. New placard drafted March 4."

**Status**: New placard installed March 12. Visitor guide not yet reprinted.

## Room 5 — "The Shipwright's Workshop"

**Placard**: "This room recreates a Victorian shipwright's workshop. The centerpiece is a half-hull model of the brig Dovetail, built at Heathwell Yard in 1847."

**Digital catalog**: The half-hull model is cataloged as the brig Dovetail, built at Heathwell Yard, but the construction date is listed as 1852, not 1847.

**Visitor guide**: Lists the Dovetail as built in 1843. This matches neither the placard nor the catalog.

**Acquisition record**: "Half-hull model, brig Dovetail. Heathwell Yard. Acquired from yard estate sale, 1923. Model label reads '1852.' Yard records indicate keel laid 1851, launched 1852."

**Curator's note**: "The acquisition record and the model itself say 1852. The catalog is correct. The placard (1847) and visitor guide (1843) are both wrong. Correct the placard. Do not redate the model."

**Status**: New placard drafted March 10. Not yet installed.

## Room 7 — "The Civic Collection"

**Placard**: "Room 7 displays civic regalia including the Heathwell Mace, presented to the town by Alderman Jessop in 1901."

**Digital catalog**: The Heathwell Mace is listed as presented by Alderman Jessop in 1901. No discrepancy noted.

**Visitor guide**: Matches placard and catalog.

**Acquisition record**: "Mace. Presented by Alderman W. Jessop, 1901. Silver-gilt, 72cm. Hallmarked Birmingham 1899."

**Curator's note**: "Room 7 placard is correct. However, the volunteer docent script for Room 7 incorrectly states the mace was 'a gift from the mayor.' Jessop was an alderman, not the mayor. The docent script is not an official museum document but has been used for guided tours since 2021. Instruct docents to correct."

**Status**: Docent script corrected March 15. No placard change needed.

## Room 9 — "The Map Room"

**Placard**: "This room contains the Bourne Chart, a 1703 coastal survey by Captain James Bourne, covering the Heathwell estuary and its approaches."

**Digital catalog**: The Bourne Chart is listed as a 1703 coastal survey. The catalog adds: "Authenticity disputed. See conservator's report 2018-CR-041."

**Visitor guide**: "The Bourne Chart is one of the museum's treasures, an authentic 1703 survey of the estuary."

**Conservator's report 2018-CR-041**: "Ink analysis suggests the chart was produced between 1740 and 1770, not 1703. The paper is consistent with mid-18th century manufacture. The '1703' date on the chart may refer to the date of an earlier survey that this chart copies, or it may be a deliberate false dating. Recommend describing the chart as 'attributed to Captain James Bourne, date disputed, possibly mid-18th century copy of an earlier survey.'"

**Curator's note**: "The conservator's report is the most authoritative source. The placard and visitor guide both present the 1703 date without qualification. This is the most serious error in the audit: we are presenting a disputed item as authentic. New placard must include the disputed dating. The visitor guide description is actively misleading."

**Curator's additional note (March 18)**: "I proposed a revised placard reading 'attributed to Captain James Bourne, date disputed.' The Board of Trustees reviewed the proposal on March 16 and rejected it. The Board's position is that the chart has been displayed as a 1703 original since acquisition and that changing the label would 'undermine public confidence in the collection.' I disagree but the Board's decision controls the public display. The digital catalog retains the dispute note. The visitor guide will not be corrected on this item."

**Status**: No placard change. Board decision controls. Digital catalog preserves the dispute.

## Room 10 — "The Fossil Alcove"

**Placard**: "The centerpiece is a complete ichthyosaur skeleton discovered at Heathwell Cliffs in 1934 by amateur geologist Winifred Pole."

**Digital catalog**: Lists the discoverer as Winifred Pole, 1934. Species listed as "ichthyosaur, species not formally determined."

**Visitor guide**: "A complete ichthyosaur, discovered by Winifred Pole in 1934. The specimen is believed to be Ichthyosaurus communis."

**Acquisition record**: "Ichthyosaur skeleton. Discovered W. Pole, Heathwell Cliffs, June 1934. Extracted and donated by Pole. Species identification pending — initial assessment suggests temnodontosaurus, not ichthyosaurus."

**Curator's note**: "The visitor guide's species identification (Ichthyosaurus communis) appears to have been added editorially during the 2020 guide printing and does not match either the catalog or the acquisition record. The acquisition record's initial assessment suggests an entirely different genus. No formal species determination has ever been made. Correct the guide at next printing. Placard is acceptable because it says 'ichthyosaur' generically without a species claim."

**Status**: No placard change needed. Visitor guide correction queued for next printing.

## Room 12 — "The Memorial Gallery"

**Placard**: "This room honors Heathwell residents who served in the Great War. The Roll of Honour lists 347 names."

**Digital catalog**: Roll of Honour lists 344 names.

**Visitor guide**: States 347 names, matching the placard.

**Acquisition record**: The original Roll of Honour manuscript lists 341 names. Three names were added in 1924 after the original compilation, bringing the total to 344.

**Curator's note**: "The placard says 347, the catalog says 344, and the original document plus the 1924 additions total 344. I cannot determine where '347' originated. It may be a typographical error from the 2019 renovation placard printing. Correct the placard to 344."

**Status**: New placard drafted. Not yet installed.
