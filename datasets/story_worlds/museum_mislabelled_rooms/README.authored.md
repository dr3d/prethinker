# The Museum of Mislabelled Rooms

## Premise
A museum audit reveals that placards, visitor guides, digital catalogs, and original acquisition records disagree on dates, facts, species, and counts. A curator's corrections are sometimes overridden by the Board of Trustees. The visitor guide copies placard errors, the docent script introduces new errors, and one conservator's report disputes an item's authenticity entirely.

## Intended Difficulty
Hard. Attacks **labels vs facts**. The "official" public-facing labels (placards, visitor guide) are wrong. The authoritative sources (acquisition records, conservator reports) are correct. But the Board can override the curator's corrections, creating a situation where the public label is knowingly wrong and the museum's own records disagree.

## Domain
Museum administration / cultural heritage / document authority hierarchy
