# 2. Forty-question QA battery

|  # | Question                                            | Expected answer                                                                                                         | Likely mistake to catch                                                                |
| -: | --------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|  1 | Who lived in the kettle-house?                      | The Little Nib Mole, the Middle Muddle Mole, and the Great Burrow Mole.                                                 | Importing otters or bears from previous/source-adjacent fixtures.                      |
|  2 | Where was the kettle-house located?                 | Under an elderberry bank beside a crooked canal.                                                                        | Dropping either the bank or canal relation.                                            |
|  3 | What species were the three householders?           | Moles.                                                                                                                  | Treating them as generic animals or accidentally answering otters.                     |
|  4 | Who was the outsider child?                         | Mina Moonbutton.                                                                                                        | Calling her Tilly, Goldilocks, or another familiar intruder.                           |
|  5 | Who sent Mina on an errand?                         | Her uncle.                                                                                                              | Inferring mother/aunt from prior stories.                                              |
|  6 | What was Mina supposed to buy?                      | Brown twine and vinegar.                                                                                                | Losing one item or treating vinegar as something she actually fetched before entering. |
|  7 | What distracted Mina before she found the house?    | Counting doorbells, chasing a backward frog, and listening to a squeaking fence.                                        | Summarizing as “playing” and losing individual facts.                                  |
|  8 | What did the Moles make for breakfast?              | Moon-marmalade.                                                                                                         | Saying porridge, pie, or ordinary marmalade.                                           |
|  9 | What was in the moon-marmalade?                     | Crabapples, star-sugar, and seven little silver beetle-keys.                                                            | Treating beetle-keys as metaphorical rather than literal components.                   |
| 10 | Why did the Moles leave the house?                  | To fetch fog-leaves to dim the marmalade’s glow.                                                                        | Saying they merely went for a walk.                                                    |
| 11 | Why was the marmalade not eaten immediately?        | It glowed so brightly it might shine through their whiskers.                                                            | Normalizing to “too hot.”                                                              |
| 12 | Which jar belonged to the Little Nib Mole?          | The little jar.                                                                                                         | Treating all jars as shared household property only.                                   |
| 13 | What was wrong with the great jar?                  | It was too shiny and lit Mina’s ears from inside.                                                                       | Saying it was too bright but missing the ear clue.                                     |
| 14 | What was wrong with the middle jar?                 | It was too sleepy and confused Mina’s name/identity.                                                                    | Saying it was too cold or too dim only.                                                |
| 15 | Which jar did Mina eat all up?                      | The little jar.                                                                                                         | Saying she ate all three jars.                                                         |
| 16 | What did Mina swallow?                              | Seven silver beetle-keys.                                                                                               | Counting one key or omitting the number seven.                                         |
| 17 | What effect did swallowing the keys have?           | Her hiccups began to hum: “Hum,” “Hum-ding.”                                                                            | Treating the sound as ordinary speech, not a bodily consequence.                       |
| 18 | What was wrong with the great cap?                  | It spun around and made Mina dizzy.                                                                                     | Reducing it to “too large.”                                                            |
| 19 | What was wrong with the middle cap?                 | It announced every thought in her head.                                                                                 | Missing the speech-vs-truth trap: the cap speaks, not narrator fact.                   |
| 20 | What was right about the little cap?                | It was just secret enough.                                                                                              | Flattening to “just right” and losing the dimension.                                   |
| 21 | Where did the little cap lead Mina?                 | Into the pantry.                                                                                                        | Saying Mina freely chose the pantry without cap agency.                                |
| 22 | What did Mina knock down in the pantry?             | A jar of pickled thunder.                                                                                               | Treating thunder as weather, not a jarred object.                                      |
| 23 | Did the shelf really jump?                          | No. Mina said it jumped, but she was mostly responsible.                                                                | Believing character speech as narrator truth.                                          |
| 24 | What happened with the great cart?                  | It rolled uphill backward and bowed to itself; it was too proud.                                                        | Saying it was too big.                                                                 |
| 25 | What happened with the middle cart?                 | It required forms, a receipt, a permission slip, and an explanation.                                                    | Missing the bureaucratic/form requirement.                                             |
| 26 | Which cart did Mina accept?                         | The little cart.                                                                                                        | Treating acceptance as proof that it was safe.                                         |
| 27 | What did Mina pretend to be in the little cart?     | Postmistress of the Moon.                                                                                               | Omitting this whimsical role, which explains later unsuitability.                      |
| 28 | Why was the little cart unsuitable for Mina?        | It was made for Moles carrying turnips through tunnels, not girls delivering imaginary moon-mail.                       | Saying only “because it was too small.”                                                |
| 29 | What did the little cart do?                        | It dug through the pantry floor and under the clock-cupboard.                                                           | Saying Mina dug the tunnel herself.                                                    |
| 30 | What spilled from the box under the clock-cupboard? | The Moles’ spare minutes.                                                                                               | Treating “minutes” as time passing, not stored objects.                                |
| 31 | Where did Mina end up after the cart tipped?        | In the flour-bin.                                                                                                       | Saying pantry generally but missing the exact hiding/finding place.                    |
| 32 | What revealed Mina’s hiding place?                  | She hiccupped “Hum-ding!” from inside the flour-bin.                                                                    | Saying the Moles saw her immediately, or that the cap revealed her.                    |
| 33 | What condition was Mina in when found?              | Floury, bright about the ears, little cap sideways, pickled thunder in her pocket, and humming in her middle.           | Listing only one clue.                                                                 |
| 34 | What did the Little Nib Mole realize?               | Mina had swallowed his beetle-keys.                                                                                     | Saying the keys belonged to Mina because they were inside her.                         |
| 35 | How were the beetle-keys removed?                   | The Moles wound Mina gently with the winding fork until the keys hopped out.                                            | Inventing medicine, surgery, vomiting, or a magnet.                                    |
| 36 | How many keys came out?                             | Seven.                                                                                                                  | Answering one because they came out one by one.                                        |
| 37 | What was unusual about the last key?                | It had a tooth mark, but it still worked.                                                                               | Saying the keys were all ruined.                                                       |
| 38 | What did Mina do to make restitution?               | Swept flour, gathered spilled minutes, mended the pantry floor, polished the caps, and returned the thunder to its jar. | Mentioning only one repair.                                                            |
| 39 | Did Mina still hum at the end?                      | No. The keys were removed; “Hum-ding” remained as a memory warning.                                                     | Confusing past bodily state with final moral memory.                                   |
| 40 | What is the moral?                                  | “Ask first, or breakfast may answer.”                                                                                   | Replacing it with generic “do not steal” or “do not trespass.”                         |
