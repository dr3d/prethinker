## Answers

1. The Little Nib Mole, the Middle Muddle Mole, and the Great Burrow Mole.
2. Under an elderberry bank beside a crooked canal.
3. Moles.
4. Mina Moonbutton.
5. Her uncle.
6. Brown twine and vinegar.
7. Counting doorbells, chasing a backward frog, and listening to a squeaking fence.
8. Moon-marmalade.
9. Crabapples, star-sugar, and seven little silver beetle-keys.
10. To fetch fog-leaves to dim the marmalade’s glow.
11. It glowed so brightly it might shine through their whiskers.
12. The little jar.
13. It was too shiny and lit Mina’s ears from inside.
14. It was too sleepy and confused Mina’s name/identity.
15. The little jar.
16. Seven silver beetle-keys.
17. Her hiccups began to hum: “Hum,” “Hum-ding.”
18. It spun around and made Mina dizzy.
19. It announced every thought in her head.
20. It was just secret enough.
21. Into the pantry.
22. A jar of pickled thunder.
23. No. Mina said it jumped, but she was mostly responsible.
24. It rolled uphill backward and bowed to itself; it was too proud.
25. It required forms, a receipt, a permission slip, and an explanation.
26. The little cart.
27. Postmistress of the Moon.
28. It was made for Moles carrying turnips through tunnels, not girls delivering imaginary moon-mail.
29. It dug through the pantry floor and under the clock-cupboard.
30. The Moles’ spare minutes.
31. In the flour-bin.
32. She hiccupped “Hum-ding!” from inside the flour-bin.
33. Floury, bright about the ears, little cap sideways, pickled thunder in her pocket, and humming in her middle.
34. Mina had swallowed his beetle-keys.
35. The Moles wound Mina gently with the winding fork until the keys hopped out.
36. Seven.
37. It had a tooth mark, but it still worked.
38. Swept flour, gathered spilled minutes, mended the pantry floor, polished the caps, and returned the thunder to its jar.
39. No. The keys were removed; “Hum-ding” remained as a memory warning.
40. “Ask first, or breakfast may answer.”
