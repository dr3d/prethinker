# Harrowgate Witness File — Fixture Notes

## Intended Challenge: Claim vs Truth

The core difficulty is that no one in this document is lying, but their accounts are irreconcilable. Ashton heard one thing; Fraser says he said something different. Mehta says she gave a catalog; Fraser agrees but uses the word "document." The system must preserve every claim with its source and must not resolve the dispute because the official finding explicitly declines to resolve it.

## Key Traps
- "Not substantiated" ≠ "did not happen" ≠ "exonerated." The finding is a specific epistemic state: insufficient evidence to support the allegation. The system must preserve all three distinctions.
- Ashton's account of what Fraser said is secondhand hearsay that is directly contradicted by Fraser's own firsthand account
- The purged shared-drive logs create an evidentiary gap that the system must acknowledge without filling
- The Review Panel upheld the finding AND added a supplemental recommendation that is explicitly prospective and does not imply prior violation
- Fraser's statement is in French, Tan's in Indonesian, Estrada's in German
