# Harrowgate Witness File

## Combined Record: Internal Investigation Report, Witness Statements, Official Findings, and Appeal Outcome

### INVESTIGATION SUMMARY

**Case:** HWF-2026-003
**Subject:** Alleged unauthorized disclosure of proprietary pricing data
**Complainant:** VP of Sales, Derek Ashton
**Respondent:** Senior Account Manager, Priya Mehta
**Investigator:** Director of Compliance, Yusuf Okonkwo
**Investigation period:** February 10 to March 28, 2026

Ashton alleges that Mehta shared Harrowgate Industries' confidential wholesale pricing schedule with Dawnridge Supply Co., a competitor, during a January 2026 industry conference in Minneapolis. Ashton states he was told this by a Dawnridge employee named Colin Fraser at a separate dinner event on February 7.

### WITNESS STATEMENTS

### INVESTIGATOR'S FINDINGS (March 28, 2026)

**Finding 1:** Mehta attended the Minneapolis conference January 20-22 and interacted with Colin Fraser. This is undisputed.

**Finding 2:** Mehta provided Fraser with a document. This is confirmed by both Mehta and Fraser. Mehta says it was a public product catalog. Fraser says he thought it was a product catalog.

**Finding 3:** Ashton's account of what Fraser told him differs from Fraser's own statement. Ashton says Fraser identified the document as "our wholesale pricing sheet." Fraser says he told Ashton that "someone from Harrowgate gave me a document" without identifying it as a pricing sheet. This discrepancy is material.

**Finding 4:** Digital forensics found no evidence that Mehta accessed or transmitted the pricing schedule from her company laptop or email. However, the shared drive logs covering the conference period were already purged before the investigation began, leaving a gap in the forensic record.

**Finding 5 (determination):** Based on the evidence, Okonkwo finds that **the allegation is not substantiated**. The available evidence does not support a finding that Mehta disclosed proprietary pricing data. The document provided to Fraser appears to have been a public product catalog. Ashton's secondhand account of Fraser's statement is contradicted by Fraser's own statement.

**Finding 6 (caveat):** Okonkwo notes that the finding is based on available evidence and that the purged shared-drive logs represent an evidentiary gap. The finding does not exonerate Mehta — it states that the allegation cannot be substantiated on the current evidence.

### ASHTON'S OBJECTION (April 5, 2026)

Ashton files a written objection to Finding 5, arguing that:
(a) Fraser's statement is self-serving because Fraser would not want to admit receiving proprietary data from a competitor;
(b) Tan's statement confirms she was not with Mehta at all times, leaving an unobserved window;
(c) The purged logs mean the investigation cannot affirmatively exclude the possibility.

### REVIEW PANEL DECISION (April 22, 2026)

The Review Panel (3 members: General Counsel Victoria Lam, CFO André Beaumont, outside counsel Dr. Mika Oshiro) reviews the file.

**Decision:** Finding 5 is **upheld**. The allegation remains unsubstantiated. However, the Panel modifies the finding by adding:

**Supplemental Finding:** The Panel recommends that Harrowgate implement a policy requiring all conference materials distributed to external parties to be pre-approved by the VP of Sales or designee. This recommendation is prospective and does not imply that Mehta violated any existing policy.

**Panel Note:** The Panel agrees with Okonkwo that "not substantiated" is the appropriate finding. The Panel explicitly declines to find either (a) that the disclosure occurred or (b) that the disclosure did not occur. The evidence is insufficient to support either conclusion.
