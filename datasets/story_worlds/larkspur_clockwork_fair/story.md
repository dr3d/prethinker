# Larkspur Clockwork Fair

## Chronicle of the Forty-First Annual Larkspur Mechanical Exhibition

### THE EXHIBITORS AND THEIR MACHINES

The Forty-First Annual Larkspur Mechanical Exhibition opened on the first day of Ashmonth in the town of Larkspur-on-Wending. Twelve exhibitors registered. This chronicle records the seven whose machines produced notable events.

**Elara Voss** entered a device called the Pearlescent Loom. The Loom weaves thread into cloth that changes color depending on the ambient temperature. At registration, the Loom was fitted with a silver shuttle and threaded with Undyed Cotton No. 4. Elara is also known among the fair-goers as "the Weaver of Fiddler's Reach," her home village.

**Stellan Voss** — no relation to Elara despite the shared surname — entered the Resonance Bellows, a mechanical organ that plays notes tuned by barometric pressure. Stellan is from Cairnhollow, not Fiddler's Reach. He is sometimes mistakenly called "Elara's brother" in fair gossip, which both deny.

**Maren Blackwood** entered the Gilt Salamander, a clockwork automaton shaped like a salamander that navigates mazes by sensing heat gradients. At registration, the Salamander was painted gold with copper joints and carried a tiny lantern in its mouth. Maren also brought a second, unregistered device: a pocket-sized Compass Rose that she used for personal navigation around the fairgrounds.

**Tobias Wren**, also known as "Old Wren" or "the Tinker of Greystone Bridge," entered the Bottled Storm, a sealed glass sphere containing a miniature weather system powered by a copper coil. When activated, the Storm produces lightning, rain, and miniature thunder inside the sphere.

**Cassia Wren** — Tobias's granddaughter — entered the Moth Lantern, a device that attracts and gently captures moths using a warm amber light, then releases them unharmed at dawn. Cassia is 15 and is the youngest exhibitor. She is sometimes called "Little Wren" to distinguish her from Tobias.

**Dorian Hale** entered the Tidal Clock, a timepiece that adjusts its rate based on lunar phase data engraved on its main gear. The Clock was built from parts salvaged from a shipwrecked chronometer Dorian found on the coast near Saltmarsh.

**Neve Blackwood** — Maren's younger sister — did not enter a device but served as Maren's assistant throughout the exhibition. She is registered as an attendant, not an exhibitor.

### THE EVENTS

**Day 1 of Ashmonth — Opening**

All devices are inspected by Fair Warden Osric Thane. The Pearlescent Loom, Resonance Bellows, Gilt Salamander, Bottled Storm, Moth Lantern, and Tidal Clock are all certified as safe and placed in their assigned stalls. The Compass Rose is not inspected because it is not a registered exhibit.

Maren lends the Compass Rose to Cassia for the evening, because Cassia is unfamiliar with the fairgrounds and her assigned stall is far from the lodging tents.

**Day 3 of Ashmonth — The Thread Incident**

Elara discovers that her Undyed Cotton No. 4 has been replaced with Dyed Silk No. 7 — a deep indigo thread. She did not authorize the substitution. The Loom still functions but the cloth it produces is now permanently indigo rather than temperature-responsive, because Dyed Silk No. 7 has already been treated with a fixative that blocks the temperature-chromatic reaction.

Elara suspects Stellan, because he was seen near her stall the previous evening. Stellan denies touching the thread and says he was adjusting his own Bellows, which is in the adjacent stall.

Fair Warden Thane inspects the Loom and confirms the thread substitution. He does not determine who made the substitution. He orders Elara's stall sealed pending investigation and removes the Loom from exhibition until the matter is resolved.

**Day 5 of Ashmonth — The Salamander Escapes**

The Gilt Salamander escapes its maze enclosure during a demonstration. It navigates through the fairgrounds following the heat signature of the forge at the blacksmith's stall, where it is recovered by Neve Blackwood. During its journey, the Salamander's lantern ignites a stack of parchment notices near the Bottled Storm's stall. Tobias Wren extinguishes the fire before it reaches the Storm sphere.

After recovery, Neve notices that the Salamander's copper joints have been bent during the escape, and the gold paint on its left foreleg is scraped off. Maren repairs the joints but does not repaint the foreleg, saying the bare copper "gives it character."

The Compass Rose, which Cassia still has, is undamaged.

**Day 7 of Ashmonth — The Storm Cracks**

During a thunderstorm (real, not mechanical), the Bottled Storm's glass sphere develops a hairline crack. The miniature weather system continues to operate, but Tobias notices that the internal humidity is slowly equalizing with the ambient air, which will eventually kill the contained system. Tobias estimates the Storm has 48 hours of operational life remaining before the internal atmosphere is fully compromised.

Tobias asks Fair Warden Thane whether he may attempt a repair using wax sealant. Thane rules that the repair is permitted because it does not alter the device's fundamental mechanism, only stabilizes a containment failure.

**Day 8 of Ashmonth — The Moth Lantern Modification**

Cassia modifies the Moth Lantern to emit a cooler blue light instead of amber, because she has observed that the amber light is also attracting beetles, which damage the release mechanism. She does not ask Thane for permission before making the modification.

Thane discovers the modification during his evening rounds. He rules that changing the light frequency is an alteration of the device's fundamental mechanism (the amber wavelength is part of the design that selectively attracts moths over other insects). The Moth Lantern is disqualified from judging but may remain on display.

Cassia appeals to Thane, arguing that the modification improved the device. Thane responds: "Improvement is not the question. Authorization is the question. You did not seek approval before altering a certified exhibit."

**Day 9 of Ashmonth — The Thread Resolution**

Fair Warden Thane completes his investigation of the thread substitution. A fair attendant, Jem Harker, comes forward and admits that he accidentally swapped the thread spools while cleaning the stalls on Day 2. Harker mistook Dyed Silk No. 7, which belonged to an unregistered exhibitor in the adjacent overflow tent, for Elara's Undyed Cotton No. 4. The substitution was accidental, not deliberate.

Thane lifts the seal on Elara's stall and returns the Loom to exhibition. However, the Loom cannot be restored to its original state because the Dyed Silk No. 7 has been partially woven into the cloth and cannot be separated without damaging the Loom's mechanism. Elara must either exhibit with the indigo cloth or withdraw.

Elara chooses to exhibit with the indigo cloth and notes in the exhibition record: "The Loom's mechanism is unimpaired. The temperature-chromatic demonstration is compromised by the thread substitution, but the weaving function is intact."

Stellan is cleared of suspicion.

**Day 10 of Ashmonth — Closing and Awards**

Tobias's wax repair holds. The Bottled Storm is operational at closing. The crack is sealed but visible.

Maren's Gilt Salamander is exhibited with the bare copper foreleg. Judges note the damage but award the Salamander second place for "most inventive navigation mechanism."

Dorian's Tidal Clock is awarded first place for "most precise timekeeping under variable conditions."

The Moth Lantern is not eligible for awards due to disqualification. It remains on display.

The Pearlescent Loom is awarded an honorable mention with the notation: "Mechanism sound; demonstration compromised by circumstances beyond the exhibitor's control."

Cassia returns the Compass Rose to Maren at the closing ceremony. It is undamaged.

### CORRECTIONS

**Correction 1:** The chronicle initially recorded the thread substitution as occurring on Day 1. Jem Harker's statement confirms the swap occurred during stall cleaning on Day 2. Corrected.

**Correction 2:** The initial record stated that the Salamander's lantern ignited "a pile of fabric" near the Storm stall. Witnesses confirm it was parchment notices, not fabric. Corrected.
