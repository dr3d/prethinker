## 3. Errata Closed During The Window

### Erratum 1 — Barcode correction on the revolver

On 2026-04-04 the revolver (item now bearing label EX-C-01) was scanned at intake and the resulting barcode was recorded as **BC-833014**. On 2026-04-08, Sergeant Vidal noticed a digit transposition during a routine reconciliation. The barcode was rescanned and the correct value, **BC-883014**, was entered. The mistaken scan **BC-833014** must not be cited in any current report. The revolver itself, its slip (SLIP-7712A), its exhibit label (EX-C-01), and its bay location (BAY-04-L, Shelf 3) were not affected by the erratum. Only the barcode field was corrected.

### Erratum 2 — Case number typo correction

The cover sheet for Property Folder EX-P-3 was originally typed with the case number **CASE-22-O917**, which uses the capital letter "O" rather than a numeral zero. On 2026-04-15 this was identified by the Records Clerk and corrected to **CASE-22-0917**, with a numeral zero. The folder contents, exhibit label, barcode, and physical location are unchanged. Only the case number string was corrected. The string **CASE-22-O917** must be regarded as void and may not appear in any active filing.

## 5.5 Prior-Window Carryforward Notes

For continuity with the prior auditor's records, the following carryforward notes are reproduced. They are not active items in this window but explain the lineage of two current labels.

The label **EX-C-1** (no leading zero) was the original tag generated when the revolver was first booked into evidence in late 2023, under a pre-migration printer that produced single-digit suffixes. During the 2024 migration to the current zero-padded format, the migration script flagged the label as ambiguous — the printed glyph had a hyphen that could read either as a separator or as part of the digit field. The migration team chose to reissue the label rather than alter the original. The replacement, **EX-C-01**, was generated in the current format and applied on 2026-03-22, which closed the migration window for the C series. The withdrawn label EX-C-1 should not appear in any active query result.

Similarly, the label **EX-E-9** was issued on 2026-02-11 for the iPhone now bearing label **EX-E-10**. The original printer rendering left the trailing "9" so faintly that property staff reported scanning failures. Rather than reprint with the same number, the property sergeant elected to retire the digit entirely and reissue under EX-E-10 to avoid further confusion with EX-E-09 (the Samsung Galaxy, an unrelated item). The reissue took effect 2026-03-29.

Auditors are reminded that, despite the visually adjacent numbering, EX-E-09 and EX-E-10 refer to two physically distinct phones, and the withdrawn label EX-E-9 refers to the same physical phone now under EX-E-10.
