# The Sable Creek Budget Amendment

## Source Document: Municipal Budget Amendment Proceedings, Staff Analysis, Public Comment Record, and Post-Vote Analysis

### SECTION A — Budget Amendment Rules (Sable Creek Municipal Charter, Article IX)

**§9.1 — Amendment Authority.**
The Town Council may amend the adopted annual budget by a vote of at least
4 of the 7 Council members. The Mayor may vote only to break a tie. The
Mayor may not introduce a budget amendment; only Council members may do so.

**§9.2 — Public Hearing Requirement.**
Any budget amendment that increases total appropriations by more than
$50,000 requires a public hearing before the vote. The hearing must be
noticed in the Sable Creek Gazette at least 14 calendar days before the
hearing date. Amendments of $50,000 or less may be voted on at any
regular Council meeting without a hearing.

**§9.3 — Revenue Offset.**
Any budget amendment that increases appropriations must identify a
corresponding revenue source or offsetting reduction. The Town Manager
must certify in writing that the amendment is fiscally neutral or
positive before the Council may vote. An amendment that the Town Manager
cannot certify as fiscally neutral or positive may not proceed to a vote.

**§9.4 — Emergency Appropriations.**
In a declared emergency, the Mayor may authorize expenditures up to
$25,000 without Council approval. Expenditures above $25,000 require
Council approval under the normal amendment process (§9.1–§9.3), except
that the public hearing requirement (§9.2) is waived during declared
emergencies. The emergency must be declared by the Mayor and ratified by
a majority of the Council within 72 hours.

**§9.5 — Line-Item Transfers.**
The Town Manager may transfer funds between line items within the same
department without Council approval, provided the transfer does not
exceed $10,000 and does not eliminate any line item entirely. Transfers
exceeding $10,000 or transfers between departments require Council
approval by simple majority (4 of 7).

**§9.6 — Fiscal Year.**
The fiscal year runs from July 1 to June 30. All appropriations lapse
at the end of the fiscal year unless specifically designated as
multi-year appropriations by the Council at the time of adoption.

**§9.7 — Recall of Amendment.**
A budget amendment may be recalled (reversed) by the same voting
threshold required to adopt it (4 of 7 Council members). A recall vote
may be held at any regular or special meeting. The recall must be
proposed by a Council member and seconded by a different Council member.
The recalled amendment's appropriations revert to their prior levels.

### SECTION B — The Proposed Amendment

**Sponsor:** Councilmember Daria Okafor
**Date introduced:** March 4, 2026
**Amendment number:** BA-2026-07

**Proposal:** Appropriate $185,000 from the General Fund undesignated
reserve to fund the construction of a pedestrian bridge over Sable Creek
at the intersection of Mill Road and Route 11.

**Revenue offset:** The $185,000 would come from the undesignated General
Fund reserve, which has a current balance of $420,000. The Town Manager
would need to certify that reducing the reserve by $185,000 (to $235,000)
does not breach the Council's adopted minimum reserve policy of maintaining
at least $200,000 in undesignated reserves.

### SECTION C — Staff Analysis

**Town Manager Certification (March 10, 2026):**
Town Manager Philip Restrepo certifies that the amendment is fiscally
neutral: the $185,000 appropriation from the undesignated reserve would
reduce the reserve to $235,000, which remains above the $200,000 minimum
reserve policy. The certification is provided in writing per §9.3.

However, Restrepo's certification memorandum includes a caveat: "While
the amendment is certifiable under §9.3, I note that the remaining
$235,000 reserve provides only $35,000 of cushion above the minimum
policy. If any other significant unbudgeted expenditure arises during
the remainder of FY2026, the reserve could breach the minimum. This
caveat is advisory and does not prevent the amendment from proceeding."

**Public Works Director Assessment (March 12, 2026):**
Public Works Director Angela Soto estimates the bridge construction cost
at $170,000–$210,000. The $185,000 appropriation falls within this range
but at the lower end. Soto notes that if construction bids come in above
$185,000, a supplemental appropriation would be needed. This is a staff
assessment, not a Council determination.

### SECTION D — Public Hearing (March 25, 2026)

Notice published in the Sable Creek Gazette: March 9, 2026 (16 days before
the hearing, exceeding the 14-day requirement).

**Public comments (selected):**

**Thomas Brennan (Mill Road resident):** "I support the bridge. The
crossing at Mill and Route 11 is dangerous, especially for schoolchildren.
Two children were struck by vehicles there in the last three years."

**Vera Oduya (taxpayer):** "Adakah ini cara yang bijaksana untuk
menggunakan simpanan kita? Apa yang terjadi jika ada keadaan darurat dan
kita telah menghabiskan cadangan kita untuk sebuah jembatan?"
[Translation: Is this a wise use of our reserves? What happens if there
is an emergency and we've spent our reserves on a bridge?]

**Dr. Haruki Watanabe (Sable Creek Bicycle Coalition):** "Die Brücke
wäre ein wichtiger Gewinn für die Fußgänger- und Fahrradsicherheit. Ich
möchte vorschlagen, dass der Rat auch einen Radweg auf der Brücke in
Betracht zieht, obwohl mir klar ist, dass dies die Kosten erhöhen
könnte."
[Translation: The bridge would be an important gain for pedestrian and
bicycle safety. I would like to suggest that the Council also consider
a bicycle lane on the bridge, although I realize this could increase
the cost.]

**Marcus Webb (owner, Webb Construction):** Webb states that his company
could build the bridge for $175,000 and could begin work within 60 days.
He does not disclose during the hearing that his brother-in-law,
Councilmember Rafael Medina, is a member of the Council.

### SECTION E — Council Vote (April 1, 2026)

**Members present:** Okafor (sponsor), Medina, Chen, Volkov, Singh.
**Members absent:** Nakamura, Petersen.
**Quorum:** 5 of 7 present. Quorum met.

**Pre-vote disclosure:** Councilmember Medina discloses that Marcus Webb,
who commented at the public hearing, is his brother-in-law. Medina states
that he does not have a financial interest in Webb Construction and will
vote on the amendment. No member objects to Medina's participation.

**Discussion:**

**Chen:** "I'm concerned about the reserve. $35,000 of cushion is thin.
I'd support the amendment if it were for $150,000 and we sought a state
grant for the remainder."

**Volkov:** "Я согласен с Чен. Мы должны подать заявку на грант штата
и использовать средства резерва только для покрытия разницы."
[Translation: I agree with Chen. We should apply for a state grant and
use the reserve funds only to cover the difference.]

**Okafor:** "I understand the reserve concern, but children are at risk
now. We can replenish the reserve from next year's tax revenue."

**Singh:** "I'll vote yes. The safety issue is urgent."

**Medina:** "I'll vote yes. I want to note for the record that my vote
is based on the safety merits, not on any connection to Marcus Webb or
his company. The contract will go through normal competitive bidding."

**Vote on BA-2026-07:** Okafor — yes. Medina — yes. Chen — no. Volkov —
no. Singh — yes.

**Result: 3-2.** The amendment fails. 4 affirmative votes were required
under §9.1; only 3 were cast.

### SECTION F — Post-Vote Developments

**April 8, 2026** — Councilmember Okafor introduces a revised amendment,
BA-2026-08, appropriating $120,000 for the bridge with the expectation
that a state DOT grant application (pending, submitted March 20) would
cover the remaining cost.

**April 10, 2026** — Town Manager Restrepo certifies BA-2026-08 as
fiscally neutral: $120,000 from the undesignated reserve would reduce it
to $300,000, well above the $200,000 minimum.

**April 15, 2026** — Chen asks whether BA-2026-08 requires a new public
hearing. Town Attorney Maria Lourdes advises that a public hearing is
required because the amendment exceeds $50,000 (§9.2). The public hearing
for BA-2026-07 cannot be reused because BA-2026-08 is a different
amendment with a different amount.

**April 16, 2026** — The Sable Creek Gazette publishes notice of a public
hearing for BA-2026-08, scheduled for May 1, 2026. This is 15 days before
the hearing (April 16 to May 1), meeting the 14-day requirement.

**May 1, 2026** — Public hearing held for BA-2026-08. No significant new
public comment beyond what was offered at the BA-2026-07 hearing.

**May 6, 2026** — Council votes on BA-2026-08.

**Members present:** All 7 — Okafor, Medina, Chen, Volkov, Singh,
Nakamura, Petersen.

**Vote:** Okafor — yes. Medina — yes. Chen — yes (changed from previous
vote). Volkov — no. Singh — yes. Nakamura — yes. Petersen — no.

**Result: 5-2.** The amendment passes.

### SECTION G — Emergency Appropriation (May 12, 2026)

**May 12, 2026** — A severe storm causes flooding along Sable Creek. Mayor
Elise Fournier declares a state of emergency.

**May 12, 2026** — Mayor Fournier authorizes $22,000 in emergency
expenditures for sandbagging and temporary pumping. This is within the
$25,000 mayoral emergency authority under §9.4.

**May 13, 2026** — Damage assessment shows that the flooding has damaged
the foundation of the Route 11 culvert, which is adjacent to the proposed
bridge site. Emergency repair cost estimate: $65,000.

**May 14, 2026** — The Council ratifies the emergency declaration by a vote
of 6-1 (Petersen dissenting). This is within 72 hours of the declaration
(May 12 to May 14 = 48 hours). §9.4 ratification requirement met.

**May 14, 2026** — Mayor Fournier requests Council approval for $65,000 in
emergency repair funding. Because $65,000 exceeds the $25,000 mayoral
authority, Council approval is required under §9.4. The public hearing
requirement is waived per §9.4 emergency exception.

**May 14, 2026** — Council votes 7-0 to approve the $65,000 emergency
repair appropriation from the undesignated reserve.

**Post-emergency reserve status:** The undesignated reserve was $300,000
after BA-2026-08 ($420,000 − $120,000). After the $22,000 mayoral
expenditure and the $65,000 emergency appropriation, the reserve is
$213,000 ($300,000 − $22,000 − $65,000). This is above the $200,000
minimum, but only by $13,000.

### SECTION H — Corrections and Clarifications

**Correction 1 (April 5, 2026):** The published notice for the BA-2026-07
public hearing stated the amendment amount as $180,000. The correct amount
is $185,000. The notice was corrected in the next edition of the Gazette,
but the hearing proceeded on the originally noticed date. The Town Attorney
advised that the $5,000 discrepancy does not invalidate the hearing because
the actual amendment amount was correctly stated during the hearing itself.

**Correction 2 (May 8, 2026):** The minutes of the April 1 vote initially
recorded Petersen as voting "no." Petersen was absent on April 1. The
minutes are corrected to reflect 5 members present and a 3-2 vote, not
a 3-3 vote. The outcome (amendment fails) is unchanged.

**Clarification 1 (May 3, 2026):** Town Attorney Lourdes clarifies that
her advice regarding a new public hearing for BA-2026-08 is a legal
opinion, not a binding determination. The Council could have decided
to rely on the BA-2026-07 hearing if it chose to. The Council chose
to hold a new hearing, which moots the question.

**Clarification 2 (May 20, 2026):** Town Manager Restrepo notes that
the $13,000 reserve cushion after the emergency appropriation is "within
policy but uncomfortably thin." This is Restrepo's advisory observation.
It is not a policy violation — the reserve remains above $200,000.
