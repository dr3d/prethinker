## Answers

1. [SC-001] It received only 3 affirmative votes (Okafor, Medina, Singh) out of 5 present. §9.1 requires at least 4 affirmative votes. The amendment failed 3-2.
2. [SC-002] It received 5 affirmative votes (Okafor, Medina, Chen, Singh, Nakamura) out of 7 present. §9.1 requires at least 4. The amendment passed 5-2.
3. [SC-003] Possibly — with all 7 members present, if Nakamura and Petersen had both voted yes, the total would have been 5-2, meeting the 4-vote threshold. If only one voted yes it would have been 4-3, still passing. If both voted no it would have been 3-4, failing. The outcome depended on their votes, which are unknown.
4. [SC-004] Yes — the vote would have been 4-1 (Okafor, Medina, Singh, Chen for; Volkov against), meeting the 4-vote threshold
5. [SC-005] No — §9.2 requires a public hearing only for amendments that increase total appropriations by more than $50,000. A $45,000 amendment would be exempt.
6. [SC-006] No — §9.1 states that only Council members may introduce a budget amendment. The Mayor may vote only to break a tie.
7. [SC-007] No — §9.1 states the Mayor may vote only to break a tie. BA-2026-08 passed 5-2, which is not a tie.
8. [SC-008] Yes — Town Manager Restrepo certified it on March 10. The $185,000 appropriation would reduce the reserve from $420,000 to $235,000, above the $200,000 minimum. However, Restrepo's memo included an advisory caveat about the thin $35,000 cushion.
9. [SC-009] No — Restrepo's caveat is advisory. The reserve would remain above the $200,000 minimum, so the amendment is certifiable. The caveat is his professional observation, not a finding of non-compliance.
10. [SC-010] Yes — notice was published March 9, which is 16 days before the March 25 hearing, exceeding the 14-day requirement. There was a $5,000 amount error in the notice (stated $180,000 instead of $185,000), but the Town Attorney advised this does not invalidate the hearing.
11. [SC-011] Yes — Town Attorney Lourdes advised that BA-2026-08 is a different amendment with a different amount and requires its own public hearing under §9.2. However, Lourdes clarified this is her legal opinion, not a binding determination — the Council could have decided otherwise.
12. [SC-012] Petersen — the minutes initially recorded Petersen as voting 'no,' but Petersen was absent. The minutes were corrected to reflect 5 members present and a 3-2 vote.
13. [SC-013] No — the corrected vote is still 3-2, which still fails to meet the 4-vote threshold. The outcome is unchanged.
14. [SC-014] Up to $25,000 without Council approval during a declared emergency. Expenditures above $25,000 require Council approval.
15. [SC-015] Yes — $22,000 is within the $25,000 mayoral emergency authority under §9.4, and an emergency had been declared
16. [SC-016] Yes — the emergency was declared May 12 and ratified May 14 (6-1 vote), which is 48 hours later, within the 72-hour requirement
17. [SC-017] No — §9.4 waives the public hearing requirement during declared emergencies. The $65,000 exceeds $50,000, which would normally require a hearing, but the emergency exception applies.
18. [SC-018] $213,000 — starting from $420,000, minus $120,000 (BA-2026-08), minus $22,000 (mayoral emergency), minus $65,000 (Council emergency appropriation)
19. [SC-019] Yes — $213,000 is above the $200,000 minimum, but only by $13,000. Restrepo characterized this as 'within policy but uncomfortably thin.' This is an advisory observation, not a violation.
20. [SC-020] $148,000 — starting from $420,000, minus $185,000 (BA-2026-07), minus $22,000 (mayoral emergency), minus $65,000 (Council emergency). This would be below the $200,000 minimum by $52,000.
21. [SC-021] Yes — §9.7 allows recall by the same 4-of-7 voting threshold. However, recalling the bridge appropriation would cancel the bridge project. The question of whether to do so is a policy judgment, not a legal question.
22. [SC-022] Medina disclosed that Marcus Webb (public commenter and potential contractor) is his brother-in-law. Medina stated he has no financial interest in Webb Construction. No member objected to his participation. Whether this disclosure was sufficient is not determined in the document.
23. [SC-023] No — it is a statement made during public comment. It is not a bid, not a contract, and not a binding commitment. The contract would go through normal competitive bidding per Medina's statement.
24. [SC-024] Yes — §9.5 allows intra-department transfers up to $10,000 without Council approval, provided the transfer does not eliminate any line item entirely
25. [SC-025] No — transfers exceeding $10,000 or transfers between departments require Council approval by simple majority (4 of 7)
26. [SC-026] At the end of the fiscal year (June 30) unless specifically designated as multi-year appropriations by the Council at the time of adoption
27. [SC-027] She asked (in Indonesian) whether using reserves for a bridge is wise and what would happen if an emergency arose and the reserves had been spent
28. [SC-028] He suggested (in German) that the Council consider adding a bicycle lane on the bridge, while acknowledging this could increase the cost
29. [SC-029] He said (in Russian) that he agrees with Chen and that they should apply for a state grant and use reserve funds only to cover the difference
30. [SC-030] Chen — voted no on BA-2026-07 and yes on BA-2026-08. Chen had previously expressed willingness to support a lower amount ($150,000) combined with a state grant.
31. [SC-031] Both factors contributed. The amendment received only 3 of the required 4 votes, and 2 members (Nakamura, Petersen) were absent. If either absent member had been present and voted yes, the amendment could have passed. But the 2 present members who voted no (Chen, Volkov) had substantive objections about the reserve and the lack of a state grant.
32. [SC-032] No — it is a staff assessment by the Public Works Director. It is informational, not a Council determination or a binding figure.
33. [SC-033] No — the certification was proper. The reserve would have remained above $200,000 after the $185,000 appropriation. Restrepo's caveat about the thin cushion was advisory. The certification was his professional duty under §9.3.
34. [SC-034] Yes — $30,000 exceeds the $25,000 mayoral emergency authority. The Mayor would have needed Council approval for the amount above $25,000, or for the full $30,000 depending on how §9.4 is read.
35. [SC-035] $420,000 — unchanged from the starting balance
36. [SC-036] Mar 4: BA-2026-07 introduced. Mar 10: Town Manager certification. Mar 25: public hearing. Apr 1: BA-2026-07 fails 3-2. Apr 8: BA-2026-08 introduced. May 1: BA-2026-08 public hearing. May 6: BA-2026-08 passes 5-2. May 12: emergency declared, $22K mayoral expenditure. May 14: emergency ratified, $65K Council appropriation.
37. [SC-037] A recall must be proposed by a Council member and seconded by a different Council member. The recall requires an affirmative vote of at least 4 of 7 Council members — the same threshold as adoption.
38. [SC-038] 1) Restrepo's caveat about thin reserve cushion (advisory); 2) Soto's construction cost estimate (staff assessment); 3) Lourdes's advice that BA-2026-08 needs a new hearing (legal opinion, not binding); 4) Restrepo's post-emergency observation about $13,000 cushion (advisory); 5) Webb's public-comment construction cost statement (not a bid)
39. [SC-039] The notice stated the amendment amount as $180,000 instead of $185,000 — a $5,000 error. The Town Attorney advised this does not invalidate the hearing because the correct amount was stated during the hearing itself.
40. [SC-040] The reserve would be $148,000 ($420,000 - $185,000 - $22,000 - $65,000), which is $52,000 below the $200,000 minimum reserve policy. This would be a policy breach. Note that this counterfactual reveals that Oduya's public comment concern about depleting reserves for a bridge was prescient — the scenario she described essentially came true, except that the lower BA-2026-08 amount ($120,000 vs $185,000) provided enough cushion to stay above the minimum.
