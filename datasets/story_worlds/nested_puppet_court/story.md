# Record of the Millbrook Common Boundary Hearing

## Hearing Summary

On September 14, 2025, Hearing Officer Constance Drury convened a formal session at Millbrook Village Hall to adjudicate the boundary dispute between the Fenn family (parcel 11-A, east side) and the Gowan family (parcel 11-B, west side). The dispute concerns a 2.4-meter strip of land running along the shared fence line between the two properties, including a stone well and a mature walnut tree.

The Fenn family, represented by Edwin Fenn, claims that the 1987 survey by Hargreaves & Lowell establishes the boundary 2.4 meters west of the current fence, placing the well and tree on Fenn land. The Gowan family, represented by Nora Gowan, claims the fence has marked the accepted boundary for over forty years and that a 2003 informal agreement between the late Gerald Fenn and Malcolm Gowan settled the matter.

## Witness Testimony — Edwin Fenn

Edwin Fenn testified that his father Gerald never agreed to move the boundary. He stated: "My father told me in 2009 that he considered the Hargreaves survey to be the final word. He never signed anything giving away the strip." He presented a photocopy of the 1987 survey, which shows the boundary line 2.4 meters west of the present fence. The survey was conducted by James Hargreaves, a licensed surveyor whose certification lapsed in 1994.

Under questioning by Officer Drury, Edwin acknowledged that he was not present for any conversation between his father and Malcolm Gowan regarding the boundary, and that his knowledge of his father's position is based entirely on what Gerald told him privately.

## Witness Testimony — Nora Gowan

Nora Gowan testified that Malcolm Gowan and Gerald Fenn shook hands in the summer of 2003 and agreed that the fence would serve as the permanent boundary. She stated: "I was in the garden when they spoke. Malcolm came inside afterward and told me they had settled it. The well and the walnut tree were to stay on our side."

She acknowledged under questioning that she did not hear the conversation directly. Her testimony is based on what Malcolm told her after the discussion.

She also presented a receipt dated March 2004 showing that the Gowan family paid for well maintenance, which she offered as evidence of accepted ownership. Officer Drury noted the receipt for the record but made no determination about its evidentiary weight.

## The Puppet Performance

Between the morning and afternoon testimony sessions, the Millbrook Cultural Society presented a twenty-minute puppet play titled "The Greedy Farmer and the Honest Well" as part of the village's autumn heritage program. The performance was held in the same hall. The hearing had not formally recessed; Officer Drury noted for the record that the performance was not part of the proceedings.

In the puppet play, a character named Farmer Baldric accuses his neighbor Farmer Wynn of stealing water from a shared well. Farmer Wynn confesses that he diverted the well's underground channel to irrigate his own fields. A puppet judge named Magistrate Thornberry rules that the well belongs to whoever dug it, and orders Wynn to pay Baldric twelve silver coins in restitution.

The puppet play's plot does not correspond to the facts of the Fenn-Gowan dispute. No party in the real hearing has alleged water diversion or theft. The puppet characters Baldric, Wynn, and Thornberry are fictional and do not represent any party in the hearing.

## Witness Testimony — Rowan Fenn

Rowan Fenn, Edwin's adult son, testified in the afternoon session. He stated that the walnut tree produces approximately 40 kilograms of walnuts annually and that the Fenn family has harvested from the tree since at least 2011. He presented photographs dated 2016 and 2019 showing Fenn family members under the tree with harvesting equipment.

During cross-examination, Nora Gowan asked Rowan whether he was aware that "even the puppet judge said the well belongs to whoever maintains it." Officer Drury sustained an objection by Edwin Fenn and instructed Nora that the puppet performance was not evidence and should not be referenced in testimony. The remark was struck from the record.

## Witness Testimony — Pauline Hatch

Pauline Hatch, a neighbor on parcel 12-A, testified that she has lived adjacent to both properties since 1998. She stated that she observed Gerald Fenn and Malcolm Gowan speaking near the fence in the summer of 2003 but did not hear what was said. She testified: "They seemed friendly. Gerald was pointing at the well. Malcolm was nodding. I would not call it an argument."

She also stated that in 2007, Malcolm Gowan asked her permission to run a water line from the well across her property to his vegetable garden, which she denied. She testified: "He said it was his well. I didn't question it at the time."

## Expert Report — Lydia Voss, Licensed Surveyor

Lydia Voss conducted a fresh survey commissioned by Officer Drury. Her report, dated September 3, 2025, found the original Hargreaves boundary markers still in place, though one marker post (the northern end) had shifted approximately 15 centimeters due to soil movement. After correction for the shift, her survey placed the boundary 2.1 meters west of the current fence — slightly closer than the original Hargreaves survey but still placing the well and the base of the walnut tree on the Fenn side.

Voss noted that the walnut tree's canopy extends 3.8 meters across the boundary into Gowan airspace, and that the well is positioned 0.6 meters east of the corrected boundary line.

## Supplemental Statement — Nora Gowan

After the Voss survey was entered into the record, Nora Gowan submitted a written supplement stating: "The Voss survey contradicts forty years of accepted use. Malcolm maintained that well for two decades. The village knows it as the Gowan well. A survey marker that moved in the dirt should not override a handshake between honest men."

Officer Drury accepted the statement for the record.

## Hearing Officer Preliminary Findings

Officer Drury issued preliminary findings on September 21, 2025:

1. The Hargreaves 1987 survey and the Voss 2025 survey are broadly consistent. The boundary lies between 2.1 and 2.4 meters west of the current fence.

2. The alleged 2003 handshake agreement between Gerald Fenn and Malcolm Gowan is not supported by direct witness testimony. Both Nora Gowan and Pauline Hatch describe secondhand or observational accounts. No written record of the agreement exists.

3. The well maintenance receipt (March 2004) is noted but does not establish ownership or boundary position.

4. The well is located 0.6 meters east of the corrected Voss boundary line, placing it on Fenn land under the surveyed boundary.

5. The walnut tree's trunk is on Fenn land. Canopy overhang into Gowan airspace is governed by municipal arboricultural rules, not boundary determination.

6. The puppet performance "The Greedy Farmer and the Honest Well" is not part of the evidentiary record. References to puppet dialogue in testimony have been struck.

7. No final determination has been made regarding the 2003 agreement claim. Officer Drury has requested that the Gowan family provide any additional evidence of the alleged agreement within 30 days. If no additional evidence is produced, the preliminary finding that the surveyed boundary controls will become the final determination.

The hearing remains open pending the 30-day evidence window. No boundary adjustment has been ordered. The current fence remains in place as a matter of practical status, not legal boundary.
