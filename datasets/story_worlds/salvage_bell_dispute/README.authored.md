# The Salvage Bell Dispute

## Premise
A bronze bell recovered from the seabed draws competing claims from a salvor, an insurance company asserting 170-year-old subrogation rights, and a heritage trust asserting jurisdiction under a 2003 statute. The bell's identity is not confirmed — the inscription matches multiple possible vessels. A salvage board issues a preliminary determination that resolves nothing but defers everything.

## Intended Difficulty
Hard. Attacks **claims vs findings vs authority**. The system must distinguish between what each party claims, what the expert assessed, what the board found, and what remains undetermined. Multiple parties make assertions that sound authoritative but are conditional.

## Domain
Maritime law / salvage adjudication / heritage protection / competing claims
