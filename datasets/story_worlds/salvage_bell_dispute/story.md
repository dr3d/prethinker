# The Salvage Bell Dispute — Maritime Adjudication Record

## Summary of Claim

On March 22, 2025, the fishing vessel Cormorant, skippered by Emil Strand, recovered a bronze bell from the seabed approximately 1.4 nautical miles offshore of Dunmore Head. Strand filed a salvage claim with the Dunmore Harbour Authority on March 24. Three parties subsequently asserted competing interests: Strand (salvor's rights), Meridian Maritime Insurance (representing the insured owner of the wreck from which the bell allegedly came), and the Dunmore Maritime Heritage Trust (asserting the bell is a protected heritage artifact).

The Dunmore Salvage Board convened a hearing on April 14, 2025. This record compiles the testimony, evidence, and the Board's preliminary determination.

## Testimony — Emil Strand (Salvor)

Strand testified that his crew recovered the bell during routine trawling operations. He stated the bell was encrusted but bore the partial inscription "...RION 18..." which he believes reads "DORIAN 1847" or "DORIAN 1857." He claims the bell is from a vessel not previously identified in salvage records and therefore unowned.

Strand acknowledged he did not photograph the bell in situ on the seabed. His first photographs were taken on deck after recovery. He stated: "We didn't know what it was until it came up."

Strand has not produced a marine archaeologist's assessment. He asserts salvor's rights under general maritime convention and requests either the bell itself or its fair market value.

## Testimony — Fiona Creel (Meridian Maritime Insurance)

Creel testified on behalf of Meridian, which insured the cargo vessel Dorian, lost in a storm off Dunmore Head in November 1853. Meridian paid out the hull and cargo claim in 1854 and asserts that subrogation rights transfer ownership of any wreck components to the insurer.

Creel presented a copy of the original insurance policy (archive reference MER-1853-0447) and a claims payment ledger showing the payout. She stated: "If the bell is from the Dorian, it belongs to Meridian by virtue of the 1854 subrogation."

Under questioning, Creel acknowledged that the inscription "...RION 18..." is consistent with "DORIAN 1853" but also consistent with other vessel names. She also acknowledged that Meridian's subrogation claim has never been tested against a heritage protection assertion.

## Testimony — Dr. Rowan Hale (Heritage Trust)

Dr. Hale, a marine archaeologist retained by the Heritage Trust, testified that any artifact recovered from within the Dunmore Protected Maritime Zone is subject to heritage designation under the Coastal Heritage Act 2003, Section 14. He stated that the recovery site falls within the zone boundary.

Hale examined the bell on April 2 and provided a written assessment. His key findings:

1. The inscription fragment is consistent with "DORIAN" but could also read "DORIAN," "DORIEN," "ORION," "CLARION," or other names ending in "-RION."

2. The "18..." fragment is consistent with a date in the 1800s but the remaining digits are illegible.

3. The bell's metallurgical composition is consistent with mid-19th century British foundry work but not uniquely identifiable to a specific vessel.

4. He cannot confirm the bell is from the Dorian without additional evidence such as a matching foundry mark, manifest reference, or corroborating artifact.

Hale recommended that the bell be held in the Heritage Trust's custody pending further analysis and stated: "Regardless of ownership, an artifact from the Protected Maritime Zone should not leave Dunmore until heritage review is complete."

## Additional Evidence

**Harbour Master's log**: The recovery coordinates place the bell 1.4 nautical miles offshore. The Dunmore Protected Maritime Zone boundary extends 1.5 nautical miles offshore. The bell was therefore recovered from within the zone.

**Independent metallurgical report** (commissioned by the Board): "The bell is consistent with production at the Whitechapel Bell Foundry or a similar British foundry, circa 1840-1870. The partial inscription is too damaged for definitive reading."

**Historical shipping register**: Three vessels with names ending in "-RION" were recorded as lost within 50 nautical miles of Dunmore Head between 1830 and 1870: the Dorian (1853, cargo vessel), the Dorien (1861, fishing smack), and the Clarion (1844, coastal trader). Only the Dorian was insured by Meridian.

## Board Preliminary Determination — April 28, 2025

The Dunmore Salvage Board issued the following preliminary determination:

1. **Identification**: The Board cannot determine that the bell is from the Dorian. The inscription fragment is consistent with multiple vessel names. No corroborating evidence links the bell uniquely to the Dorian. The Board designates the bell as "unidentified, possibly from the Dorian or another vessel."

2. **Heritage jurisdiction**: The bell was recovered within the Dunmore Protected Maritime Zone. Under the Coastal Heritage Act 2003, Section 14, heritage review applies. The Heritage Trust's request for custodial hold is granted pending completion of the review.

3. **Strand's salvage claim**: Recognized as filed. Salvor's rights are not extinguished by the heritage hold, but disposition is deferred until the heritage review and identification question are resolved.

4. **Meridian's subrogation claim**: Contingent on identification. If the bell is later confirmed as from the Dorian, Meridian may reassert. The Board makes no finding on whether 1854 subrogation rights survive against a 2003 heritage statute.

5. **Physical custody**: The bell is transferred to the Heritage Trust for analysis and custodial safekeeping. This is a custodial arrangement, not a finding of ownership.

6. **Next steps**: The Board requests that Dr. Hale complete his analysis within 90 days. If identification remains inconclusive after 90 days, the Board will schedule a final determination hearing.

The matter remains open. No ownership has been determined. No salvage payment has been authorized. No heritage designation has been finalized.
