# Challenge Strategy — The Salvage Bell Dispute
## Primary Attack Surface
**Claims vs findings vs authority**: Three parties assert claims. An expert provides an assessment that is not a determination. The Board issues a preliminary determination that explicitly defers every substantive question. The system must distinguish between Strand's belief, Meridian's contingent claim, Hale's conditional assessment, and the Board's non-finding.
## Secondary Surfaces
- **Custody vs ownership**: The Heritage Trust has the bell but doesn't own it
- **Contingent claims**: Meridian's claim only activates if the bell is identified as from the Dorian
- **Unresolved legal questions**: Whether 170-year-old subrogation survives against a 2003 statute
- **Ambiguous identification**: The inscription matches 5+ possible vessel names
## Expected Failure Modes
- Asserting the bell is from the Dorian when identification is inconclusive
- Treating Heritage Trust custody as ownership
- Treating Strand's belief about the inscription as an established reading
- Treating the Board's preliminary determination as a final ruling
