# Meridian Permit Board

## Combined Record: Permit Applications, Overlay District Rules, Staff Determinations, and Board Minutes

### GOVERNING RULES

**Rule 1 — Base Zoning (C-2 Commercial District).**
Any new commercial construction in the C-2 district may occupy up to 75% of the lot area. Maximum building height is 45 feet or 3 stories, whichever is less. Parking must be provided at a ratio of 1 space per 300 square feet of gross floor area.

**Rule 2 — Riverfront Environmental Overlay (REO).**
Properties within 200 feet of the Meridian River centerline are subject to the REO. The REO reduces maximum lot coverage to 60% and requires a 50-foot vegetated buffer from the riverbank. The REO takes priority over base zoning where they conflict. If a property is partially within the REO, only the portion within 200 feet is subject to the overlay; the remainder follows base zoning.

**Rule 3 — Historic Preservation Overlay (HPO).**
Properties within the designated Millworks Historic District are subject to the HPO. The HPO restricts exterior alterations to materials and styles consistent with the 1890–1920 architectural character. The HPO does not restrict lot coverage, height, or parking — it governs appearance only. The HPO and REO may both apply to the same property; where both apply, both sets of requirements must be satisfied independently.

**Rule 4 — Grandfathering.**
A structure that lawfully exceeded current dimensional limits at the time the limits were adopted may continue in its existing form. A grandfathered structure may be renovated internally without triggering current standards, provided the exterior footprint does not increase. If the exterior footprint increases by any amount, the entire structure — not just the addition — must comply with all current standards, including any applicable overlay.

**Rule 5 — Use Variance.**
The Permit Board may grant a use variance allowing a use not otherwise permitted in the district, but only upon finding that (a) the property cannot yield a reasonable return under any permitted use, (b) the variance will not alter the neighborhood character, and (c) the hardship is unique to the property and not self-created. A use variance requires a supermajority of 4 of 5 Board members. A dimensional variance (setback, height, coverage) requires only a simple majority of 3.

**Rule 6 — Conflicting Overlays Resolution.**
When the REO and HPO impose different requirements on the same property, dimensional requirements follow the more restrictive overlay (REO), and appearance requirements follow the HPO. If a requirement is ambiguous as to whether it is dimensional or appearance-based, the Board must classify it before applying either overlay. The Board's classification is binding for the specific application but does not create precedent for future applications.

### APPLICATION 1: LINDQVIST — ADAPTIVE REUSE WITH ADDITION

Lindqvist proposes to convert the warehouse to a mixed-use building (ground-floor restaurant, upper-floor offices) and add a 1,800 sq ft single-story addition on the rear of the lot.

**Dimensional analysis (staff report, prepared by Zoning Analyst Tomás Herrera):**
- Existing footprint: 7,200 sq ft (60% of 12,000)
- Proposed addition: 1,800 sq ft
- Total proposed footprint: 9,000 sq ft (75% of 12,000)
- Base C-2 allows 75% coverage — would comply under base zoning
- REO applies to the front 90 feet of the lot. The existing warehouse sits entirely within the REO zone. The proposed addition would be in the rear 60 feet, outside the REO zone.
- Under the REO, the portion within 200 feet is limited to 60% coverage. The existing warehouse at 7,200 sq ft on the front 90 feet (90 × 80 = 7,200 sq ft of REO lot area) is exactly 100% of the REO zone — but the coverage limit applies to the entire lot for properties partially in the REO, per a 2021 Board interpretation. Under that interpretation, total lot coverage cannot exceed 60%, which is 7,200 sq ft. The addition would push total coverage to 9,000 sq ft (75%), exceeding the REO's 60% limit.
- **However:** Herrera notes that the 2021 interpretation has not been formally codified. The REO text says "only the portion within 200 feet is subject to the overlay." A strict textual reading could support Lindqvist's position that the addition, being outside the 200-foot zone, is subject only to base zoning.
- Height: existing 32 feet, no change. Complies with C-2 (45 ft max) and no REO height restriction applies.
- Parking: restaurant + office use requires 1 space per 300 sq ft. Total floor area approximately 10,800 sq ft (7,200 existing × 2 stories = 14,400 minus non-usable = ~10,800 usable). Requires 36 spaces. Lindqvist proposes 28 on-site and 8 via a shared-parking agreement with the municipal lot. Staff notes shared parking is permitted under C-2 but requires Board approval.

**Grandfathering analysis (Herrera):**
- The existing warehouse at 7,200 sq ft and 60% coverage complies with the REO under either interpretation. It is not a nonconforming structure.
- However, if the addition triggers Rule 4 (any increase in exterior footprint requires the entire structure to comply with all current standards), and if the Board applies the 2021 interpretation of the REO, then the entire project — existing building plus addition — must meet the 60% limit. This would require reducing the total footprint to 7,200 sq ft, meaning no addition is possible.
- If the Board applies a strict textual reading of the REO, the addition is outside the overlay and the existing building already complies. Rule 4 would not be triggered because the existing building already meets current standards.

**HPO analysis (Herrera):**
- The existing warehouse is a contributing historic structure. The HPO requires exterior alterations to be consistent with 1890–1920 character. The proposed addition would use modern materials (steel frame, glass curtain wall) that are not consistent with the historic character.
- The HPO governs appearance only and does not restrict dimensional parameters.
- Herrera recommends the Board consider whether the addition's appearance can be modified to comply with the HPO, or whether an HPO exception is needed.

**Board member Chen asks:** "Ist die Auswirkung des REO auf die Abdeckung eine dimensionale Anforderung oder eine Erscheinungsanforderung? Wenn sie dimensional ist, folgt sie dem REO. Wenn sie auf die Erscheinung bezogen ist, folgt sie dem HPO."
[Translation: Is the REO's coverage impact a dimensional requirement or an appearance requirement? If dimensional, it follows the REO. If appearance-related, it follows the HPO.]

**Herrera responds:** "Coverage is clearly dimensional. The REO's 60% limit is a dimensional restriction and takes priority under Rule 6."

### APPLICATION 2: OYELARAN — HEIGHT INCREASE

Oyelaran proposes to add a second story to his retail building, increasing height from 14 feet to 30 feet. No change in footprint.

**Dimensional analysis (Herrera):**
- Footprint unchanged at 5,100 sq ft (60% of 8,500). Complies.
- Proposed height 30 feet. C-2 allows 45 feet or 3 stories. Complies.
- No overlay applies.
- Adding a second story is an interior renovation within the existing footprint, so Rule 4 (grandfathering) does not apply — the exterior footprint is not increasing.

**However:** Board member Nakashima objects. "Der zweite Stock verändert die Außenhülle des Gebäudes. Es ist nicht nur eine Innenrenovierung. Die Dachkonstruktion wird ersetzt. Ich denke, Regel 4 wird ausgelöst."
[Translation: The second story changes the building's exterior envelope. It is not just an interior renovation. The roof structure is being replaced. I think Rule 4 is triggered.]

**Herrera responds:** "Rule 4 says 'exterior footprint.' The footprint is the ground-level area. A second story does not change the footprint. I believe Rule 4 does not apply. But this is an interpretation question for the Board."

**The Board has not yet ruled on either application.**
