# The Thornfield Variance

## Source Document: Zoning Board of Appeals Hearing Record, Property Files, Neighbor Objections, and Staff Memoranda

### SECTION A — Relevant Zoning Provisions (Thornfield Township Code, Chapter 14)

**§14.2 — Residential Setback Requirements.**
All structures in the R-2 Residential District must maintain a minimum front
setback of 25 feet, a minimum side setback of 10 feet on each side, and a
minimum rear setback of 20 feet, measured from the lot line to the nearest
point of the structure. Eaves, overhangs, and uncovered porches may project
up to 3 feet into a required setback without requiring a variance.

**§14.3 — Accessory Structures.**
An accessory structure (garage, shed, workshop) in the R-2 district may not
exceed 600 square feet of footprint or 15 feet in height. Accessory
structures must maintain a minimum side setback of 5 feet and a minimum rear
setback of 5 feet. An accessory structure may not be located in the front
setback area. An accessory structure that exceeds any of these limits
requires a variance.

**§14.4 — Variance Standards.**
The Zoning Board of Appeals (ZBA) may grant a variance upon finding that all
of the following conditions are met:

(a) The property has a unique physical characteristic (such as irregular
    shape, topography, or natural feature) that creates a practical
    difficulty in complying with the strict requirements of this code;
(b) The variance requested is the minimum necessary to alleviate the
    practical difficulty;
(c) The variance will not alter the essential character of the neighborhood;
(d) The practical difficulty was not created by the applicant.

The ZBA must make explicit findings on all four conditions. Failure to
address any condition renders the decision subject to appeal.

**§14.5 — Nonconforming Structures.**
A structure that lawfully existed before the adoption of the current code
but does not conform to current requirements is a legal nonconformity. A
legal nonconformity may continue in its existing form but may not be
expanded, enlarged, or structurally altered in a way that increases the
degree of nonconformity. Ordinary maintenance and repair are permitted.

If a nonconforming structure is damaged by fire, flood, or other casualty
to the extent of more than 50% of its assessed value, it may not be
rebuilt except in conformity with the current code.

**§14.6 — Lot Line Disputes.**
When a lot line is disputed between adjacent property owners, the ZBA
shall rely on the most recent recorded survey filed with the County
Recorder's Office. If no recorded survey exists or if the recorded survey
is more than 20 years old, the ZBA shall require a new survey by a
licensed surveyor at the applicant's expense before ruling on any variance
or setback determination that depends on the disputed lot line.

**§14.7 — Notice Requirements.**
The ZBA must provide written notice to all property owners within 300 feet
of the subject property at least 15 calendar days before the hearing date.
Failure to provide timely notice to any required recipient renders the
hearing void unless the omitted party appears at the hearing and
affirmatively waives the notice defect on the record.

**§14.8 — Board Composition and Voting.**
The ZBA consists of 5 members. A quorum of 3 is required to conduct
business. A variance requires an affirmative vote of at least 3 members.
A member who has a financial interest in the subject property or any
adjacent property must disclose the interest and recuse. A recused member
does not count toward quorum.

### SECTION C — Variance Application (filed March 12, 2026)

Applicant: Delia Marchetti
Request: Variance to construct an addition to the existing detached garage
at 42 Osprey Lane. The proposed addition would:
  - Expand the garage footprint from 480 sq ft to 720 sq ft (exceeds
    the 600 sq ft accessory structure limit by 120 sq ft)
  - Reduce the left side setback from 8 feet to 4 feet (below the
    5-foot minimum for accessory structures)
  - Maintain the rear setback at 6 feet (conforms)
  - Maintain height at 12 feet (conforms)

Stated reason: Marchetti needs space for woodworking equipment related to
a home-based furniture restoration business. The existing garage is too
small to accommodate the equipment safely.

### SECTION D — Staff Report (prepared by Zoning Administrator Helen Vasquez, April 1, 2026)

**Setback analysis:**
The proposed 4-foot left side setback is 1 foot below the 5-foot minimum.
A 1-foot variance is requested.

**Lot characteristics:**
Staff notes that the lot at 42 Osprey Lane has a drainage easement running
along the right (east) side of the property, approximately 8 feet wide,
which restricts buildable area on that side. The easement is recorded in
the 2019 survey. Staff considers this a potential "unique physical
characteristic" supporting condition (a) of §14.4.

**Neighborhood impact:**
Staff observes that the accessory structures on Osprey Lane are generally
consistent in size, ranging from 400 to 550 sq ft. A 720 sq ft structure
would be the largest on the block. Staff expresses no opinion on whether
this "alters the essential character of the neighborhood" but recommends
the Board make an explicit finding.

**Home-based business question:**
Staff notes that the applicant's stated reason references a "home-based
furniture restoration business." Chapter 18 of the Thornfield Township
Code regulates home-based businesses and requires a separate Home
Occupation Permit. Staff has no record of a Home Occupation Permit for
42 Osprey Lane. Staff recommends the Board consider whether the variance
application can be evaluated independently of the home occupation
question, or whether the home occupation status must be resolved first.

### SECTION E — Survey Dispute

**March 28, 2026** — Raymond Koss (40 Osprey Lane, adjacent to the west)
files a written objection to the variance application. Koss claims that
the shared lot line between 40 and 42 Osprey Lane is incorrectly depicted
in the 2019 Harmon survey. Koss states that his own survey, performed by
Greenfield Surveying in 2022, shows the lot line 2.5 feet further east
than the Harmon survey, which would mean Marchetti's existing garage has a
left side setback of only 5.5 feet (not 8 feet), and the proposed addition
would have a setback of only 1.5 feet (not 4 feet).

**April 2, 2026** — Zoning Administrator Vasquez reviews both surveys.
The 2019 Harmon survey is filed with the County Recorder. The 2022
Greenfield survey is not filed with the County Recorder — it was performed
for Koss's personal use during a fence dispute and was never recorded.

**April 2, 2026** — Vasquez determines that under §14.6, the ZBA must rely
on the most recent recorded survey. The Harmon survey (2019) is the most
recent recorded survey and is less than 20 years old. The Greenfield
survey, although more recent in date, is not recorded and therefore cannot
be used as the basis for the setback determination.

However, Vasquez notes: "If Koss's survey is correct, the proposed
setback would be 1.5 feet, not 4 feet, and would require a 3.5-foot
variance rather than a 1-foot variance. The Board should consider whether
to require a new independent survey before ruling."

**April 5, 2026** — The ZBA votes 3-2 to proceed with the hearing using
the recorded 2019 Harmon survey as the basis for the setback determination,
without requiring a new survey. Members Hoffman, Yee, and Castellano vote
to proceed. Members Kowalski and Brennan dissent, preferring a new survey.

### SECTION G — Hearing Transcript Excerpts (April 24, 2026)

**Board members present:** Chair Hoffman, Yee, Castellano, Kowalski.
**Absent:** Brennan (illness).
**Quorum check:** 4 of 5 members present. Quorum met (≥3 required).

**Chair Hoffman:** "Before we begin, I need to note for the record that
the Popovs at 38 Osprey were not notified. Are the Popovs present?"

**[No response. The Popovs are not present.]**

**Kowalski:** "Under §14.7, the hearing is void if a required recipient
was not notified and does not appear to waive. The Popovs are within 300
feet and were not notified. I move we adjourn and re-notice."

**Hoffman:** "I disagree. Section 14.7 says the hearing is void unless the
omitted party appears and waives. They're not here, so they can't waive.
But I believe we should proceed and address the notice defect as a
curable irregularity."

**Kowalski:** "That's not what the code says. It says 'void.' Void means
void."

**Castellano:** "Kan vi niet gewoon de hoorzitting houden en hen daarna
op de hoogte stellen? Als ze bezwaar maken, kunnen we opnieuw horen."
[Translation: Can't we just hold the hearing and notify them afterward?
If they object, we can rehear.]

**Hoffman:** "We'll note the objection. The chair rules that we proceed.
Mr. Kowalski's objection is preserved for the record."

**The hearing proceeds over Kowalski's objection.**

**Marchetti testimony:** "The drainage easement on the east side of my
property makes it impossible to expand in that direction. The only
feasible location for the addition is on the west side of the existing
garage, which brings it closer to the Koss property line. I've spoken
with Mr. Koss and he initially had no objection, but after his survey
showed a different lot line, he changed his position."

**Koss testimony:** "J'ai fait faire un arpentage par Greenfield en 2022
parce que je pensais que la clôture entre nos propriétés était au mauvais
endroit. L'arpentage a montré que la ligne de lot est à 2,5 pieds plus
à l'est que ce que montre l'arpentage Harmon. Si l'arpentage Greenfield
est correct, le garage existant n'est qu'à 5,5 pieds de la ligne de lot,
et l'ajout proposé serait à seulement 1,5 pieds. C'est trop près."
[Translation: I had Greenfield do a survey in 2022 because I thought the
fence between our properties was in the wrong place. The survey showed
the lot line is 2.5 feet further east than the Harmon survey shows. If
the Greenfield survey is correct, the existing garage is only 5.5 feet
from the lot line, and the proposed addition would be only 1.5 feet.
That's too close.]

**Rao (44 Osprey Lane):** "I have no objection to the garage expansion.
It's on the opposite side from my property and doesn't affect me."

**Engel (15 Birch Court):** "Saya tinggal di belakang properti Marchetti.
Garasi yang ada sudah cukup dekat dengan pagar belakang. Ekspansi tidak
akan lebih dekat ke belakang, jadi saya tidak keberatan, tetapi saya
khawatir tentang kebisingan dari peralatan pengerjaan kayu."
[Translation: I live behind the Marchetti property. The existing garage
is already fairly close to the back fence. The expansion would not come
closer to the rear, so I don't object, but I am concerned about noise
from the woodworking equipment.]

**Vasquez (staff presentation):** "I want to flag two issues the Board
should address. First, the home occupation question. Ms. Marchetti's
application states the expansion is for a furniture restoration business.
If she is operating or intends to operate a home-based business, she
needs a Home Occupation Permit under Chapter 18. I don't have a record
of one being issued. The Board may want to condition any variance
approval on obtaining the permit, or the Board may decide the variance
can be evaluated on its merits regardless of the home occupation issue.

Second, the nonconforming structure question. The existing garage was
built in 1987 under the prior code, which had a 400 sq ft accessory
structure limit. The current code increased the limit to 600 sq ft in
2005. The existing garage at 480 sq ft conforms to the current code.
However, if the lot line is actually where Koss's survey says it is, the
existing garage's left side setback would be 5.5 feet — conforming to
the current 5-foot minimum but only barely. The proposed addition at 1.5
feet would clearly not conform. I mention this only to note that the
existing garage is not itself nonconforming under either survey, which
means §14.5 is not directly at issue here."

### SECTION H — Board Deliberation (April 24, 2026, continued)

**Hoffman:** "Let's work through the four variance conditions.

Condition (a) — unique physical characteristic. The drainage easement
is documented in the recorded survey. It restricts buildable area on the
east side. I find this is a unique physical characteristic. Is there
disagreement?"

**Kowalski:** "I agree the easement is a unique characteristic, but I'm
not satisfied that we're using the right survey to evaluate the setback.
My vote will reflect that."

**Yee:** "I agree on condition (a)."

**Castellano:** "Agreed."

**Hoffman:** "Condition (b) — minimum variance necessary. The applicant
requests 120 square feet over the 600 sq ft limit and a 1-foot side
setback variance. Staff, is there a smaller addition that would work?"

**Marchetti:** "I had an architect look at it. To fit the planer, the
bandsaw, and a finishing bench with safe clearances, 720 square feet is
the minimum. I can't go smaller and operate safely."

**Kowalski:** "Has the Board verified this? We have her word and an
unnamed architect's opinion."

**Hoffman:** "We'll note that. I'm inclined to accept the applicant's
representation on the minimum size. Condition (b) is met in my view."

**Kowalski:** "I disagree. No documentation from the architect has been
submitted."

**Hoffman:** "Condition (c) — neighborhood character. Staff noted that
720 sq ft would be the largest accessory structure on the block. Views?"

**Yee:** "It's larger but not drastically so. The homes on Osprey Lane
have garages ranging from 400 to 550. An extra 120 to 170 square feet
doesn't change the neighborhood feel. I find condition (c) met."

**Castellano:** "Agreed."

**Kowalski:** "I disagree. 720 is 30% larger than the next largest.
That's significant."

**Hoffman:** "Condition (d) — difficulty not created by applicant. The
drainage easement predates Marchetti's ownership. She didn't create it.
I find condition (d) met."

**All members agree on condition (d).**

**Hoffman:** "I also want to address the home occupation question. I
propose we approve the variance without conditioning it on the Home
Occupation Permit. The variance request is about the physical structure,
not the business use. If she needs a Home Occupation Permit, that's a
separate application under a separate chapter. We shouldn't hold up the
variance for it."

**Kowalski:** "I disagree. The stated purpose of the expansion is a
business use. If the business use isn't permitted, the justification for
the expansion evaporates."

**Yee:** "I see both sides but I'll go with the chair. The variance is
about dimensions, not use."

**Vote on variance:** Hoffman, Yee, Castellano vote to approve.
Kowalski votes to deny. **Approved 3-1.**

Conditions of approval: None. The home occupation question was not made
a condition.

### SECTION I — Corrections and Post-Hearing Developments

**Correction 1 (April 28, 2026):** The hearing minutes initially recorded
the vote as 4-1 with all members voting. The minutes are corrected to
3-1, reflecting that Brennan was absent and did not vote. The approval
remains valid (3 affirmative votes meet the §14.8 threshold).

**Post-hearing development 1 (May 2, 2026):** Victor Popov contacts the
Zoning Office to inquire about the garage expansion. He was not aware of
the hearing. He states that he has concerns about the project's impact on
street parking and about increased truck traffic from a woodworking
business.

**Post-hearing development 2 (May 5, 2026):** Kowalski files a written
dissent with the Township Clerk, arguing that (a) the hearing was void
under §14.7 due to the Popov notice failure, (b) the variance should have
been conditioned on the Home Occupation Permit, and (c) the setback
determination should not have relied on the 2019 survey when a more recent
(though unrecorded) survey exists showing a materially different lot line.

**Post-hearing development 3 (May 8, 2026):** Koss files a formal appeal
of the variance approval with the Township Board of Trustees, citing the
notice defect and the survey dispute. The appeal is filed within the
30-day appeal window.

**Post-hearing development 4 (May 10, 2026):** Marchetti applies for a
Home Occupation Permit under Chapter 18. The application is pending.

**Clarification (May 12, 2026):** Zoning Administrator Vasquez issues a
memorandum clarifying that the ZBA's decision to proceed with the hearing
over Kowalski's notice objection is a procedural ruling, not a
determination on the merits of the notice defect. The question of whether
the hearing is void under §14.7 is a legal question that the Township
Attorney must resolve if challenged. Vasquez's memorandum is her
professional opinion, not a legal determination.
