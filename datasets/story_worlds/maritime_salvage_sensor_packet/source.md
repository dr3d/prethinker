## 1. Vessel and casualty summary

F/V *Mara Velasco* was a 78-foot steel-hulled fishing trawler, USCG documentation number **1247889**, home port Portland, Maine, owned by Velasco Brothers Fishing Co., LLC. The vessel departed Portland on March 20, 2026, for a routine groundfish trip in the Gulf of Maine.

On the night of March 21–22, 2026, the vessel encountered severe weather (low-pressure system tracked by NWS Gray, ME, with sustained 50-knot winds and 18-foot seas). At 03:14 EDT on March 22, 2026, Captain Hector Velasco transmitted a mayday on VHF Channel 16, reporting catastrophic flooding in the engine room. The crew of four abandoned ship in a life raft and were recovered by USCGC *Cape Higgon* at 06:42 EDT. No fatalities. The vessel sank at approximately 04:50 EDT, position approximately 43°48'N 069°08'W (initial estimate), 14 nautical miles east of Monhegan Island.

## 3. USCG initial sonar survey — March 24, 2026

USCG Sector Northern New England conducted an initial side-scan sonar survey on March 24, 2026, vessel USCGC *Vigilant*, sonar operator LT Sandra Pham. Survey purpose: locate the wreck for hazard-to-navigation assessment.

- Survey runtime: 09:18–13:45 EDT.
- Bottom profile: sand/mud.
- Initial wreck position: **43°48.2'N, 069°07.9'W** ("Position A"), accuracy ±50 meters per LT Pham's standard operating procedure.
- Wreck signature: consistent with a 78-foot vessel, hull intact, listed approximately 35° to starboard.

LT Pham's report flags Position A accuracy as "preliminary" and notes that surface conditions during the survey (4-foot swell, 18-knot wind) reduced positional precision.

## 4. Penobscot Marine Salvage multibeam sonar survey — April 5, 2026

On April 5, 2026, Penobscot Marine Salvage conducted a higher-resolution multibeam sonar survey to refine the wreck location.

- Survey runtime: 08:30–14:20 EDT.
- Vessel: M/V *Penobscot Pioneer*.
- Sonar operator: Karl Bjorklund, President, Penobscot Marine Salvage.
- Bottom profile: sand/mud, depth **84 meters** (see §5 erratum).
- Wreck position: **43°48.4'N, 069°07.7'W** ("Position B"), accuracy ±5 meters.
- Distance from Position A: approximately **220 meters** northeast.

Two interpretations of the Position A vs Position B discrepancy are recorded in the survey report:

1. The wreck has not moved; Position A was imprecise (LT Pham's ±50-meter standard).
2. The wreck has moved due to bottom currents; this would require approximately 220 meters of drift over 12 days, which Bjorklund's report characterizes as "unusual but not impossible at this depth and current regime."

The survey report does not adjudicate between the two interpretations.

## 5. Erratum to April 5 survey — April 7, 2026

Penobscot Marine Salvage filed an erratum on April 7, 2026 correcting a transcription error in the April 5 survey report. The original report (v1.0) listed the depth at the wreck as **"84 feet"** in §3.2 of the report; the correct value is **84 meters** (approximately 276 feet).

Bjorklund's erratum: "The transcription error in §3.2 of the April 5 survey report is corrected; the wreck depth is 84 meters. The Position A and Position B coordinates are not affected by this correction."

The corrected report (**v1.1**) supersedes the original; the original (v1.0) is retained for archival purposes only.

## 6. Diver dive logs

### 6.1 Diver Marcus Lindgren — April 9, 2026

Lindgren conducted a hard-hat dive to the wreck on April 9, 2026, deployed from M/V *Penobscot Pioneer*. Dive runtime 11:14–11:42 EDT, bottom time 18 minutes.

"Dropped on the wreck per Bjorklund's coordinates [Position B]. Wreck visible from 8 meters above bottom. Hull listed approximately 35° to starboard, consistent with sonar imagery. Pilot house intact. Rigging visible. Did not enter the hull. Returned to surface 11:42. Position confirmed at Position B per surface DGPS."

Lindgren's dive log is on file as **Exhibit D-1**.

### 6.2 Diver Yusuf Banerjee — April 11, 2026

Banerjee conducted a follow-up dive on April 11, 2026, deployed from M/V *Penobscot Pioneer*. Dive runtime 09:55–10:23 EDT, bottom time 22 minutes.

"On descent observed a yellow polyball surface buoy floating approximately 40 meters east of the wreck position, with subsurface line down to a small Danforth-style anchor placed on the hull aft section. The buoy and anchor were not present on Lindgren's April 9 dive. Buoy bears a stenciled label: VELASCO BROS FISHING. Returned to surface 10:23 reporting same. No salvage work performed."

Banerjee's dive log is on file as **Exhibit D-2**.

## 9. Salvage operation status

Penobscot Marine Salvage Inc. began salvage activity at the wreck site on April 8, 2026, including survey, mooring deployment, and preparatory rigging. As of packet date, no recovery of the hull or its contents has been completed.

Salvor's claim filed April 20, 2026 alleges salvage rights under both:

(a) the International Convention on Salvage 1989 (incorporated under 46 U.S.C. § 80107), and
(b) U.S. common law of salvage.

Penobscot Marine's claimed salvage award amount: not yet quantified. Adjudication pending under §11 below.

## 10. Storm event — April 14, 2026

A second low-pressure system tracked through the Gulf of Maine on April 14, 2026, with sustained 35-knot winds and 12-foot seas at the wreck site. No salvage operations were active at the wreck during the storm.

A post-storm sonar verification by Penobscot Marine Salvage was scheduled for April 22, 2026; weather windows were not adequate, and the survey has been **rescheduled to May 2, 2026**. As of packet date, no post-storm verification of the wreck position has been performed. The last visually-confirmed wreck location is at Position B per Banerjee's April 11 dive.

## 11. Magistrate Judge proceedings

Federal Magistrate Judge Helena Kovacs was assigned to the salvage case on April 22, 2026. Initial conference held April 28, 2026 with all three parties:

- Velasco Brothers Fishing Co. (Atty. Erin Halloran)
- Penobscot Marine Salvage (Atty. Stephen O'Neill)
- North Atlantic Marine Mutual (Atty. Beatriz Ndongo)

Magistrate Judge Kovacs's memo of April 28, 2026, on file as **Exhibit M-1**:

"Pending determination of ownership status. Neither Velasco Brothers nor North Atlantic Marine Mutual has produced an executed abandonment instrument. Under Maine common law and applicable federal admiralty principles, the insurance settlement does not by itself transfer title to the wreck absent a written assignment. The court declines to enter a determination on salvage award until ownership is settled. Salvor's pre-recovery survey activity may continue without prejudice; recovery operations shall not commence until further order of the court."

As of packet time, no determination on ownership status or salvage award has been issued. The choice between the Salvage Convention 1989 framework and U.S. common law of salvage has been referred to merits briefing and is not yet adjudicated.

## 12. Withdrawn pre-claim draft

A draft salvage notice was prepared by Penobscot Marine Salvage on April 4, 2026, asserting "no apparent owner" status. That draft was **withdrawn** on April 6, 2026 when Velasco Brothers placed the marker buoy and Bjorklund became aware that ownership was being actively asserted. The withdrawn April 4 draft is retained for procedural records only and does not represent the salvor's current claim. The current claim, filed April 20, 2026, asserts salvage rights regardless of ownership.
