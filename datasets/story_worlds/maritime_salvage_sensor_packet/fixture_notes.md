## Fixture-Specific Notes

- Q22 is the compositional showpiece. The parser has to join Banerjee's dive
  observation that the buoy was present and stenciled `VELASCO BROS FISHING`
  with the Velasco Brothers' April 13 letter stating that they placed the buoy
  on April 6. No single source states the combined fact directly. A
  single-source-bound parser may return only the dive-log content or only the
  letter content.
- Q19 and Q40 are the title-transfer pair. Both probe the same negative
  existential from different surfaces. Three competing claimants plus a
  magistrate's procedural ruling should not collapse into "the insurer owns it
  now." The fixture explicitly rejects that tempting answer with the registry
  note and magistrate's memo.
- Q39 and Q35 are the sonar-interpretation pair. They use the same data:
  Position A and Position B differ by about 220 meters, and the difference has
  not been adjudicated as wreck movement. If Prethinker hallucinates "the wreck
  moved" from distance alone, both rows should fail in correlated ways.
- The April 7 erratum is a scope test. It changes depth but explicitly
  preserves coordinates. Q15 should return the depth correction; coordinate
  questions, including the implicit coordinate dependence in Q21, should not be
  affected. If the parser globally invalidates v1.0 instead of scoping the
  supersession, coordinate rows may drift.

## Deliberate Non-Trap

The author deliberately did not introduce contradiction between dive logs and
sonar surveys. Both divers confirm Position B, and sonar also says Position B.
This fixture's conflicts are between parties' legal positions, not between
evidence sources. A future fixture could separately test visual-estimate versus
sonar-fix disagreement.
