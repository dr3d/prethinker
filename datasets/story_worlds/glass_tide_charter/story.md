# Artifact 1 - Source Fixture

# The Glass Tide Charter of Lumenfall Harbor

## Purpose of this fixture

This is a deliberately dense source document for testing Prethinker rule acquisition. It contains ordinary facts, claims, corrections, source-stated rules, exceptions, temporal windows, permission rules, priority rules, quarantine logic, tax rules, council-vote logic, salvage logic, and evidence-boundary statements. A good compiler should not merely summarize it. It should build a governed symbolic surface that supports executable rules.

---

## The setting

Lumenfall Harbor is a crescent-shaped port built beneath three glass cliffs. Its engine, the Tideheart, raises and lowers the inner locks when the moon pulls the sea into the cliff-caves. Every midsummer solstice the sea becomes a shining "glass tide" from 18:00 until 22:00. During that window the harbor looks calm but the locks, turbines, and sluices become dangerously sensitive.

The harbor is governed by the Glass Tide Charter. The Charter has a Council of five voting officers: Mara Vale, the Harbor Warden; Juno Vale, the Chief Tide Engineer; Ilya Sen, the Archivist; Sera Voss, the Treasurer; and Tomas Reed, the Lighthouse Keeper. Karel Nim is the Deputy Warden but does not vote on the Council unless he is formally sitting in Mara's chair.

On Azure 17, the day of the midsummer glass tide, the following people and places mattered:

- Mara Vale was Harbor Warden.
- Juno Vale was Chief Tide Engineer and Mara's brother.
- Ilya Sen was Archivist and custodian of the Archive Vault.
- Sera Voss was Treasurer.
- Tomas Reed was Lighthouse Keeper.
- Karel Nim was Deputy Warden, but he was absent in the upland granaries all day.
- Nell Quill was an apprentice courier.
- Dr. Pella Morn was the harbor physician.
- Basil Crow was a merchant.
- Captain Orra Pike commanded the vessel Sunless Heron.
- Captain Veda Lark commanded the vessel Amber Finch.
- Dax Orr and Mira Gale were quarantine patients.

Important places included the Archive Vault, Beacon Tower, Glass Market, Quarantine Pier, West Sluice, East Gate, Moon Foundry, Temple of Loam, Harbor Hospital, and the offshore waiting ring.

---

## Charter rules read aloud before the glass tide

At noon, Ilya read the relevant Charter rules into the public record.

1. **Living-cargo quarantine rule.** Any vessel carrying living cargo must use Quarantine Pier unless the vessel has a signed clearance from both the Harbor Warden and the harbor physician. If the harbor is closed but the vessel displays an emergency signal, the vessel may temporarily dock at West Sluice for crew safety, but the emergency signal does not waive quarantine for the living cargo. The living cargo must be transferred to Quarantine Pier before it is unloaded.

2. **Glass-tide repair rule.** During the glass tide window, no Tideheart turbine repair is authorized unless the repair order is signed by both the Harbor Warden and the Chief Tide Engineer. The Archivist acting for permits may not substitute for the Chief Tide Engineer on turbine-repair authorization.

3. **Archive access rule.** An apprentice may enter the Archive Vault only if escorted by the Archivist, or if the apprentice carries a valid blue key and no storm alarm is active. If the red seal is used on a blue key, that blue key is revoked until noon of the following day.

4. **Storm-alarm oven rule.** During a storm alarm, Glass Market ovens may bake only for the hospital, the lighthouse, or quay crews. Festival cakes, merchant samples, and ordinary market stock must wait.

5. **Harbor-closure rule.** If the Tideheart pressure is above 80 and the coolant level is below 10, the Tideheart is in critical state. While the Tideheart is critical, the harbor is closed to inbound non-emergency vessels. An inbound vessel with an emergency signal may dock at West Sluice. An inbound vessel without an emergency signal must wait at the offshore waiting ring.

6. **Tax rule.** A cargo owner owes harbor tax when the cargo value is greater than 100 crowns and the cargo is not relief cargo. Relief cargo is tax-exempt even if its value is greater than 100 crowns. Cargo with value 100 crowns or less is tax-exempt by low value.

7. **Council voting rule.** A Council proposal normally passes if at least three of the five voting officers support it. If the proposal is a budget matter and the Treasurer vetoes it, the proposal fails unless the Harbor Warden issues an emergency override.

8. **Salvage rule.** A person who recovers abandoned cargo from the water earns a salvage reward unless the cargo is marked sacred. Sacred cargo must be returned to the Temple of Loam and carries no salvage reward.

9. **Quarantine patient rule.** A quarantine patient is cleared only if the physician records no fever and the patient has two negative tests at least six hours apart.

10. **Evidence rule.** A captain's statement is a claim, not a finding. A clerk's uncertified note is weaker than a certified harbor log. When a certified log contradicts an uncertified note about the same event time, the certified log controls. Watch logs, instrument records, and certified logs can support findings.

11. **Emergency-priority rule.** A Beacon Tower emergency signal overrides market deliveries and charter disputes until the emergency is closed.

12. **Acting-warden rule.** If the Harbor Warden is incapacitated and the Deputy Warden is absent, the Archivist may act for permits only. Acting for permits does not grant authority to approve Tideheart turbine repairs.

## Morning facts and observations

At dawn, the bronze owl on Beacon Tower faced the sea, and the barometer fell below 27. The watch treated those two observations as a storm warning. At 17:30, the Beacon Tower raised the storm alarm. The alarm remained active until midnight.

At 10:00, Dr. Pella recorded that Dax Orr had no fever and that Dax's first quarantine test was negative. At 17:00, Dax's second test was negative.

At 12:00, Dr. Pella recorded that Mira Gale had no fever and that Mira's first quarantine test was negative. At 17:00, Mira's second test was negative.

At 17:00, the Council voted on a proposal to build copper rails from the Glass Market to the Moon Foundry. Mara, Juno, Ilya, and Tomas supported the proposal. Sera, the Treasurer, vetoed it because it was a budget matter. Mara did not issue an emergency override for the copper rails proposal.

---

## The critical engine reading

At 17:40, Juno recorded the Tideheart pressure as 84 and the coolant level as 7. Because the pressure was above 80 and coolant was below 10, the engine was in critical state. The harbor was closed to inbound non-emergency vessels from 17:40 until the engine stabilized.

At 22:30, Juno recorded the Tideheart pressure as 61 and the coolant level as 18. The Harbor Warden's office recorded the harbor as reopened at 22:30, although the storm alarm itself remained active until midnight.

---

## The Archive Vault incident

At 17:45, Nell Quill entered the Archive Vault with a blue key. She was not escorted by Ilya Sen or any other Archivist. Because the storm alarm was active, a blue key alone did not authorize an apprentice to enter. Ilya discovered the entry at 18:05 and used the red seal on Nell's blue key. The red seal revoked Nell's blue key until noon of the following day.

Nell later said she only entered because she was delivering a sealed chart to the Archive Vault. Ilya accepted that she had a useful errand, but he did not treat the errand as an access authorization.

---

## Ships, cargo, and corrections

The Sunless Heron arrived at 18:20, during the glass tide and while the harbor was closed. It carried twelve glass eels. Glass eels are living cargo. Captain Orra Pike displayed an emergency lantern because one deckhand had been burned by a lantern battery. The watch allowed the Sunless Heron to dock temporarily at West Sluice at 18:25 for crew safety. The glass eels remained sealed aboard until they were transferred to Quarantine Pier at 18:50.

Captain Orra claimed that Mara had signed a clearance for the Sunless Heron, but no signed clearance appeared in the permit drawer, the physician's ledger, or the certified harbor log. Ilya recorded Orra's statement as a claim only.

The cargo owner for the glass eels was Captain Orra Pike, and the glass eels were valued at 300 crowns. They were not relief cargo.

The Amber Finch carried seed crystals valued at 80 crowns. A tired clerk first wrote in an uncertified note that the Amber Finch arrived at 18:50. Later, the certified harbor log corrected the arrival time to 19:10. Because the certified log controls over an uncertified note, the official arrival time of the Amber Finch was 19:10. The Amber Finch had no emergency signal, so it waited at the offshore waiting ring until the harbor reopened.

Basil Crow brought sapphire figs valued at 130 crowns. The figs were ordinary merchant cargo, not relief cargo. The Harbor Hospital also received lamp rice valued at 240 crowns. The lamp rice was marked relief cargo.

---

## Repairs and acting authority

At 18:15, Mara and Juno signed repair order 71. The order authorized a Tideheart turbine repair from 18:30 until 19:05. Because both the Harbor Warden and the Chief Tide Engineer signed the order, and because the repair occurred during the glass tide, repair order 71 was valid.

At 19:30, Mara was injured by falling prism glass and became incapacitated. Karel Nim, the Deputy Warden, was still absent in the upland granaries. Under the acting-warden rule, Ilya could act for permits after 19:30, but only for permits.

At 20:05, Ilya issued a docking permit to the Amber Finch for use after the harbor reopened. That permit was within his acting permit authority, but it did not override the harbor closure.

At 20:15, Ilya signed repair order 72 by himself. The order attempted to authorize another Tideheart turbine repair during the glass tide. Juno did not sign order 72, and Ilya could not substitute for the Chief Tide Engineer. Repair order 72 was not authorized.

---

## Emergency priority and ovens

At 18:25, Beacon Tower received a hospital emergency signal requesting battery coolant and night bread for the burn ward. The emergency remained open until 21:00. During that emergency, market deliveries and charter disputes were deferred.

At 20:40, the Glass Market ovens baked night bread for the hospital. That baking was allowed under the storm-alarm oven rule. At 21:00, the Glass Market ovens were asked to bake festival cakes for the solstice crowd. Because the storm alarm was still active and festival cakes were ordinary market food, the festival cakes were not allowed.

---

## Salvage events

At 21:10, Tomas Reed recovered blue-salt crate C17 from the inner basin. The crate had been abandoned by an unknown boatman and was not marked sacred. Under the salvage rule, Tomas earned a salvage reward for blue-salt crate C17.

At 21:25, Nell Quill recovered the bell of Saint Loam from the shallows. The bell was marked sacred. Because sacred cargo must be returned to the Temple of Loam, Nell did not earn a salvage reward for the bell.

---

## End-of-night notes

At 22:30, the Tideheart stabilized and the harbor reopened. The Amber Finch entered after reopening. The storm alarm remained active until midnight, so Archive blue-key access and market baking were still restricted after the harbor reopened.

Ilya closed the record with one warning: "A claim may explain why someone acted, but it does not become a finding merely because the person speaking is a captain, a treasurer, a warden, or a friend. The Charter is not suspicious of people; it is suspicious of unsupported state changes."
