# Halcyon Heights Homeowners Association — 2025 Annual Census Reconciliation

## Purpose

Each January, the Halcyon Heights HOA conducts a property census to determine voting eligibility, assessment rates, and common-area allocation. The 2025 census was complicated by a boundary annexation, a condominium conversion, three ownership transfers during the count period, and a disputed unit classification. This reconciliation was prepared by Association Manager Corinne Flagg and presented to the Board on March 15, 2025.

## Base Property Count (as of December 31, 2024)

The HOA covers a planned development with the following unit types:

- **Single-family detached homes:** 84 units (Lots 1–84)
- **Townhomes:** 36 units (Units T-1 through T-36)
- **Condominiums (The Terrace):** 24 units (Units C-1 through C-24)

**Total base units: 144**

Each unit carries one vote and one assessment. Condominium units pay 75% of the standard assessment because their building exteriors are maintained by the Terrace Condominium Association, not the HOA.

## Annexation — Meadow Ridge Addition

Effective January 15, 2025, the HOA annexed the Meadow Ridge subdivision under a boundary agreement approved by the Board in November 2024. Meadow Ridge adds:

- **Single-family detached homes:** 12 units (Lots 85–96)

These 12 units are subject to full assessment and full voting rights effective January 15. For purposes of the January census, they are counted as HOA members.

**Post-annexation unit count: 156**

## Condominium Conversion — The Terrace Building B

On February 1, 2025, Building B of The Terrace (Units C-13 through C-18, six units) completed a conversion to individually owned townhome-style units. The conversion was approved by the Terrace Condominium Association and the HOA Board.

The conversion changes the unit classification:
- Before: 6 condominium units paying 75% assessment
- After: 6 townhome units paying 100% assessment

The 6 converted units are reclassified from condominium to townhome for voting and assessment purposes. The total unit count does not change (they were already counted), but the unit-type totals shift:

- Condominiums: 24 − 6 = 18
- Townhomes: 36 + 6 = 42

**Post-conversion unit count: still 156 total**

## Ownership Transfers During Count Period

Three ownership transfers occurred between January 1 and March 1:

**Transfer 1:** Lot 47 (single-family) sold from the Eriksen family to the Chao family. Closing date: January 22. Both parties were assessed. The HOA's policy is that the seller is responsible for assessments through the closing date and the buyer from the day after closing. For voting purposes, the unit carries one vote regardless of transfer — the current owner as of the census date (March 1) holds the vote.

**Transfer 2:** Unit T-19 (townhome) sold from Delgado to Krishnamurthy. Closing date: February 14.

**Transfer 3:** Lot 91 (Meadow Ridge, single-family) sold from the original developer, Highmark Homes, to the Yuen family. Closing date: February 28. This is a Meadow Ridge unit that was annexed on January 15. Highmark Homes held the unit from annexation through February 28.

**Ownership transfers do not change the total unit count.** The count is 156 regardless of who owns each unit.

## Disputed Classification — Lot 52

Lot 52 is listed in the original plat as a single-family detached home. However, the current owner, Gerald Nast, converted the property into a duplex in 2019 with a building permit from the county. The HOA was not notified of the conversion. Nast has been paying one assessment and casting one vote since 2019.

Flagg's assessment: "Under HOA bylaws, a duplex is two dwelling units and should carry two assessments and two votes. However, the bylaw provision has never been enforced against a converted property. The Board should determine whether to retroactively reclassify Lot 52 as two units or grandfather Nast's single-unit status."

The Board discussed the matter on March 15 and **deferred the decision** to the April meeting pending legal review. For the 2025 census, Lot 52 is counted as one unit under its current classification.

**Census unit count as of March 1: 156** (the disputed classification does not change the count because the Board deferred the reclassification).

## Vacancy and Occupancy

Flagg reported the following occupancy data:

- Occupied units: 141
- Vacant units: 15 (including 4 Meadow Ridge units still held by Highmark Homes as unsold inventory, 3 Terrace condominiums listed for sale, and 8 other units vacant for various reasons)

**Vacancy does not affect the unit count, assessment obligation, or voting eligibility.** Vacant units are still assessed and still carry votes (exercisable by the owner).

## Assessment Revenue Calculation

Flagg presented the following assessment calculation:

- Standard annual assessment: $3,600 per unit
- Condominium reduced rate: $2,700 per unit (75% of $3,600)

| Unit type | Count | Rate | Subtotal |
|-----------|-------|------|----------|
| Single-family (original) | 84 | $3,600 | $302,400 |
| Townhomes (original + converted) | 42 | $3,600 | $151,200 |
| Condominiums (post-conversion) | 18 | $2,700 | $48,600 |
| Meadow Ridge single-family | 12 | $3,600 | $43,200 |
| **Total** | **156** | | **$545,400** |

**Note:** If the Board reclassifies Lot 52 as two units at the April meeting, the total would become 157 units and revenue would increase by $3,600 to $549,000.

## Voting Eligibility Summary

- Total eligible votes: 156
- Quorum for annual meeting: 40% = 63 votes (rounded up)
- Meadow Ridge units have full voting rights as of January 15
- The 4 Highmark Homes developer units carry votes exercisable by Highmark as owner
- Lot 52 carries one vote under current classification
