# Carrow Valley Community Land Trust — Board Meeting Record, October 2025

## Board Composition

The Carrow Valley Community Land Trust is governed by a seven-member Board of Directors. The officers are: Chair, Vice-Chair, Secretary, and Treasurer. Officers serve one-year terms. The Board meets monthly.

Current Board members (as of October 1, 2025):

- **Suki Okonkwo** — Secretary
- **Rajan Patel** — Treasurer
- **Bram Janssen** — Director
- **Kendra Lowe** — Director

## Governance Rules

**GR-1: Quorum.** Four members constitute a quorum. No binding vote may occur without a quorum present.

**GR-2: Chair Authority.** The Chair presides over meetings, recognizes speakers, and casts tie-breaking votes. The Chair may not make motions.

**GR-3: Vice-Chair Succession.** If the Chair is absent or recused, the Vice-Chair assumes all Chair authority for the duration of the absence or recusal. If both are absent, the Secretary presides but may not cast tie-breaking votes.

**GR-4: Recusal.** A member with a financial interest in a matter under vote must recuse. Recusal reduces the quorum requirement by one for that vote only.

**GR-5: Delegation.** The Chair may delegate signing authority on contracts valued under $10,000 to any officer. Delegations expire at the end of the meeting in which they are made unless the Board votes to extend.

**GR-6: Emergency Authority.** Between meetings, the Chair may authorize expenditures up to $5,000 without Board vote. The Vice-Chair may exercise emergency authority only if the Chair is formally unavailable (traveling, ill, or recused from the specific matter).

## October 14 Meeting — Regular Session

**Present:** Marsh (Chair), Herrera (Vice-Chair), Okonkwo (Secretary), Patel (Treasurer), Janssen, Lowe. **Absent:** Engel (travel, notified in advance).

**Quorum check:** Six of seven present. Quorum met.

### Item 1: Trail Maintenance Contract

Marsh presented a trail maintenance contract with Greenline Services for $8,400. Janssen moved to approve. Patel seconded.

Before the vote, Marsh disclosed that her brother-in-law is a subcontractor for Greenline Services. She recused herself from the vote under GR-4. Upon recusal, Herrera assumed Chair authority under GR-3.

**Vote (Herrera presiding):** Herrera (yes), Okonkwo (yes), Patel (yes), Janssen (yes), Lowe (no). Motion passed 4-1. Marsh did not vote.

After the vote, Marsh resumed the Chair.

### Item 2: Community Garden Lease

Okonkwo presented a draft lease for a community garden on Trust parcel 14-C. The lease is valued at $2,400 per year. Discussion ensued about whether the lease should require the tenant to carry liability insurance.

Lowe moved to approve the lease with an insurance requirement. Janssen seconded.

**Vote (Marsh presiding):** Herrera (yes), Okonkwo (yes), Patel (no), Janssen (yes), Lowe (yes). Marsh did not vote (the Chair does not vote except to break ties). Motion passed 4-1.

### Item 3: Signage Contract Delegation

Marsh requested Board approval to delegate signing authority for a signage contract with Alderton Design, valued at $6,200, to Vice-Chair Herrera. Under GR-5, the Chair may delegate signing authority on contracts under $10,000 to any officer.

Patel moved to approve the delegation. Okonkwo seconded.

**Vote:** Herrera (yes), Okonkwo (yes), Patel (yes), Janssen (yes), Lowe (yes). Passed 5-0. Marsh did not vote.

Marsh noted for the record: "The delegation to Vice-Chair Herrera for the Alderton Design signage contract expires at the end of this meeting unless the Board votes to extend it."

No extension vote was held.

### Item 4: Emergency Expenditure Ratification

Herrera reported that on October 3, while Marsh was traveling, he authorized an emergency expenditure of $4,800 for storm-damage tree removal under GR-6. He presented the invoice from Bark & Branch Arborists.

Lowe asked whether Herrera had authority to authorize the expenditure. Herrera stated: "Chair Marsh was traveling from October 1 through October 5. Under GR-6, the Vice-Chair may exercise emergency authority when the Chair is formally unavailable."

Marsh confirmed she was traveling and formally unavailable during that period.

Patel moved to ratify the emergency expenditure. Janssen seconded.

**Vote:** Herrera (yes), Okonkwo (yes), Patel (yes), Janssen (yes), Lowe (abstain). Passed 4-0-1. Marsh did not vote.

### Item 5: Parcel Acquisition Discussion

Marsh introduced a discussion about acquiring a 2.3-acre parcel adjacent to Trust land from the Whitford family. Estimated price: $45,000. No motion was made. Marsh noted: "This item will return at the November meeting with a formal proposal and appraisal."

## October 21 — Between-Meeting Events

**October 16:** Herrera signed the Alderton Design signage contract on behalf of the Trust.

**October 18:** A water main break on Trust property required emergency repair. Marsh was available. She authorized an emergency expenditure of $3,200 for the repair under GR-6, within the $5,000 limit.

**October 20:** Lowe emailed the Board requesting that the November meeting agenda include a vote on whether to rescind the trail maintenance contract with Greenline Services, citing new information about Greenline's licensing status.

**October 21:** Secretary Okonkwo confirmed receipt of Lowe's request and added it to the November agenda.

## Authority Questions Arising

The following questions were raised by Treasurer Patel in an email to the Board on October 22:

1. Was Herrera's October 16 signing of the Alderton contract valid? The delegation was made during the October 14 meeting and was stated to expire at the end of that meeting. No extension vote was held. October 16 is after the meeting.

2. Was Herrera's October 3 emergency expenditure within his authority? Marsh confirmed she was traveling and formally unavailable.

3. Can Lowe request a rescission vote on the Greenline contract given that she voted against the contract originally?

Marsh responded on October 23: "Question 1 is a valid concern. The delegation appears to have expired. I will ask counsel whether the signing is binding. Question 2: I confirm Herrera acted within his authority under GR-6. Question 3: Any Board member may request an agenda item. Lowe's prior vote does not disqualify her from requesting reconsideration."
