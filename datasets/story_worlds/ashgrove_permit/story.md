# Ashgrove Permit Lifecycle

## Permit Record: Commercial Renovation at 280 Elm Street

### PERMIT RULES (Ashgrove Township Building Code, Chapter 5)

**§5.1 — Permit Duration.** A building permit is valid for 12 months from
the date of issuance. If construction has not commenced within 6 months
of issuance, the permit expires automatically. "Commenced" means the
foundation inspection has been requested and passed.

**§5.2 — Extensions.** The permit holder may request one 6-month extension
before the permit expires. The Building Inspector must approve or deny the
extension within 10 business days. An approved extension adds 6 months to
the original expiration date, not to the date of the extension request.

**§5.3 — Suspension.** The Building Inspector may suspend a permit if a
stop-work order is issued due to a code violation, an unresolved safety
hazard, or a failure to schedule required inspections. A suspended permit's
expiration clock is tolled: time does not run during the suspension period.
The clock resumes when the suspension is lifted.

**§5.4 — Reinstatement.** A suspended permit is reinstated when the
condition that caused the suspension is resolved and the Building Inspector
issues a written reinstatement notice. The permit holder has 15 business
days after reinstatement to schedule the next required inspection. Failure
to do so results in automatic permit revocation.

**§5.5 — Inspections.** Required inspections in order: (1) foundation,
(2) framing, (3) mechanical/electrical/plumbing (MEP), (4) final. Each
inspection must be requested by the permit holder and completed by the
Building Inspector. The Inspector must complete a requested inspection
within 5 business days of the request. If the Inspector fails to inspect
within 5 business days, the permit holder may request an extension of
equal length at no cost.

**§5.6 — Certificate of Occupancy.** A Certificate of Occupancy (CO) is
issued upon passing the final inspection. No occupancy is permitted before
the CO is issued. A temporary CO may be issued for a portion of the
building if that portion passes all inspections; the temporary CO is
valid for 90 days.
