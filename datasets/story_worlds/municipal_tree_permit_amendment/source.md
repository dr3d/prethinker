## 1. Site context

The project site is a 0.8-acre lot at 247 Maple Crest Drive, currently improved with a single-family residence to be demolished. The applicant proposes a 12-unit residential infill development requiring extensive site preparation. A pre-permit tree survey conducted March 24, 2026 by Apex Land Survey identified 25 trees on the property, of which **23 trees have diameter at breast height (DBH) of 6 inches or greater** and are subject to Brookhaven Heritage Tree Ordinance (Code Chapter 18, hereafter "the Ordinance"). The remaining 2 trees are below 6" DBH and are not subject to the Ordinance.

The Ordinance protects trees meeting any of the following criteria, per **§18-12(b)**:

(i) Native species with DBH ≥ 24 inches;
(ii) *Acer saccharum* (Sugar Maple), *Quercus alba* (White Oak), *Quercus rubra* (Red Oak), or *Ulmus americana* (American Elm) with DBH ≥ 18 inches; or
(iii) Any tree designated by the Tree Warden as having historic, ecological, or community significance.

Invasive species per **§18-13(c)**, including *Acer platanoides* (Norway Maple) and *Ailanthus altissima* (Tree of Heaven), are explicitly excluded from heritage protection.

## 3. Post-permit arborist re-survey — April 14, 2026

On April 12, 2026, the applicant's project manager Joaquin Estevez retained **Dr. Robin Tahir, ISA Certified Arborist (NE-7421A)**, to conduct a more detailed re-survey of the trees designated for removal in connection with an updated landscape mitigation plan. Dr. Tahir's re-survey, conducted April 14, 2026, produced two material findings.

### Finding 1 — Tree #19 species reclassification

Dr. Tahir's bark, bud, and twig examination indicated that tree #19, identified by Apex as Norway Maple, is in fact ***Acer saccharum* (Sugar Maple)**. Apex's identification was made on March 24, when the tree was unleafed; Apex relied primarily on canopy silhouette and twig morphology. Dr. Tahir's April examination, with leaves emerging, observed the broadly serrated, U-shaped sinus pattern characteristic of Sugar Maple, distinguished from Norway Maple by sap analysis (clear, not milky) and bark plate pattern.

### Finding 2 — Tree #19 DBH re-measurement

Dr. Tahir's re-measurement at standardized DBH height (4.5 feet above grade per ANSI A300 Part 7) yielded **24.5 inches DBH**, not the 22 inches reported by Apex. Apex's measurement appears to have been taken below the standardized height.

Combining Findings 1 and 2: tree #19 is a Sugar Maple of 24.5" DBH. Under Ordinance §18-12(b)(ii) (Sugar Maple ≥ 18" DBH), and independently under §18-12(b)(i) (native species ≥ 24" DBH), tree #19 is a heritage protected tree.

Dr. Tahir's re-survey report is on file as **Exhibit T-1**.

## 12. City Solicitor memo — May 4, 2026

Memo from City Solicitor **Adriana Brennan, Esq.**, on file as **Exhibit C-1**:

"The question presented is whether the removal of tree #19 on April 21, 2026 was a violation of the Ordinance, given the following timeline: (a) Dr. Tahir's certified arborist report identified tree #19 as a heritage Sugar Maple on April 14; (b) the applicant filed amendment TR-2026-014-A1 on April 18 incorporating Dr. Tahir's findings; (c) tree #19 was felled on April 21 by Kowalski & Sons under the original permit; (d) the amendment was issued on April 25.

"Two principles apply: (i) the 'permit in force' principle, under which the original permit's authorization for tree #19's removal as a Norway Maple remained operative until the amendment was formally issued on April 25; and (ii) the 'constructive notice' principle, under which a permit-holder may be bound by information of which they had actual knowledge, even before formal amendment.

"The question therefore turns substantially on whether Kowalski & Sons (or the applicant) had actual knowledge of Dr. Tahir's findings before April 21. The contractor's April 28 email and the project manager's April 30 statement together indicate that Estevez did not notify Kowalski & Sons before April 21. The actual-knowledge question is not resolved on this record.

"Pending hearing scheduled May 12, 2026. No determination is issued at this time."
