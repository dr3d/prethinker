# Challenge Strategy — The Orchard Inheritance Game

## Primary Attack Surface
**Possession vs ownership vs inheritance**: Pye possesses but doesn't own the Medlar. Wye Valley owns but doesn't physically possess the Blenheim Orange. Carys possesses the Greengage but her ownership is disputed. The Cider Row was pledged but not transferred. Each tree has a different relationship between who has it, who owns it, and how they got it.

## Secondary Surfaces
- **Informal vs formal transfer**: A birthday card vs a bill of sale vs a verbal loan vs a pledge agreement
- **Surviving rights**: Does a verbal harvest loan survive the grantor's death?
- **Stale obligations**: The £600 equalization payment was never made and the creditor estate is distributed
- **Over-recovery**: The cooperative harvested more than the debt amount

## Expected Failure Modes
- Treating Carys as the owner when the gift is disputed and unresolved
- Treating Pye as the owner when he only has harvest possession and hasn't filed a claim
- Treating the Blenheim Orange as part of the estate when it was validly sold
- Treating the Cider Row pledge as still active when it was satisfied and released
