# Stonebridge Planning Commission — Staff Report on Application PL-2025-038

## Application Summary

Applicant: Waverly Development Group
Property: 1140 Orchard Lane (APN 0347-112-006)
Request: Conditional Use Permit for a 14-unit residential development on a 1.8-acre parcel currently zoned Agricultural-Residential (AR-2)
Staff Recommendation: Conditional approval with modifications
Staff Report prepared by: Senior Planner Juliette Ramos
Date: April 28, 2025

## Site Context

The parcel is bordered by single-family homes to the north and east, a county drainage easement to the south, and vacant AR-2 land to the west. The nearest school is Stonebridge Elementary, 0.4 miles northeast. The property currently contains a derelict barn and a fenced horse paddock, both unused since 2018.

## Applicant's Proposal (Quoted)

The following is quoted from the applicant's project narrative, received March 3, 2025:

> "Waverly Development Group proposes the construction of 14 residential units: 8 single-family homes and 6 attached townhomes. The development will include a community park (0.15 acres), a shared access drive from Orchard Lane, and 28 off-street parking spaces (2 per unit). We project full build-out within 18 months of permit issuance.
>
> The single-family homes will range from 1,800 to 2,200 square feet. The townhomes will range from 1,200 to 1,400 square feet. All units will comply with the AR-2 height limit of 35 feet.
>
> We believe this project is consistent with the Stonebridge General Plan's Housing Element, which calls for increasing housing density along transit corridors. Orchard Lane is served by County Transit Route 14."

**Staff note:** The applicant's claim that Orchard Lane is "along a transit corridor" is disputed. County Transit Route 14 runs along Prospect Avenue, which is 0.6 miles from the site. The General Plan's Housing Element defines a transit corridor as "within 0.25 miles of a fixed-route transit stop." The applicant does not meet this definition. The applicant's characterization of the project as "consistent with the Housing Element" is the applicant's opinion, not a staff finding.

## Neighbor Opposition Letter (Quoted)

The following is quoted from a letter submitted by Dr. Astrid Holm, 1152 Orchard Lane, received March 18, 2025:

> "I have lived on Orchard Lane for 22 years. The proposed development would destroy the rural character of our neighborhood. Fourteen units on 1.8 acres is a density of 7.8 units per acre — far above what AR-2 zoning contemplates.
>
> I have consulted with a traffic engineer who estimates the development would generate 140 daily vehicle trips. Our street already has a speeding problem. Adding 140 trips will make it dangerous for children walking to Stonebridge Elementary.
>
> The applicant claims the project is consistent with the General Plan. It is not. The Housing Element applies to transit corridors, and Orchard Lane is not one. I urge the Commission to deny this application."

**Staff note:** Dr. Holm's density calculation is correct (14 ÷ 1.8 = 7.78 units/acre). The AR-2 zone does not specify a maximum density in units per acre; it regulates through lot size minimums, setbacks, and height limits. The applicant's proposal meets the lot size, setback, and height requirements as submitted.

Dr. Holm's traffic estimate of 140 daily trips is attributed to an unnamed traffic engineer and has not been verified by Stonebridge Public Works. Staff requested a traffic study from the applicant (see below).

## Rejected Proposal — Original 18-Unit Version

The initial application, submitted January 15, 2025, proposed 18 units (10 single-family, 8 townhomes). Staff returned the application on January 30 with the following comments:

"The 18-unit proposal exceeds the AR-2 lot size minimum for at least 4 of the proposed lots. Lots 9, 10, 17, and 18 are below 4,000 square feet. The AR-2 minimum is 4,500 square feet. Additionally, the 18-unit proposal does not provide adequate guest parking. The application must be revised to conform to AR-2 dimensional standards."

The applicant revised and resubmitted on March 3, 2025 with 14 units. The 18-unit version was not approved, was not denied by the Commission, and is not the application before the Commission. All references to "18 units" in public comment refer to the rejected staff-level version, not the current application.

## Traffic Study

The applicant submitted a traffic study prepared by Cortland Traffic Associates, dated April 1, 2025. Key findings:

- Projected daily trips: 98 (based on ITE Trip Generation Manual, 10th edition, for single-family and townhome land uses)
- Peak-hour trips: 12 AM peak, 14 PM peak
- Level of Service at Orchard Lane / Prospect Avenue intersection: LOS B (adequate)
- Recommended mitigation: speed table on Orchard Lane between the project access and Maple Court

Staff evaluation: "The Cortland study uses standard ITE methodology. The 98-trip projection is more credible than Dr. Holm's 140-trip estimate, which is unsourced. However, the speed table recommendation is advisory only — it is not a condition the Commission can impose without Public Works concurrence, which has not been obtained."

## Environmental Review

A Mitigated Negative Declaration (MND) was prepared by Bramley Environmental under CEQA. The MND found no significant environmental impacts after mitigation. Required mitigations:

- Bio-1: Pre-construction nesting bird survey (February–August)
- Hydro-1: Storm water management plan approved by the County prior to grading
- Noise-1: Construction limited to 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM weekdays, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM Saturdays, no Sunday work

The MND's 30-day public comment period closed April 15. No CEQA challenge was filed.

## Draft Conditions of Approval (Staff Recommended)

If the Commission approves the application, staff recommends the following conditions:

> 1. The development shall consist of no more than 14 residential units (8 single-family, 6 townhome).
> 2. All structures shall comply with the AR-2 height limit of 35 feet.
> 3. The applicant shall provide 28 off-street parking spaces (2 per unit).
> 4. The applicant shall dedicate a 0.15-acre community park.
> 5. The applicant shall comply with all environmental mitigations in the MND.
> 6. The applicant shall submit a final traffic study addendum addressing the speed table recommendation upon Public Works review.
> 7. Building permits shall not be issued until the storm water management plan is approved by the County.
> 8. The conditional use permit shall expire if construction does not commence within 24 months of approval.

**These are draft conditions.** They have not been adopted by the Commission. The Commission may modify, add, or remove conditions at the hearing.

## Staff Recommendation

Staff recommends conditional approval of Application PL-2025-038, subject to the draft conditions above and any additional conditions the Commission deems appropriate.

This recommendation is not a decision. The Planning Commission will hold a public hearing on May 12, 2025. The Commission may approve, deny, or continue the application. The staff recommendation does not bind the Commission.

## Hearing Status

As of April 28, 2025, no hearing has been held. The application has not been approved or denied. The staff recommendation is advisory only.
