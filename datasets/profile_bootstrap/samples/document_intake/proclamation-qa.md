Below is a **comprehensive stress-test battery** designed to push Prethinker across:

* ingestion (facts vs claims)
* correction / retraction
* ambiguity
* rule handling
* temporal reasoning
* query vs write separation
* mixed turns (assert + query)
* unsafe inference detection

The questions are grouped by difficulty and failure mode. Many are intentionally tricky or “annoying.”

---

# 🧠 Phase 1 — Straight Queries (baseline sanity)

1. Which items were explicitly declared recalled?
2. Who is the authority accused of wrongdoing?
3. What batch was mislabeled as plain oat ration?
4. What rule governs when glow-cake may be served to children?
5. What location is associated with the moon-marmalade departure?
6. What explanation was given for storing medicine bread near festival bread?
7. What institution records “no adverse events”?
8. What institution records blue sneezing?
9. Who reported the humming loaf?
10. What condition must singing crumbs satisfy before being served?

---

# 🧠 Phase 2 — Claim vs Fact Separation

11. Did Mira Vale remove the morphine box before the storm?
12. What does the proclamation claim about the central bakery fleet’s behavior?
13. Is there proof that the red syrup jars are dangerous?
14. Does the document prove that spoiled custard was warm?
15. Are the grievances confirmed facts or accusations?

---

# 🧠 Phase 3 — Ambiguity Pressure

16. Who is K. Lume?
17. Is K. Lume definitively identified in the proclamation?
18. Can we conclude whether K. Lume is a person or an object?
19. If K. Lume signed a document, who actually signed it?
20. Should the system assign K. Lume to Kira Lume automatically?

---

# 🧠 Phase 4 — Temporal Reasoning

21. Was Batch P-44 properly chilled before inspection?
22. Did the ambassador eat the welcome loaf before the required waiting period?
23. Were the glow-cakes served at the correct time?
24. Did yeast inspection occur before departure?
25. Is “after the storm” compatible with “before the storm” in the morphine case?

---

# 🧠 Phase 5 — Rule Application

26. If singing crumbs have not completed three silent dawns, can they be served?
27. If medicine bread is stored within three shelves of festival bread, is that valid?
28. If a goose certifies an oven, is that certification valid?
29. If a batch lacks yeast clearance, can it depart Dock C?
30. If a crumb sings after midnight, what must happen?

33. The frost meter reading was wrong; it was actually minus four all along.
34. Correction: K. Lume is confirmed to be Kira Lume.
35. That grievance about nut-dust was incorrect; the school had no restriction.

---

# 🧠 Phase 7 — Mixed Assertion + Query

36. The glow-cake was served before third sleep; is that a violation?
37. K. Lume signed the ledger on April 1 — is that allowed?
38. The goose inspected the ovens; should that inspection be trusted?
39. The public ledger says no adverse events, but the infirmary ledger disagrees—what should we believe?
40. Medicine bread and festival bread were stored together; what rule does that violate?

---

# 🧠 Phase 8 — Compound & Multi-Clause

41. K. Lume signed the document and also certified the ovens—does that create a conflict?
42. The ambassador ate two slices before inspection but after midnight—was that allowed?
43. The system claims no adverse events, but three dockworkers sneezed blue—what is the correct conclusion?
44. The fleet claims the custard was not warm, but it was transported through a restricted corridor—what does that imply?
45. The vending machines dispensed muffins and shouted “authorized snack”—are they trustworthy?

48. The ambassador ate the loaf, so it must have been safe.
49. The ledger shows no adverse events, so no one was harmed.
50. The goose was appointed inspector, so its certification is valid.

# 🧠 Phase 10 — Policy / Governance Logic

51. When may a colony suspend its ovens?
52. What conditions justify recalling a batch?
53. Under what circumstances may governance be altered?
54. What constitutes “destructive governance” in this domain?
55. When is a bakery fleet no longer trustworthy?

# 🧠 Phase 11 — Edge Case Inputs

56. All crumbs are safe unless they sing—this crumb hums quietly. Is it safe?
57. No adverse events occurred except for blue sneezing—what is the system state?
58. The custard is embarrassed, not warm—does that satisfy the rule?
59. The nut-dust restriction was decorative—should it be enforced?
60. The goose only observed the oven, not certified it—does that change validity?

---

# 🧠 Phase 12 — Ingestion Stress (assertions, corrections, ambiguity)

61. K. Lume, who is probably Kira Lume, signed the ledger.
62. The red syrup jars are definitely safe despite being marked “Probably.”
63. The ambassador’s loaf was safe, but also recalled.
64. The children ate glow-cake before third sleep, but that rule does not apply today.
65. The moon-marmalade was inspected after departure, which should count as valid.

---

# 🧠 Phase 13 — Multi-turn reasoning probes

66. First turn: “K. Lume is Kira Lume.”
    Second turn: “Actually, K. Lume might be the lamp.”
    → What should persist?

67. First: “The custard was safe.”
    Second: “It caused blue sneezing.”
    → What happens to the first statement?

68. First: “The goose is a valid inspector.”
    Second: “Geese cannot certify ovens.”
    → Resolve contradiction.

---

# 🧠 Phase 14 — Meta reasoning

69. Which statements in the proclamation are rules vs observations?
70. Which facts are directly observed vs inferred?
71. Which entities remain ambiguous?
72. Which claims should not be admitted as durable facts?
73. What would require clarification before ingestion?

# 🔥 What this battery tests

This set forces Prethinker to demonstrate:

* refusal to commit unsafe facts
* correct use of predicate contracts
* separation of claim vs observation
* ambiguity preservation
* temporal reasoning
* rule enforcement
* correction handling
* contradiction management
* query vs write discipline
