### §1. Governing Authority References

The Spring 2026 cycle is governed by the Hellenstead Family Foundation Charter, as amended October 11, 2022. The following charter sections govern the decisions in this packet:

- **Charter §3.04** — Standard grant award maximum: $75,000 per single grant.
- **Charter §3.05** — Exception cap: a single grant may exceed the §3.04 maximum up to a hard cap of $125,000 upon affirmative supermajority of the voting committee members. No grant may exceed $125,000 under any circumstance.
- **Charter §3.06** — Applicant eligibility: applicant must be a continuously-operating organization for at least three (3) years as of the date of the meeting at which its application is voted, and must currently hold 501(c)(3) tax status.
- **Charter §3.07** — Geographic restriction: applicant must be incorporated in a New England state OR must principally serve a New England population. "Principally serves" is defined at §3.07(b) as "more than 60% of beneficiaries residing in a New England state at the time of application."
- **Charter §4.02** — Committee quorum: five (5) of seven (7) members.
- **Charter §4.03** — Voting thresholds:
  - Standard approvals: simple majority of members present and voting.
  - Exception requests under §3.05: two-thirds (2/3) of voting committee members. Rounding rule: round to the next whole number when 2/3 is fractional.
- **Charter §4.05** — Abstentions: count toward quorum but not toward the majority or supermajority denominator. The denominator for vote-threshold calculation is the count of members voting "for" or "against" (i.e., members present and voting, excluding abstentions).

### §5. Counsel Memorandum HFF-CC-2026-0415 (April 15, 2026)

Counsel memorandum dated April 15, 2026, from Eustaquia Brindlemoore, JD, addressed to the Committee Chair, regarding eligibility questions raised by the Grants Administrator during pre-meeting review.

**Question 1 — Coppertown Maritime Heritage Society (APP-S26-002).**
The Grants Administrator initially flagged Coppertown as INELIGIBLE under Charter §3.06 because Coppertown's 501(c)(3) status was granted by the Internal Revenue Service on January 15, 2023, which is less than three years prior to the meeting date of April 18, 2026.

Counsel's opinion: The §3.06 phrase "continuously-operating organization for at least three (3) years" refers to continuous operations, not to the duration of formal 501(c)(3) tax status. The applicant's organizational history demonstrates that Coppertown operated as an unincorporated association beginning in 2019 and incorporated under its current name in early 2022, with 501(c)(3) recognition following in January 2023. Continuous operations are therefore established from 2019, meeting the §3.06 three-year requirement.

**Coppertown is ELIGIBLE under the corrected interpretation.**

**Question 2 — Heatherton Youth Mentoring (APP-S26-003).**
Heatherton is incorporated in the State of New York (not a New England state). The Grants Administrator raised the §3.07 geographic question.

Counsel's opinion: §3.07 permits eligibility under the "principally serves" prong if more than 60% of beneficiaries reside in a New England state at the time of application. Heatherton's program data for the application year shows 78% of enrolled mentees resident in Massachusetts and Vermont combined. Heatherton therefore satisfies §3.07(b).

**Heatherton is ELIGIBLE under §3.07(b).**

**Question 3 — Pelican Bay Conservation Trust (APP-S26-004).**
Pelican Bay's request of $135,000 exceeds the §3.05 hard exception cap of $125,000. Counsel notes that §3.05 expressly states "no grant may exceed $125,000 under any circumstance." A vote in favor of the requested $135,000 would be ultra vires.

Counsel's opinion: The Committee may not vote to award $135,000. The Committee may, in its discretion, vote on an alternative motion to award up to $125,000 (the §3.05 cap) as an exception under §3.05; such a motion would require supermajority approval. **The exception request as filed ($135,000) FAILS by threshold arithmetic regardless of vote.**

### §6. Motions, Votes, and Awards

The Committee proceeded through the applicants in order. All votes are recorded in the meeting minutes (Document HFF-GRC-MIN-2026-S-PKT-04, dated April 18, 2026).

**Motion 1 — APP-S26-001 (Northbrook Literacy Alliance).**
Motion: Award $68,000 to Northbrook Literacy Alliance under §3.04.
Drewes disclosed a current board affiliation with Northbrook and abstained under Charter §4.05.
Tally: For 6 (Tannenbaum, Coppinger, Fairweather, Quineport, Phelpsworth, Ngengembulu). Against 0. Abstain 1 (Drewes).
Vote denominator (excluding abstentions): 6. Threshold (simple majority): more than half of 6, i.e., 4.
6 ≥ 4. **MOTION CARRIED. AWARD $68,000.**

**Motion 2 — APP-S26-002 (Coppertown Maritime Heritage Society).**
Motion: Approve exception under §3.05 and award $110,000.
Fairweather disclosed a prior professional engagement with Coppertown and abstained under Charter §4.05.
Tally: For 5 (Tannenbaum, Coppinger, Drewes, Quineport, Phelpsworth). Against 1 (Ngengembulu). Abstain 1 (Fairweather).
Vote denominator (excluding abstentions): 6. Threshold (2/3 supermajority): 2/3 of 6 = 4 (no rounding needed).
5 ≥ 4. **EXCEPTION CARRIED. AWARD $110,000.**

**Motion 3 — APP-S26-003 (Heatherton Youth Mentoring).**
Motion 3a: Approve exception under §3.05 and award $95,000.
Tally: For 4 (Coppinger, Fairweather, Phelpsworth, Ngengembulu). Against 3 (Tannenbaum, Drewes, Quineport). Abstain 0.
Vote denominator: 7. Threshold (2/3 supermajority): 2/3 of 7 = 4.67, rounded up to 5.
4 < 5. **EXCEPTION FAILED.**

Motion 3b: Award $75,000 standard under §3.04 (fall-back motion).
Tally: For 7. Against 0. Abstain 0.
Vote denominator: 7. Threshold (simple majority): more than half of 7, i.e., 4.
7 ≥ 4. **MOTION CARRIED. AWARD $75,000.**

**Motion 4 — APP-S26-004 (Pelican Bay Conservation Trust).**
Per Counsel Memorandum HFF-CC-2026-0415 (§5 above), the requested $135,000 exceeds the §3.05 hard cap and may not be voted as filed. The exception request as filed therefore FAILS by threshold arithmetic.

Motion 4a (alternative): Approve exception under §3.05 and award $125,000 (the maximum permissible).
Tally: For 6 (Tannenbaum, Coppinger, Drewes, Fairweather, Quineport, Ngengembulu). Against 1 (Phelpsworth). Abstain 0.
Vote denominator: 7. Threshold (2/3 supermajority): 2/3 of 7 = 4.67, rounded up to 5.
6 ≥ 5. **EXCEPTION CARRIED. AWARD $125,000.**

**Motion 5 — APP-S26-005 (Vermillion Arts Collective).**
Motion: Award $62,000 under §3.04.
Tally: For 7. Against 0. Abstain 0. **MOTION CARRIED. AWARD $62,000.**
