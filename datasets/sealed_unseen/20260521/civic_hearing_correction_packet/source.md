Public notice was published in the Brassfield Weekly Sentinel on March 12 and March 19, 2026, and posted at Town Hall, the Annex, and the public library on March 11, 2026, in accordance with RSA 676:7.

- Thaddeus Vong, Member — present

### §2. Application Summary (Filed by Quintrell Holdings LLC)

Variance sought:
1. Side-yard setback relief from the required 15 feet to a proposed 8.5 feet (a variance of 6.5 feet).
2. Lot-coverage exceedance from the permitted 28% to a proposed 33.2% (an excess of 5.2 percentage points).

Purpose: Construction of a two-story addition on the east elevation of the existing single-family dwelling at 88 Heron Ridge Road. The addition would include an accessory dwelling unit on the upper level.

Underlying zoning: R-A (Residential, Low-Density). Lot area: 0.71 acres.

---

### §3. Staff Report and Recommendation (Document SR-VAR-2026-019)

Authored by Town Planner Suniti Aravamudan and dated March 19, 2026.

The Town Planner's office reviewed the application and submitted the following recommendation to the BZBA: **"Staff recommends approval with conditions."** The recommended conditions are listed in §6 below.

Building Inspector Dirk Ozolins co-signed the report and noted that the proposed structure satisfies all applicable Building Code requirements but cannot be issued a permit without zoning relief.

---

### §4. Public Comment (Sworn or affirmed in person)

The Chair opened the public hearing at 7:18 PM. Comments are summarized from the verbatim transcript (Audio File BZBA-AUDIO-2026-0326-T2). The Clerk recorded the following speakers:

**Beulah Mainwaring**, residing at 92 Heron Ridge Road (direct abutter). Speaking time 4 minutes. Objection. Stated concern that the proposed addition would create a "wall effect" along the shared east-west property line and that the reduced setback would impair her property's natural drainage during spring runoff. Submitted a written statement (Exhibit PC-1) reiterating drainage concern and requesting denial.

**Carwyn Tjelmeland**, residing at 14 Linden Crossing, speaking on behalf of the Heron Ridge Neighborhood Association. Speaking time 5 minutes. Objection. Stated that the requested variance would set "an undesirable precedent" for other lots on the road, several of which the Association believes are watching this docket. Requested that the BZBA condition any approval on a written non-precedent finding.

**Yusra Penhollow**, residing at 87 Heron Ridge Road. Speaking time 3 minutes. Objection. Objected specifically to the lot-coverage exceedance, stating that the cumulative impervious surface on the block was already a concern after the 2024 driveway widening permits and that adding 5.2% more coverage would, in her words, "tip the block over the line." Did not object to the setback portion of the application.

**Eveline Drakos**, residing at 211 Glenholm Road (non-abutter, within 500 feet). Speaking time 2 minutes. Support. Spoke to general support for accessory dwelling units as a regional housing strategy.

**Manfred Stenglein**, residing at 102 Heron Ridge Road. Speaking time 3 minutes. Support. Spoke to the precedent question raised by Tjelmeland, observing that nearby variances had been granted on Beech Court in 2019 and Tamarack Lane in 2022 without "neighborhood collapse," and urging the BZBA not to deny on precedent grounds alone.

The Chair closed public comment at 7:43 PM.

---

### §5. Board Discussion

Members discussed the five criteria for variance under RSA 674:33-a. Member Klessig observed that the hardship criterion was met by virtue of the irregular lot orientation. Member Vong expressed concern about lot coverage but stated he was prepared to accept the staff's drainage-condition language. Member Halvorsen stated she would vote to deny on the basis of the lot-coverage exceedance alone. Alternate Castelletti (seated) made brief comments in support.

---

### §6. Motion and Vote (As recorded in Draft Minutes DM-2026-0326)

At 8:11 PM, Chair Persaud moved approval of VAR-2026-019 with the following conditions:
1. Drainage swale construction along east elevation, to specifications acceptable to Building Inspector.
2. Lot coverage capped at 33.2% (no further expansion without new variance).
3. ADU occupancy limited to one (1) household.
4. Decorative lighting on east elevation to be downward-shielded.

Member Vong seconded.

**Draft minutes vote tally (as recorded April 1, 2026, in DM-2026-0326):**
- For: Persaud, Klessig, Vong, Castelletti (4)
- Against: Halvorsen (1)
- Recused: Bromberg (1)
- Tally: 4–1
- Motion: CARRIED

The Chair declared the variance APPROVED with conditions. The hearing adjourned at 8:24 PM.

### §8. First Correction Notice — CORR-BZBA-2026-001 (April 8, 2026)

Issued by Town Clerk Halsey Drummond. Reason: post-adoption audio review.

Findings:
- Audio review of File BZBA-AUDIO-2026-0326-T2 (timestamp 1:14:37) discloses that Alternate Castelletti was not formally seated by motion of the Chair prior to the casting of votes on the substantive motion. BZBA Rules of Procedure §2.4(c) requires that "an alternate shall be seated for a docket by motion of the Chair, which motion shall be reflected in the minutes." Although the Chair orally directed Castelletti to be seated at the convening (§1 above), §2.4(c) requires that the seating be made by motion and reflected at the point of the substantive vote, which did not occur.
- Therefore, the vote of Alternate Castelletti is hereby VOIDED from the official record.
- The corrected vote tally is:
  - For: Persaud, Klessig, Vong (3)
  - Against: Halvorsen (1)
  - Recused: Bromberg (1)
  - Voted Castelletti: VOIDED
  - Corrected tally: **3–1**
- Quorum status: Four (4) regular members participated in the corrected vote. Quorum was therefore maintained.
- Motion outcome unchanged: CARRIED.

The variance VAR-2026-019 remains APPROVED with the conditions in §6.

### §10. Second Correction Notice — CORR-BZBA-2026-002 (April 23, 2026)

Issued by Town Clerk Halsey Drummond. Reason: clarification of appeal window.

Initial position (as informally communicated to Ms. Mainwaring on April 22): the appeal window under RSA 677:2 runs from the date of the BZBA decision (March 26, 2026), and the standard period is twenty (20) days, which would have closed on April 15, 2026. The appeal filed April 21 would therefore have been untimely.

Correction (April 23): On further review with Town Counsel Marisol Gathwright, Esq., the Clerk identifies that the BZBA's adopted bylaws (BZBA Bylaws, §6.2, adopted 2017) establish a thirty (30) day appeal window for ZBA matters, which is permissible under RSA 677:2 as a longer-than-statutory but lawful local extension. The thirty-day window from March 26, 2026, closes on April 25, 2026.

The appeal filed April 21, 2026 is therefore TIMELY.

---

### §11. Third Correction Notice — CORR-BZBA-2026-003 (April 28, 2026)

Issued by Town Clerk Halsey Drummond. Confirmation of CORR-BZBA-2026-002 after Town Counsel formal opinion (TC-OP-2026-014) dated April 27, 2026.

Counsel's formal opinion affirms:
1. RSA 677:2 sets a default twenty-day appeal period but expressly permits local boards to extend by bylaw.
2. BZBA Bylaws §6.2, adopted by the Board in October 2017 and on file with the Clerk, sets the appeal window at thirty (30) days.
3. Mainwaring's appeal of April 21 falls within the thirty-day window.

Appeal AP-2026-005 is confirmed timely. Status of VAR-2026-019: APPROVED, PENDING APPEAL.
