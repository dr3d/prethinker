### §1. Recall Summary

On April 4, 2026, Sayfield Biomedical, Inc. issued Field Bulletin SBI-FB-2026-114 advising customer institutions of a firmware-related anomaly affecting certain TQ-9 portable infusion pump units. The bulletin identified firmware version 2.11.3 as the affected version and named three production lots as potentially affected:

- TQ9-LOT-2025-0312
- TQ9-LOT-2025-0418
- TQ9-LOT-2025-0521

Sayfield categorized the action as a Class II voluntary correction. The remedy specified is field deployment of firmware patch v2.11.5. Bulletin states that pumps running v2.11.5 are considered remediated and may be returned to service following local biomedical engineering verification.

On April 10, 2026, Sayfield issued supplemental Field Bulletin SBI-FB-2026-114-S1, narrowing the affected lot list. The supplemental bulletin states that lot 0521 shipped from a separate production line using firmware v2.11.4 (not v2.11.3) and is therefore NOT subject to the recall. Only lots 0312 and 0418 remain subject.

### §4. Nurse Reports (Source-Attributed Claims)

The following reports were filed during the inventory review window. Each report is attributed to its filing source. These reports are not findings; they are source-attributed observations and the manufacturer field service team will adjudicate them.

**Report NR-2026-031, filed April 4, 2026 by RN Ojárrè Bibulwicz, Floor 2A.**
Bibulwicz reports: "On April 3, 2026, at 03:14 local time, Pump TQ9-S/N-A7831 alarmed mid-infusion with error code E-32. Infusion was paused and the unit was swapped for a backup. No patient harm observed; patient remained stable. Pump was returned to BMET for inspection."

**Report NR-2026-033, filed April 6, 2026 by RN Phaedra Kontoleon, Floor 3B.**
Kontoleon reports: "Pump TQ9-S/N-A8104 showed pressure drift on two consecutive shifts (April 5 day shift and April 5 night shift, observed across handoff). Drift was within ±2.5 mL/hr of programmed rate. Reported to charge nurse and BMET on April 6 morning."

**Report NR-2026-035, filed April 7, 2026 by RN Wamukota Indaharu, Floor 4C.**
Indaharu reports: "Pump TQ9-S/N-A7990 (lot 0312) operated continuously through April 1 through April 6 patient encounters. No anomalies observed. No alarm events recorded. No drift observed by visual check."

**BMET Note BMET-NOTE-2026-019, filed April 8, 2026 by Stoneleigh Trumbauer, BMET.**
Trumbauer comments on the three preceding nurse reports: "Preliminary opinion: All three reports are consistent with a firmware-level anomaly affecting alarm-generation and pressure-sensor polling, not hardware fault. Recommend hold pending Sayfield bulletin and patch deployment. The Indaharu negative report does not exclude latent firmware vulnerability; the device may simply not have hit the triggering condition during her observation window."

Trumbauer's note is the BMET's opinion and is not a manufacturer finding; the operative authority on whether a device is affected remains the Sayfield bulletin chain (SBI-FB-2026-114 and SBI-FB-2026-114-S1).

### §5. Internal Correction Notice WAIC-CORR-2026-0411

Issued April 11, 2026 by Erskine Drabolwicz, PharmD, Director of Pharmacy, Wheelock AIC.

Subject: Adjustment of hold status for TQ-9 units from lot TQ9-LOT-2025-0521.

Background: The initial Sayfield Field Bulletin SBI-FB-2026-114 (April 4, 2026) named lot 0521 among the affected lots, and on that basis the Wheelock AIC inventory of April 8 placed all lot-0521 units on PROVISIONAL HOLD pending verification. Sayfield's supplemental Field Bulletin SBI-FB-2026-114-S1 (April 10, 2026) corrected the affected-lot list: lot 0521 shipped with firmware v2.11.4 (not the vulnerable v2.11.3) and is NOT subject to the recall.

Action: Effective immediately, all five (5) TQ-9 units in the Wheelock AIC inventory from lot TQ9-LOT-2025-0521 (rows ROW-INV-010 through ROW-INV-014) are RELEASED to service. No firmware patch is required for these units, as their installed firmware v2.11.4 is not subject to the recall.

The corrected affected-lot count at Wheelock AIC is therefore two (2) lots: TQ9-LOT-2025-0312 and TQ9-LOT-2025-0418. The corrected on-hold count is nine (9) units.
