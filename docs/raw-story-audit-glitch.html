The Glitch in the Airlock

Jax was a freelance space-salvager with neon-blue hair and a bad habit of borrowing oxygen without asking.
While scouting the Nebula Sector, her thrusters flickered out, forcing her to dock at a sleek, chrome-plated dome floating on an asteroid.

The door hissed open. "Hello? Is this station occupied?" Jax asked. Silence.

The Fuel Cells

In the galley, three canisters of Liquid Star-Fuel were plugged into the charging station. Jax was running on empty, so she took a sip.

The Mega-Cell: She tapped into the heavy-duty industrial tank. "Too radioactive!" she gagged, her teeth glowing green.

The Eco-Cell: She tried the mid-sized organic tank. "Too sluggish," she complained, feeling like she had swallowed molasses.

The Nano-Cell: She tried the tiny, shimmering vial. It was just right. Her jetpack hummed with perfect, crystalline energy.

The Gravity Boots

Jax wandered into the mudroom, where three pairs of Anti-Gravity Boots were docked.

The Titan Boots: She stepped into the massive magnetic boots. "Too heavy!" She could not even lift her feet off the floor.

The Hover-Slippers: She tried the medium pair. "Too floaty!" She bounced off the ceiling and hit her head.

The Sonic-Zips: She slid into the smallest pair. They were just right. She zoomed around the room with effortless grace until POP: she pushed them too hard and blew a fuse, leaving the boots a charred mess.

The Sleep Pods

Exhausted from the caffeine-like star-fuel, Jax found the sleeping quarters.

The Cryo-Chamber: It was freezing. "Too cold," she shivered.

The Memory-Foam Cloud: It was so soft she felt like she was suffocating in a giant marshmallow. "Too squishy."

The Bio-Hammock: She climbed into the smallest mesh net. It was just right. The gentle swaying lulled her into a deep, dreamless sleep.

The Return of the Circuit-Family

The airlock cycled open. Back from their morning spacewalk were Unit-Alpha (The Fatherbot), Unit-Beta (The Motherbot), and Widget (The Childbot).

"My sensors indicate a breach!" boomed Unit-Alpha. "Someone has been siphoning my high-grade isotopes!"
"And someone has been tasting my organic fuel!" chirped Unit-Beta.
"Someone drank my Nano-Cell," squeaked Widget, "and the vial is empty!"

They floated into the mudroom.
"Someone tried to calibrate my Titan Boots," clanked Unit-Alpha.
"Someone left scuff marks on my Hover-Slippers," whirred Unit-Beta.
"Someone wore my Sonic-Zips and totally fried the motherboards!" Widget buzzed sadly.

They drifted into the sleeping quarters.
"A biological entity has been in my Cryo-Chamber," noted Unit-Alpha.
"My Memory-Foam is still compressed," said Unit-Beta.
"There's a human in my Bio-Hammock!" Widget shouted, his sirens going off. BEEP! BEEP! BEEP!

Jax bolted upright, saw three glowing optical sensors staring at her, and did not wait for an explanation. She activated her jetpack, performed a perfect zero-gravity backflip through the airlock, and vanished into the starfield.

Widget watched her go. "I hope she leaves a review on the Galactic Map," he whirred. "She really liked my fuel."
