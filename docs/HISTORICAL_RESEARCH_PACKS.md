# Historical Research Packs

Last updated: 2026-05-07

Some research packs are no longer active frontiers, but they remain valuable.
They preserve the pressure that taught the current instrument how to separate
semantic proposal from durable admission.

## Lava Packs

The `semantic_ir_lava_pack_*` files and `scripts/run_semantic_ir_lava_sweep.py`
come from the ladder-rung era: broad mixed-domain stress tests with noise,
context switches, truth-maintenance pressure, source fidelity traps, rule
exceptions, multilingual pressure, and admission-safety checks.

They are not the current fixture-farming frontier. Current work has moved toward
source-specific compiled KB artifacts, story-world fixtures, rule acquisition,
clarification eagerness, cross-fixture repair slices, and portable Autolab
cycles.

Do not delete the Lava material casually:

- it records useful calibration history;
- it still exercises mixed-domain Semantic IR behavior;
- it can smoke-test parser/router/admission regressions;
- it contains examples of hazards that later became named lenses, guards, and
  uncertainty distinctions.

But do not cite Lava v5 as current product evidence. Treat it as historical
calibration capital: data that helped get here, not the main proof of where the
system is now.

Current front-door evidence should prefer:

- compiled KB artifact fixtures;
- story-world and enterprise-guidance QA scorecards;
- clarification-eagerness batteries;
- cross-fixture repair slices;
- model-transfer and portability evidence once those lanes exist.
