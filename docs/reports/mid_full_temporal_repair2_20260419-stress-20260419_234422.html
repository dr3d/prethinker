The Greenhouse at Westhaven Commons

Westhaven Commons sat between the old canal and the Saturday tram stop. The nonprofit campus had five places people mentioned by name: Market Hall, Glasshouse A, the Tool Barn, the Canal Classroom, and Lot 12, a narrow teaching plot beside the footbridge. In January 2021, the director of the Commons was Nora Lin. Nora lived in a small apartment above Market Hall with her son Ben Lin, except on storm weeks when Ben sometimes stayed closer to school. The grounds supervisor was Mateo Ruiz. Mateo lived in Cedar House at the edge of the property with his partner Hana Bell and Hana's daughter June Bell. The bookkeeper was Elias Shore, who rented a room at the Finch Inn. Priya Das, a city planner, chaired the grants committee that worked with the nonprofit but did not manage day-to-day operations. Willa Quade ran the seed library out of the Canal Classroom.

The Westhaven Trust charter had two clauses that seemed dull until they mattered. First, if the greenhouse manager was absent for more than twenty-one consecutive days, the assistant manager automatically became acting manager until the board voted otherwise. Second, if annual operating surplus exceeded eighty thousand crowns, then fifteen percent had to be transferred into the roof reserve before any staff stipends were paid. A separate lease covered Lot 12 and the educational cart parked beside it. The lease said the cart could stay with the tenant only if maintenance logs were filed every quarter. If two quarterly logs were missed, title to the cart reverted to the trust even if the tenant still physically used it.

In the spring of 2021, Nora hired Leif Morrow as greenhouse manager for Glasshouse A. Leif's assistant manager was Amara Cole. Leif's older sister, Dr. Sana Morrow, directed River Clinic on the east side of town. Ben Lin boarded at Hillcrest School two nights a week during flood season because the tram bridge occasionally closed after hard rain. Mateo kept the keys to the Tool Barn and to the pump shed behind the glasshouse.

In September 2021, the trust accepted a public micro-grant for Glasshouse A. The grant required at least two free public classes every month for eighteen months. Willa Quade and Hana Bell ended up teaching most of those classes, usually seed-starting, preserving herbs, or winter soil care. Elias Shore kept the attendance sheets because the grant required proof that the classes were open to any resident, not only paying members.

A February storm in 2022 broke part of the canal wall and flooded the lower path. Mateo and Amara moved trays of seedlings into Market Hall overnight. Leif was not there; he had gone to Port Sterling to care for Sana after surgery and stayed away for twenty-six days. On day twenty-two of Leif's absence, the charter made Amara acting manager. During those acting days, she signed a compost supply contract with Greenthread Supplies so the spring planting would not fail. Nora complained that Amara should have waited. The board later reviewed the contract and ratified it because the charter had authorized her to sign it.

In June 2022, Elias closed the yearly books and reported an operating surplus of ninety-two thousand crowns. He suggested delaying the roof reserve transfer until late summer because one delivery truck needed repairs. Priya and Willa pointed back to the charter. Fifteen percent had to move before stipends, so thirteen thousand eight hundred crowns were transferred into the roof reserve that month.

In November 2022, Nora allowed a delivery vendor to store empty produce crates in the North Shed next to Market Hall. The vendor was publicly called Blue Ramp Couriers, but some invoices came in under the name Blue Ramp Logistics. The mailing address and tax identification number were the same on both sets of paperwork. Elias noticed the duplication and mentioned it once, but he did not push the issue because the autumn market season was busy and the crates themselves were not missing.

In March 2023, Leif resigned and accepted a job in another city. Amara Cole became the full greenhouse manager. Around the same time, her younger brother Tomas Cole moved into the Cedar House annex to help Mateo repair storm damage on the boundary fence. Ben Lin stopped boarding at Hillcrest once the tram bridge was reinforced and the flood detours ended.

Lot 12 became contentious later that year. The tenant on paper was Hana Bell because she had started the youth garden program there. During 2023, however, Hana missed two quarterly maintenance logs for the educational cart. She missed them while helping Mateo recover from a broken ankle after he slipped off the footbridge stairs. Because two logs were missing, title to the cart reverted to the Westhaven Trust in October 2023 even though Hana continued using the cart for classes until the annual review.

In January 2024, an internal audit found that Blue Ramp had billed the Commons twice for one compost delivery, once under Blue Ramp Couriers and once under Blue Ramp Logistics. Nora was criticized for approving invoices too quickly, but she was not accused of theft. She stepped down as director in April 2024 under pressure from the board. Priya Das became interim director until the annual meeting. Elias Shore left for a bank job that same month, and Mira Sol took over as bookkeeper.

At the annual meeting in July 2024, Priya was elected permanent director of Westhaven Commons. Mira's review confirmed that title to the educational cart had reverted properly in October 2023. The trust reassigned the cart to the free class program rather than to Hana personally, and Willa Quade became the cart's formal custodian. Mateo remained grounds supervisor. Hana shifted more of her classes into the Canal Classroom while roof repairs closed part of Market Hall.

By May 2025, the Commons looked steadier than it had two years earlier. The free classes in Glasshouse A had exceeded the micro-grant requirement. The roof reserve had paid for repairs to Market Hall. Amara still managed the greenhouse. Priya still directed the Commons. Nora no longer held office but volunteered at the weekend market. Blue Ramp no longer had a vendor contract. June Bell won the youth horticulture award with herbs she had grown using seedlings started from the reassigned educational cart.
