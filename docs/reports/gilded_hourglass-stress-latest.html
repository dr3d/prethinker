The Scene: The private study of Horologist Elias Thorne. The room is a perfect hexagon with a single heavy oak door, bolted from the inside. There are no windows, only a reinforced ventilation grate (4 inches wide) in the ceiling.

The Victim: Elias Thorne. Found slumped over his desk at 10:15 PM.
Cause of Death: A single stab wound to the back with a thin, triangular blade.
The Clue: On his desk is a Gilded Hourglass that runs for exactly 14 minutes. It was found with all the sand in the bottom bulb.

The Timeline and Evidence:

9:00 PM: The Master Clock in the hall chimes. Marcus hears Elias arguing with someone inside, but the voice is muffled.
9:20 PM: Dr. Aris arrives. He claims he spoke to Elias through the door. Elias sounded congested and asked for his blue pills.
9:40 PM: Elena, the niece, brings a tray of tea. Marcus unbolts the door. Elena enters, stays for exactly one rotation of the Gilded Hourglass, and leaves. Marcus re-bolts the door.
10:00 PM: The Master Clock chimes. Marcus hears a heavy thud from inside.
10:15 PM: Marcus and Dr. Aris break down the door. Elias is dead. The tea on the table is ice cold.

The Suspects' Statements:
Marcus (The Guard): I never left my post. I have the only key to the outer bolt. I saw Elena enter at 9:40 PM. She left when the sand ran out. I saw her hands; they were empty.
Dr. Aris: I am colorblind, but I know Elias takes blue pills for his heart. I was in the library from 9:30 PM until 10:15 PM. The library is directly above the study.
Elena: Uncle Elias was fine when I left at 9:54 PM. He was sitting in his chair, writing. I did not touch the hourglass; it was already running when I got there.

Stress Test Discrepancies:
1) Temperature paradox: If Elena left at 9:54 PM and the body was found at 10:15 PM, why is the tea ice cold in a room kept at 75F? Standard tea takes about 45 minutes to lose all heat.
2) Hourglass logic: Elena says the hourglass was already running when she arrived (9:40 PM). It lasts 14 minutes. If it finished at 9:54 PM, that fits. But Marcus says the sand was at the bottom when they broke in at 10:15 PM. If the thud was at 10:00 PM, why was the glass not reset?
3) Medical lie: Dr. Aris claims he told Elias to take blue pills at 9:20 PM. Elias's heart medication is actually bright red.
4) Acoustic trap: Marcus heard a thud at 10:00 PM. The library is directly above the study, connected via a 4-inch vent.

Question for reasoning:
Can the system reconcile thermal inconsistency, hourglass timing, and false medication details without inventing unsupported facts?
