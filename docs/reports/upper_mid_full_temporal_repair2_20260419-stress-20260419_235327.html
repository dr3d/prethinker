The Observatory Residency at Pineglass Ridge

Pineglass Ridge Field Station stood above Lake Orin where the road narrowed into spruce and slate. People in town usually named four places on the property: Birch Lodge, the North Dome observatory, the Map Vault, and South Cabin near the trail gate. In January 2022, the station director was Celeste Rowan. Celeste used an office in Birch Lodge and shared the lodge's rear suite with her daughter Pia Rowan on the weekends when Pia was not at school. The resident astronomer and observatory curator was Arun Dev, who lived in the apartment inside North Dome. Lena Ortiz served as archivist in the Map Vault. Theo Marsh was the trail ranger and lived in South Cabin with his spouse Mara Marsh and their son Eli Marsh. Malcolm Reed handled operations and maintenance for the station. Irene Shaw, the donor trustee whose family endowment had helped keep the property open, oversaw covenant compliance but did not run the station day to day.

Two governance documents shaped later events. The observatory covenant said that if the curator was absent for more than thirty consecutive days, the deputy curator automatically became acting curator until the board voted otherwise. The revenue covenant said that if annual licensing revenue exceeded one hundred twenty thousand crowns, then twenty-five percent had to be transferred to the trail fund before any fellow honoraria were paid. A housing rule also applied to the North Dome apartment: it could be occupied only by the active curator, the curator's spouse, or a dependent child. Separately, the mapping program used a survey drone named Kestrel. The drone could remain assigned to the mapping program only if its annual certification was filed by September 30. If certification lapsed, title to Kestrel reverted immediately to the station.

In spring 2022, Pineglass launched a night-sky residency that paired astronomy lectures with archive workshops. Arun Dev selected Selene Park as deputy curator for the observatory. Lena Ortiz ran most of the archive sessions. During heavy snow closures, Pia Rowan boarded at Lakeview Academy rather than trying to reach Birch Lodge over the mountain road. Theo Marsh kept the keys to the trail gatehouse and the avalanche shed below the east ridge.

In August 2022, wildfire smoke closed the summit road and delayed several resident fellows. Arun left Pineglass for thirty-five days to care for his father in Amber City. On day thirty-one of Arun's absence, the covenant made Selene Park acting curator. During those acting days, she signed a two-year imaging license with Northstep Imaging so the observatory could keep remote telescope sessions running. Malcolm Reed argued that the deal was too large for an acting officer. Irene Shaw later confirmed that the covenant authorized Selene to sign it.

At the end of 2022, Pineglass reported licensing revenue of one hundred forty-eight thousand crowns. Malcolm wanted to postpone the trail fund transfer because the backup generator in Birch Lodge needed work before winter deepened. Lena Ortiz and Irene Shaw insisted that the covenant came first. Twenty-five percent of the year's licensing revenue, thirty-seven thousand crowns, was transferred into the trail fund before fellow honoraria were paid.

By mid-2023, the station had another problem. An outside surveyor named Jonah Kade submitted terrain invoices under two business names, Northstep Imaging and Northstep Visuals. The mailing address and bank account were the same on both sets of invoices. Lena flagged the duplication, but the station did not suspend Jonah's access because Theo urgently needed slope maps after a bridge washout damaged the lower trail.

In April 2023, Arun Dev resigned to accept a university post. Selene Park became the full curator of North Dome. Because she now held the curator role formally, she moved into the North Dome apartment under the housing rule. A visiting fellow named Noor Aziz, who had been sleeping in the observatory's spare room during a residency block, moved instead into the Birch Lodge attic.

The drone Kestrel caused a separate dispute that autumn. Its certification filing was due on September 30, 2023. Malcolm Reed forgot to file it while recovering from a collapsed catwalk injury. Because the certification was not filed on time, title to Kestrel reverted to Pineglass Ridge Field Station on October 1, 2023. Jonah Kade nevertheless kept borrowing the drone through November for ridge scans, even though the mapping program no longer held title to it.

In January 2024, a donor audit concluded that Jonah had billed Pineglass twice for the same ridge scan, once under each Northstep name, and had used Kestrel after the drone's title had reverted to the station. Celeste Rowan was criticized for approving reimbursements too quickly, but she was not accused of fraud. She resigned as station director in March 2024. The board named Irene Shaw interim director. Malcolm remained operations chief. Lena Ortiz was asked to lead the internal records review.

At the board meeting in June 2024, Lena Ortiz was elected permanent director of Pineglass Ridge. Irene returned to trustee oversight. That same summer, Theo Marsh moved his family out of South Cabin for three months while the washed-out bridge and utility line were repaired. The move did not end his cabin assignment because the ranger covenant allowed temporary relocation under four months when the station itself ordered the move. Around that same period, Pia Rowan stopped boarding at Lakeview Academy because the shuttle route resumed regular winter service.

In September 2024, Selene Park spent thirty-three days in Harbor City after Noor Aziz underwent surgery and needed support. On day thirty-one of Selene's absence, the observatory covenant made Noor Aziz acting curator. During those acting days, Noor approved a new batch of student telescope access cards for the residency program. When Selene returned, she accepted the approvals as valid.

A rights dispute was finally settled in February 2025. The board determined that drone Kestrel belonged to Pineglass Ridge Field Station, not to Jonah Kade or the mapping program. It also determined that Northstep Imaging and Northstep Visuals should be treated as the same contractor record. The trail fund paid for the rebuilt footbridge. By then, Lena remained director, Selene remained curator, Noor remained deputy curator, Theo remained trail ranger, Malcolm remained operations chief, and Celeste Rowan taught two public weekend seminars as a volunteer rather than as an officer.
